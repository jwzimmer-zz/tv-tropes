Significant Birth Date

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Laconic
  • Quotes
  • Create New

If the birthday of a character is included in a work, then it is either plot-relevant or significant for the character. Or both.

In Real Life, people are born on random dates. In fiction, while it's possible for the creator to just choose a random date, if a character's date of birth is given (even if it's just in supplementary material), then nine times out of ten, it has some sort of symbolic meaning. This can be a character obsessed with romance being born on February 14th, or an unlucky character being born on Friday the 13th. The meaning can also be ironic, such as a bright and cheerful character being born on October 31st. It can also, owing to When the Planets Align, have actual meaning.

Advertisement:

May lead to an I Remember Because.... Can often overlap with Significant Reference Date, if the birthday is a reference to some real-world date, such as the date of their first appearance, the premiere/publication of some work within the franchise, the birthday of the person who inspired their creation, etc.

    Anime & Manga 

In General:

  • Based on a sample of about 1800 anime and manga characters (in 2004), the most overrepresented date is February 29, occuring about eight times as often as random chance would predict. Second is March 3 (7), followed by May 5 (5), January 1 (4.6), April 1 (4.4), December 24 (4.1), July 7 (4), June 6 (3.8), August 8/October 10 (3.6), and April 4/December 25 (3). note February 29 is Leap Day. March 3 is Girls' Day (Hina Matsuri). May 5 is Children's Day. January 1 is New Year's Day. April 1 is April Fools' Day and the start of Miyako Odori. December 24 is Christmas Eve. July 7 is Tanabata. June 6, August 8, and October 10 are days that can be written as X/X (6/6, 8/8, and 10/10) ... so are March 3 and July 7, for that matter. April 4 is written 4/4, and 4 is very unlucky in Chinese and Japanese culture. December 25 is Christmas Day.

Works:

  • Aoi from Ai Yori Aoshi was born on the day of Tanabata, which celebrates the once-a-year meeting of Orihime (Vega) and Hikoboshi (Altair) across the Milky Way. It's the Asian equivalent to Valentine's Day.
  • Attack on Titan joined in this trope with the release of the second official Guidebook.
    • Levi was born on December 25th.
    • Armin's birthday is November 3rd, which is celebrated in Japan as Culture Day.
    • Krista was born on January 15th, the original date Coming Of Age day was celebrated in Japan (1948 - 2000).
    • Reiner shares his birthday, August 1st, with the unofficial "holiday" known as Yaoi Day.
  • In Axis Powers Hetalia, character birthdays are often a country's Independence Day. This is because said characters are personified countries.
  • In Bakuman。, Azuki's birthday is November 5. This has absolutely no bearing on the plot of Bakuman。, but it seems to be the creators' olive branch to the month of November after Death Note, which had particularly nasty developments occur in all three Novembers featured in the series, and especially November 5.
  • Interesting variation in Bleach: Rangiku Matsumoto did not have a birthday until her childhood friend Gin found out and made the day they met her birthday.
    • The reason for that is because she never really counted days until she met Gin.
  • Played With in Blue Exorcist: Rin and Yukio celebrate their birthday on December 27, two days after Christmas, so they grew up celebrating the two events together. Given that the pair are both Anti Anti Christs, a Christmas birthday seems to have some interesting symbolism.
  • All characters in Bungou Stray Dogs share their birthdate with the author they're named after. This has become such a thing that it is a sure-fire way for fans to determine the birthday of characters who didn't get officially named one, but are still named after a writer. For example, Andre Gide is assumed to have his birthdate on November 22.
  • In The Cherry Project, Chieri and her love interest share the same birthday.
  • Death Note:
    • What could be a better day for a sugar loving detective to be born than Halloween, the day random strangers give candy to whoever knocks on their door, and being anonymous is perfectly normal?
    • There's also Misa, whose birthday falls on Christmas. Quite appropriate for a character who's childlike, cute and willing to give of herself to others (specifically, Light); this is Inverted, however, by the end of the series, in which her death date is revealed to be Valentine's Day, a little over a year after she lost the love of her life.
  • In Detective Conan, Shinichi's birthday is 4th of May, the day when Sherlock Holmes and Dr. Moriarty supposedly fell of the Reichenbach falls.
  • In Earth's Defense Family, Seiko was born on July 7, the same day as the Japanese festival Tanabata. She notes the irony of being born on a romantic holiday while she's currently in a failing marriage, and spends the day with her not-quite-lover, Hayakawa.
  • In Digimon Savers, Masaru Daimon's birthday is stated to be April 2nd, the same as the airdate of the anime's first episode.
  • Played With in Eden of the East: the main character has Identity Amnesia and discovers among his possessions numerous fake passports. He notices that one, "Takizawa Akira," has a birthday just one day different from Saki, the soon-to-be-Love Interest whom he just met; he takes this as a sign and chooses that as his new identity. Incidentally, both dates were in the final week of the Showa Era, which fits into the show's major theme of building a new society.
  • In Full Metal Panic!, Kaname was born on December 24. So was Tessa. In fact, every Whispered has the same birth date and time, to within three minutes. There's a reason for this...
  • Victorique of Gosick was born on December 25, Christmas Day. Fitting and ironic, considering her intended fate.
  • Xxx Holic
    • Watanuki in was born on April 1st, a date with significance in both the East and the West. It's also a date personally significant to CLAMP; they consider it the anniversary of their founding. Sakura Kinomoto of Cardcaptor Sakura was also born on April 1st, which is a pretty important coincidence... The main character Sakura of Tsubasa Chronicle also has the same birthday. Guess what. It's a plot point.
    • Her brother was born on February 29th, meaning the poor guy could only have a birthday every leap year.
  • Hunter × Hunter:
    • Cheerful Child Gon was born on May 5th (a.k.a. Children's Day in Japan).
    • Killua's birthday (a.k.a. 7/7) falls on Tanabata, a Japanese festival holiday centering around a folklore tale of star-crossed lovers. Killua and the character of Orihime share some similarities, and the beginning of the Tanabata myth also shares some themes with the beginning of Killua's character arc as both are employed by their fathers, both are unhappy in their lives and wish for connections with others that their work prevents, both eventually abandon their work entirely after meeting another person (which displeases their fathers), and both of their fathers (Silva and Orihime's father) allow their children to pursue their important bond, but expect that their children will return to their roles in time.
    • Kurapika's birthday is April 4th. In Japanese culture, the number 4, due to one of it's possible pronunciations, is considered the number of death, and April is the fourth month of the year. This date reflects his determination to apprehend or kill the members of the Phantom Troupe, and he is indeed referred to as a "god of death" in one of their predictions.
    • Leorio's birthday is on March 3rd which is the date the manga first came out. This is more of an irony since Leorio receives the least amount of screen-time out of all of the main characters in the story.
    • All of the four characters listed above share a feature where the number of the month and day of his birthday are the same. This is also true for Hisoka who was born on 6/6 (though his birthday in itself has no significance unless you consider the number 666).
  • Jewelpet: Most Jewelpets have their birthdays in the months equivalent to the birthstones they represent. So Ruby was born in July, Garnet in January, Sapphie in September, Diana (diamond) in April, etc.
  • Juni Taisen: Zodiac War: Every character was born on the same day as their birth month. ex: Tiger was born on January 1, Ox on February 2, and Rat on March 3. The Twelve Criminals from the sequel were also born on these days.
    • Invoked with Snake and Dragon. Despite being twins, they were registered by their parents on October 10 and November 11, respectively.
    • Subverted with Rabbit, whose birthday (and real name) is unknown, but implied to be December 12, which is also the day the tournament takes place.
  • All five members of the student council in Kaguya-sama: Love Is War have their birthdays on holidays (and all of them are on the same day of the month as month of the year).
    • Kaguya's birthday is New Years, a time of new beginnings. She shares her birthday with her Shadow Archetype Maki (and, by extension, Maki's twin brother Mikado).
    • Shirogane's birthday is Chrysanthemum Day. Chrysanthemum's are the symbol of the Imperial house of Japan, which is both fitting with his role as Student Council President, and ironic due to him being a Penny Among Diamonds.
    • Fujiwara and Ishigami were both born on Hina-matsuri.
    • Miko was born on Children's Day.
  • Komi Can't Communicate: Komi's birthday is December 25th.
  • In Lucky Star:
    • Kou Yasaka's birthday is on Setsubun.
    • Akira Kogami: Valentine's day, but not much is made of it.
    • Hiyori's birthday is May 24th; Konata's is the 28th. This proximity gives Hiyori a greater sense of them being kindred spirits. (No word on how Konata perceives it.)
    • The Hiiragi twins' birthday is on Tanabata.
    • Misao's birthday is July 20th, which is the original fixed date of Marine Day (Umi no Hi), formerly Marine Memorial Day (Umi no kinen bi), and still falls on it when the 1st of July is a Wednesday. Hiyori says the day predicts a high-energy life.
    • School Nurse Fuyuki Amahara's is September 17th, which is Respect for the Aged Day (Keiro no Hi) when the 1st falls on a Saturday.
  • Lyrical Nanoha:
    • Nanoha's birthday is March 15, probably the single most ironic date possible. First, it's the Ides of March, a day associated with the antithesis of her life philosophy. Second, it's Hounen Matsuri, a Shinto fertility festival that features the procession of a giant wooden phallus, something she would have no interest in as a lesbian.
    • Hayate Yagami was born June 4th (6/4). It invokes both the Number of the Beast and Four Is Death, which fits her status as master of The Book of Darkness.
  • Mai Hime: Miyu's birthday is July 4th being a Robot Girl made by a secret organization from America. Alyssa's birthday is April 1st being an artificial HiME, also by that secret organization.
  • Every single one of Monthly Girls' Nozaki-kun's seven main characters has a birthdate that has some relevance to their characterization.
    • Nozaki's first name means "Plum boy" and he was born on Plum day (6/6).
    • Sakura was born on Sakura (Cherry Blossom) day (3/27).
    • Mikoshiba is known around school for being a flirty Chick Magnet and was born on Valentine's day (2/14).
    • Kashima often wears costumes because she acts in the drama club and was born on Halloween (10/31).
    • Hori has a leg fetish and was born on Nice-Knee-High's (as in the socks) day (11/28),
    • Seo, who has tons of admirers who have never seen her face, was born on Tanabata (7/7) which celebrates two deities who are star-crossed lovers.
    • Wakamatsu suffers from insomnia and was born on Sleep Science day (9/3).
  • The title character of Naruto was born on October 10th (10/10). When the manga was first serialized, October 10th was Health and Sports Day in Japan, a fitting birthday for the energetic protagonist. This lost some meaning when the holiday was moved to the second Monday in October to create a three-day weekend.
    • Kiba Inuzuka's birthday is July 7th (7/7), as is the case for his loyal nin-dog, Akamaru.
  • According to this timeline of Neon Genesis Evangelion, some of the characters' birthdays just so happen around the same time as various tragedies throughout the show.
    • Kaji is killed on December 7th (the day the Japanese attacked Pearl Harbor), the day before Misato's birthday on December 8th (the day the U.S. declared war on Japan in World War II) and three days after Asuka's birthday on December 4th (the Japanese were preparing their attack on Pearl Harbor).
  • Kaworu is killed on December 25th (Christmas), and the next day, December 26th (Boxing Day), is Toji's birthday as well as the first day of End of Evangelion.
  • Ojamajo Doremi: Hazuki Fujiwara's birthday is on Valentine's Day, and this plays a role in a "Naisho" episode.
  • One Piece:
    • Luffy's birthday is on Children's Day (May 5), Usopp's is April Fool's Day, and Chopper's is Christmas Eve. Sanji's, Franky's, and Brook's are word puns. Zoro's birthday is also a word pun, but it falls on Armistice Day.
    • Nearly every major character has a birthday like this, and almost all were chosen by the fans — Oda just said "Sure, why not?."
  • Ouran High School Host Club does this. Honey, who is older than he looks, was born on February 29, the joke being that he ages only every four years. The twins Hikaru and Kaoru were born on June 9 (6/9), showing that they're alike, but different. Or, you know...
  • Rose of Versailles: Oscar's birthday is on December the 25th. Plus she dies at age 33 on July 14th.
  • Skip Beat!
    • Maria Takarada's birthday is December 24th. Shortly before her fifth birthday, she begged her overseas-working parents to please come home to spend her birthday with her. Unfortunately, this led to her mother dying in an airplane crash on her fifth birthday. She's been rather reluctant to celebrate since.
    • Protagonist Kyouko's birthday happens to be the day after, December 25th.
  • For Slam Dunk, super rookie Rukawa is born on New Year's Day (1/1), The Ace Sendoh on Valentine's Day and protagonist Sakuragi on April Fool's.
  • In Star★Twinkle Pretty Cure, the birthday of every Cure is also the same day as a significant event in space travel.
    • Hikarus birthday is April 12, the day the Soviet Union launched Yuri Gagarin into space.
    • Lalas birthday is July 7, the day of the Tanabata.
    • Elenas birthday is September 8, which is also the premiere date of Star Trek: The Original Series.
    • Madokas birthday is November 23, the day Edwin Hubble made public his discovery of the Andromeda galaxy.
    • Finally, Yunis birthday is October 11, the same day the Apollo 7 mission was launched.
  • Mochizou of Tamako Market was born on 10 October, a day known as the "mochi day"— fitting for the child of mochi makers whose name also means "mochi maker". Tamako herself was born on 31 December (New Years Eve), and is always forgotten because it's the busiest day of the year for mochi makers like her family.
  • Tiger & Bunny has a curious subversion. Barnaby's birthday is on Halloween, a date chock full of implications, but this isn't alluded to at all in his Birthday Episode.
  • Tokyo Ghoul uses this with a few characters.
    • Protagonist Ken Kaneki and the legendary Kishou Arima were both born on December 20th. It turns out to be one of many similarities between them.
    • Shuu Tsukiyama's birthday, March 3rd, is Hina-matsuri.
    • Donato Porpora was born on Valentine's Day.
    • Nimura Furuta's birthday is February 29th, also known as Leap Day. His given name, Nimura, is a play on 2-2-9.
  • On Urusei Yatsura, the absurdly unlucky protagonist Ataru Moroboshi was born on April 13th, the second-unluckiest day of the year in the Japanese calendar, and in the year he was born was also the anniversary of the Buddha's death.
  • All four main characters of Weiß Kreuz have significant birthdays: Youji's is March 3, which is Girls' Day in Japan; Aya's is July 4, American Independence Day, and he lives in the U.S. for a couple of years, plus his Evil Counterpart is American; Ken's December 23, the Emperor's Birthday and the day before Christmas Eve, he's a lapsed Catholic; and Omi is the ever-popular February 29. Interestingly, it's been deduced that the WK anime takes place in 1998. Omi's canon age is 17. Count backwards and you realize that Omi's year of birth wasn't actually a leap year. Of course, Omi Tsukiyono is a made up identity and doesn't technically exist; why should his birthday?

  • According to a Freeze-Frame Bonus in Terminator 2: Judgment Day, John Connor's date of birth is February 28th, 1985, a year after a leap year (1984) and the last day of February in non-leap years.

    Literature 
  • Circleverse: Plant mage Rosethorn was born on Longnight, the winter solstice. Ironic because she's a plant mage, and her leafy friends are mostly all asleep on her birthday. She prefers to celebrate at Midsummer instead for this very reason.
  • The Curious Case of Benjamin Button: Benjamin's birthday was the day World War I ended.
  • In The Dark Is Rising, Will was born on the winter solstice. He was also born under a caul and is the seventh son of a seventh son. No wonder he ends up saving the world.
  • The Dresden Files:
    • Harry Dresden was born on Halloween. His mother deliberately had him around that time so that he'd be born with the ability to affect Outsiders.
    • Horny Devil Thomas Raith was born on Valentine's Day.
  • The main character of The Eight by Katherine Neville was born on April 4, which ties in with a lot of the book's numerology and is also said to be an Islamic holy day.
  • Harry Potter:
    • The Weasley twins — who are known for being practical jokers — were born on April Fool's Day.
    • Tom Marvolo Riddle Jr., better known as Voldemort, was born on New Year's Eve.
    • Harry Potter was born on author J.K. Rowling's birthday, July 31st. And there was a prophecy in book 5 that the Messianic Archetype would be born as the seventh month closes. It's stated that it could also have referred to Neville — born on July 30th.
    • Hermione was born on September 19th, a.k.a. Talk Like a Pirate Day. This is presumably a coincidence, unless Rowling just liked the idea of giving a silly birthday to her most serious main character.
  • In the Horatio Hornblower books, or at least the ones published later, Hornblower was born on July 4, 1776, something which he is occasionally teased about (it otherwise never becomes important). It is worth noting that Hornblower was written to be a man of 20th century sensibilities who is fighting in the Wars of French Revolution and the Napoleonic Wars, and thus has some opinions that are regarded as a touch "Republicist" by those who he has offended (or who just feel like needling the nervous skinny guy for fun). Mind you, the author, C. S. Forester, an Englishman though born in Egypt, eventually moved to California to stay, and was likely aiming the books at an American audience. Making Hornblower the same age as the U. S. — and why not exactly the same age? — helps fix his place in history in the readers' minds.
  • In The Hunger Games, Katniss Everdeen's birthday falls on May 8, the date most European countries celebrate as the day Germany surrendered and World War II came to an end.
  • James Bond nemesis Ernst Stavro Blofeld was born on May 28, 1908. So was Bond creator Ian Fleming.
  • Lolita: Dolores Haze was born on January 1st, 1935 — and "Mrs. Richard F. Schiller" died in childbirth on Christmas Day, 1952, a week before her eighteenth birthday. Dolly Haze, whose childhood was stolen from her, never lived to grow up.
  • In The Lord of the Rings, Bilbo and Frodo were born on the same day, seventy eight years apart. This is part of the reason why Bilbo chose his eleventy-first birthday as the day to leave the Shire - it was also Frodo's thirty-third birthday, which meant that he was now old enough to inherit Bag End without the Sacksville-Bagginses being able to contest Bilbo's will on the grounds of Frodo's youth.
  • Salman Rushdie's novel Midnights Children is about a man who was born on the exact moment of Indian independence — 15 August 1947 at midnight — and who realizes he can communicate telepathically with all the other people in India who were also born on that date.
  • Misery: Annie was born on April Fool's Day.
  • In Something Wicked This Way Comes, the two main characters were born on Halloween and the day after. The day after being All Saints' Day.
  • Till Eulenspiegel: The title character is born on the same day as king Philippe II of Spain. As fate will have it Philippe II of Spain will terrorize the Lower Countries as an adult and the grown up Eulenspiegel will lead the Dutch/Flemish resistance against the Spanish king.

    Live-Action TV

    Real Life 
  • Isaac Newton was born on December 25, 1642. For him, every Christmas is his birthday.
  • In an inversion, at least three famous people died on Christmas Day: W.C. Fields in 1946, Dean Martin in 1995 and Jason Wingreen (voice actor of Boba Fett) in 2015.
  • In another inversion, three US Presidents, John Adams, Thomas Jefferson and James Monroe (the 2nd, 3rd and 5th, respectively), all died on July 4th, while a fourth (Calvin Coolidge, the 30th President) was botn on that date.
  • Metal guitar virtuoso Steve Vai was born on June 6, 1960 (6-6-60). May explain where he got his near-otherworldly skills...
  • Princess Beatrice of York (granddaughter of Queen Elizabeth II) was born on August 8, 1988 (8-8-88). So was Italian basketball player Danilo Gallinari, to the point where he wears #8.
  • Vietnam veteran Ron Kovic was indeed born on July 4, 1946.
  • Muppeteer Steve Whitmire, who succeeded Jim Henson as the performer of Kermit the Frog for many years, was born on September 24, 1959 — which happened to be Henson's 23rd birthday.
  • Similarly, Molly Ringwald was born on February 18, 1968, which was the day John Hughes turned 18.
  • Physicist Stephen Hawking was born on January 8, 1942 - three hundred years to the day after the death of astronomer Galileo Galilei. And inverted with his death date, March 14, 2018 which was Albert Einstein's birthday and Pi Day.
  • Squick to anyone born exactly 9 months after a parent's birthday or anniversary.
  • Gioacchino Rossini was born on February 29, 1792, something pianist and comedian Victor Borge saw as evidence of the composer's laziness - he only had to celebrate his birthday every four years!
  • Andy Kaufman was born January 17, 1949. Jim Carrey, who played Kaufman in Man on the Moon, was born January 17, 1962. (One TV ad for the movie even pointed this out.)
  • Astronaut Ken Mattingly was born in Chicago on March 17, 1936. Gary Sinise, who played Mattingly in Apollo 13 was born March 17, 1955...also in Chicago.
  • Christian Bale (January 30, 1974) played former Vice President Dick Cheney (January 30, 1941) in Vice (2018).
  • Retired Buffalo Bills Quarterback Jim Kelly (February 14, 1960) and his son, Hunter (February 14, 1997).
  • Elvis Presley and David Bowie, two of the most famous Real Life examples of The Rock Star, share a birthday — January 8th (1935 and 1947, respectively). Bowie has joked about his birthday getting stuck in the shadow of Presley's.
  • If we also included significant birth weeks in this trope in regads to date proximity, William Shatner and Leonard Nimoy would certainly fit. They were born four days apart in March 1931 (Shatner on the 22nd, Nimoy on the 26th).
  • Sylvester McCoy (the Seventh Doctor), Sophie Aldred (his companion Ace), and Anthony Ainley (the Master) were all born on the 20th of August, in 1943, 1962, and 1932, respectively. The three of them would go on to star together in the last story of Doctor Who's original series, "Survival".
  • Novelist Emily Brontë, famous for Wuthering Heights, was born on July 30, 1818. Pop singer Kate Bush was born on July 30, 1958, and would later write a hit song called "Wuthering Heights".
  • John Lennon (October 9, 1940) and his son, Sean (October 9, 1975). John considered 9 his lucky number, which explains why he wrote a song called "Revolution #9".
  • Charlie Chaplin was born on April 16, 1889. Adolf Hitler was born four days later on April 20. Even in the 1930s people found it funny that both men had the same toothbrush moustache and looked similar. Chaplin would later satirize Hitler in The Great Dictator. It doesn't help that April 20 is also International Weed Day and the day of the Columbine shootings. note There's been speculation the shooters chose the date deliberately due to being neo-Nazis or to spare the school's stoners who skipped 4/20 to get high; other evidence points towards the shootings having been planned for April 19, the day of the Oklahoma City bombing and the end of the Waco Siege, but delayed due to a cache of ammo being delivered only on the night of the 19th.
  • Stan Laurel (Laurel and Hardy) was born on June 16, 1890. This makes him a Gemini (the "twins" sign), which is fitting for half of the most famous comedic duo in the world.
  • Classic horror film legends Vincent Price and Christopher Lee were both born on May 27th (Price in 1911, Lee in 1922). Lee's fellow Hammer Horror film star Peter Cushing was born on May 26th (in 1913). Additionally, Lee was born a day after the 25th anniversary of the original publication of Dracula, which was one of his most famous roles.
  • Comedian Brian Doyle-Murray was born on Halloween 1945, exactly three weeks after the Chicago Cubs lost the World Series for the last time, not making any further appearances until 2016, when they beat the Cleveland Indians. Doyle-Murray once compared his situation to that of Mark Twain, who famously was born and died around appearances put in by Halley's Comet, and joked that he would die the year the Cubs next made it to the World Series and also about having a deal with God regarding that that he'd get to stick around for the Series itself. (He is still alive in 2019, three years after the Cubs won the World Series.)
  • Actress Mackenzie Davis' date of birth is April Fools' Day 1987, other people who are April Fools Day babies include Debbie Reynolds, Asa Butterfield and Ali MacGraw.
  • A variant of this trope involves people dying on their own birthdays. Elizabeth of York, wife of Henry VII, was born on February 11, 1466 and died on February 11, 1503. William Shakespeare was born on April 23, 1564 and died on April 23, 1616. Ingrid Bergman was born on August 29, 1915 and died on August 29, 1982.
  • In terms of sharing birthdays, it should be noted that statistically speaking, there is a 50/50 chance that a random group of twenty-three people will have at least two people who share a birthday. Increase the size of the group to 75, and the odds increase to 99.9% note The odds don't reach 100% until you have 367 people in one room, due to leap years.
  • Averted (sort of) in real life, data on the average number of births per day in the UK showed that the average number of births per day was generally lower on special occasions than on normal days. In particular, the number of births on New Year's Day, Christmas Day and Boxing Day was hugely decreased compared to the average. This was probably because these are the only bank holidays which occur on a specific date each year, and hospitals usually don't schedule caesareans and induced births for bank holidays. The number of births on April Fool's Day and Christmas Eve were also both lower than average.
  • Jeff Goldblum's first son, Charlie Ocean, was born on July 4, 2015 — fitting for the son of one of the leads of Independence Day! In the runup to the sequel's release Conan O'Brien said "That's fantastic: You had a son to promote the movie you were coming out with a year later!"
