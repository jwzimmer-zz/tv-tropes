What Beautiful Eyes!

Shiny!

"I must admit that I'm still not great at interacting with young men. But I think I am finally able to speak to Master Link while looking him in the eyes! Those wonderful eyes... Naturally I still get a little embarrassed... but I can't help it! I always follow his gorgeous gaze with my own. I blame his beautiful blue eyes. Ugh. This is all so strange..."
— Paya's Diary, The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild
Advertisement:

This is where a character has very unique, "beautiful" eyes. These eyes are one of their main defining characteristics, and many people become enamored with them because of it. Maybe their eyes are an unusual color, or perhaps it's a very piercing, striking hue of a color. Or possibly, their eyes have very pure, untainted emotion inside that other people can clearly see. Whatever the reason, these eyes will be known for being gorgeous and special. In fact, characters will many times come out and tell them that their eyes are beautiful. Occasionally, the character themselves will hate their own eyes because it makes them "different".

"The eyes are the window to the soul" may be a factor.

Warning: Possible sign of a Mary Sue. Be very careful if you want to make that a character trait for one of your own creations.

See also Gaussian Girl, a type of picture that emphasizes the eyes. This trope is the bolder cousin to When She Smiles. The page name isn't meant to imply a threat. Probably.

However, they had quite different reasons and different situations: Saya loved his eyes for the conventional reasons, but Creed loved the eyes that he had as an emotionless, perfect killer.

However, the first time Kyoko sees him without his glasses, she says that she would totally fall for him if she weren't already devoted to Black Cat.

    • Samara has some intensely bright blue eyes, and a lot of her conversations have shots of some close up looks at her face so you get some long looks at them.
    • While we never see her in the flesh, Thane repeatedly flashes back to his first encounter with his late wife, and a constant memory is her "sunset eyes".

  • Katawa Shoujo:
    • Rin has lovely green eyes, according to Hisao. In his own words:

    • Shizune has pretty eyes, described as "deep and alluring blue eyes".
  • Fate/stay night
    • Ilya's red eyes often get loving descriptions.
    • Rider's eyes are described as "unnaturally beautiful". This one comes with a couple of caveats: first, they're not technically her eyes, but a couple of magic gemstones occupying her eye sockets. Second, if you're actually able to see them, you're about to be Taken for Granite. There's a reason Medusa wears a magic blindfold called Gorgon Breaker.
  • Togainu no Chi: Akira's eyes. Rin especially seems to like Akira's eyes but he has also gotten comments from others. Shiki likes Akira's eyes as well.
  • The gods of the zodiac in Star-Crossed Myth are very taken by the protagonist's eyes, which they describe as having stars in them.
  • The first thing that Tyrna notices about Gavin in Sword Daughter is that he has "the most beautiful violet eyes [she's] ever seen."
  • In Shall We Date?: Ninja Shadow, if Eduard is the chosen boyfriend, the Player Character Saori will immediately notice how gorgeous his purple eyes are.

  • In Sinfest, one of the things that makes Criminy so attractive to Fuchsia besides his gentle disposition is that he has "pretty brown eyes".
  • Girl Genius: Othar Tryggvassen, GENTLEMAN ADVENTURER!, goes from an intimidating Genius Bruiser to a full-fledged (if rather bulky) Bishounen simply by removing his visor.
  • In El Goonish Shive, Tedd's eyes are apparently quite beautiful if Tony's reaction after seeing his form without his glasses is any indication.

  • The Nostalgia Critic's huge blue-green eyes. Lampshaded by the Moulin Rouge! review bloopers.

  • The SCP Foundation tale His Kind Eyes plays this trope for drama and horror. The eyes in question belong to 087.

  • Pontoffel Pock, Where Are You?: The entire reason Pontoffel falls for Neefa Feefa is her "winkin' blinkin' eyes".
  • A wolf-like creature in the first chapter of Over the Garden Wall possesses wide, multicolored eyes not unlike the ones in the page image... and otherwise resembles a hairy, slavering monster. Says Greg, panicked, upon seeing it for the first time at close range: "You have beautiful eyes!"

    Real Life 
  • Alexis Bledel◊, Her eyes are one of the few things in colour◊ in Sin City because the director liked them and insisted.
  • Elijah Wood:
    • Many fans asked WETA how they digitally enhanced Elijah Wood's◊ eyes◊ in The Lord of the Rings. They responded, "We didn't".
    • Dominic Monaghan pranked Elijah by conducting a satellite interview posing as a German talk show host. Several times he discusses Elijah's big blue eyes, culminating with, "You have very big eyes, big blue eyes. Do you think this is the reason why you're successful?"
    • On the Fellowship cast commentary, Sean Astin brings up how his daughter was completely fascinated by Wood's eyes when they first met. The documentary features then go into the Irony that Wood may have those big beautiful eyes, but is virtually blind without corrective lenses.
  • Saoirse Ronan has memorably striking pale blue eyes◊, which are usually heavily focused on by the cinematography in her film roles. The Grand Budapest Hotel, for instance, featured a close up facial shot of her staring directly into the camera, surrounded by swirling lights that lit up her eyes from different angles.
  • Paul Newman. Allegedly, the film version of Cat on a Hot Tin Roof was made in color because of his (and Elizabeth Taylor's) famous eyes. There's even a song titled, "I Wish I Had Paul Newman's Eyes."
  • Much of the world had this reaction to National Geographic's famous "Afghan Girl" photo.
  • One contemporary of Alexander Hamilton stated that "These [Hamiltons eyes] were of deep azure, eminently beautiful, without the slightest trace of hardness or severity, and beamed with higher expressions of intelligence and discernment than any others that I ever saw"
  • Elizabeth Taylor was known for her violet eyes and was rather commented about it during life and after her passing.
  • Bette Davis◊ was known for having beautiful eyes. There's even a song about them.
  • Amanda Seyfried has very big green eyes that dominate her face. Tina Fey even said she had "Bette Davis eyes" in the directors commentry for Mean Girls. Several different film reviewers have gone further and stated she has "Anime eyes."
  • Almost everybody who met T. E. Lawrence in person remarked how attractive he was in general and how beautiful his eyes were in particular. Fittingly, he is portrayed by Peter O'Toole in the biopic.
  • British actor Jamie Bamber has gorgeous blue eyes that have become a major trait of every character he's played. To the point where when his Law & Order: UK character Matt Devlin was shot and killed, they were the last we saw of him
  • Cillian Murphy has◊ piercing blue eyes.
    • Batman Begins director Christopher Nolan outright admitted that he kept trying to invent excuses for him to take his glasses off in close-ups.
    • ''Sunshine features many close-up shots of his eyes, especially the scenes when he's in a space suit so his eyes are literally the only thing you see from him.
  • Then there is British actor Benedict Cumberbatch, whose eye color can go◊ from green to blue to gray, depending on the lighting.
  • Travis Fimmel (star of Vikings and formerly a Calvin Klein underwear model) has such intensely blue eyes◊ that many scenes are built around close-ups of them.
  • Daniel Craig is widely known for his piercing blue gaze◊, something which got no shortage of closeups in Casino Royale (2006). In fact, some fans protested his casting as Bond at first because his eyes were considered too pretty for the character (who is supposed to have cold, dark, unfeeling eyes.)
  • Sterling Beaumon is known for his intense stare, particularly in Lost where it was further amplified by his character's thick glasses.
  • One of James McAvoy's most striking features are his bright, baby blue◊ irises. Coupled with his youthful, Pretty Boy looks, they naturally evoke the Innocent Blue Eyes trope, so he is often type cast as the Wide-Eyed Idealist. His baby blues were also acknowledged by InStyle magazine with the title, James McAvoy May Have a Filthy Mouth, But All We Can Focus on Are His Soulful Blue Eyes
    "The movie-star blue eyes, however, command your attention the way a forehead tattoo might. They light up to punctuate profanities (every other word) and stay lit when he makes fun of you for being so skittish."
  • Cody Linley◊ has unusual silver-green eyes that are downright animalistic at times. Unsurprisingly, he has played a feral child and is often cast as the Determinator.
  • Aaron Paul has soulful,◊ crystaline◊ blue◊ Puppy-Dog Eyes, a fact that's even pointed out by a minor character on Breaking Bad.
  • Alexandra Daddario. Zac Efron is one of many bowled over by her amazingly blue eyes.
  • Ana de Armas is known for her unique and unforgettable golden eyes (not golden as in greenish-yellow, as in actually yellow like Commander Data's.) Like the Elijah Wood example above, (since green eyes are a sign of being a Replicant, and because she plays a holographic character) many fans of Blade Runner 2049 assumed that her eyes were digitally edited. They weren't.
  • Henry Cavill has a set of magnificent◊ blue eyes. Is it any wonder he was picked to play Superman?
  • Clark Gregg, aka the MCU's Agent Phil Coulson◊, if the number of fanfics which stop dead to rhapsodise over said eyes are any indication.
  • Roger Taylor of Queen, known for his huge, round, heavy-lidded blue eyes◊ with lashes that, in their day, could have set a Guinness world record for length and volume.
  • Florence Welch has intensely expressive blue-green eyes◊ that shift colors in different lighting. Especially striking against the bright red hair.
  • Grand Duchess Maria Nikolaevna "Marie" Romanova (yes, Anastasia's sister) had a beautiful pair◊ of bright blue eyes known among her family as "Marie's saucers".
  • Tom Hiddleston◊. During an interview for the movie War Horse, Love Actually director Richard Curtis commented that his eyes are so piercing that Jeremy Irvine started crying in the scene where Nicholls buys Joey just to "distract people from the blueness." Even more intensified when he has black hair, for example when he plays Loki◊.
  • Kurt Cobain of Nirvana was known for having beautiful◊ blue eyes which happened to be part of his attractive appeal.
  • Neil Young had dark brown hair as a young man (it's silver now) but his eyes are a surprising sea-blue. Fans who have met him remark on their beauty.
