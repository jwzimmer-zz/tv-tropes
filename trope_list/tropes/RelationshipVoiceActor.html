Relationship Voice Actor

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

A form of Typecasting, linked with Pigeonholed Voice Actor. More notable in Japanese (because of the larger pool of voice actors available), two or more voice actors are often put together in one anime series, sometimes as characters with a special relationship. Just like Pigeonholed Voice Actor, although they're not typecast into one type of character, but if one voice actor shows up, there's a good chance that a certain voice actor will show up too, sometimes as characters with a unique relationship with the first voice actor's characters. And their role relationship will also occasionally be pigeonholed. Many Japanese examples can be explained by the fact that their voice actors are generally managed by talent agencies and it is convenient for production to cast all parts from the same agency. Take note that the examples below may or may not list all the shows in which two actors have worked in together.

Advertisement:

Across the seas, this trope can occur as well. This happens when there's one actor from Country A (mostly Japan) and another actor from Country B (commonly American/English). Two or more shows aired where Actor A played a character, and when the shows were imported to Country B, Actor B plays the same characters played by Actor A. Therefore, it's more akin to a relation of sharing characters.

Also see Those Two Actors for the live action version.

    Cross-Language Examples 
  • Lisa Ortiz shared two characters with French voice actor Shandra Schadt: Lina Inverse, and Amy Rose
  • Akira Ishida and Latin American voice actor Eduardo Garza both voiced Gaara, Falkner and Shinkohyo.
  • Yuri Lowenthal share two characters with Italian voice actor, Patrizio Prata: Ginta Suou and Ogron.
  • Fumiko Orikasa and Venezuelan dub actress Yensi Rivero were both Kirara in Samurai 7 and Seras Victoria from Hellsing.
  • Brazilian voice actor Marcelo Campos shares two roles with Toshihiko Seki: Shurato and Duo Maxwell.
  • Brian Drummond and Brazilian voice actor Alfredo Rollo voiced both Vegeta and Zechs Merquise.
  • Both Son Goku and Trowa Barton were voiced by Kirby Morrow and Wendell Bezerra.
  • Korean voice actor Su-Jin Gang respectively shares the voices for both Inuyasha and Shinichi Kudo with Kappei Yamaguchi.
  • Junko Takeuchi shares two characters with Mexican voice actress Isabel Martiñón, those being Naruto Uzumaki and Gumball Watterson.
  • Tara Strong shares two characters with Mexican voice actress Carla Castañeda, those being Twilight Sparkle and the titular character of Unikitty!

    Other Language Examples 
  • In Mexican dubs, Gerardo Reyero and Mónica Manjarrez sometimes play characters romantically involved with each other, such as Han Solo and Leia Organa in Star Wars and Stu and Didi Pickles in Rugrats. Interestingly, the Manjarrez voiced-Rei Hino also had a short crush on the Reyero-voiced Mamoru Chiba in Sailor Moon.
  • Also, Enzo Fortuny has not one, but two pigeonholed relationships: one with Cristina Hernández (Cardcaptor Sakura) and a more recent one with Leyla Rangel (Inuyasha, Kim Possible).
  • Mexican voice actresses Carla Castañeda and Analiz Sánchez feature together in both My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic as Twilight Sparkle and Rainbow Dash respectively, and Unikitty! as Unikitty and Puppycorn respectively.
  • Philippe Peythieu and Véronique Augereau, the french voices of Homer and Marge Simpson, are married.
  • While not an item in real life, french voice actors Georges Aminel and Arlette Thomas were frequently heard together: Planet of the Apes, The Omen, Tarzoon Shame Of The Jungle and most notably Sylvester the Cat and Tweety Bird.
  • In Spain we have several fiction-couples examples:
    • David Robles and Mar Bordallo, including in LazyTown.
    • Salvador Vidal and Maria Luisa Solá.
      • Maria Luisa Solá has crossed paths with Manolo Garcia quite often too.
    • Roger Pera and Graciela Molina since the Spider-Man Trilogy. Happy Feet, Birdman, Rio... are all proof of this.
    • Manuel Gimeno and Isabel Valls after voicing Edward and Bella in Twilight though they were previously heard in Juno.
    • Nuria Mediavilla and... well... pretty much every single important Spaniard male voice actor of the last 15 years established in Barcelona (and some in Madrid).
    • Sara Vivas and Isacha Mengibar as siblings, with Vivas as the brother... The Simpsons (Bart and Lisa), Dexter's Laboratory (Dexter and Dee-Dee), South Park (Kyle and Ike), and Rocket Power (Otto and Reggie).
  • At the end of 2011, in Italy, Deborah Morese and Gea Riva voiced Diamond Tiara and Silver Spoon. Some months later, they became Hitomi Shizuki and Sayaka Miki.
  • In the 1990s, Brazilian actors Wendel Bezerra and Raquel Marinho voiced Goku and Chichi. About 15 years later, they reunited on My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic as Shining Armor and Princess Cadance.
    • Meanwhile in Rio de Janeiro, Luisa Palomanes and Charles Emmanuel first met as Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley. They reunited on the set of Teen Titans as Starfire and Beast Boy. After Yin Yang Yo! ended (guess who Luisa and Charles are), Luisa/Sierra would reunite with Charles/Cody on the set of Total Drama.
  • In early Portuguese dubs, like Dragon Ball and Dexter's Laboratory, most of the actors almost always worked together. But the ones who were the most heard together were Henrique Feist (Goku, Dexter...) and Cristina Cavalinhos (Bulma, Dee Dee...), plus Ricardo Spínola as a common narrator, among other characters.
  • Santiago Ziesmer and Oliver Feld are the German voices of Ren and Stimpy, and they frequently appear in each other's works, such as Spongebob Squarepants and The Piratesof Silicon Valley.
  • In Quebec, we have Hugolin Chevrette and Catherine Brunet. Shortly after Tangled, where Hugolin and Catherine voiced Flynn Rider and Rapunzel, the two reunited on Total Drama: Revenge of the Island, as Mike and Zoey, respectively.
  • Isabel Martiñón and Rossy Aguirre, before working on The Amazing World of Gumball as Gumball and Nicole Watterson, respectively as their Latin American Spanish voices, also worked on the Mexican dub of Sailor Moon as Unazuki and Sailor Mercury, respectively.
  • Mexican voice actors Óscar Flores, Víctor Ugarte, and Roberto Mendiola feature together respectively in Camp Lazlo as Lazlo, Raj, and Scoutmaster Lumpus, and in Aqua Teen Hunger Force as Meatwad, Master Shake, and Frylock.
  • Laura Torres, Patricia Acevedo, Rocío Garcel, Mónica Manjarrez, Rossy Aguirre, and Gerardo Reyero all worked together in the following (listed by their respective characters):
    • Rugrats: Tommy Pickles, Angelica Pickles, Susie Carmichael (and Dil Pickles), Didi Pickles, Phil DeVille, Stu Pickles (second voice)
    • Dragon Ball: Child Goku (and child Gohan and child Goten), Chi-Chi (and Chaotzu), Bulma and Baby Trunks (until the Buu Saga of Z, reprises her role as Bulma in Kai and Super), Bulma's second voice (in the Buu Saga, later voicing Tights in Super), Krillin (first voice up to midway through the Frieza Saga in Z), and Frieza
    • Sailor Moon: Princess Kaguya (S movie), Usagi Tsukino/Sailor Moon, Luna, Rei Hino/Sailor Mars, Ami Mizuno/Sailor Mercury, and Mamoru Chiba/Tuxedo Mask (second voice)
    • María Fernanda Morales worked together with all of the above in Rugrats as Kimi, and in Sailor Moon as Minako Aino/Sailor Venus; the late Araceli de León did the same in Dragon Ball as the first voice of Yajirobe, and in Sailor Moon as Makoto Kino/Sailor Jupiter, as did the late Jesús Barrero as the first voice of Yamcha and Puar in Dragon Ball, and Motoki Furuhata in Sailor Moon.
  • Annie Rojas and José Ángel Torres feature together in The Familiar of Zero as Louise and Saito, Naruto Shippuden: Ultimate Ninja Storm 4 as Sarada and Boruto, Riverdale as Veronica and Archie, and Elena of Avalor as Elena and Skylar.
  • Argentinian voice actors Agustina Cirulnik and Demián Velazco have worked together in Pucca as Ching and Abyo, Gravity Falls as Candy and Gideon, and Star vs. the Forces of Evil as Star and Marco.
