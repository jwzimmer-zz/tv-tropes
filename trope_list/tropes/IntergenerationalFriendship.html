Two people are good friends. Only, one is about twice, or even three times the other person's age! Considering their differences in life experiences and worldviews, how do they relate? What could they possibly have in common? Assuming this is actually a platonic friendship and not something far more sinister, or for that matter an overtly stated mentoring situation, intergenerational friendships can be pretty weird. As the Real Life section indicates, while not everyone finds older or younger people completely unapproachable, there's still frequently a gap there.

This can take the form of a young adult from one generation befriending someone old enough to be their parent or even grandparent, such as in the comedy Finding Forrester. It can take the form of an adult befriending a misguided or vulnerable youth, as in the French film Leon. Sometimes, the two happen to have more in common than they think, such as having surprisingly similar life experiences. Sometimes the younger generation is more mature, or the older generation is less mature - the latter especially helps writers to dodge away from worrying questions being asked about sexual attraction.

Advertisement:

As for how they become friends, it could well be contrived circumstances that bring them together, or a shared life experience. Sometimes it's a teacher trying to help a troubled teen, leading to them becoming close friends. Either way, the situation that leads to their friendship would often be something out of the ordinary, since in real life, people of two widely different age groups generally do not reach out to each other.

Sadly though, if it's an adult and a child who are friends, in modern stories, expect someone to misinterpret it as being sexual, and violence to possibly result. Sometimes the younger one will have an innocent Precocious Crush on the older one.

This trope does not generally apply if it's a parent-child relation between the two.

A subtrope of Odd Friendship. Contrast Age-Gap Romance and MayDecember Romance, in which there is a generation gap between romantic partners. Sister Trope to MayflyDecember Friendship, which involves friendship between beings of vastly different lifespans.

    Real Life 
  • Message boards, online games and other websites where people who share a common interest gather. Sports, video games, politics and TV shows are just a few of the subjects that can provide common ground for people who become close friends long before they even learn each other's relative age.
    • Or gender, race, or religion. The internet has a lot of problems, but damn if it doesn't bring us together.
  • This can also happen in community theatre, especially with shows that have characters that span many age groups.
  • Young people who like older music often make good friends with people who were around when that music came out. This is especially true if they frequent second hand record stores - they may well come in just for the conversation.
    • For that matter, retired people or stay-at-home parents with internet connections and exposure to new music (be it through the radio or Pandora or YouTube) will often stumble across message boards full of liked-minded teenagers talking about that great new song that just came out.
  • Real Life social clubs attract people of a wide range of ages. However, given that the people all share whatever common interest the club is themed on, intergenerational friendships are quite common within them.
  • Laura Dern and Shailene Woodley (who is 24 years Dern's junior) became close friends during filming The Fault in Our Stars. They are so close that Woodley lives part time in Dern's house.
  • Dame Margot Fonteyn and Rudolf Nureyev. Best friends, best ballet partners... and she was 19 years older than him. In fact, Dame Margot was about to retire when they started dancing together, but then she decided otherwise and they were a ballet duo for fifteen more years.
  • The whole Judd Apatow Production Posse is made up of actors in their early 30s to 40's. Paul Rudd is pushing 45 compared to Seth Rogen who pushing 32 and Jonah Hill, barely 30. Not a huge gap, but generation wise its large enough to fit this.
  • Lady Gaga and Cyndi Lauper. Despite their age difference of 32 years, they get along very well; Gaga considers Cyndi to be a Cool Big Sis figure, and Cyndi feels as though she can be as weird as she likes when she's with Gaga.
    • Gaga is also friends with Danny Trejo, a 42 year age difference.
    • And with Elton John (39 years), even becoming the godmother of his adoptive kids.
    • And Tony Bennett (60 years!), starting when they recorded a song (specifically, "The Lady Is A Tramp") for Bennett's Duets II CD. It was so well received that they did a whole album together, entitled Cheek To Cheek, released in autumn 2014.
  • While she was in her eighties, Alice Roosevelt Longworth (daughter of Theodore Roosevelt) became friends with forty-something Bobby Kennedy.
  • Michael Jackson and either Dame Elizabeth Taylor (difference of 26 years) or Diana Ross (14 years). Jackson was also good friends with Macaulay Culkin (a 21 year age difference).
  • Emma Roberts and Jennifer Aniston have a 22 year age gap between them.

When Zachary heard of Leonard's death, he was in Berlin doing reshoots for a film, so quickly dropped everything to fly back to the States for the funeral.
  • Despite being 26 years older than him, John McCain is friends with Jon Stewart. Even more impressive considering how different their political beliefs are.
  • Disney star Zendaya and country singer Wynonna Judd, with an age difference of 32 years.

Not to fear—if you just ask, some friendly college-aged (or older!) veterans who've done this a thousand times will be happy to show you around.

  • Kurt Cobain and William S. Burroughs. Cobain loved Naked Lunch and would often keep a copy of the book with him. The two collaborated on a project, The "Priest" They Called Him.

  • Katharine Hepburn and BRIAN BLESSED (29 years difference) became friends during filming Trojan Women.

  • Brie Larson and Jacob Tremblay are shown to have become close friends after filming Room
  • Patty Duke and Dove Cameron, starting when the former appeared on the latter's TV show.
  • Peter Capaldi and Jenna Coleman of Doctor Who who, despite a 25-year age gap, struck up an intense friendship that is clearly evident whenever they get together off-screen for interviews and conventions (a prime example being the 2016 AwesomeCon event in Washington, DC). In fact their friendship is so close, and the two are so open about indicating their love and affection for one another - with Coleman directly identifying Capaldi as one of her best friends and Capaldi speaking at length at how unusual it is for two people of their disparate ages to have such a friendship - that a subset of Doctor Who fandom began to ship the actors, despite Capaldi having been happily married for nearly as long as Coleman has been alive.

  • Lucille Ball and Carol Burnett developed a friendship when Lucy showed up to one of her stage performances. Later, when Carol had a TV special, she hesitantly called Lucy to be a part of it, and before she could even get it out, Lucy asked, "What do you want me to do?" After that, Lucy would send Carol flowers on her birthday. Lucy ended up dying on Carol's birthday in 1989, and that afternoon when she heard the news, her flowers arrived.

  • Conan O'Brian and his assistant Sona Movsesian, with 17-year age gap.
