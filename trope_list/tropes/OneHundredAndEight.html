Count 'em.
Advertisement:

The number 108 is a significant number in Buddhism. It represents the number of temptations that humans face, which tie them to the Wheel of samsara. There are many religious elements related to this number — a bell is rung 108 times to bring in the New Year in Japan. It is the number of beads on a Buddhist o-juzu or rosary. A classic pilgrimage in Japan visits 108 temples. Also, it's 2 to the second power times 3 to the third power — obviously important.note  But 2 and 3 are the first two prime numbers... wait, 23? Oh crap! Doubled, it becomes 216, which is "6 by 6 by 6".

This number crops up frequently in anime, video games, and other media produced in Japan. Like the numbers 13 or 7 for Western culture, it is ascribed a mystical significance far outside the original definitions. It has no readily comparable analogue to a modern Western viewer, however, though lots of similarities with the Medieval or Renaissance use of the number 9, which was considered perfect, the trinity of trinities, square of the first "real" prime (2 didn't count, sometimes, because it is even), etc.

Advertisement:

Nevertheless, as these examples will bear out, 108 does appear a disproportionate number of times in Western popular culture. It's not clear why: it's not a stereotypical "big" number one can easily use for exaggeration and/or laughs, and it's not terribly easy to remember, either. Perhaps 108 is simply an easy three-digit number for even the most tongue-tied actors to recite in English, since it has only five syllablesnote or as few as three syllables—"one-oh-eight" and contains almost no guttural consonant sounds - as well as being unusual enough that to most people it sounds unique enough to not come off as a cliché.

You might know 108 from puzzles, though — in which case it's merely 12 x 9.

Compare Four Is Death and its Western equivalent, the Number of the Beast; however, note that the number 108 is not always used in a negative sense as these other two examples generally are.

    Myths & Religion 
  • Hinduism: 108 is just as important in Hinduism as it is in Buddhism. Some of the various uses of it are shown below.
    • The Sudarshana Chakra, the weapon of Vishnu, and in particular, his avatar Krishna, is a serrated throwing disc with 108 serrations, making this Older Than Feudalism.
    • The Gayatri Mantra is traditionally chanted 108 times in prayer. In auspicious occasions, it goes up to 1008.
    • It is considered to be auspicious to chant 108 or 1008 of Vishnu or Shiva's many names.
    • According to the mythology, 108 devas and asuras churned the Ocean Of Milk.
  • Greek Mythology:
    • In Homer's The Odyssey, 108 is the number of suitors coveting Penelope, the wife of Odysseus.
  • Islam: Sufis regard this number highly, because in Sufism one of the most significant names of Allah is al-Haqq, 'the Truth' or 'the Real': Haqq is composed of the Arabic letters ha' (numerical value 8 in gematria) and qaf (numerical value 100).
  • Judaism: The number 18 (6+6+6) is considered symbolic of life itself. Similar to Islam, Judaism also uses gematria, where 10 and 8 spell 'hi' or 'life'.
    • Perhaps more of a stretch, but Judaism also has the 72 Names of God, where 72 is 2/3rds of 108, being 2x2x2x3x3.

    Real Life 
  • Wat Thamkrabok is a Buddhist monastery in Thailand that has gained a reputation for being the toughest rehab center in the world. One key aspect of their treatment, besides Buddhist meditations and spartan living, is a daily detox via Gargle Blaster-induced vomiting. The recipe is a closely held secret, but all that is known about it is that it was revealed to the founder in a dream and it consists of 108 herbal ingredients.
  • The angles of a regular pentagon are 108 degrees each.
  • Before it received its permanent name Hassium, element 108, had the placeholder name Unniloctiumnote formed from the digits: un-nil-octium with the corresponding symbol Uno. That is to say, element numero uno. Of course, this is an entirely meaningless coincidental result of the pig Latin/Greek used for placeholder element names.
  • Speaking of Uno, the Uno card game has 108 cards.
  • Giving someone 108 roses is a form of asking for someone's hand in marriage.
  • The 108 Martyrs of World War II are 108 Polish priests, bishops, and other Catholic church leaders that were killed by the Nazis.
  • If your fever hits 108 degrees Fahrenheit, you're not likely to survive. (On the off-chance that you do, you're likely to be pretty messed up, and possibly permanently disabled afterwards, because so many of your body's proteins, including the ones that make up important things like your brain, have been denatured.) At that point, you're cooked.
  • Coincidentally, Pepsi Beverages Co typically stacks their 12-pack and 24-pack can packages in pallets with 108 cases. For the 24-packs, this is a literal value (108 cases = 108 packs); for 12-packs it's twice the units (108 cases = 216 packs). There are 9 cases per layer on these pallets and they are 12 layers tall, hence the value 108.
