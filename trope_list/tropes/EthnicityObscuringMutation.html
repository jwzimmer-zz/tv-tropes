Ethnicity Obscuring Mutation

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

Beast Boy: You... uh... don't look...
Jinx: I don't look Indian?
Beast Boy: Yeah.
Jinx: Side effect of my chaos magic. Turned my skin gray and gave me this hot pink hair.
—Pinkette

Did you know that Alice is black? No? Well, that's understandable, because it's hard to tell between the horns and the fur.

Basically, this trope is when a character belongs to a certain ethnicity, but this is made unclear because they have some physical attributes that cover up the features that might mark them as a member of that ethnicity.

Advertisement:

Examples

Anime and Manga

  • Homunculi in Fullmetal Alchemist are people brought Back from the Dead (or close replicas thereof). After an initial monstrous phase, they look nearly identical to the deceased, but most will be Eerie Pale Skinned Brunettes regardless of their original hair or skin tone. This concealed that Lust was based on a brown-haired, dark-skinned Ishvalan woman—specifically the lover of Scar's brother.
  • In Interviews with Monster Girls, vampires are considered a human mutant that, among other traits, have fair skin and blond hair and irises. This is why the ethnically Japanese father of the ethnically Japanese vampire Hikari dyes his hair blond; to avoid doubts about Hikari's parentage out of this trope.

Comic Books

  • In Avengers Academy, Mettle is black and Jewish, but resembles a giant red robot, and frequently gets mistaken for the Red Skull, a Nazi supervillain, much to his chagrin.
  • This has occurred several times in New X-Men:
      Advertisement:
    • Bling is an African-American girl, but has a crystalline form that gives her purple skin. This has occasionally resulted in artists, particularly fan artists, mistakenly giving her Caucasian features.
    • Indra is an Indian boy, but this is not always clear due to his having purple skin and red hair.
    • Rockslide is a Latino boy, but has a rocky form.
    • Prior to losing his powers, Beak had a face like a baby bird. After Sofia Mantega encountered him post-depowering, she was shocked to see that he was a white boy.
  • In Muties Ankhi, a Swedish girl who is shown to have been white with black hair before her mutation, becomes bald and has pitch-black skin after the mutation kicks in.
  • As revealed in Manhunter, Mr. Bones has translucent skin, which makes him resemble a skeleton in a suit. When he puts on makeup in order to talk with Kate Spencer in public, she's shocked to discover that he's actually a black man.
    • Advertisement:
  • In X-Statix, Venus Dee Milo is black, but her mutation has turned her into a cloud of energy, forcing her to wear a suit which obscures her ethnicity.
  • In The Atomics, the Slug has a slug-like physiology that obscures the fact that she is a black woman.
  • Glow Worm in Astro City is a glowing green worm man with energy projection powers. It turns out he was African-American before the accident that caused his transformation. He is not happy when a comic book publisher implies that he is a white supremacist whose crimes are driven by racism.
  • Inverted with the heroic Skrulls Xavin and Crusader, who take on the forms of black men to obscure the fact that they're aliens.
  • Sparx, a friend and ally of Superboy, has electric powers which have made her hair so filled with electricity that it looks like it's made of it, and her body and eyes are a glowing and stark white even though she's naturally a tan Caucasian girl with curly brown hair.
  • Ben Grimm and Sharon Ventura, both members of the Fantastic Four, were mutated into bulky, rock-like forms as a result of exposure to cosmic rays, obscuring the fact that they are both white and, in Grimm's case, Jewish.
  • Black Lightning's daughter Jennifer, who goes by Lightning, inherited a form of her father's powers that eventually has her body turn into lighting, almost completely disguising her African American heritage.
  • In the New 52 version of Teen Titans, Solstice is an Indian girl who was mutated into charcoal-black creature with glowing eyes and Throat Light. The series also introduced Skitter, a black girl who developed an insect-like body.
  • Invoked but subverted in an issue of Deadpool's initial run. The One World Church hires Deadpool to steal the Facade Virus, and intends on wiping out racial inequity by turning the entire population of the planet blue. After they turn people blue, Deadpool points out you can still tell someone's ethnicity from their features and their plan was for naught and is undone.
  • In Secret Warriors, Pod is a Finnish girl named Aikku Jokinen who became fused with a sentient alien suit that she was unable to take off for fear of killing the suit. Eventually, the suit ejected her, but left her with undersuit. For added obscuring, Aikku is a lesbian, but the suit effectively prevented from having sex and made it very hard for her to date.
  • Beast Boy from Teen Titans is completely green, from his skin to his hair. He was originally a blond-haired white boy, however he was turned green in the same incident that gave him his powers.
  • In V for Vendetta, Codename V was left horribly disfigured as a result of the hormone experiments, and then they became even more disfigured when they blew up Larkhill and got caught in the flames. And the few records of V's life before Larkhill have long since disappeared. As Eric Finch notes, there is no way to determine V's race or even gender. Subverted in the finale, when the original V dies and Evey Hammond takes over their identity.
  • The obscure Marvel UK hero Night Raven has mixed ancestry, but exposure to a poison has left him so disfigured that his race is not clear to others.
  • One story in Love Romances involves a middle-aged Ambiguously Brown couple who get suckered into having their consciousnesses downloaded into robot bodies. It turns out that the robot bodies only come in a set range of appearances, so they both end up looking more Caucasian.
  • Exiles (2018) attempted to avert this trope, giving pink-skinned Blink noticeably black facial features to reflect the fact that she has black Bahamian heritage. This led to some controversy with the fandom, many of whom had long believed that Blink was Caucasian and accused the artist of Race Lifting her for the sake of "political correctness".
  • Early in her career, the modern, Pakistani-American Ms Marvel used her newfound morphing abilities to pose as the original Ms. Marvel, who is white. She later abandoned this disguise because it became impractical when the actual Carol Danvers was now a more public figure, but the fact that she was able to do this means that during Outlawed, when she became wanted by the police, the cops declined to set up a dragnet for every South-Asian teenager in Jersey, because they couldn't be sure that Ms. Marvel wouldn't just use her powers to hide her ethnicity.

Fan Works

  • Teen Titans:
    • This frequently pops up in fanfics involving Jinx. Jinx is grey-skinned but the comic character she's based off is Indian. Fan Wank is that Jinx is still of Indian descent in the cartoon adaptation, but her metahuman genetics gave her grey skin, abnormal eyes, and pink hair.
    • When questioned on how she learned how to make Indian food in Pinkette. Jinx tells the Titans that she's Indian. Her powers gave her pale skin and pink hair.
    • Jinx mentions in Changing Luck that she was ostracized growing up due to being born with grey skin and pink her. Her Indian father all-but abandoned her as a child.
    • Jinx in To Catch A Raven is Indian but people, even other Indians, don't usually notice this because of her gothic style and her grey skin color (though she does have racial features).

Films - Live-Action

  • In Captain America: The First Avenger, the Red Skull mentions that he no longer fits Hitler's criteria for racial purity because his transformation into a man with red skin and a skull-like countenance makes it hard for him to prove that he is white.
  • Kroll from Star Trek Beyond is a black man, but when the crew meets him for the first time, they can't even identify him as human because of the mutation-inducing technology he uses to extend his life.
  • Freddy Krueger, the antagonist of the Nightmare on Elm Street series, is Caucasian, but it is difficult to discern his ethnicity due to his severe burns.

Jokes

  • Invoked in a joke where a kindergarten teacher in the Deep South has had enough of her white students refusing to sit next to black students, so she tells them finger paint blue stripes on their faces and says that from now own, there are no longer whites or blacks in her class: only blues. Then she tells the light blues to sit next to the dark blues.

Literature

  • Whateley Universe:
    • Basically any large amount of what is termed "Gross Structural Dystrophy" could lead to this. Cerulea's mutation turned her coloration into variants of blue, so her original ethnicity as a white American is utterly obscured.
    • Fubar underwent some transformation due to Cthulhu Mythos influence, turning a normal mutant human into some semi-aquatic being.
    • Blot, who absorbs all energy, and therefore just looks like a black humanoid shape, as there's no light to show any of his features.
  • The Hunchback of Notre Dame: Esmeralda has not only black hair and eyes but also the dark skin typical of the Gypsies, obscuring her actual ethnicity as a white girl.
  • Star Smashers of the Galaxy Rangers features a war between two subspecies of an insectoid race, with black being good and white being evil. A member of the former race was born an albino, so they sent him to infiltrate the enemy.

Live-Action TV

  • The alien signal on Threshold has about a 50/50 chance of either turning its victims into super powered alien agents or killing them. If it kills them, it causes their corpses to undergo a disfiguring (and painful) transformation. In one episode, Nigel is able to have one such victim transferred to their lab for autopsy by claiming she's an Orthodox Jew. At the lab, he comments that for once, the disfigurement worked in their favor because she was actually Chinese.
  • In Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., Ambiguously Brown Raina undergoes terrigenesis, causing her skin to turn grey and scaly and her curly hair to be replaced with quill-like growths.
  • In Legion, Cary Loudermilk was born to Native American parents, but appears to be white, to the point that when he was born, his father assumed that his mother had an affair.
  • Subverted in Watchmen (2019) - Even after Cal Abar reverts to being Doctor Manhattan and regains his glowing blue skin, he still has African-American features, and thus his adopted kids immediately recognize him.

Tabletop Games

  • In Warhammer 40 K, the Salamanders chapter of Space Marines are black-skinned. Depending on the Writer, this means they're black as in of African descent and facial features, or black as in of various ethnicities but with charcoal-black skin (and glowing red eyes).

Video Games

  • In God of War Kratos is much paler than your typical Greek due to the curse laid upon him by the Gods of Olympus after he killed his wife and daughter in a rage.
  • Fallout: New Vegas: According to a developer blog post, the companion Lily Bowen is of African decent. Not that you can tell now that she's a muscle-bound Nightkin with blue skin and a Guttural Growler voice.
  • In Plants vs. Zombies: Battle for Neighborville, one of the zombie classes- Electric Slide -is modeled after a black woman. This isn't too apparent at first, though, since her decaying skin is now green.

Web Comics

  • All of the Freakangels are born with pale skin and purple eyes. While most of them are white anyway, Caz made their connection the most obvious since she's black.
  • The Joker suggests he might be black in Injustice For All, saying to Batman, "You never met me before the bleach".
    Batman: But all the public beatings... I'll need the entire Justice League coverup fund.
  • In Tales To Behold and Strange Tales of the DA Multiverse, heroine Burst Lion is a white girl with auburn hair who turns into a red-and-yellow-furred Cat Girl with blue hair.

Western Animation

  • In Adventure Time, Ice King was once an Ambiguously Brown man before he became the Ice King, which gave him a pale countenance.
  • Steven Universe: Lars is half Filipino; after dying and getting revived, his orange skin and hair are permanently bleached pink, making him resemble a Gem more than a human.
  • The Venture Bros. has White Noise, a white supremacist who, after a freak TV repair accident, became living static. Monarch is incredulous that White Noise refuses to be part of a gang that allows people of color considering no one can even tell he's actually white.
  • Played for Laughs in an episode of Sealab 2021, where Quinn is ReTooled into a snake-like symbiote creature, which the director claims was done because the writers were having trouble scripting an "urban" character.
