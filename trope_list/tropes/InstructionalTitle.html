Instructional Title

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

A title of the form "How To X", where X is descriptive of what happens in the story (and typically, not something the audience would have ever wondered how to do).

Examples:

    open/close all folders 

     Anime & Manga  

  • How to Raise a Boring Girlfriend
  • How NOT to Summon a Demon Lord
  • How to Make an Invisible Man

Advertisement:

     Fan Works 

  • How To Drill Your Way Through Your Problems

     Film - Animated  

  • How to Train Your Dragon

     Film - Live Action 

  • How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days
  • How To Talk To Girls At Parties
  • How To Be Single
  • How to Marry a Millionaire
  • How to Be a Serial Killer
  • How to Get Ahead in Advertising
  • How to Make an American Quilt
  • How to Be

     Literature  

  • How to Teach Physics to Your Dog
  • How to Live Safely in a Science Fictional Universe by Charles Yu.
  • There's a The Zack Files book called How To Speak Dolphin in Three Easy Lessons.
  • How To Eat Fried Worms
  • Dale Carnegie's How to Win Friends and Influence People line of books. Possibly the Trope Maker.
  • How NOT to Write a Novel
  • How to Lose Friends and Alienate People is the title of an obscure 1937 book, a 2001 memoir, and a 2008 film based on the memoir. Not to be confused with Carnegie's book.
  • John Barth's novel The End of the Road was originally going to be called What To Do Until the Doctor Comes, but the publisher was concerned it would be mistaken for a book on first aid.
  • How to Succeed in Evil is titled this way.
  • A slight variation: So You Want to Be a Wizard, the title of both the first book in a series by Diane Duane and the in-universe textbook the main character finds.
  • How to Be a Superhero
  • How To Stop Time
  • How to Avoid Death on a Daily Basis
  • How to Survive a Horror Movie

     Live Action TV  

  • How To Irritate People
  • Monty Python's Flying Circus:
    • Sketch "How Not to be Seen"
    • "Episode 3: How to Recognize Different Types of Trees from Quite a Long Way Away"
    ''"Number one: The larch. The. Larch."
  • How to Get Away with Murder
  • How To Rock
  • How to Be Indie. Additionally, the Idiosyncratic Episode Naming means that every episode is titled "How To (do peculiar thing relevant to the episode)".
  • How To Make It In America

Advertisement:

     Music  

  • "How to Save a Life" by The Fray.
  • The U2 album How to Dismantle an Atomic Bomb.
  • "How to Disappear Completely" by Radiohead, from Kid A.
  • How to Operate with a Blown Mind by Lo Fidelity All-Stars
  • "How to Handle a Rope" by Queens of the Stone Age
  • "How to Fight Loneliness" by Wilco
  • How to Steal a Jet Fighter by The Prodigy
  • "How to Say Goodbye" by The Magnetic Fields
  • Variation: The Byrds' "So You Wanna Be a Rock-n-Roll Star"
  • Another variation: "50 Ways to Say Goodbye" by Train.
  • "How to Be A Heartbreaker" by Marina & the Diamonds.

     Radio  

  • Every episode of The Burkiss Way: "Peel Bananas The Burkiss Way", "Become a Rock Star the Burkiss Way", "Discover Gravity the Burkiss Way", ...

     Theatre  

  • How to Succeed in Business Without Really Trying

     Video games 

  • How To Survive

     Visual Novels 

  • How to Take Off Your Mask

Advertisement:

     Web Animation 

  • How to Kill a Mockingbird
  • How To Be An Adventurer

     Web Comics  

  • Every Basic Instructions strip is titled this way.
  • How to be a Werewolf

     Western Animation  

  • Many Classic Disney Shorts starring Goofy.
    • How to Hook Up Your Home Theater
