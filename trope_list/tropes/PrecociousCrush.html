"Just thinkin' about someone special"

Charlie Brown: I think our teacher is in love.
Linus: I was afraid of that. I've always hoped that she'd wait until I grew up, and then marry me.
Charlie Brown: How would you support her?
Linus: We could live off her retirement pay.

Children having "crushes" on one another isn't uncommon. To most children, loving someone just means liking them a lot. They have no notions of sexual—and sometimes even romantic—love. For the most part, innocent. They may make a Childhood Marriage Promise with their best friend just because, and even pretend to get "married." Older kids generally have a basic idea of the birds and the bees, but still have to deal with raging hormones and lots of confusion.

What happens when kids get crushes on adults?

In some cases, Squick happens. In most instances, you have an ordinary kid who has to deal with the ordinary awkwardness of having a crush on someone way out of their league.

The Precocious Crush is any instance of a child (usually pre-teen or younger, though young teenagers are sometimes susceptible) having a crush on an adult. Teachers are common targets, as are other, non-related caretakers. While having this kind of crush is more often associated with girls, it is also common to portray boys as having this kind of crush. Usually, nothing ever comes of it—at least not until the character in question is much older, and even then, it still might seem a bit Squicky to some. In animation, the trope can be invoked without fully using it in the case of teenagers, where one character is only a few years younger than their crush, but differing rates of physical development makes the crush look a lot older than them.

Advertisement:

This trope is Truth in Television. Lots of kids get crushes on their teachers or on their older neighbors, or friends of their older siblings—and older siblings of their friends. (One resolution is for differences between the older and the same-age siblings to be mostly maturity; when the kid and the same-age sibling grow up, Settle for Sibling can be a happy ending.)

See also Puppy Love, where two little kids crush on each other, and Father, I Want to Marry My Brother, which relies on an innocent inability to distinguish between different types of love. If the interest is clearly sexual, then it's Dirty Kid.

If the object of the crush happens to be a hot single mom (commonly known as a MILF), see Stacy's Mom. Should anything actually happen in the relationship, see Wife Husbandry, Jail Bait Wait, Teacher/Student Romance, and MayDecember Romance. See also Likes Older Men, Likes Older Women, Fille Fatale and Kidanova. Possible ways this crush can work out is if the the kid becomes older or their crush becomes their age.

My Goddess, she begins exhibiting this trope towards Keichi, but Urd tried to sabotage it and deliberately create a Snap Back—commenting to herself that it was for Skuld's own good. The twist that plays with the trope is that Haibara is actually an 18-year-old physically regressed to about age 6 and is typically Little Miss Snarker on top of that... yet her behavior throughout these chapters ends up matching almost exactly what one would expect of her grade-schooler appearance.

  • One episode of ALVINNN!!! and the Chipmunks, "Principal Interest", shows that Alvin has a crush on the principal.
  • In the DreamWorks Dragons episode "Gone Gustav Gone", Gustav Larson (16) develops a crush on Astrid (19) the same way Snotlout does, which in a way also grosses her out.
  • You can add Arthur Read to the list of preteen boys with crushes on teenage babysitters.
  • In an episode of As Told by Ginger, Ginger (12-ish) attempts to ask her teacher on a date with her. While competing in a school quiz competition. Which is being televised. Yikes.
  • In Birdz, Eddie Storkowitz has one of these on his teacher, Miss Finch.
  • In the ChalkZone episode "The Crush", Rudy (who's 10) gets a chance to see Queen Rapsheeba's creator, Trina (who appears to be around 14), and instantly develops a crush on her. Because of that, he joins the same theater group as her (even though he had made fun of one of their productions earlier in the episode), and asks Rapsheeba what she likes in a guy, hoping that what she likes would apply to her creator as well. After many attempts to get her to fall in love with him, she realizes that he has a crush on him. His crush wears off at the end of the episode when she tells him that while he's a sweet kid, she's too old for him and he should be doing all the things he did for her for a girl his own age.
  • Codename: Kids Next Door:
    • Numbuh Four started to get a crush on his fourth grade teacher, Mrs. Thompson. Unfortunately, that turned out kind of weird later, when it turned out she was the Were-Dog Queen, cursed with lycanthropy by her jealous ex-husband. (He was able to break the curse in the end.)
    • Likewise, Numbuh Two also had a crush on Numbuh Five's older sister, Cree, which created a lot of problems since Cree was a teenager and didn't return his feelings for her.
  • Daria has a teenager-and-adult example in its first three seasons, when Daria had a crush on her best friend's brother, Trent, who is 5 years older than her. She's smart enough to realize that it would never work out, for their personalities if not the age difference, but it still takes a while to shake off.
  • The Deep: In "Lonesome Jim", it is revealed that Ant has a crush on Fontaine's best friend Jess Gorman (who is Fontaine's age or slightly older).
  • Dexter's Laboratory: Dexter has a crush on his teenage babysitter in one episode, and it didn't end well when he aged up, making the result not so great, and a punch to the face by her boyfriend!
  • In one episode of The Fairly OddParents, while on a vacation in Hawaii with his parents, Timmy grows a crush on a hula girl.
  • Family Guy
    • In "8 Simple Rules for Buying My Teenage Daughter", Stewie has a crush on his new babysitter, but when he finds out she has a boyfriend, he tries to kill him.
    • In one episode, Chris has a crush on his substitute teacher who is very buxom.

  • In an episode of Foster's Home for Imaginary Friends, Mac (8) and Bloo (chronologically 5) and some random imaginary friend and a pizza delivery guy develop a crush on Frankie (22). Mac and Bloo's 'feelings' for her are referenced a couple more times in later episodes.
  • In Gravity Falls Dipper (12) has a crush on Wendy (15). He finally confesses it in the second season and Wendy reveals she already knew (as it was pretty damn obvious to everyone) and gently lets him down. Ironically during a time travel episode the 12-year-old Dipper meets the 5-year-old Wendy and she crushes on him, something Dipper finds a little uncomfortable and awkward, and he sort of understands how Wendy might have felt about him having a crush on her.
    • Likewise Mabel (also 12) crushes on older boys, such as sock puppeteer Gabe in the episode "Sock Opera". Her reaction to one of her crushes (that she actually gets her first kiss from) having managed to grow a very precocious mustache is to pretty much squee, and judging from Dipper's complete non-reaction to her dating a teenager in the first episode this is not a new thing for her.
  • In Hey Arnold!, Arnold develops a crush on a substitute teacher. Hilarity Ensues when he overhears her talking about planning a romantic evening with "Arnold," since that happens to also be her fiancé's name.
    • He also crushes on Ruth McDougall, a sixth-grader, early on. In the Valentine's Day episode he actually talks to her and realizes that she's not anything like he expected, so he moves on. And then there was that one Beach Episode with one-time love interest two-timing him...
    • This may or may not count but that one episode where Gerald's little sister Timberly (who's about 5 or 6) had a crush on Arnold (who's 9). The age difference is small but Arnold acts much more mature than Timberly.
    • Alongside Arnold, Gerald had one too in the episode "Sixth Grade Girls". He also has another one in "Gerald vs. Jamie-O" with his new neighbor Chloe (who was middle-school aged). But she was only agreeing to hang out with him because she had a crush on his brother Jamie-O (who is high school aged).
  • In Home Movies, Jason develops a crush on the school nurse.
  • In Johnny Bravo, a young neighbor girl named Suzy is said to have a crush on the titular character, who (while his age is never revealed or confirmed) is probably roughly a decade older than her. Suzy clings to Johnny at every possible, and he usually must go to great lengths to get rid of her.
    • Though soon after the art style shift happened in the show, her implications of having a crush on him disappeared. Likely as it would be mildly creepy to have a girl infatuated with the town lecher.
  • In A Kind of Magic, Tom's crush on his teacher, Miss Tinker.
  • Aang's (12) feelings for Katara (14) in Avatar: The Last Airbender are so close to the border limit for this trope that they actually cross it over the course of the series. A childlike fondness soon becomes a crush that grows as he matures over the course of his journey.
  • The Legend of Korra:
    • Meelo (who is 5) has a crush on Asami (who is 18). Mako (Asami's then-boyfriend) really gets a kick out of it:
    Meelo: You're pretty, can I have some of your hair?
    Mako: Looks like I have some competition.
    • In "The Calling" the now-8-year-old Meelo is clearly trying to flirt with a girl several years older than him; when his sister drags him away, he says that she "drove away the love of my life."
    • Kind of a downplayed example, but Ikki (one of Meelo's older sisters) is implied to have a crush on Opal's older brother, Huan—for example, in the series finale, Ikki ends up dragging Huan out onto the dance floor on Varrick and Zhu Li's wedding.
  • The Lion Guard has Bunga, a preteen Honey Badger who has a crush on Dhahabu, an adult Golden Zebra.
  • Moral Orel: 11-year-old Orel develops a crush on Stephanie, who works at the town's only sex shop. She turns him down nicely at the end of the episode and they retain and Intergenerational Friendship. Even if there weren't an age gap, Incompatible Orientation would have set in. In the second season, Doughy develops a crush on his teacher Miss Sculptham, who ends up taking advantage of him so he'll (and later by extension, Mr. Creepler) keep buying her expensive gifts.
  • My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic has Spike, who is sort of like an adopted son/brother to Twilight Sparkle, and in personality is much like a preteen. He gained a major crush on Rarity, who is clearly an adult, the moment he saw her. She knows about the crush and has gently brushed him off, thought she's not above exploiting it for her own benefit at times (though she generally at least remembers to reward him in other ways).
  • In the Peanuts cartoon, Be My Valentine, Charlie Brown, Linus' crush for Miss Othmar is seen, but then cooled off quickly when he sees his teacher ride off with her boyfriend. Cue Linus chucking every chocolate from a big heart-shaped candy box over the bridge and Snoopy and Woodstock getting a free treat for every toss!

    • Ferb ("less than 15", probably about 10) has a crush on Vanessa Doofenshmirtz (16). She's dating someone else and doesn't seem to notice his crush, but he's saved her life and given her helpful advice, and Word of God actually supports the idea of them getting together "when the age thing won't matter as much." In the flash-forward episode "Act Your Age", Ferb and Vanessa end up getting together.
    • Irving hits on Candace and/or Stacy, though this was probably just meant as a single one-off joke in this show's perpetual Ship Tease.
    • Thanks to his voice actor Bobby Gaylor, we now know that Buford (same age as Ferb) has a crush on Candace (15). As evidenced by his sneaking his number into her cellphone, and being very upset when she won't abide by the Bully Code and serve him for his having saved her life. This might also explain why he refused to let Candace and Jeremy into the restaurant in "Chez Platypus"; he wanted to ruin their date out of jealousy.
  • Buttercup in The Powerpuff Girls episode "Buttercrush." She has a crush on Gangreen Gang leader Ace, which lasts up until she sees the Gang about to kill Blossom and Bubbles. The short-lived crush is even lampshaded in a comic book story ("Five Green Vandals," issue #16). They're full-on dating in the game FusionFall, though she doesn't seem much older than 13.
  • Ready Jet Go!: It's really easy to think that Jet has a crush on Sean's mom as of "Visit to Mom's Office". When he first sees her, he immediately gives her a big hug (Sean's mom looks surprised, Sean looks annoyed, and Sydney looks sad, since she has frequent ship tease with Jet and possibly likes him), and even calls her "Dr. Sean's Mom" even though he knows that their last name is Rafferty. He is later seen smiling widely at her and raising his eyebrows trying to catch her attention, which also made Sydney upset. To top it off, he seemed all too excited to tell her that he is from Bortron 7. This crush can also be seen in other episodes. In "Ice Moon Enceladus", when Dr. Rafferty is passing by Sean's snow cone stand, Jet immediately greets her, even though she ignores him, and in "Mindy's Meteorite Stand", when Dr. Rafferty was inspecting Mindy's "meteorite", Jet also looked through a magnifying glass to impress her. In "Asteroid Patrol", Sean's telescope needs adjusting and Jet happily offers to go to Sean's house to get some items. Of course, when he sees Sean's mom, he acts awkward around her. However, in later episodes, the crush is no longer apparent.

  • The Simpsons:
    • In "Lisa's Substitute", Lisa gets a crush on the (substitute) teacher Mr. Bergstrom. (Voiced by Dustin Hoffman, who just can't seem to find a happy medium with respect to the ages of women who fall for him.)
    • In earlier seasons, Lisa is obsessed with Corey magazine and its hotline, all full of boys who were at least in their teens. One example in the "Brother from the Same Planet" subplot, when Marge found out about Lisa's phone calls to the hotline, she revealed to Lisa that she was in the same situation in her childhood when she had a crush on Bobby Sherman:
      Marge Simpson: Oh, honey, I know how you feel. When I was a girl, I had a crush on Bobby Sherman
      [Lisa bursts out laughing]
      Marge Simpson: [annoyed] The point is, I want you to stop making these calls!

When he found out she was going out with Jimbo, one of the bullies who picks on him, he arranged a plan to break them up. It was successful — by the end of the episode, Laura had broken up with Jimbo and even told Bart that she would date him if he were older. The character was never seen again.
    • In "The Fabulous Faker Boy", Bart falls for his piano teacher, Zhenya.
    • In one episode, in regards to Marge, Milhouse says "She's HOT! ...sorry that just slipped out."
    • Also happens in "The Devil Wears Nada" with Nelson and Milhouse looking at a pin-up calender featuring Marge.

    • Butters also has a crush on Kim Kardashian.

But in the 2012-series, Donnie's confirmed to have a crush on April, who's 16 (and a high school student), instead of being a woman in her twenties.
    • In the original cartoon from the 1980s, all four of the turtles have obvious crushes on April (who, according to some sources, is supposed to be 28). However, other than the fact that they're simply "teenagers," it's never said how old the turtles are.
