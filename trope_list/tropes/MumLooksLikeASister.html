Mum Looks Like a Sister

The "mums in aprons" style can be useful in telling the kids from the parents.

"No matter how much you wish for it, nothing is going to happen between you and my mother."
— Rias Gremory, High School Dx D
Advertisement:

Because of either Older Than They Look or Younger Than They Look, (or both) a parent looks about the same age as the parents child, enough to be mistaken for siblings. This happens most often with mothers and daughters, but it can work with any gender combination.

See also Absurdly Youthful Mother and the other subtropes, especially Adoptive Peer Parent and Mistaken Age. This can be invoked in a Family Relationship Switcheroo, to prevent anyone from realizing they actually are mother and child. In live-action, Playing Gertrude introduces this effect, because due to age of the actors Mum should be a sister.

  • Nana Sawada, mother of protagonist Tsunayoshi from Katekyō Hitman Reborn!, is in her mid-late thirties but looks barely older than her teenage son.
  • In Zodiac PI, Lili's mother mentions in a flashback that she (again) was mistaken as Lili's older sister.
  • Miyuki Asaka of Game×Rush falls squarely into this trope; even the mangaka mentions that "I feel like her face and personality are stuck as a little girl forever." Also a borderline Type 1 case since simple math shows she'd have been 18 when she had her son.

  • Kusanagi in The Sky Crawlers looks about twelve to fifteen, but is older than she looks.
  • In a similar vein to Sanae, Miyuki Takara's mother Yukari in Lucky Star looks like she could pass herself off as an older sister. Then again, it is an Improbably Female Cast. And if Yukari, then certainly Miki, the mother of Inori, Matsuri, Kagami, and Tsukasa. She's claimed to be 47, and although it was a lie (she thought it was cute and expected), a 47-year-old woman could easily have two college-age and two teenage daughters... but she still looks like she could be one of the sisters herself.
  • Ah! My Goddess: Takano Morisato could pass for a sister of Keiichi and Megumi, instead of their mother.

  • In Air Master When Maki's friend see her father for the first time, they mistake him for a big brother. He was only 15 when he had her.
  • Medaka Box plays this one further than many other examples, with Zenkichi's mother Hitomi looking like a preteen girl as an apparent side-effect of her Abnormality. She's first introduced when Akune finds her rollerblading around the school and assumes she's looking for her "big brother"; when Zenkichi says "That's not my sister, that's my mom", Akune spits blood in shock.

The result is that in some of her last appearances she actually looks younger than her own daughter. And Bulma herself still looks younger than 43. It's hinted that she used the Dragon Balls between story arcs to make herself younger.

    • Once Saiyans reach adulthood, they retain their youth and prime far longer than humans and don't show visible signs of aging until around 80. To the left in that screenshot is his 17 year old son Goten, who looks more like his dad's twin brother with a different hairstyle. Goku looks young enough that Pan (the little girl also found on the left) can pass as his daughter, even though she's his granddaughter.
  • Akira's parents in Family Complex could pass off as 17 despite being in their 40s. Lampshaded in the Princess Princess bonus story when Toru and Yuujiro visits the Sakamoto household try to guess who is who in the Sakamoto family. Then they mistook his father as Akira's older brother, "the legendary Sakamoto-sama" they were so keen to meet.

The only indication she's older is that she doesn't dress as wild as Jolyne and her fellow inmates.
  • Suite Pretty Cure ♪ has Kanade's mom Misora. When the fans first saw her on the previews for episode 4, they thought she was a candidate for a possible Sixth Ranger.
  • Fuyuki and Natsumi's mother Aki, in Sgt. Frog, is another pretty good example: she is drawn with a childish face, which makes quite a contrast with her striking figure.
  • Tamako Market: Tamako's mother, Hinako◊, looked almost exactly like her daughter, one can assume she was this.
    • Mochizou's mother, Michiko, definitely counts. She looks young enough to be mistaken for her son's older sister◊.

Misuzu in particular has a 14 year old daughter and looks like she's twenty, which caused both Touma and his father to mistake her for Mikoto's sister when they first met her. She's noted to be a bit jealous of Shina, though, because Misuzu has to go to a great deal of effort to look and act that young, while for Shina it comes naturally.

The real disconnect comes when you put her alongside◊ her 17-year-old son Mahiro, which makes Yoriko look much more like an overly affectionate Cool Big Sis than a mother. This gets emphasized even further in an episode where Yoriko flashes back to the day her husband proposed; the only visible change after roughly two decades is her hairstyle.
  • Karin:
    • Fumio looks to be about the same age as her sixteen-year old son, Kenta, which is maybe not quite so outrageous when you learn she had Kenta as a teenager. Seeing different characters react to this is a bit of a Running Gag in the manga.
    • Elda takes it even further. With her girl-like body she could probably pass as another younger sister of Karin, and Karin is her granddaughter. This is related to Elda's vampire powers, and contrasts with how her husband became old and frail because he was reluctant to suck the blood of humans.
  • Umi Monogatari has Miyako, Kanon's mother. She looks young enough to be one of her daughter's friends, and has the personality to match.
  • Pokémon Ranger and the Temple of the Sea: In the film May compliments Lizabeth's mother Meredith by saying she thought Meredith was Elizabeth's sister. This is Playing with a Trope because Meredith is clearly an adult and older than Lizabeth as can be seen here◊.
  • Soul Eater: Spirit Albarn looks more like Maka's older brother. Justified as he was 18 when Maka was born.
  • Lyrical Nanoha:
    • Momoko Takamichi from Lyrical Nanoha. In the first two series, she at least looks old enough to possibly be an Absurdly Youthful Mother to Nanoha, a third-grader...but certainly not old enough to have two children in high school. Then several Time Skips happen, aging Nanoha up to 23, and Momoko still looks like she's in her twenties.

    • Amy mistakes Quint for being Subaru's older sister in INNOCENT. Rather impressive when you consider the fact that -in this continuity- she has given birth to six children.   • In World Trigger, Osamu's mother Kasumi is this, and people constantly assume she's his older sister. Osamu and Kasumi even correct Karasawa in unison after he calls Kasumi his sister, much to his surprise.
  • In Silver Spoon, when Yugo first sees Tamako's mother, he assumes she's Tamako's older sister. She's obviously flattered.
  • In High School Dx D, Venelana looks like a brunette version of her redheaded daughter Rias, to the point Issei thinks Venelana is Rias's sister. He doesn't get to say anything out loud because Rias sees him staring at Venelana and correctly concludes what he must be thinking in a heartbeat. The way she breaks the news provides the current page quote.
  • Valt's mother in Beyblade Burst has three elementary-aged children yet looks like a teenager.
  • In Anne Happy, Anne's mom, Sakura, is absolutely flattered after Botan and Ruri mistakenly identify her as Anne's sister at first.
  • In Tomo-chan Is a Girl!, Carol's mother Ferris not only looks like an older sister but acts just as "fluffy" as her daughter; in fact Carol messes with her friends by introducing Ferris as her big sister, then seems a little surprised when they believe her.   • My Hero Academia:
    • Mitsuki Bakugo is 38-year-old but looks surprisingly young for a woman with a 15-year-old son. Supplementary info states it's thanks to her Quirk which secretes glycerin.

Pretty sure his dad might've been arrested at least once on supposed pedophilia charges. It apparently runs in the family, as her sister and her niece all look the same.
  • The Quintessential Quintuplets: Isanari Uesugi sometimes looks more like Fuutarou's and Raiha's big brother rather than their father. The resemblance is even more pronounced when Fuutarou disguises himself as "Kintarou" while wearing a blonde wig.
  • Telne from Miss Kobayashi's Dragon Maid initially introduces herself as Elma's older sister (and looks younger than her), though she's actually her grandmother.
  • Do You Love Your Mom and Her Two-Hit Multi-Target Attacks?: Masato complains that between her uncannily youthful looks, her clinginess, and her childlike demeanor, there's no way Mamako could be his real mother.
  • Inverted in New Game!, in which Catherine's younger sister Sophie is mistaken for her daughter- the former is an adult and a businesswoman, while the latter is a grade schooler. It doesn't help that Catherine is fairly close in age to her older sister Christina.
  • In Haru and Midori, Midori's mother, Youko, looks almost the same as the 31-year-old Midori, even though Youko would be, at minimum, in her mid forties or even early fifties. Haru, who also looks almost exactly like her mother did at her age, makes note of the resemblance.
  • In The Demon Girl Next Door, Seiko, who has a teenage daughter, still looks like she's in the twenties. Justified by the fact that she largely stopped aging when she became her husband's vassal.
  • Mendou and Ryoko's father from Urusei Yatsura looks more like their older brother, he looks exactly like a taller, slightly older version of Mendou with a mustache.
  • And You Thought There is Never a Girl Online?: The only ways to tell apart Ako and her mom is that Mrs. Tamaki is taller and combs her hair. Hideki was rather surprised when he first met her and initially assumed she was Ako's older sister.
  • Uzaki-chan Wants to Hang Out!: Tsuki Uzaki is over 40 years old, but barely looks older than her 20-year old daughter Hana. Since both are short, but well-stacked women, the only telltale signs are that Tsuki has longer hair, Eyes Always Shut and wears different clothing.

  • In Marvel Comics, the Eternals of Earth are immortal, and appear as whatever age they want. Thus, Cybelle looks no older than her daughter Thena, although both are several millennia old.
  • In Robin Tim's stepmom Dana is young enough to be his older sister, though there'd be a significant age gap that gap is less than 10 years, and she looks younger than her actual age. She is near Dick Grayson's age, and he would eventually become Tim's older brother through adoption.
  • Marvel Invoked: A blood transfusion from the original Human Torch gave Spitfire her super-speed powers. A second transfusion forty years later not only reactivated her then-faded powers, but de-aged her back to sixteen, the age when she first became Spitfire. When her grown son takes her to his favorite pub, the barman jokes "Is your mum old enough to drink?"

  • Things Jade Hates: Tori initially guesses that Jade's mom is her sister.

    Films — Animation 
  • Anastasia: Based on the few short glimpses we see of Anastasia's family in the prologue, flashbacks and portrait, her mother looks like the oldest of her many red-haired older sisters. (Apparently◊ Truth in Television.)
  • Disney is way too fond of this trope. If a mother can be found in a Disney movie, odds are she'll never look a day over 25, even if her child is in his or her late teens.
    • When Aurora reunites with her parents in Sleeping Beauty, she looks to be her mother's only slightly shorter, slightly finer-featured twin.
    • In comparison to her daughter Wendy, Mary Darling from Peter Pan looks like she could be her older sister.
    • The Lion King: When Simba returns to Pride Rock bearing a Strong Family Resemblance to his deceased father, his mother Sarabi doesn't look any different from when he was a cub. (In fact, she shares a similar body model and facial structure to his own childhood sweetheart, Nala.)
    • In The Little Mermaid II: Return to the Sea, Ariel looks almost identical to how she looked in the first film—barely a bit older, at most—and could be mistaken as the older sister of her daughter Melody. Of course, there are factors to consider; she is a mermaid, and mermaids likely don't age the same rate as humans, and she was subject to magic from her father's trident.
    • Treasure Planet: Sarah to Jim. One could be forgiven for thinking she's his older sister rather than his mother.
    • Tangled: Mother Gothel actively tries to invoke this trope by using Rapunzel's hair to keep herself young while posing as her mother. Rapunzel's real mother has no such access to round-the-clock de-aging hair, yet she hasn't aged a day since Rapunzel's birth 18 years before. She also looks like a twenty-something version of Rapunzel post-Important Haircut.
    • Frozen: In life, Elsa's and Anna's mother looked to be a brunette version of their adult selves (21 and 18)... even when they were in their late and mid-teens (18 and 15). She in fact uses a slightly edited version of Elsa's model.
    • Averted for Mulan's and Tiana's mothers, who both look to be middle age by the time their daughters reach late teenhood.
  • Quest for Camelot: Juliana is this for Kayley.
  • Toy Story 3: Andy's and Molly's mom looks to be about the same age as her college-aged son, and shares a Strong Family Resemblance with her pre-teen daughter to boot.
  • Tip's mother Lucy Tucci from Home looks more like her older sister.
  • Coco gives a rare justifiable In-Universe example of this trope. Youd never guess that Hector was twelve-year-old protagonist Miguels great-great grandfather. They look more like brothers. This is because Hector is only biologically nine years older than Miguel because he died when he was only twenty one.

  • Byzantium: Clara looks like Eleanor's older sister rather than her mother, due to being vampires who stopped aging. Clara is shown to have only been around twenty when giving birth to Eleanor anyway. Due to this, in the present both pretend they are sisters.

    Literature 
  • It's been mentioned a few times in The Hollows that witches age slowly once they reach adulthood, which presumably leads to a lot of this. Case in point: Rachel's mother is past retirement age for a human, but can pass for Rachel's sister with the right clothes.
  • In Sweet Valley High, Jessica and Elizabeth's mother is in her late 30s-early 40s (thanks to frequent Retcon between different ghost writers, it changes) but it is often stated that she looks like she is in her 20s, and is usually mistaken for the twins' older sister.
  • In War of the Dreaming, Wendy and her mother, Queen Titania, are said to look about the same age, or even that her mother is younger looking. Justified, since she is an immortal who never ages.
  • In The Riftwar Cycle, Miranda is often commented on as looking younger than her (presumably mid-30s) son. As a kicker, she's at least 300 years older.
  • Inverted in the Maeve Binchy novel Evening Class, where despite not being much older than her daughter (she had her at sixteen), most outsiders correctly assume that Fran and Kathy are mother and daughter rather than sisters, as the daughter has been led to believe.
  • Any Danielle Steel character who either is or becomes a mother in the course of the book is frequently said to look more like her child's older sister.
  • In Discount Armageddon, then 22-year-old Verity Price muses on the fact that because both of her grandmothers look much younger than their chronological ages (her paternal grandmother spending the bulk of her time in dimensions where time passes differently and her maternal grandmother being a cuckoo, i.e. a human-looking being evolved from something rather closer resembling a parasitic wasp), they look more like her sisters than her elders, and will probably continue looking young when Verity herself is their current ages (though it's doubtful if anyone at all knows how old Angela Baker actually is). In Chaos Choreography, four years later, we meet Alice Price, and she looks young enough to be a close-in-age sibling of Verity.
  • Honor Harrington's mother, Allison Harrington, still looks like she is in her thirties, even though she is 104 years old. Justified, though, because of the prolong treatments applied to her.
  • In the Logan Series by V. C. Andrews, Melody's Missing Mom Haille (in her late 30s) turns out to be living in California, where she is trying to launch a movie career, lying that she's at least ten years younger than her actual age. Not only does she look young enough to get away with this, but has no trouble convincing others that she and Melody are sisters.
  • In the Margaret Weis novel Robot Blues, it's mentioned that one of the characters (who's in his 30s) has a mother who, thanks to extensive plastic surgery, is often mistaken for his younger sister.
  • DFZ: Opal's mother doesn't look a day over twenty-five, due to judicious application of makeup, a rigorous beauty regimen, no small amount of plastic surgery, and supposedly "just a bit of actual Black Magic." They are mistaken for sisters or cousins on a regular basis. Since Opal herself is twenty-six and rather average looking, they're fast approaching the point where Opal will look older than her mother.

Judge for yourself: Hunith◊ and Merlin◊ in Series 1, Hunith◊ and Merlin◊ in Series 4.
  • Gilmore Girls:
    • Lorelai and Rory are so close in age (Lorelai was a teen mom) that they often seemed more like sisters. The beginning of the series even has an unsuspecting male who hits on both of the Gilmore girls, separately.
    • In one episode, Lorelai takes her own mother, Emily, to a bar, and a man Emily's age starts to flirt with her. He claims to be surprised that they're a mother and daughter—but Lorelai warns him that if he finishes that statement with this trope, she'll take it as an insult.
  • On Once Upon a Time, due to a curse that left Snow White and Prince Charming effectively frozen in time for 28 years, they look like they're around the same age as their daughter, Emma.
  • On Lost Girl, Fae age considerably slower than humans, so, for instance, succubi Bo and her mother Aife could easily pass as sisters; Inga Cadranel (Aife) is younger than Anna Silk (Bo).
  • On Mom, Bonnie says that this trope is one of the benefits of having a child at an incredibly young age. She used to pass Christy off as her own sister. Christy really does look young enough to pass for Violets older sister, but acknowledges that she's a mother.
  • Reign: Anna Walton, who portrays Diane de Poitiers, is less than three years older than Torrance Coombs, who plays her son Sebastian, and it shows.
  • In Torchwood: Children of Earth, Captain Jack is revealed to have an adult daughter, Alice. But, as he is immortal and doesn't age, she looks a few years older than he does. In order to keep Jack's true nature a secret from Stephen, Alice's son, the boy knows Jack as his uncle.
  • Oddly inverted in the sixth season of My Kitchen Rules, where contestants Will & Steve were mistaken by other teams as father & son, even though they are not blood related and Will is only 5 years older than Steve.
  • Inverted on Buffy the Vampire Slayer—in her early twenties, Buffy is Promoted to Parent for her teenage sister, Dawn. In the first episode of Season 7, Principal Wood mistakes her for Dawn's mother, prompting a tiny freak-out. ("It's the hair, isn't it? I have mom-hair.")
  • Invoked by the mother herself on Providence. As the brother Robbie prepares for his wedding, his fiancee's mother introduces herself to everyone she meets as, "I'm Tina's mother. But don't I look more like her sister?", thus revealing herself to be incredibly vain and self-absorbed.
  • On ER, after Sam drops her son off at school, one of his friends comments to him, "Your sister's hot." This is because she had him at only 15.
  • Played with in season 2 of Stranger Things. 43 year-old Cara Buono certainly is old enough to be Natalia Dyer's (20) mother. And while Buono could probably pass for younger, Karen Wheeler nonetheless looks reasonably like the mother of 16-17 Nancy. However Billy makes use of this trope in the season finale to seduce a lead on Max's whereabouts out of her, flustering her by calling her Nancy's older sister.
  • In seaQuest DSV Tony and Lucas both wig out when they find out the hot babe at the bar is Tony's mother, whose youthful appearance is due to taking cosmetic drugs. Naturally, Tony has the more intense reaction.
    Dagwood: Good looks should please you.
    Tony: I'm pleased. That's what's making me sick!
  • The Marvelous Mrs. Maisel: In "We're Going to the Catskills!" while Midge and her mother are getting their hair done at the beauty salon, another woman gossiping with them comments that they look like sisters. This in spite of the 24 year age gap between 52 year old Marin Hinkle (Rose) and 28 year old Rachel Brosnahan (Midge).
  • War of the Worlds (2019): Chloe looks like an older sister of her son, as she gave birth to him at fifteen.
  • Dark Desire: Due to the small age gap between the actresses playing them (fifteen years) Alma looks more like Zoe's older sister than really her mother.
  • Underground: Due to their actors' close ages, Ernestine appears more like Rosalee and Sam's older sister than really their mother.

    Newspaper Comics 
  • In a later Peanuts sunday strip, Charlie Brown accompanied by Linus goes to return some mittens he bought for the little Red-Haired Girl as a Christmas gift, but wasn't able to muster the courage to give them to her. Surprisingly, the person who he is talking to is the girl's mother. Linus then comments that when they first came in, they thought she was her older sister. She's apparently so flattered by this that she lets them return the mittens without a second thought.

    Theatre 
  • In Gilbert and Sullivan's Iolanthe, Phyllis sees her 24-year-old boyfriend Strephon embracing a beautiful 17-year-old and assumes he's cheating on her. He hadn't gotten around to mentioning that his mother was a fairy.

    Video Games 
  • Yuki's mother in Grandia III, who is 34 while he is 16, but looks about 20. For most of the game, they have more of a sibling relationship than a typical parent/child one, made clearest by the fact that Yuki calls her "Miranda" rather than "Mom." Hilariously lampshaded in the X-Play review of the game and in Unskippable. ...heyyukiyourmomishot...
  • In Dragon's Lair 2, Princess Daphne has 13 kids, the oldest of whom looks about 13, and she still looks exactly the same as she did in the first game.
  • Fire Emblem:
    • In Fire Emblem: Genealogy of the Holy War, most of the ladies in the first generation do actually look legit old enough to become mothers. Tailtiu on the other hand is the youngest of the team and keeps her 'childish' face throughout the time she's in your party, though her appearance at the time of her death when she died a broken girl remains unknown. As a result, if she's drawn in a fanart with her daughter Tine, it's kinda easy to mistake Tailtiu as her more outgoing sister instead of her mother.
    • Fire Emblem: The Blazing Blade has Eleanora, who appears to be in her mid-20s at most, despite having a 17-year-old son.
    • In Fire Emblem: Radiant Dawn, Almedha appears to be a beorc woman in her early or mid thirties, a bit too young to be the mother of Pelleas (who appears to be in his early twenties). She is neither. Fridge Logic means the same applies to Apostle Misaha, who, due to having the exact same brand as her oldest granddaughter, would have still appeared to be in her early thirties when said granddaughter was born.
    • Fire Emblem Awakening uses this a lot, but it's justifed due to the children being Kids From The Future. Many support conversations lampshade how the children's parents look more like their siblings.
    • Fire Emblem Fates uses this too, but it's also justified since the children were raised in Year Inside, Hour Outside "Deeprealms", and grew to maturity in what seemed like mere moments to from the parents' perspective. It's actually specifically noted that Rhajat is now older than her father Hayato, and she even describes herself as being almost like Hayato's 'big sister'. There's also Mikoto, who looks rather youthful for someone who had five kids. This is actually a hint that she's not the mother of the four Hoshidan siblings, just the Avatar. However, she still looks closer to the Avatar's age than one would think she would.
  • In Metal Gear Solid 4: Guns of the Patriots, Big Mama actually looks very well for being almost 80 (and still doing some pretty crazy motorcycle stunts during a highly destructive car chase). Her son Solid Snake also suffers from severe Clone Degeneration and has rapidly aged in the last years to look actually older than her, even though she was over 30 when he was born.
  • In Dragon Age: Origins, it is possible to make a character with grey hair and wrinkles. Their relatives, including parents and stated-older siblings, may wind up looking much younger (in game files, the Warden's "official" age is noted as 20).
  • Samurai Warriors: In the third game, Aya looks younger than her generic-faced son, Uesugi Kagekatsu. In the fourth game, once her son has become playable character with unique, they look around the same age.
  • Pokémon Sun and Moon: Despite being over 40, Lusamine could easily pass for a (very tall) teenager, and her roughly 11-year-old daughter and slightly older brother look more like her younger siblings.
  • Borderlands series: Eye candy Moxxi is the mother of Scooter and Ellie. All three look roughly the same age, even when Moxxi is briefly seen without her exaggerated makeup. She's probably had work done.
  • Due to the Time Crash driving the plot in Mortal Kombat 11, Cassie Cage and Jacqui Briggs come face to face with the past counterparts of their parents, who appear young enough to be siblings to them.
  • In Tales of Symphonia, Raine Sage and her mother Virginia look identical except for the fact that Virginia has longer hair. Since elves live for a thousand years, this is probably standard for them.
  • Tekken has three examples of this trope:
    • Nina and Anna Williams are the mother and aunt of Steve Fox. While the Williams sisters were placed in cryogenic sleep by the Mishima Zaibatsu for research, Nina's eggs were harvested to create Steve. Thanks to the cryo-sleep, both Nina and Anna look like they are in their 20s despite being in their 40s and could easily pass for Steve's sisters instead of his mother and aunt.
    • Jun Kazama doesn't seem to have aged a day in her return in Tekken Tag Tournament 2. A possible explanation is that whatever transformed her into Unknown preserved her youth.
    • Michelle Chang is the adoptive mother of Julia Chang. Without this knowledge one could mistake them for siblings if they were seen together.

    Visual Novels 
  • In CLANNAD, Sanae Furukawa looks like she's only slightly older, at most, than her daughter Nagisa. Fitting, seeing as she's a hot mom. She looks young enough to pass for being the granddaughter of her granddaughter's paternal great-grandmother, who is herself quite young for being a great grandmother.
    • Despite the show/game being filled with Generic Cuteness, Tomoya specifically states Sanae's youthful appearance is strange and mysterious. This is made extremely apparent when you compare her to the midwife who delivered little Ushio. This midwife was actually a classmate of Sanae's in high school.
    • She also genuinely was a young mother, explicitly stated to be fresh out of high school when she got pregnant Combine it with this subtrope and it comes as no surprise that she is also a hot grandma, who perfectly could to be mistaken by the mother of her granddaughter, at least.
    • Played for Laughs in the anime, where Sunohara is set up on a "date" with Sanae. She introduces herself as Nagisa's older sister, and he actually believes it.
  • Emi's mother Meiko in Katawa Shoujo, as Hisao points out:
    Meiko: I'm sorry. Forgive an old woman her amusements.
    Hisao: Old woman? She sure doesn't look that old to me. Clearly Emi gets her youthful features from her mother.
  • Asa's mother Ama from SHUFFLE! looks the same age as Asa. But with Ama's character, her cute Nice Hat and her high-pitched voice, she appears even younger than her daughter.
  • In Danganronpa Another Episode: Ultra Despair Girls, Hiroko Hagakure, the mother of Yasuhiro Hagakure from Danganronpa: Trigger Happy Havoc, looks young enough to be Hagakure's older sister (Hagakure's older than the other students by a few years), resulting in much of the fandom mistaking her for his sister, and Komaru and Fukawa being confused about her age.
  • In Zero Time Dilemma Sigma and Phi are actually mistaken for siblings, courtesy of the former being Supernaturally Young Parent.

    Web Animation 
  • Lampshaded heavily in Bonus Stage when Jessica, Phil's mom, is introduced. We later learn that she looks younger when one of Joel's experiments in de-aging goes haywire.

    Web Comics 
  • In Jodie Troutman's extended universe, Robyn, the matriarch of the Flint family is 46 but looks 30 because she discovered the Fountain of Youth when majoring in Historical Archaeology.
  • Frigg in Brat-Halla looks to be in her 20s or 30s and takes a lot of turns as Ms. Fanservice. Justified because she's the Norse goddess of fertility, is very much Older Than She Looks.
  • In Fluttering Feelings both Seol-A and No-Rae's mothers don't look much older then their youngest children, both 23. No-Rae's oldest brother is in his 30's.
  • Taken to an impossible extent in the Touhou fan-comic Four Seasons House, thanks to its central conceit. In this story, Suwako Moriya◊ is said to be Sanae Kochiya◊'s birth mother...and yet Suwako is a grade schooler while Sanae is in high school. This is remarked upon in-universe, and the rest of the cast is just as mystified by it as the reader.
  • In The Gamer, Han Jee-Han's mother is 40, but appears to be 20, while Han Jee-Han himself is in his late teens. Jee-Han thinks she's hiding something important, going by what he can see of her thanks to his abilities.

    Western Animation 
  • Daphne's mother from Scooby-Doo! Mystery Incorporated doesn't look too different than her daughter.
  • Queen Glimia from Dave the Barbarian. Same reason as Jane Jetson and Crystal Zilla.
  • Voltron: Legendary Defender: Keith' mother Krolia could easily pass herself off as his older sister. In a flashback to shortly after her son was born, she only looks a little bit younger than she does now. Her species appear to be rather Long-Lived.
  • In The Jetsons, Jane's 33 and Judy's 16, but they look more or less the same age.
  • Crystal Zilla from My Dad the Rock Star, who looks barely older than her teenager daughter Serenity.
  • Mrs. Ridgemount from Stōked. Let's give an example: look at this◊ picture. One of these women is a middle-aged woman with three kids, the other is a girl in her late teens and the middle-aged woman's daughter. Guess which is which.
  • June Darby of Transformers: Prime has a high school aged son (Jack), but could easily pass for a girl in her twenties.
  • In the GoodTimes Entertainment cartoon "Curly The Littlest Puppy" Hayley's mother doesn't look too much older than her oldest brother Michael who's stated to be 16 and all together the parents have 5 kids.
  • Kim Possible and her mother, Anne, look pretty much identical to each other, with a few exceptions: Mrs. Possible is slightly taller in height, has a more pinkish-color to her skin, her hair's shorter and a lighter shade of red, and she's got blue eyes instead of green eyes. There's also this exchange from the Mother's Day episode;
    Dr. Drakken: Kim Possible! And her...sister?
    Mrs. Dr. Possible: Is he hitting on me?
    Kim Possible: Nah, sidekicks just really confuse Drakken.
  • In Total Drama Presents: The Ridonculous Race, this is what Kelly assumes. Her daughter Taylor is understandably confused.
  • In a couple of made-for-TV movies about The Flintstones, Pebbles and Bamm-Bamm have grown up and have kids of their own, yet the Flintstones and Rubbles still look very much the same.
    Wilma: We're going to be grandmothers, Betty!
    Barney: Very young grandmothers, I might add. Right, Grandpa Flintstone?
    Fred: Right you are, Grandpa Rubble.
  • On Daria, this was the title character's reaction upon meeting Brittany's stepmom.
    Daria: Um, hi. I didn't know Brittany had an older sister.
    Ashley-Amber: She does? Cool. Maybe we can get manicures together.
  • A rather strange case in Totally Spies!. The mothers of the main trio don't start out with too strong of a resemblance to the girls. You could tell who was related to who but later in the show, they go through a totally unexplained redesign where they look less like their parents and more like older twin sisters. This left many viewers scratching their heads.
  • Although nothing really calls attention to it, Rusty and Rocky Spokes' dad in The Loud House easily looks more like their older brother than their dad.
  • Bean and her mother Queen Dagmar from Disenchantment, this is a justified example as Dagmar had been in suspended animation, having drank poison that turned her into a stone statue and therefore never aged even though 15 years have passed.

    Real Life 
  • This picture of a woman and her twin daughters set the Internet ablaze, as many who saw couldn't tell at all which one of them was the mother (the woman on the left, if you're wondering). This has been aided by the fact that she's also an Absurdly Youthful Mother—the mother was only 19 when she had her daughters, and at the time that the photography was taken, the twins were 16 while their mom was 35. The rest [the mother] attributes to good genes and good self-care.
    • Same deal with this family too.
  • Kikuko Inoue and her daughter posed as schoolgirls for an ad promoting headphones specifically made to listen to anime songs. Not only Inoue is believable as a classmate of her own daughter, this also goes on to show why she's called the "perpetually 17" voice actress.
  • Actress and model Sofía Vergara was only 19 when she had her son (and only child), Manolo, and yet they appear to be fairly close in age—in fact, the actress/model has remarked that there have been times where people have actually mistaken them for a couple when they've been seen together.
  • Singer Tom Jones fathered a son when he was only 17, but his son could easily pass for being a younger brother.
  • Brazilian volleyballer Virna Dias had a son at the age of 20, and when he had reached said age, both could easily pass as siblings◊.
