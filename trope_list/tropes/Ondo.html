Ondo

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

A subtrope of the Dancing Theme, an ondo is a type of Japanese folk music with a distinct beat pattern. This style is often used as an ending song or an Image Song in a lot of anime, although it may be present in other works of fiction.

Examples:

  • Bokusatsu Ondo de Dokuro-chan (Bludgeoning Angel Dokurochan)
  • Shin-chan Ondo (Crayon Shin-chan)
  • Agumon Ondo (image song for Digimon Adventure 02)
  • Osomatsu-kun Ondo (Osomatsu-kun Second Anime)
    • Advertisement:
  • Doraemon Ondo (Doraemon)
  • Let's Go-Ondo (Engine Sentai Go-onger), one of many Super Sentai examples.
  • Hunter Ondo (Hunter × Hunter)
  • Pekopon Shinryaku Ondo (Sgt. Frog)
  • Kinniku Ondo (Ultimate Muscle)
  • Pinky Ondo (Kira Kira Pop Princess)
  • Shinobu Ondo (Ninja Nonsense)
  • Naruto Ondo (Naruto)
  • Ojamajo Ondo de Happipi (Ojamajo Doremi)
  • Ichijo Ondo (Pani Poni Dash!)
  • Persona Ondo (Persona 2: Innocent Sin)
  • Pokemon Ondo and Pokemon Kazoeuta (Pokémon)
  • The Bon Odori (Rhythm Tengoku)
  • Shrimp Shuffle (Ebi Ondo) (Rhythm Heaven Fever)
  • Gekitei Ondo (Sakura Wars)
  • Zetsubou Ondo (Zoku Sayonara, Zetsubou-Sensei)
  • Di Gi Charat Ondo (Di Gi Charat Nyo)
  • Nekoneko Ondo (Nyan Koi!)
  • "Wa! Wa!! World Ondo" (Axis Powers Hetalia)
  • Choi Bounge Ondo (The King of Fighters)
  • Binchou Ondo (Binchou-tan)
  • Ai No Ondo from Mermaid Melody Pichi Pichi Pitch.
  • Chen's Gensokyo Ondo (Touhou)
    • Advertisement:
  • Bon Dance Ondo from episode 24 of Goldfish Warning!.
  • Dare ga Koroshite Cock Robin Ondo from Boku Patalliro!.
  • Kaiju Ondo from Return of Ultraman.
  • Lupin III: Lupin Ondo from The Mystery of Mamo.
  • Anpanman Ondo (Anpanman)
  • "K.K. Rally" from Animal Crossing, originally titled "Keke Ondo" in the Japanese version.
  • Ankou Ondo from Girls und Panzer. Also called the Anglerfish Dance.
  • Monokuma Song from Danganronpa: The Animation, used as the fourth episode's opening.
  • Arale-chan Ondo, the 2nd ending theme of Dr. Slump and Arale-chan.note The original series that aired from 1981 to 1986 (when it was replaced by the original Dragon Ball).
  • The "One Punch Marching Song" and its Genos version (One-Punch Man)
  • The Noragami Ondo, which didn't get featured in the show itself but was part of an event/campaign about Noragami (specifically the anime's second season).
    • Advertisement:
  • Ondo of Puyo Puyo, the ending theme to the first Puyo Puyo.
  • Sunshine Pikkapikka Ondo!, the B-side to the second ending theme of Love Live! Sunshine!!.
  • Pretty Cure Ondo, which was featured as an insert song in episodes of Go! Princess Pretty Cure and Maho Girls PreCure.
