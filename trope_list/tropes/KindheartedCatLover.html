The more benevolent version of Crazy Cat Lady and Right-Hand Cat.

It has commonly been said that the dog is man's best friend, but there are many people out there who prefer company of a more feline variety. Unlike the above mentioned evil and crazy versions of the cat loving tropes, these characters have an honest affection for cats and are usually decent individuals themselves.

The range of love for cats in these characters runs a wide spectrum. Some people simply have a cat as their best friend or just adore them for their inherent cute factor. Other characters have a strong affinity toward them and will even go as far as to adopt some of their mannerisms or have felines flock to their location.

Characters who follow this trope aren't necessarily good guys, but the kind gestures they make are a way of showing that they aren't all bad — in other words, a scene where they're petting a cat is an example of Pet the Dog. This one crops up in anime a lot due to the way cats are perceived in Japan, as opposed to much of the Western world.

Compare Cuteness Proximity, and Heroes Love Dogs for its canine equivalent (real-life examples go in Canine Companion). See also Crazy Cat Lady, Cats Are Mean, Meganeko, and Introverted Cat Person.

  • Ian Anderson, lead singer/flautist of Jethro Tull, loves his kitties. He's been featured in Cat Fancy and wrote a song about how awesome cats are, "And The Mouse Police Never Sleeps."
  • As mentioned above, the inhabitants of Istanbul are considered this as a whole. The relationship apparently existed before both Islam and Ataturk. On the internet, most photos and videos of cats lounging in busy public areas undisturbed will likely be from Istanbul.note When they're not, they are often from Naha, a town in Okinawa with a similar relationship to its street cats. The inhabitants of the city's Kadıköy district even made the news when they put a small statue for a beloved street cat after his death.
    • On the cats' part, Turkey's street cats — and Istanbul's in particular — are far more human-friendly than most feral cat populations elsewhere in the world. It is not at all unusual for a cat to wander up to a human, headbutt them, and begin purring in a clear bid for petting and/or food. They have even been known to jump into people's laps!note While said cats are, on the whole, surprisingly clean and healthy for street cats, please do wash your hands after you inevitably pet said cat.
    • There's even a whole documentary about Istanbul's loving relationship with cats. The documentary was even considered to be titled Catstantinople.
  • Fashion designer Karl Lagerfeld adored his cat Choupette.
  • Frank Fritz, from American Pickers had a much loved cat called Arby who sadly died of old age (he was 18, and that's old for a cat). It was discussed in passing on the series that Frank has always been a cat man, and it can be seen that Frank, whilst not actually crying over the loss of his cat, was VERY subdued for quite a while after his death.
  • SungWon Cho, better known as ProZD, has two black cats named Effie and Sophie. While he occasionally makes fun of them in his videos, it's clear that he genuinely loves them.
  • A twitter thread by Paris Zarcilla went viral in 2018 when the screenwriter/producer discovered an unknown cat nursing four newborn kittens under his bed. She had apparently entered through a back door he often left open. It has completely changed his life; The monsters under my bed have turned into kittens...
  • A common piece of advice when it comes to dating is to see how they feel about cats. Someone who loves cats is likely to be a big softy, someone who likes cats okay is probably nice enough, and someone who hates cats is probably a jerk.
  • The atheist/humanist writer and speaker Greta Christina is known to have had sixteen cats throughout her life.
  • Mark Twain was one if this quote is anything to go by:
    "When a man loves cats, I am his friend and comrade without further introduction."
