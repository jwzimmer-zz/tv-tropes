Better Off with the Bad Guys

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

"I want to go back... right?"
Advertisement:

A character falls in with a group generally regarded with ill-repute, be it gangsters, monsters, or the literal hordes of hell. However, their lives before this were so terrible that such a situation is an unambiguous step up.

Most often the "bad guys" are more sympathetic to the character's plight than a regular person would be, or their situation is such that they genuinely fit in better with this darker group than they ever did with normal society.

This can be played for laughs or for drama. When Played for Laughs or lightheartedness, a starker contrast between the "bad" guys being decent people vs where the character came from can be seen. Often, despite their bad reputation, they're absurdly kind and are only considered 'bad' for something outside their control, such as a frightening appearance.

Advertisement:

When Played for Drama, things take a darker turn as the "bad" guys are likely every bit as evil and horrible as their reputations suggest... and they're still an improvement over where the character was previously. Characters in this situation are prone to be very loyal due to the kindness they've been offered, even if it means becoming an outright villain. Odds on an eventual HeelFace Turn are fifty-fifty.

For related tropes see Raised by the Supernatural and Raised by Orcs, which often involves a similar situation of a human being raised by something not typical, and often considered evil, and Changeling Fantasy, which often sees a human entering a strange, new, world for adventure. When this trope concerns supernatural creatures, it can also include Humans Are the Real Monsters. If the villains are actively creating this situation, they're promising Better Living Through Evil and/or Evil Pays Better.

Advertisement:

Examples:

    open/close all folders 

    Anime & Manga 
  • My Hero Academia:
    • As a child, Tomura Shigaraki grew up in a strict home, with a father who would punish him by forcing him to sit outside until he behaved himself, a mother and grandparents who offered only platitudes, and a sister as likely to sell him out as console him. When Tomura's quirk to cause objects he touches to decay awakened, it was after his father's emotional abuse turned physical and resulted in Tomura massacring most of his family by accident, then deliberately killing his father when he attacked Tomura in an attempt to defend himself. It was soon after this, and after being left alone due to Bystander Syndrome, that he fell into the clutches of Greater-Scope Villain All For One, who raised him from that day forward, becoming his mentor, encouraging his more destructive tendencies, and teaching him how to achieve his goal of upending Hero Society. All For One is perhaps the one thing in the world Shigaraki doesn't hate with a passion.
    • La Brava became enamored with the self-proclaimed villain Gentle Criminal after seeing the videos he posted of himself committing crimes, lending her hacker skills to his cause to become a name worthy of the history books. Prior to this, she had been a Hikikomori following mockery from her first love and had even been contemplating suicide. Gentle's presence in her life gives her cause to go on, despite doing so making La Brava herself an accomplice to his crimes.
  • Negative Yuusha To Maougun Kanbu: Rene is the heroine raised to fight the demon lord. However, she doesn't actually want to fight and responds to demon army leader Hyudooru's threats of death with relief. The whole situation makes Hyudooru change gears, offer her a place in his castle as his assistant studying magic, and his soldiers, once they hear about the situation, welcome Rene with open arms.
  • Takeo-chan Bukkairoku: Takeo is Born Unlucky to the degree that even the hundred yokai who inhabit the home she's given to live in can't shock her. However, the negative energy that's built up in her because of this is so delicious to those same yokai that they make a deal to make her cheerful, the only thing that can shock Takeo enough to release her negative energy, for as long as she can provide it and, in the process, figure out how to alleviate the curse that causes her abysmal luck.
  • Trigun: Legato Bluesummers didn't even have a name before he met Big Bad Knives, to say nothing of how horrifically badly he was treated in his former life. Knives might not view him as much better than any other human, but there's no doubt Legato is far happier being his Dragon.
  • Welcome to Demon School! Iruma-kun: The story starts with fourteen year-old Iruma being sold to the devil by his own deadbeat parents and brought to Hell. Fortunately, all said devil wants is a grandson to show off to his friends and Iruma starts to live a life of luxury. He stops trying to go back to the human world when he remembers how his parents stole anything he produced and made him live in the wilderness.
  • Yokai Ame Onna Ni Yoru Kamigakushi (Spirited Away by the Youkai Rain Woman) starts with a rain youkai encountering a little boy and says if he doesn't stop crying, she'll take him away to become her child. She's surprised that he agrees immediately, then learns exactly what the kid's parents are doing to him, so she takes him in (after murdering his parents, fortunately offscreen) and becomes a devoted mother to the child, who goes on to become a yokai in his own right called a Sunshine Boy.

    Fan Works 
  • Dungeon Keeper Ami: As Jared says in "Vampire Solution", his status has gone from being a common adventurer, to the high position of working under royalty, due to being hired by someone most regard as an Evil Overlord:
    Jered could sympathise with the feeling. He hadn't properly internalised yet that he was now working directly underneath an empress. Him!

    Film - Animation 
  • In Beauty and the Beast, Belle is treated as nothing more than eye candy by most of the townsfolk and scorned for her interest in books. After agreeing to live with the Beast in exchange for her father's freedom, she is delighted to discover that the Beast owns a massive library. All of the Beast's servant treat her respectfully and are happy to have her around. She only goes back to the village when she learns that her father is being denounced as insane for his account of what happens to him, and in the end she ends up living with the now restored Prince Adam.

    Film - Live-Action 
  • In Guardians of the Galaxy, a young Peter Quill is abducted by aliens shortly before his mother died of cancer, and was being raised by Yondu Udonda as a Bounty Hunter before eventually becoming a hero. In Vol. 2, Quill find his real father, who wanted take over his mind for a galactic Assimilation Plot (and would have killed him if he failed). Quill's real bonds are with Yondu, who in the end raised him as his son.
  • The Man from Colorado: When Del realises that his friend Owen has gone completely insane, he quits his job as marshal and joins the outlaws he had been previously trying to capture, because the outlaws are the only force providing any opposition to Owen, and he knows that most of them are decent men who have been forced into banditry by Owen's reign of terror.

    Tabletop Games 
  • Warhammer: Subverted with beastmen. People whose mutation give them animal traits are soon driven out from their native homes, and often find refuge in beastmen hordes. However, they form the absolute dregs of beastman society (as they have a Might Makes Right mentality and use horns as an indicator of power). They can at best make themselves useful to beastmen by selling out their former neighbors.

    Video Games 
  • The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim: The Hearthfire DLC allows the Player Character, who may very well be a member of the Thieves' Guild or the Dark Brotherhood, to adopt children. The DLC also adds four orphans who are either living on the streets or are living a life of exhausting labor to earn a sleeping place that is only technically under a roof. If they are adpoted by the Player Character, they live in an actual house, sleep in a real bed, get regular meals, and overall get to be kids again.
  • Ensemble Stars!: Akatsuki were antagonists during the war and were involved in quite a few morally questionable acts that hurt many other people, so Keito feels guilty for bringing Kuro into all of that as a member. However, Kuro entirely disagrees and is actually grateful he was brought in, as before he joined Yumenosaki he was an aimless thug who hated his life and himself. He regrets his actions in Akatsuki, but they weren't worse than anything he was doing before, and being in Akatsuki introduced him to new friends and a new purpose he never would have otherwise been able to find.

    Web Original 
  • In Brig Scarlet Flamingo, Joseph Avery is desperately unhappy and lonely both in his hometown and later at the orphanage (other children bully him, and as no adult looks out for him or gives him any advice, his only solution is to beat them up at the slightest provocation). He eventually runs away and joins a pirate crew, finding happiness and fulfillment, and the pirate captain becomes his Parental Substitute.
  • Morty Kombat: Upon being warped to Outworld, Morty finds himself hanging out with Raiden and Jax before being approached by Shao Kahn with the request to join the bad guys. At first, Morty refuses due to considering Raiden and Jax his friends, but he is quick to change his mind after he remembers how they done show him any respect even when he loses his death fights. Upon joining the villains, Morty finds himself winning more fights, being treated with more respect, and even usurping Shang Tsung as Shao Kahn's right-hand man after beating Shang in a fight. He does go back to good after Shao Kahn forces him to fight Sonya, though.
