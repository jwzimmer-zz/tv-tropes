"Lion King" Lift

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Laconic
  • Create New

The Circle of Life and its many, many imitators note From top left: The Lion King (Ur-Example), Animaniacs, Teen Titans Go! To the Movies, Uganda Be Kidding Me, Sam & Max and The Simpsons.
Advertisement:

Disney's The Lion King opens with the song "Circle Of Life" playing over Rafiki lifting the newborn Simba in presentation to the animal kingdom. It was such a distinctive scene that it served as the teaser trailer for the film, and it has since turned into a stock parody and stock shout-out in pop culture.

Humorous examples can include the lifted character getting dropped.

The music accompanying the scene is usually an African Chant.

One of The Newest Ones in the Book.

Advertisement:

Examples:

    open/close all folders 

    Advertising 
  • One of the teasers for Lilo & Stitch depicts the iconic scene with Rafiki lifting Stitch instead of Simba. Timon then says, "Hey, that's not Simba!". After Stitch speaks in his native alien language, all the animals run away.

    Anime and Manga 
  • In My Love Story!!, Takeo's mother recounts how when Takeo was born, he was held up for everyone to see a la Simba. Rinko then asks if it was a reference to The Lion King, only for Mrs. Gouda to not understand the reference.

    Comedy 
  • The poster◊ for Chelsea Handler's Uganda Be Kidding Me: Live Netflix special has her holding up a dog on a rock that looks like Pride Rock flanked by a couple of lions.
Advertisement:

    Film — Animation 
  • Quest for Camelot: During Cornwall's verse of "If I Didn't Have You," Devon holds him up from a Pride Rock-esque ledge, matching the lyric: "Cornwall, they would sing/ 'Cause I would be the dragon king."
  • Teen Titans Go! To the Movies: After Robin's humiliation at the movie premiere, he has a nightmare spoofing "Circle of Life" where he's presented by Batman, only to get dropped off the rock for being a "sidekick".
  • In the wraparounds of Around the World with Timon & Pumbaa, Pumbaa gets struck by lightning and develops amnesia. Timon tries to restore his friend's memories through the episodes featured in that video. In the seventh wraparound, Timon lifts Pumbaa on Pride Rock in front of all the animals, then asks him, "Well?". Pumbaa then says, "Nope, doesn't ring a bell.".
  • The cover art for the original home video release of The Lion King 1½ depicts Timon lifting Pumbaa on Pride Rock in a parody of Rafiki lifting Simba.
  • The source movie itself has fun with this in the Animated Outtakes, when Rafiki goes to lift Simba...and drops him off Pride Rock.

    Film — Live-Action 
  • George of the Jungle: The movie parodies this at the end with George holding up Ursula and his newborn son for the animals to see as the movie closes out.

    Live-Action TV 
  • Doctor Who: In The Christmas Invasion, after awakening from a regeneration-induced coma, The Doctor starts into one of his Rousing Speeches, only to break off when he realizes he's quoting The Lion King.
  • On the pilot episode of Modern Family, Mitch and Cameron introduce their newly adopted daughter Lily to the family with this, complete with the "Circle of Life" music. In the Series Fauxnale "A Year of Birthdays", Haley and Dylan's twins were given a similar introduction.
  • The Office (US) episode "Baby Shower" has a scene where Michael raises the baby above his head and yells "Lion King!"
  • The Weird Al Show: During the Expository Theme Tune, when Al is said to "really [make] a mighty fine jelly bean and pickle sandwich, for what it's worth", just as the CGI Al places the last jelly bean and slaps on the top piece of bread, the shot zooms out to show him standing on Pride Rock, holding the sandwich up to the sun as a beatific choir sings. Then the rock snaps and falls out from under him.

    Webcomics 
  • Schlock Mercenary: Lieutenant Foxworthy (temporarily legless) asks an uplifted gorilla to lift her up so she can issue an order by line-of-sight. The page includes a footnote on how the similarity to a certain movie poster is coincidence.
  • In Square Root of Minus Garfield, "Hakuna Lasagna" by Densetsu Bros has Garfield about to throw Odie off of Pride Rock. Jon, in the distance, shouts "Put the dog down!"
  • This was how the 'off brand' pregnancy test Leona and Carl purchased showed that Leona was pregnant in Kevin & Kell. For bonus points, Leona's a lioness.

    Web Original 
  • In the lead-up to the release of Star Wars: The Force Awakens, some fan art had Darth Vader lifting Kylo Ren this way, as a reference to the fact that James Earl Jones provided the voices of both Darth Vader and Mufasa. This became Hilarious in Hindsight when The Force Awakens revealed that Kylo Ren was Darth Vader's grandson.

    Web Video 
  • Ask a Ninja: The Stinger of Episode 16 "How To Kill A Ninja" ends with the Ninja in heaven, singing the Circle of Life while lifting a miniature version of himself like Simba.
  • The Nostalgia Critic: The end of the The Lion King (2019) review, naturally, ends on Critic lifting his cat Buster into the air to christen him as the fandom's "cash cow."
  • The Nostalgia Chick: The title card features Chick on Pride Rock, lifting her dog Kali into the air. At the end of the review, she embarrassingly admits to doing that pose in real life, then shows a montage of her and many others lifting their pets into the air.

    Western Animation 
  • The Adventures of Sam & Max: Freelance Police: In the fake Previously On segment in the very first episode, the last of of the random flashbacks has Sam holding up Max on top of Pride Rock, beams of light shining through the clouds. Then the rock instantly snaps and falls out from under them, taking them with it. The flashback before that has them both riding an angry, bucking warthog through the jungle, which might or might not be an intentional reference to Pumbaa.
  • Animaniacs spoofs this scene with Yakko playing Rafiki, raising a newborn tiger cub only to accidentally drop him from a Pride Rock-esque rock formation, then saying "Oh, I thought they landed on their feet."
  • Brickleberry: The episode "Squabbits" has a scene where African chanting is heard over a magnificent sunrise and tourists gathering at the park to see a squabbit dramatically raised in the air.
  • In an episode of The Critic, a preview of The Cockroach King is shown, in which New York City wildlife (sewer alligators, pigeons, alley cats, seagulls, rats, and so on) gather in front of a crate to be shown their new king, a cockroach voiced by Howard Stern.
  • Drawn Together: In "Clum Babies", Wooldoor masturbates for the first time and produce a "clum baby", to which the other housemates bow in respect to as faux-African chanting is heard.
  • Family Guy: In "Peter Problems", Peter shows up a monkey holding up a lion cub by lifting a cub with his new forklift.
    Giraffe #1: Uh, are you gonna stay for the bris?
    Giraffe #2: Nah, I don't wanna see that!
  • Parodied in the House of Mouse episode "King Larry Swings In". While preparing for the arrival of King Louie's twin brother, Mickey reminds Donald that he's had bad luck with royalty in the past; cut to a Flashback recreating Simba's presentation, with Donald holding the baby Simba... only to drop him.
    Donald: Uh-oh.
  • In Phineas and Ferb the Movie: Across the 2nd Dimension, early on in "Everything's Better With Perry", Phineas recreates the exaltation of baby Simba with Perry in his arms.
  • The Simpsons: As "Itchy & Scratchy" partially parodies Disney animation, they had to spoof it in the episode "Girls Just Want To Have Sums", with the musical Stab-A-Lot: The Itchy & Scratchy Musical directly parodying the Broadway adaptation as depicted in the page image.
  • South Park: Occurs in "A Very Crappy Christmas", at the end of the song "The Circle of Poo", with Mr. Hankey lifting up his son Cornwallis from a high rock as many African animals look on (and take a dump).
  • "Tink'n Bout Animals" is a bonus short from Tinker Bell and the Legend of the NeverBeast, in which Fawn sings the collective names of groups of animals. When she gets to "pride," she naturally does one of these.

    Real Life 
  • Fittingly, the Detroit Lions team from the National Football League introduced the "Lion Cub Cam" in 2016, where Lions fans raise their kids for the jumbotron camera between quarters. Most notably, when Lions quarterback Matthew Stafford became a father in 2017, his wife Kelly introduced their twin daughters this way to the Lions fandom.
