I Can't Believe I'm Saying This

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Laconic
  • Create New

"Fern, I have never, and would never, utter these words under any other calculable context hypothetical or hyperthetical in the infinity of omnixistence, but this is no laughing matter."
— Crash, Awful Hospital

Sometimes, someone says something that is completely out of character for them. It may be agreeing with another character who is usually either prone to dumb ideas or of an opposite mindset. Or maybe someone is disagreeing with another's point of view when the other person is obviously wrong/making the wrong arguments. In other cases, someone is in a position that is so unique that if the phrase were to be taken out of context, it would stick out like a sore thumb. Either way, this phrase is used to highlight the oddness of one person saying it.

Advertisement:

Another common phrasing is: "I never thought I'd say this, but..." and "As much as I hate to say this, ..." Very similar to A Rare Sentence. Similar is, "I can't believe I'm agreeing with X", where X is the The Ditz and the person saying it is often the Only Sane Man, in which case it would be Dumbass Has a Point.

For added irony, this may precede the Flashback to Catchphrase.

Not to be confused with Can't Believe I Said That, where the regrettable utterance has already occurred. Compare A Rare Sentence, where the statement is so bizarre that it would normally be out of character for anybody to say it. Contrast I Always Wanted to Say That.

    Anime & Manga 
  • Said word for word in Chapter 551 of Naruto.
    Naruto: I can't believe I'm saying this, but thanks for the save, Itachi.
  • In the last episode of Cute High Earth Defense Club LOVE!, during the first revelation of the episode, the main characters all shot "What did you say!?" and En points out that he never thought he'd ever say that in his life. Throughout the episode, they say it two more times, and it's always lampshaded.
    All: What did you say!?
    En: I never thought I'd say "What did you say!?" in my life, but this is seriously a "What did you say!?" moment.
    (later...)
    All: What did you say!?
    En: Okay, that's already the second "What did you say!?" of my life.
    (later...)
    En: Oh fine, I'll say it as many times as you want!
    All: What did you say!?

    Comic Books 
  • Groo the Wanderer became hyper-intelligent during one miniseries. Everyone who knew him was shocked by this, because his normal state is hyper-unintelligent. When the witches Arba and Dakarba saw him reason out a solution to a problem, Arba exclaimed, "Groo, you are brilliant!" and then thought, "There is a sentence I never thought I would say."

  • In the Ready Jet Go! special "Back to Bortron 7", Sean, who is always reluctant to go to space, is convinced to go to the Propulsions' home planet of Bortron 7 through the power of song. After they sing, he says this:
    Sean: I can't believe I'm saying this, but.....let's go to Bortron 7.
  • The Simpsons:
    • Episode "Dangerous Curves":
      Homer: I never thought I'd say this, but [mumbling to himself] stupid Flanders.
    • In "Bart Gets Famous", Bart becomes "the I-Didn't-Do-It Boy" and the whole class is waiting for him to say his line.
      Bart: I can't believe I'm saying this, but... shouldn't we be learning something?
  • SpongeBob SquarePants:
    • In "Krab Borg", Spongebob says they need to get "the poop" on Mr. Krabs to find out if he's really a robot or not:
      Squidward: I never thought I'd say this, but SpongeBob, let's get that poop.
    • Also in the episode "Born Again Krabs":
      Squidward: Mr. Krabs, I can't believe I'm saying this, but how could you trade SpongeBob for 62 cents?!
      Mr. Krabs: [examines coins] You think I could've gotten more?
    • In "Teacher's Pests", Mrs. Puff says this when she gets tired of Mr. Krabs and Plankton destroying the boats with their competing:
      Mrs. Puff: Now these are my last boats and if you wreck 'em, none of you will pass my class. I can't believe I'm saying this, but be more like SpongeBob. He hasn't acted out once.
  • Star Trek: Lower Decks: In "Second Contact", Ensign Boimler utters this nearly verbatim when he discloses to Ensign Mariner that he didn't report her breach of protocol to Captain Freeman.
    Boimler: I can't believe I'm saying it — Starfleet is better with you in it.
  • Star vs. the Forces of Evil, season 3 episode "Princess Turdina", when Marco decides to take Pony Head's advice and keep up the façade of being a princess.
    Marco: I can't believe I'm saying this, but I'm going with Pony's advice.
  • Star Wars: The Clone Wars: In "Deal No Deal", Ahsoka says something very similar to the trope name when she admits that she agrees with Rafa, who doesn't trust her and has been clashing against her all episode, that Trace's impulse decision to eject their cargo of unrefined spice was a very bad idea.
  • Steven Universe: In "Legs From Here to Homeworld", before Connie heads off with Steven on a important mission, her mother asks her if she has her sword, then saying she can't believe she's actually asking Connie that.
  • Stōked: When two girls asked Reef out on a date, his refusal by claiming he was taken was this trope.
  • In Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (2012), Splinter has a moment of this.
    Splinter: You made a wise decision, Michelangelo.
    Turtles: Huh?
    Splinter: Yes, I never thought I'd hear myself say that, either.
  • Transformers: From the Beast Wars episode "Crossing the Rubicon":
    Ratrap: I never thought I'd say this, but I'm gonna miss her.
