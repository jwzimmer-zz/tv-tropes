I was thinking about creating a genre page to cover works like The Nostalgia Critic and it’s imitators. I know there's Video Review Show, but that lumps NC in with things like Zero Punctuation, which I think is fundamentally different. The defining feature for me is that they aren't really reviews, in the traditional sense. The purpose of most NC videos (particularly during the shows pre-revival days) isn't to make an analysis or criticism of the works they talk about or even a very basic "should you buy this?" quality judgement, but to mine those works as raw material for comedy. It's much more accurate to call them riffs, rather than reviews.

I want to be clear that I'm not being snobbish, criticism is not superior to comedy and reviews are not better than riffs. I just think these videos represent a fascinating genre that I want to analyze. Name idea: Riff Review.

The problem I have with using Mid-Review Sketch Show is that it implies that the videos are actually reviews, which they really aren't. That's also why I don't like Caustic Critic. What's unusual about the AVGN/Critic-spawned review comedy genre is the lack of consistency. You have some reviewers like Lindsay Ellis even in her Nostalgia Chick days and Todd in the Shadows having more salient review content upfront, whereas you also have creators like CinemaSins who can barely be offered the distinction of critic despite their schtick. And even so, a lot of valid criticism can make its way into a zany, irreverent review. Online review comedy is something of a spectrum in my mind, even if a lot of it was influenced by the same things.

Maybe we can call it the Video Riff Show?

I don't know, there are lots of different types of reviewers and riffers that could qualify for that. Something like The Nostalgia Critic or CinemaSins certainly mostly are just there to mock, rather than review, but then there's weird examples like The Mysterious Mr. Enter, who combines elements of both riffers like the Critic and more serious reviews (arguably not well IMO, but that's for another discussion.) Where would he fit in?

And what about shakier examples like Pan Pizza of RebelTaxi, who makes fun of bad movies and shows sometimes in a vaguely-similar style, but is far from a Caustic Critic, and has an overall very different style influenced less by the angry style of the Nerd or the Critic, but a more laid-back style influenced mainly by old Toonami bumpers and the like? Where would someone like Pan fit in to this?

I don't know how to trope this idea. Critique channels on YouTube vary wildly in style and tone, from Caustic Critic types like the Nerd, the Critic, and Mr. Enter, to long, intellectual video essays by people like Lindsay Ellis and ContraPoints, to everything in between. Maybe we should stick to just "angry" reviewers, to make things less complicated? We could call it the Angry Review Show.

I was looking for a trope that covers Equal Opportunity Armies. Basically the same as Equal-Opportunity Evil, but then for complete armies (or other combative groups like superhero teams) that can be anywhere on the sliding scale of good vs. evil.

I found that we have Gender Is No Object, but this is only for mixed-gender armies. however, the equal opportunity army would also include being unconcerned with Sexuality, race and (in the case of Sci Fi and Fantasy) species.

Some samples to show what I'm referring to:

  • the Republic City army in Legend of Korra which has benders and non-benders, coming from all different parts of the world, male and female.
  • the hunters in RWBY which include Humans and Faunus; different races; all genders and sexual orientations.
  • the Rebel Alliance in Star Wars Legends, and the Resistance in the Sequel Trilogy.

The problem is that if this were to be created as a separate trope, there would be large overlap with Gender Is No Object.

What would be the best way to go about this? Create a new trope and accept that there will be overlap? Or expand Gender Is No Object through TRS in a larger trope that also includes other non straight white males?
