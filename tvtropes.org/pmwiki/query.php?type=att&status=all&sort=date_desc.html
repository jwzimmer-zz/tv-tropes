Ask The Tropers

Go To

Have a question about how the TVTropes wiki works? No one knows this community better than the people in it, so ask away! Ask the Tropers is the page you come to when you have a question burning in your brain and the support pages didn't help. It's not for everything, though. For a list of all the resources for your questions, click here.

Ask the Tropers is for:
  • • General questions about the wiki, how it works, and how to do things.
  • • Reports of problems with wiki articles, or requests for help with wiki articles.
  • • Reports of misbehavior or abuse by other tropers.

Ask the Tropers is not for:
  • • Help identifying a trope. See TropeFinder.
  • • Help identifying a work. See YouKnowThatShow.
  • • Asking if a trope example is valid. See the Trope Talk forum.
  • • Proposing new tropes. See TropeLaunchPad.
  • • Making bug reports. See QueryBugs.
  • • Asking for new wiki features. See QueryWishlist.
  • • Chatting with other tropers. See our forums.
  • • Reporting problems with advertisements. See this forum topic.
  • • Reporting issues on the forums. Send a Holler instead.

Does this "work" really deserve a page?

So, Greeny Phatom has a page here... In case you're unfamiliar with it, let me fill you in: It's a series of crudely drawn MS Paint characters with Limited Animation in the form of stop-motion or playing out like a comic. It uses Microsoft text-to-speech voices as its form of "voice acting" and relies heavily on irrelevant humor and plots related to logos. It was created by Robert Stainton (sorry if that's not the correct way to spell his surname) in 2008 and has a fanbase of prepubescent grade-school boys.

So... I know There Is No Such Thing as Notability, but this really pushes the limit. For one, the Wikipedia page for this "web show" was deleted on account of it not being a real show, but rather a fanmade series of MS Paint drawings made into a "cartoon", and has been protected indefinitely after repeat attempts to recreate it in the past. The series is not created by a public icon; Robert has no celebrity notability whatsoever and doesn't make money off of these videos. He is not signed to a web animation industry or any other form of entertainment business that paints him or his work as official. In fact, there have been many rip-offs/spin-offs of this series created by young boys on YouTube and those never resulted in plagiarism lawsuits because the entire series is too unprofessional to be taken seriously. There have even been attempts to pass it off as a real show by fans creating IMDB and TV.com pages for it a few years back, but those got rightfully deleted.

In conclusion, is it really worth it to have a page for this here? Again, TiNSTaN, but by keeping a page on it here it would give the series undeserved public notability and fool people into thinking it's a real show. Speaking of which, this "show" is listed on the Long Runner page under the 10+ years folder; for a web series as unprofessional and obscure as this, do you think that it could at least be removed from that list lest it deceives readers?

EDIT: Also the creator has several times tried to make it look like a real show that aired on real-life TV networks like Cartoon Network and PBS Kids, and also claimed it to have come out in the 90s despite not opening his channel until 2009 and the fact that the series contains numerous references to things that didn't exist until the 2000s, which alone gives me the impression that it's far too deceiving to be worthy of its own page.
