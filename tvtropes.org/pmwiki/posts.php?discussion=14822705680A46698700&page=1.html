More character development-driven thread here, this thread’s focusing on the action.

This is the sixth Arena thread bah gawd.

Alright, this is gonna be a bit wordy, but don’t worry, most of it is just me clarifying rules, and adding Examples of Play to help people out a bit.

What is the Arena?

I’m gonna put it quick: The Arena is basically pretend fights online. You put on an Avatar, throw them/it into the thread, and have them fight other avatars to see who comes out on top. Simple dumb fun, worked for over six years, and many more if we count the countless other forums that have similar threads (we ain’t the first, people!)

So getting on with it, The Rules.

  • Standard TV Tropes rules apply! I can’t control what will happen if you break these, and most of them apply here anyway (etiquette and such.)
  • Play as your avatar. It’s simply easier for everyone to assume your avatar is who you’re playing as, as it should be. While it’s a big big pet peeve of mine, it’s also honestly not THAT severe of an offense, so at most, if someone repeats this countless times, a stern little reminder might be given.
  • Be reasonable. If your character’s likely going to take a hit, let him take a hit, please. If Bob Sagat’s getting shot at by a machine gun, he’s PROBABLY gonna take a bullet or two even if its nonlethal. Example: Steve Austin lost track of Geese Howard in the middle of a fight, when Geese suddenly comes up with a kick to the back of the head. In all fairness, it's pretty unlikely Steve’s gonna see it coming, unless Geese gives a warcry beforehand, or another indicator of his presence.
  • Ghosting and Autohitting. This is when you either dodge attacks continuously to the point of ridiculousness, or write posts as if your attack is absolutely guaranteed to hit. While normally a pretty easy to understand rule, sometimes there’s a bit of confusion. Let’s take the move Aerial Ace from Pokémon for example. Ingame, it never misses, no matter how evasive the enemy is. Instead, of that, however, you could write that the Pokemon using the move is instead “slicing at an incredible speed”. Still highly likely to hit, but it gives room for the character to dodge if they’re able to. As for ghosting, sometimes characters might do a thing where they turn invincible, example being the Medic and whoever he’s paired with’s Ubercharge. For this, I’d say decide on a post limit for how long it lasts, and don’t abuse it.
  • Original Characters: I’m not gonna be super hard on this one, but I’m not gonna be as lenient as before. If you wanna play an OC, notify me first. I promise myself that I won’t abuse this myself, either, since I’ll likely be playing OCs here myself.
  • The Fourth Wall Rule: The fourth wall’s always had a bit of negative history in the Arena, but I think I’m gonna be a bit lenient with it in this generation. If you want to have a character who is already an established “wall breaker” character make a quick little remark on the “Almighty nerds who run this showbiz”, go ahead. If this gets out of hand and people start using it to give these characters advantages (Example: Deadpool is facing off against Crocodile. Deadpool suddenly looks at a wiki page to learn that Crocodile is weak to water, before tossing a bucket of the stuff at him.), there will be consequences and I might have to tighten this rule. If anybody uses this to complain about other players unironically, there will be a full ban on this.
  • Character Balancing: I’m not gonna make people unable to play characters they like for whatever reason, but if you’re worried that a character might be TOO powerful, message me! We can work out checks and balances together. Example: Bigg Kok 9001 wants to play Superman, but he’s concerned that he might be a bit too busted for the Arena (which, well, he’s right by a wide margin). He messages me, and we work out a few checks and balances to make him a fair character.
  • Character Bans: Please no religious figures, unless it’s either a fictional representation (many Shin Megami Tensei characters, as an example), or a sillier take on somebody else (Jesus Christ, except he’s ripped and has guns and smokes, or stuff like that). If, however, you abuse this to soapbox, or even intentionally try to piss off other people about something (Jesus Christ except he’s an insane normal man who gets put down easily), I will restrict this rule further. No one likes a bigot. Characters who fight in an explicitly sexual manner will also be banned. This includes stuff such as Senran Kagura characters, unless you ignore the “they get stronger with less clothes” thing and just play them with their other abilities. I wanna keep this at a relatively low level in terms of sexual themes. When it comes to gore and such though, go nuts.
  • Gun Laws: You may only carry a firearm large enough to not be able to be concealed in one’s clothing no this isn't britain. If you’re playing a character that can easily tank attacks that aren’t excessively powerful, then at least let them take SOME damage from those things in this thread. I’m using guns as an example, because it’s a good one. Say John Rambo is fighting off against Goku. In Dragon Ball, characters far weaker than Goku can take bullets without flinching, so for fairness sake, reduce the durability to a semi-reasonable standpoint. You don’t have to have your normally invincible character die to a single pistol shot, but it’s gonna ‘’hurt’’ still.
  • Entry “Rule”: This isn’t a rule so much as a suggestion, but if there’s a fight going on, and you wanna jump in, don’t have your entry post be “My Character comes in and attacks Your Character.” Instead, try something more like this: “Quote appears in the Arena, looking around confused a bit, until he sees Reaper maniacally laughing as he shoots at Kirby. Readying his gun, he rushes to assist the puffball.” This helps get your character involved, and gives the other players an opportunity to react to the new guy.
  • Mind control! This is a really fun and interesting idea, but it can also be abused. So if you wanna have your character possess someone and make them do something they wouldn’t otherwise, try having some sort of “inner battle” type deal. The exception, of course, is if the player allows it to go through easily anyway.
  • This one’s more a suggestion, but I’ve seen this issue crop up anyway. If you’re playing a sniper character, an interesting move I recommend is to give the other players a warning that something is off. A strange glint in the distance, an odd red dot moving around, or even, the sniper takes a shot, but either misses or grazes. This makes a sniper fight go from horrible one-sided to far more entertaining. Now the sniper has to be on the move to keep his target from closing in, while the other character is now aware that someone is after their head, and has to stop them without being taken out by the next shot.
  • If the setting allows it, nameless NP Cs may be used as simple props (if you want a psycho murderer to start a fight by murdering innocents, go ahead. If you wanna have someone drain another NPC’s life to get a little energy back, go ahead, within reason of course.) Of course, if this is abused, I might have to remove this, but that’s unlikely to happen.

  • Bosses: These are mostly gonna be GM controlled, but if a player really wants to try their hand at playing a boss-like character, gimme a ring. I’ll go over these at a later time. Don’t expect to see them very often.

  • Looting. This has never really been a concern in the Arena, so to make it quick: If a character dies, another is allowed to take their stuff, with permission from the player. A character respawns with their stuff if they haven’t been played for a little bit, but if the character respawns immediately after, try playing around with them not having their toys, might be fun.

  • If a player has been absent for long enough that the action stalls out significantly, feel free to continue on without them and have your character do something else. Preferable wait time is a full 24 hours.

  • no nukes pls

The current setting is a simple freeform city in the modern era. Settings change when a significant number of players want a new setting, and a vote is held on what setting that is. This should take a week or two, which realistically translates to months, but I won’t stop action during the vote. But first, we need to talk about parallel universes.

If anybody has any questions, lemme know. Without further ado…
