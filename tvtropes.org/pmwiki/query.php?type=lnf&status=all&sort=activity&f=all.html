May or may not be obvious, but I'd really appreciate the help.

I've started making contributions to pages for media local to me, and a trope that I see fit to add to one of them hasn't been added yet. However, there's another aspect in this show's use of this trope that makes me wonder if there's others that would also fall into it or if a more precise one I'm looking for exists. I suppose I want to share the word around for this page to support it since that's how I understand about how it works, but I'll also be giving spoilers too so be wary of that.

The show is Ejen Ali and I won't be naming names, just the important details.

The protagonist Stumbled Into the Plot by accidentally receiving a gadget that was to be sent to another evil operative with a food stall vendor being the middle person. The password told to the vendor happened to be the exact words of what the protagonist wanted to buy from the vendor for lunch, since the password was a food product. It wasn't until his next customer that he realised he had given the gadget to the wrong 'operative'.

After the course of the pilot's events, the agency the gadget formerly belonged to recruited him since the gadget was now only functional to the protagonist and they figured he would be useful in missions with the gadget. Now here's where the big fat spoiler comes in We later learn that the protagonist's deceased mother was a secret agent of that agency and made the very gadget that happened to fall into her son's hands.

So with that being said, he stumbled into the plot, but he was always meant to?

You did, sir. Film

This usually appears in any medium, but usually movies. It's essentially where the big boss of something (usually quite inept at his job) tries to use something or ask about current plans. When the machine doesn't work or the boss is informed about some weird thing that causes his plans to fail, he usually asks "What idiot came up with that idea?!" or "What moron designed this thing?!" to which the underling responds "You did, sir."

Obvious joke nobody makes Western Animation

Something lends itself to an obvious joke or pun (such as a character's name or appearance), but nobody makes the joke in-universe. That's either because the joke is too dirty for the show's rating, because the characters are too dumb, or because the creators want to Troll the audience.

Does Toy Ship apply to teenagers?

Most of the trope description entails childhood innocence and naivety, which aren't traits we associate with teenage characters unless they're a Cutie or Genki Girl. Do teens who at least fall under the childlike and cutesy category like those two other tropes qualify as examples of Toy Ship?

Either way, if Toy Ship doesn't apply to teenagers regardless, then I don't see how setting age 12 as the cut-off point is accurate, because it's literally only a year away from the teens and so many preteens are entering middle school and puberty and have left behind the naive sense of wonder and curiosity associated with early childhood in favor of sexual experimentation. I'm not saying that 12-year-olds can't be childlike, but then again, teenagers aren't exempt from childlike qualities either, and those in their early teens seem like a fair game for this trope because they're in the same early stage of puberty and junior high education that preteens are in and are far behind from post-pubescent late teens almost leaving high school. For further proof, it obviously wouldn't be a case of Precocious Crush for a 12-year-old to have a crush on a 13-year-old because it's just one year and both of them are in middle school together.

...so, what age range is Toy Ship cut-off at?
