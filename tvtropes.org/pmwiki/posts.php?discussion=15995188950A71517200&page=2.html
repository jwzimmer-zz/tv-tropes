GrigorII
Sep 29th 2020 at 1:59:05 PM

Well, if we're going to draw comparisons with the other wiki and their good and featured articles, let me point something: an article is approved, or not, based on its own merits, not on its relations to other articles. If there was no article about "Ant-Man" (to keep my previous example), I could create it and work on it until it can become a featured article... and if there was no article on "Ant-Man and the Wasp", I can create it as well. But if I wanted that article to be featured as well, I would have to do all the hard work as well. "It's a sequel of a film with a featured article" would not get me any leniency over a half-made or substandard work.

There is, however, something similar to those groupings: Featured topics. But it's the same story in there: all articles in the series must be good or featured, or fall into specific exceptions.

As for the need to keep the information about the franchises and how several related works are related... as I said, we already have creator, series and franchise pages for that!

Does this mean that, after a cleanup, there would be less pages listed? Well, yes. That's what cleanup is all about.

Are pages with the most wicks listed so high because they're magnets to poor and zero context examples? Probably, but then they should receive some cleanup as well, and then recategorized to the category they have once the cleanup is ready.
