    Masaaki Endoh 
Not to be confused with Masaki Andoh, this member of JAM Project is good enough to stand on his own two feet.
  • And to kick things off... "GA-GA-GA GA-GA-GA GaoGaiGar! GA-GA-GA GAGAGAGA GaoGaiGar!"
    • Also from the same series: "Power of Desire".
  • Aquarion OP, Endoh style! Now with SRWZ goodness! "Sousei no Aquarion, Masaki Endoh ver."
  • From Muv-Luv Alternative: "Carry On"
    • From the side story The Day After: "The Song of Heavenbound Lights"
  • "Fellows" (Carnival Phantasm)
  • Endoh is capable, on his own, of making ALREADY Awesome Music even more awesome. Case and point: "God Knows...".
    • "Ai wo Torimodose" (Fist of the North Star}
    • "Arashi no Nakade Kagayaute" (Mobile Suit Gundam: The 08th MS Team )
    • "Butter-Fly" (Digimon Adventure)
    • "Cha-La Head Cha-La" (Dragon Ball Z)
    • "Eternal Blaze" (Magical Girl Lyrical Nanoha A's)
      • And for bonus points: a live duet of Eternal Blaze with Nana Mizuki herself!
    • "Go Go Power Rangers" (We shit you not!)
    • "Sorairo Days" (Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann)
    • "Asu e no Brilliant Road" (Uchuu no Stellvia)
    • "Uninstall" (Bokurano)
    • "Muv-Luv" (OP of the eponymous Visual Novel)
    • Tori no Uta (AIR)
    • "Resolution" (Gundam X)
    • "Yuzurenai Negai" (Magic Knight Rayearth)
    • You know how we said the only time you'd ever hear a JAM Project member sing a Yoko Kanno composed song was Angel Voice? We lied.
      • He also sings "Live from Baghdad" from the Cowboy Bebop soundtrack.
    • "Kimi ga Tame" (Utawarerumono)
    • "Ultraman Dyna"
    • "Sketch Switch" (Hidamari Sketch), a ska-inspired cover that puts the MANLY in a Moe opening theme.
  • Endoh Masaaki also did a triple-turned-quadruple crown for Yu-Gi-Oh! 5D's Op4 "Believe in Nexus", "Op5 Road to Tomorrow ~Going my Way~", and what may count as Yusei's true character song "Clear Mind", before adding a final jewel to the set at the end of the series with the soul-touchingly beautiful "Melody of Promises".

    Kenji Kawai 
  • "Making of a Cyborg", the main theme of the classic Ghost in the Shell film.
    • Which is turned into a leitmotif with the addition of "Ghost City" (which plays during the montage halfway through the film) and "Reincarnation" (during the ending credits).
    • Nightstalker"
    • "Floating Museum", played during the climax of the film for luscious Soundtrack Dissonance.
    • From the second film, Ghost in the Shell: Innocence, comes "The Ballade of Puppets: The Ghost Awaits in the World Beyond", a work of genius that contrasts the ass-kicking finale so perfectly it elevates the climax to what can only be described as a spiritual experience.
      • Kawai's interpretation of "Follow Me" is also exquisite.
      • Also from the second film are "The Ballade of Puppets: Flowers Grieve and Fall", "River of Crystals", "The Ballade of Puppets: In a New World, Gods Will Descend", and "The Doll House I" and "II".
  • The soundtrack for the "live action manga" Avalon. Log Off and Log In are the bookend songs of the movie that frame the rest of the soundtrack nicely, most notably Voyage to AVALON.
  • His work on the Patlabor movies count as well. Notably the ending to Patlabor the Movie (Into the Morning Sunshine) highlighting the upbeat "success" of stopping a potential robotic catastrophe, and the track Wyvern to Patlabor the Movie 2, played during the more tense moments of the film, namely the "Kill Wyvern" and the "AH-88 attack sequence on Tokyo" scenes from the movie.
  • His score for the Fate/stay night anime brings us "Night of Fate", "Lonesome Pilgrimage", "Master", "Quirk of Fate", "Holy Grail", "Hereditary Seal", "The King of Heroes", "Winter Fairy", "Bloody Rider", "Contract", "Bonds", "Roar of the World", "La Sola", and "Dash of the Rival Chiefs".
    • There's also his absolutely badass remixes of the two iconic staples from the Visual Novel: "Emiya -Kenji Kawai ver.-" and "Sword of Promised Victory -Kenji Kawai ver.-".
    • The two OPs, "disillusion" and "Kirameku Namida wa Hoshi ni", also qualify.
      • Sachi Tainaka may as well get a section just for these songs. This Symphonic Arrangement of the anime's two opening themes from her CD deserves some consideration, too. Another version called "disillusion -2010-" is arranged for the Fate/Stay Night TV Reproduction OVAs.
    • From the Unlimited Blade Works movie: "Imitation", which perfectly captures Shirou's defense of his ideals against Archer, his future self, and Archer's faith being renewed.
      • Also from Unlimited Blade Works, the thunderous "Binding Realms".
      • Thought Saber's theme "Night of Fate" couldn't get more epic? Think again.
      • More on the "D'awww..." side of things: "Eternity ~last episode~", which plays when Archer finally bids goodbye.
  • And of course, the Death Note live action movies. Hot damn.
  • The OP of the Vampire Princess Miyu OAV series. As well as Now comes the conclusion, from the third OAV. Heartbreakingly beautiful.
  • Moribito: Guardian of the Spirit and this song, "Nahji no Uta". A perfect match for the anime's Heartwarming Moment.
    • Pretty much all of Moribito's soundtrack is so spectacular. "Omoi Haruka" makes you happy to be alive.
  • Pretty much the entirety of the score from Mobile Suit Gundam 00. Notable tracks of awesomeness can be found here.
  • Higurashi: When They Cry: The anime OSTs were made by him and feature some of the scariest tracks in anime.
    • And "Oshin". If there's a more terrifying theme that could be used in that scene...
    • Then for pure musical awesome: "Ran" and "Main Theme".
  • The Sky Crawlers:
    • The appropriately named "Adler Tag" is a highlight of his work from that movie, accompanying a gradual buildup of warplanes for a massive Battle of Britain-style raid.
    • To say nothing of the gorgeous main theme.
    • "Krakow", played during the team's visit to the eponymous city.

    English Fandubs 
As with anything fan-produced, Sturgeon's Law applies to fandubs. However, on occasion you stumble upon that elusive 10% that's absolutely worth it. Here you may learn what your favorite anime songs sound like translated to English.
  • GeekyFanDubs: In addition to having a good singing voice, she reimagines the lyrics of the song so that they flow as naturally as possible, while keeping the song's original meaning.
    • "Uninstall" (Bokurano)
    • "Sorairo Days" (Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann)
    • "Ash Like Snow" (Mobile Suit Gundam 00)
    • "My Soul, Your Beats" (Angel Beats!)
    • "World's End" (Code Geass)
    • And now, "Shangri La" from Fafner in the Azure: Dead Aggressor.
    • "again" (Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood)
  • Cristina Vee: She works as an English voice actress, and she uses her talent to create some of the best fandubs you can find anywhere.
    • "Higurashi no Naku Koro Ni" (Higurashi: When They Cry)
    • "Super Driver" (Haruhi Suzumiya)
    • "Bokutachi wa Kore Kara" (Ranma ½, as Shampoo)
    • "Motteke! Sailor Fuku" (Lucky Star)
    • "Sakura Kiss" (Ouran High School Host Club)
    • "Don't Say Lazy" (K-On!)
    • "High School Of The Dead" (High School Of The Dead)
    • "Do You Remember Love?" Yes, she sang the Macross song!
  • A YouTuber called MidiGuyFDdp21 also does some really nice covers of anime openings.
    • "Crazy Rainbow" (One Piece)
    • "Share The World" (One Piece)
    • "Alones" (Bleach)
    • "Ore Wa Tokoton Tomaranai" (Dragon Ball Z Budokai 3)
    • "Dragon Soul" (Dragon Ball Kai)
    • "We Go" (One Piece)
  • Flairs from Hell sang a few good fandubs, including a few from Touhou.
    • "Naraku no Hana". Second season opening to Higurashi: When They Cry
  • Shadowlink4321. Fall in love. All over again.
    • "Uso", Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood's 1st Ending.
    • "Rain", same anime, 5th opening.
    • "Unbeatable", the English opening to Pokemon Advanced Battle.
    • "Blaze", the first opening of the Tsubasa -RESERVoir CHRoNiCLE- anime.
    • Not an anime example, but from Sonic Adventure 2, he gives us "Escape From The City".
    • Also, already referenced in Sonic the Hedgehog's section, "Golden Rings", a Generations Tribute.
    • "Butter Fly", TV Size.
    • "Sorairo Days", Gosick Style Fandub. (Sorairo Days belongs to a certain Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann).
    • "Golden Time Lover", FMAB's 3rd Opening.
    • "Colors", Code Geass' first opening (now lacking in JIBUN WO~!).
    • "Light My Fire", the first opening to Shakugan no Shana III Final.
    • "Snow fairy", the first opening to Fairy Tail.
    • "Innocent sorrow", one of the D.Gray-Man openings.
    • "STRENGTH", the last ending for the first run of Soul Eater.
    • "We Go", one of the openings to the ever beloved One Piece, complete with the opening recreated with Super Smash Bros. Brawl Machinima.
    • He's also got a particularly good one for "In My World", the second opening to Blue Exorcist.
    • He can also make some good mashups. What happens when you mix "STRENGTH" with "Serment"? Or "Light My Fire" with "Knight of the Wind"? How about "Joint" with "With Me"... Mashed up TWICE?
    • "Hirari", the second opening to Digimon Savers, which is also synced up to a Brawl Machinima; in this case, Smash King.
  • Amanda Lee has done a number of fandubs.
    • "My Dearest", Guilty Crown's first opening theme.
    • "Magia", the ending theme to Puella Magi Madoka Magica.
    • "Crossing Field", the opening theme to Sword Art Online.
    • "Innocence", the opening theme to Sword Art Online's Fairy Dance arc.
    • "Ignite", the opening theme for Sword Art Online II's Phantom Bullet arc.
    • "Rising Hope", The Irregular at Magic High School's first opening theme.
    • "aLIEz", Aldnoah.Zero's first cour ending theme.
    • "Butter-Fly", opening theme of Digimon Adventure.
    • Both Amanda Lee and Shadow Link pooled their efforts and made an English cover to one of the most popular Anime songs of recent history: "Guren no Yumiya", the opening theme to Attack on Titan's first half.
  • StawberryCreme26 has done some amazing fandubs.
    • "Get Along" - Theme of Slayers.
    • "Cutie Honey" - Theme of Re: Cutie Honey.
    • "Fight Together" - 14th Theme of One Piece.
  • This cover of the song "Exist" from the anime of Btooom!. Extra note: the singer is a native German singing in flawless English.
  • Sapphire and the rest of her associates.
    • Y. Chang, one the primary lyricists of the group, has his own channel, and writes lyrics for other artists!
  • KY0MI takes on the challenge of singing Tokyo Ghoul's "unravel" in full English and does a perfect job at keeping the original feel intact.
  • NateWantsToBattle has all sorts of videos, including several hard rock covers of famous anime pieces. He's got loads of them.
    • Again, the first opening to Fullmetal Alchemist Brotherhood.
    • Resonance, Soul Eater's first opening.
    • Papermoon, Soul Eater's 2nd OP.
    • Excalibur's Song. Yet again, from Soul Eater.
    • Sorairo Days, from Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann.
    • Go!!, from Naruto.
    • Dragon Soul, from Dragon Ball Kai.
    • the WORLD, from Death Note.
    • THE HERO !!, from One-Punch Man.
  • TYERecords is another group of fandubbers who does a sizeable amount of good and awesome covers of notable anime songs in the English language.
    • Black Bullet OP.
    • Hacking to the Gate from Steins;Gate.
    • Trio cover of Secret Base, the ending theme of Anohana: The Flower We Saw That Day.
    • Toki Wo Kizamu Uta, the opening theme of Clannad After Story, the later part of CLANNAD.
    • Synchronicity, the opening theme of Tsubasa Tokyo Revelations, an anime adaptation of a Darker and Edgier storyline in Tsubasa -RESERVoir CHRoNiCLE-.
    • Dramatic, first opening song of Honey and Clover.
  • Tara St. Michel is also pretty cool, whether she's singing a sweet feelings-fest or something that goes harder.
    • gravityWall from Re:CREATORS is an intense jam, especially what she does with the hard-to-decipher bits.
    • Nandemonaiya from Your Name. She'd just seen the movie, and it shows with the emotional weight in the song.
    • The eponymous song of Shelter is a great cover also. The harmonies!

    Other Language Fandubs 
  • Nika Lenina is a Russian singer who has done many, many covers of anime songs. Some of her particularly awesome ones are:
    • "Magia" from Puella Magi Madoka Magica.
    • "This Beautiful & Cruel World"—the 1st ending theme of Attack on Titan.
    • "Toki Tsukasadoru Juni no Meiyaku", the ending theme to Steins;Gate (video contains major spoilers for said series, so viewer beware).
    • "Wired Life" from Blue Exorcist.
  • In addition to Nika, the singers from the Radiant Records Youtube channel have done quite a large number of spot-on and really well-made Russian fandubs:
    • "Hacking to the Gate" from Steins;Gate.
    • "Red Fraction" from Black Lagoon.
    • "Tadoritsuku Basho" from the Fate/stay night: Unlimited Blade Works movie.
    • A spot-on group cover of Tenshi no Fureta Yo from K-On!.
    • Really awesome group covers of Love Live! songs on this one: START:DASH!!, Snow Halation and Bokuwa wa ima no naka de.
    • Bad Apple, a famous song from Touhou soundtracks.
  • Paperblossom has done many fandubs in German.
    • "Bauklötze" from Attack on Titan. Bonus points for being a song that was originally written completely in German too.
    • "To the beginning" from Fate/Zero.
    • "Bïos Delta" from Guilty Crown. This one was also originally written in German.
    • "Diamond Crevasse" from Macross Frontier.
    • A German version of "Magia" from Puella Magi Madoka Magica.
    • "Blumenkranz" from Kill la Kill. In this case the song was already in German, but the Japanese singer didn't have the best pronunciation.
  • Cotton Candy Bird, who is a friend of Paperblossom, has also done a few covers. One very notable one is her cover of "Vogel im Kafig", another Attack on Titan song that was written in German.
  • Amaya Kagami, a Russian singer, has done an incredible cover of Fate/hollow ataraxia's PS Vita opening theme "Broken Night" as well as one for the Fate/stay night 2006 anime's ending theme.
