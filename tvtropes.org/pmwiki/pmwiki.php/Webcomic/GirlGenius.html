Mad Scientists rule the world. Badly.

Girl Genius is a webcomic (originally a print series) by Phil and Kaja Foglio, which has become one of the classics of the Steampunk (called Gaslamp Fantasy by the creators) genre.

The title character of the story is Miss Agatha Clay, who begins as a young apprentice to a type of Mad Scientist known to the world as a "Spark". Her adventures span 17 published volumes as of 2018, and that number increases by roughly one volume a year.

Can be found at the Studio Foglio website, accessible through this elegant and finely-crafted link. Please note here, "We recommend Girl Genius for an audience of teens and up". Was also the winner of the Graphic Story Hugo Award for three years running (2009-2011).Fun Fact They actually removed themselves from the running in 2012 because they wanted to give others a chance to win and prove that the award was viable. Since returning to the category, they have been nominated for one more award (in 2014), but did not win.

Advertisement:

There are also novelizations of the comic:

  • Agatha H. and the Airship City
  • Agatha H. and the Clockwork Princess
  • Agatha H. and the Voice of the Castle
  • Agatha H. and the Siege of Mechanicsburg

Other media includes:

  • Girl Genius: The Works (2001); A Card Game based on an the previous game tie-in to Foglio's XXXenophile
  • Girl Genius and the Rats of Mechanicsburg (2014): A mobile game funded through Kickstarter.
  • Legendary Showdown: Machines & Magic (2015): A tabletop Card Game crossover with Gunnerkrigg Court funded through Kickstarter.
  • Munchkin Steampunk Girl Genius (2019): An expansion for Munchkin.
  • Girl Genius - Adventures In Castle Heterodyne (2022): An action adventure game funded through Kickstarter.
  • The Girl Genius RPG (TBA): A GURPS RPG adaptation that seems to have descended into Development Hell... Until 2020, with both the Foglios and Steve Jackson Games confirming that they're finally getting the gears turning again.
    • Advertisement:
  • Girl Genius (TBA): An Animated Adaptation.
