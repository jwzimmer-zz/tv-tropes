Frenemy Tropes

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

" 'Cause we're frenemies
We like disliking one another
Yes, we're frenemies
He's like my least favorite brother"
— Buford and Baljeet, Phineas and Ferb

An index of tropes having to do with character relationships that combine enmity with friendship. Compare and contrast Rivalry Tropes which separates friendship from rivalry. For the romantic/familial versions, see the Dysfunctional Romance Index and Dysfunctional Family Index.

Advertisement:

Tropes:

  • Affably Evil (if a villain is so affable to the point of having a Friendly Enemy relationship with a hero)
  • Aw, Look! They Really Do Love Each Other: Can apply to friends; they fight a lot but ultimately prove they do care about each other.
  • Best Her to Bed Her
  • Bickering Couple, Peaceful Couple
  • Big Bad Friend: A protagonist's close friend is revealed to have been working for the Big Bad, or is the Big Bad themselves.
  • Bully Turned Buddy: A character is friends with someone who used to bully them.
  • Can't Live With Them, Can't Live Without Them: A character finds their companion annoying, yet misses them when they're apart.
  • Commonality Connection: Characters who are initially neutral or hostile bond over sonething they have in common.
  • Cooperation Gambit
  • Dating Catwoman
  • Defeat Means Friendship: A character beats another character at something, leading to them becoming friends.
    • Advertisement:
  • Defeat Means Respect: A character gains respect for another after they beat them at something, which can lead to friendship.
  • Did We Just Have Tea with Cthulhu?
  • Did You Just Romance Cthulhu?
  • Dislikes the New Guy: Someone dislikes the new member of a group for whatever reason.
  • Dysfunctional Family
  • Enemy Mine: Two enemies team up to achieve a common goal, which can lead to a genuine friendship (whether it sticks is another matter).
  • Evil Former Friend: A character who used to be friends with a protagonist until they had a Face–Heel Turn.
  • Fair Weather Friend: A character's friend ditches them when things get tough.
  • Fire-Forged Friends (when they start out as enemies)
  • Foil (when they're on the same side)
    • Bromantic Foil
  • The Friend Nobody Likes: A member of a friend group who is merely tolerated by everyone else in the group.
  • Friend Versus Lover: A character is torn between being loyal to their friend and their love interest.
    • Advertisement:
  • Friendly Enemy: Enemies who are amiable to each other; they may even consider each other a true friend.
  • Friendly Rivalry: A pair of rivals who are amiable to each other and may even consider each other a true friend.
  • Friendly War
  • Friendship Denial
  • Friendship Moment
  • Friendship-Straining Competition
  • Go-Karting with Bowser
  • God Karting with Beelzebub
  • Headbutting Heroes: Heroes on the same side clash with each other.
  • Hero, Rival, Baddie Team-Up: A character teams up with both their rival and their enemy to achoeve a common goal.
  • Hypocritical Heartwarming
  • I Don't Like You and You Don't Like Me
  • Insult of Endearment
  • Jerk with a Heart of Gold: Someone who is capable of being both very rude and kind, sometimes towards the same individuals.
  • Kiss-Kiss-Slap
  • The Lancer
  • Like an Old Married Couple
  • Living with the Villain
  • Noble Demon
  • The Only One Allowed to Defeat You
  • Poisonous Friend: A friend who means well but tends to go for extreme or immoral methods to help out their buddy (sometimes making things worse for their friend in the process).
  • Rivalry as Courtship
  • Rivals Team Up: Two rivals join forces to achieve a common goal; it may lead to a genuine friendship.
  • Roommate Drama (When the roommates are friends normally)
  • Save the Villain: A hero chooses to save a villain when their life is endangered, which can lead to mutual amity, or they may have done it because they already had rapport with them.
  • Science Foils
  • Slap-Slap-Kiss
  • Sympathy for the Devil: The hero pities the villain.
  • Sympathy for the Hero: A villain or rival pities the hero.
  • Teeth-Clenched Teamwork: People grudgingly work together to achieve a goal.
  • Terms of Endangerment
  • Third Wheel: A character who constantly hangs around with a romantic couple (they're usually friends with one or both of them), which can cause issues with the friendship, romance or both.
  • Token Evil Teammate: A member of a group of heroes who is notably immoral.
  • Token Good Teammate: A member of a group of villains who is notably more moral than the others.
  • Tough Love: Treating someone you care about harshly to make them a better person, which can apply to friends.
  • Tsundere: A person who switches between being abrasive and caring with their friends.
  • Villainous Rescue: The villain rescues the hero, which can be due to them having positive regard for the hero and/or may lead to a more positive relationship between them.
  • Villain Over for Dinner
  • Vitriolic Best Buds: Two friends who argue and insult each other constantly, but do genuinely like each other.
  • We Used to Be Friends: Friends have a falling out and stop being friends.
  • With Friends Like These...: A character who is treated poorly by their friends (and it's genuinely harmful to them rather than lighthearted ribbing and so on).
  • Worthy Opponent
  • Yearning for a Nemesis
