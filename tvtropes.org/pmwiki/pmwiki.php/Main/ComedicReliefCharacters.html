Comedic Relief Characters

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

Some characters are well-rounded. Some are flat. Some rarely ever appear in works except to be picked apart and made fun of.

This is a list of characters frequently used for comedy.

  • Abhorrent Admirer: Someone who has a repulsive crush on another.
  • Absent-Minded Professor: Scientist who is lost in thought and out of touch with reality.
  • Accidental Athlete: Someone exhibited athletic qualities unintendedly.
  • Acquainted with Emergency Services: Emergency services are on certain terms with you.
  • Adam Westing: An actor plays a parody of themselves.
  • Adolf Hitlarious: Adolf Hitler and Those Wacky Nazis, Played for Laughs.
  • The Ahnold: Affectionate parodies of 80s action stars.
  • Amazingly Embarrassing Parents: Parents who embarrass their children.
  • Amusing Alien: Extraterrestrials who are funny.
  • Annoying Younger Sibling: A character's younger sibling is a pain in the ass.
  • Anti-Climactic Parent: A variant of The Reveal in which a character spends the episode getting the cast in an uproar about a parental visit.

  • Anything You Can Do, I Can Do Better: Character who is on your tail whenever you make a statement, prepared to respond with something they've done twice as better.
  • Bad Liar: Someone who is terrible at telling lies.
  • Basement-Dweller: A nerdy manchild who is unemployed and still lives with his parents.
  • Beary Funny: Jolly, friendly, rambunctious bears.
  • Blind Without 'Em: Character gets blind without their glasses.
  • Big Eater: Character consumes an abundance of food.
  • Boke and Tsukkomi Routine: Stupid and straight comedy.
  • Boogie Knights: Dancing while wearing knightly armor.
  • Boomerang Bigot: Character hates one of their own kind.
  • Born Unlucky: Bad things happen to this character on a daily basis.
  • Brainless Beauty: Unintelligent person is notably attractive.
  • Bratty Teenage Daughter: Teenage girls in fiction are angsty and argumentative.
    • Advertisement:
  • Bumbling Dad: Fathers in fiction are slow on the uptake.
  • Bumbling Sidekick: The villain's sidekick is stupid.
  • Bunny-Ears Lawyer: A cloudcuckoolander who performs their job competently.
  • Butt Biter: Animal bites a character's butt.
  • Butter Face: A female with an attractive body but an ugly face.
  • Butt-Monkey: Does anything ever go right for this character?
  • Cannot Convey Sarcasm: A character who tries and fails to be snarky.
  • Cannot Keep a Secret: A gossipy person who really can't be trusted to keep their big mouths shut about anything confidential.
  • Cannot Tell a Joke: Someone who tells really lame and unfunny jokes.
  • The Casanova: A perverted womanizer who is constantly seeking out new sexual partners.
  • Casanova Wannabe: A would-be womanizer who thinks they're great with the ladies, but are anything but.
  • Cheerful A.I.: Artificial Intelligence that is friendly to a comedic effect.
  • The Chew Toy: A chronically unlucky character whose constant misfortune is consistently played for comedy.
  • Cloudcuckoolander: Character who is incredibly insane.
  • Comedic Sociopath: An extreme jerkass whose frequent abuse of other people is played for humor.
  • Comically Invincible Hero: Invincible Hero Played for Laughs.
  • The Comically Serious: A rational character is brought into goofy situations to their discomfort.
  • Cordon Bleugh Chef: Good at cooking, but often makes disgusting experiments.
  • Crazy Jealous Guy: Male character is insanely possessive of a certain girl.
  • Cuckoosnarker: An eccentric character delivers sarcasm.
  • Cute, but Cacophonic: A cute character with an obnoxious vocal delivery.
  • Date Peepers: Couple tries to make their date low-key when it is anything but.
  • Deadpan Snarker: Character who responds to eccentricity with sarcasm.
  • Demoted to Comic Relief: When a character becomes downgraded to a more minor role as comic relief.
  • Depraved Dentist: A dentist who is evil.
  • Dirty Coward: Character who selfishly flees at the first sight of danger, often leaving the others doomed.
  • Dirty Old Man: An old geezer who is rather perverted.
  • Dirty Old Woman: The Distaff Counterpart of the above.
  • Disguised in Drag: When men decide to crossdress like women, it's often used as a joke.
  • The Ditz: This character is so incredibly stupid and nonfunctional it's amazing how they're able to look after themselves.
  • Ditzy Secretary: A bumbling secretary not competent enough for their job.
  • Doctor Whomage
  • Dodgy Toupee
  • Donut Mess with a Cop: Police officers love doughnuts.
  • Dumb Blonde: Blonde-haired people are idiots.
  • Dumb Dodo Bird: There's a good reason why these flightless birds all went extinct.
  • Dumb Jock: Athletes are idiots.
  • Dumb Muscle: Strong characters whose muscle mass can only make up for their lack of brains.
  • Dumbass Teenage Son: Teenage boys in fiction are rarely ever intelligent.
  • Elvish Presley
  • Equal-Opportunity Offender: Character loves insulting people, no specific preference.
  • The Family for the Whole Family: A villain who could be a Knight of Cerebus fails to be anything more than ineffectually sympathetic.
  • Fantastic Anthropologist
  • Fat Comic Relief: Overweight characters are used for comedic effect.
  • Fat Idiot: Fat characters in fiction are rarely ever intelligent.
  • Fat Slob: Fat characters are lazy, slovenly, and unpleasant.
  • The Fool: An idiot who is so irritatingly lucky it's amazing karma hasn't hit them.
  • The Freelance Shame Squad: Something embarrassing happens and the rest of the characters laugh at it.
  • From the Mouths of Babes: Malice comes from innocent children's mouths.
  • Funny Foreigner: A foreign character and their cultural differences are played for comedy.
  • Funny Robot: A robot that is funny.
  • Genki Girl: A female character who is incredibly hyperactive.
  • Gasshole: Someone who has the tendency to burp and/or fart.
  • Girlfriend in Canada: Character falsely claims to have a distant partner.
  • Girl Scouts Are Evil: Girl scouts are portrayed as spiteful.
  • Goldfish Poop Gang: Incredibly persistent recurring antagonists who never pose any real threat.
  • Gonk: A character who is very ridiculously ugly.
  • Good Angel, Bad Angel: A characters conscience takes the separate forms of an angel and a devil.
  • Grumpy Old Man: A senior man with temperamental issues.
  • Half-Witted Hillbilly: A person from the country is amusingly backwards or dumb.
  • Ham and Deadpan Duo: A Large Ham is paired with a Deadpan Snarker.
  • Harmless Villain: A villain who poses no actual danger.
  • Heroic Comedic Sociopath: An otherwise amoral character fighting for a good cause, whose more sinister deeds are usually played for laughs.
  • Historical Hilarity: Dead famous people are funny.
  • Hollywood Dateless: Lots of Love Interests, complains about being dateless.
  • Hyde and Seek
  • Idiot Hero: The Hero is a complete moron.
  • Immune to Drugs: Someone has a rejective reaction to drugs.
  • I'm Mr. [Future Pop Culture Reference]: Time traveler uses the name of a present-day fictional character or popular figure as an alias.
  • Ineffectual Sympathetic Villain: A villain who fails so often you feel sorry for them.
  • Inspector Oblivious
  • Insufferable Imbecile: Someone who's both mean and stupid.
  • Iron Butt Monkey: A character illogically maintains the ability to withstand all sorts of extreme physical abuse.
  • Jive Turkey: A character speaks in supposedly funny slang.
  • Kavorka Man: A homely womanizer who's quite successful at getting laid, despite lacking The Casanova's good looks.
  • Keet: A hyperactive male character.
  • Kent Brockman News: A Show Within a Show parodies news reports.
  • Kiddie Kid: A child character behaves younger than their stated age.
  • Kung-Fu Jesus: Jesus is reimagined as a badass.
  • Large Ham: A character who's utterly rambunctious and prone to exaggerating everything they say or do.
  • Laughably Evil: The villain's antics are hilarious to watch.
  • Lethal Chef: Someone who's terrible at cooking. Eat whatever "food" they serve at your own risk.
  • Literal-Minded: Someone literally interprets figurative or metaphorical speech.
  • Little Girls Kick Shins: A little girl who kicks bigger people in their chins.
  • Little Jimmy: A young character whose only distinguishable trait is their lack of understanding of the subject at hand.
  • Lovable Coward: A cowardly character whom one still wants to root for.
  • Mall Santa: A man dressed up in a Santa Claus suit at a shopping mall.
  • Manchild: An immature adult who behaves like a kid.
  • Man in a Bikini: A man wears a bikini for comedic effect.
  • Mischief-Making Monkey: Monkeys like to play pranks and mess with others.
  • Mistaken for Pedophile: Hilarity Ensues when a character is mistaken for a child molester.
  • Mr. Seahorse: A man gets pregnant.
  • Moose Are Idiots: These goofy-looking deer are often portrayed as rather unintelligent.
  • Naked People Are Funny: Nudity is played for laughs.
  • Naked People Trapped Outside: Nude people are embarrassingly stuck outside.
  • Nerd Nanny: Non-nerd girl who puts up with nerd guys.
  • The Nicknamer: Character comes up with nicknames for characters they frequently encounter but never directly interact with.
  • Nuns Are Funny: Nuns are portrayed according to Rule of Funny.
  • Odd Couple: A couple whose traits are incompatible.
  • Only Sane Man: The only rational person in a setting full of crazy and/or stupid people.
  • Overreacting Airport Security: Airport security resort to excessive methods to screen their passengers.
  • Packed Hero: A hero mistakenly gets trapped inside a package.
  • Panicky Expectant Father: A man panics while his wife is in labor with their child.
  • Pet Heir: Deceased relative leaves fortune to their pet.
  • Plucky Comic Relief: A character whose main purpose is to be funny, particularly in stories which are not (completely) comedic.
  • The Prankster: Someone who plays practical jokes on others for their own amusement.
  • Pretty Fly for a White Guy: A white character who's fond of black culture, often taken to stereotypically cringe-worthy extremes.
  • Psychopathic Manchild: An evil manchild whose extreme immaturity can make them humorous.
  • Queer People Are Funny: Stereotypical gay guys who are portrayed as comic relief.
  • Replacement Flat Character: A Flat Character becomes well-rounded so another Flat Character is added to the cast.
  • Rubber Face: Stretching another character's face.
  • Sarcasm-Blind: A character who is oblivious to sarcastic remarks.
  • Sassy Black Woman: A black lady who is very snarky.
  • Sassy Secretary: A secretary who snarks at her coworkers.
  • Spanner in the Works: An idiotic character accidentally ruins a plan.
  • Stoners Are Funny: Cannabis smokers (and other psychoactive drug users) are eccentric weirdos.
  • Straight Man: The mature character who is paired up with a not-so-mature character.
  • Straight Man and Wise Guy: A duo consisting of a Straight Man and Plucky Comic Relief.
  • Stupid Crooks: Idiot felons who can't even commit crimes properly.
  • Sue Donym: A fake alias that's very similar to its real name.
  • Super Zeroes: Incredibly lame superheroes.
  • Too Dumb to Live: Someone gets themselves in deep trouble due to their extreme obliviousness to danger.
  • Troublemaking New Pet: New pet is up to no good, blames their actions on the older.
  • Ugly Guy, Hot Wife: A married couple consisting of an ugly husband and attractive wife.
  • Uncle Tomfoolery: Stereotypical black guys who are portrayed as comic relief.
  • Unsatisfiable Customer: A picky customer.
  • Unsympathetic Comedy Protagonist: A comedy protagonist who's a complete jerk, but is rooted for anyways because of their amusing personality.
  • White Dude, Black Dude: Jokes about the differences between white and black people.
  • Yes-Man: The obedience to shenanigans or villainous plots.
