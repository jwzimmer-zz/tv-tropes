Elders

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Quotes
  • Create New

"Old men and old women, each with staff in hand because of old age, shall again sit in the streets of Jerusalem."
— Zechariah 8:4, The Bible
Advertisement:

Entries about older people and about aging. See Youngsters for the opposite. See also Mentors.

Tropes:

  • Absurdly Elderly Mother: Mothers are too old to have children.
  • Age Without Youth: An immortal character who looks elderly.
  • Alter Kocker: A senior with a Yiddish accent.
  • Ancient Evil: A character, concept or thing that's old and evil.
  • Ancient Keeper: An elderly, slightly creepy Mr. Exposition that is some sort of keeper.
  • Bleak Abyss Retirement Home: Retirement homes are seen as gloomy.
  • Character Aged with the Actor: When the actor grows old, so does the character.
  • *Crack!* "Oh, My Back!": An old or middle-aged character's joints crack.
  • Cool Old Guy: An old man who's seen as cool.
  • Cool Old Lady: An old woman who's seen as cool.
  • Curse of the Ancients: Elderly people use old exclamations instead of swear words or newer exclamations.
    • Advertisement:
  • December–December Romance: Two old people start dating.
  • Dented Iron: Somebody is Made of Iron, mostly, but sustains a few injuries.
  • Dirty Old Man: An old man who's sexually attracted to younger women.
  • Dirty Old Woman: A perverted elderly woman.
  • Doting Grandparent: Elder who has a close relationship with their grandchildren.
  • Eccentric Mentor: A character (usually an old man) who's wise and a bit of a Cloudcuckoolander.
  • Elder Abuse: Somebody abuses an old person.
  • Elder Employee: Someone older, usually an elderly person, takes a job usually reserved for a younger person.
  • Elderly Blue-Haired Lady: An old woman tries to dye her hair but her hair turns the wrong colour.
  • Elderly Immortal: An immortal or long-lived character who appears elderly.
  • Evil Old Folks: An elderly villain, who looks their apparent age.
    • Advertisement:
  • False Teeth Tomfoolery: Old people goofing about with dentures.
  • Father Neptune: A feisty old (usually male) sailor.
  • Feeling Their Age: An elderly action hero is starting to weaken or thinks they are.
  • First Gray Hair: Someone angsts about getting old when they see their first grey hair.
  • The Fog of Ages: An immortal character becomes forgetful because their brain is running out of memory space.
  • Former Teen Rebel: An older character who is stodgy or polite now was rebellious and/or impolite as a teen.
  • Fun-Hating Confiscating Adult: An adult (usually elderly) who children hate because (s)he doesn't want them to play and wants to take toys.
  • Games of the Elderly: Stereotypical old-people board games like bingo and gin rummy.
  • Grandparent Favoritism: When a grandparent adores their grandchild or grandchildren more than their children.
  • Granny Classic: A stereotypical old woman who usually has grandchildren.
  • Gruesome Grandparent: An abusive and/or antagonistic grandparent.
  • Grow Old with Me: An old couple who have been together since they were young.
  • Grumpy Old Man: An old person with a negative attitude.
  • Handbag of Hurt: Somebody (usually an elder) uses a bag as a weapon.
  • Helping Granny Cross the Street: Somebody helps an old lady across the street when they want to be helpful/appear noble.
  • Hippie Parents: A (sometimes elderly) hippie couple with at least one child.
  • Inflation Negation: An old person complains about how things were cheaper in their youth.
  • I Was Quite a Looker: A senior who was seen as attractive in their youth.
  • Late Coming Out: A character comes out of the closet later in life.
  • Likes Older Men: Somebody wants to date men who are at least a few years older than them.
  • Likes Older Women: Somebody wants to date an older woman.
  • Little Old Lady Investigates: An old, female detective.
  • Long-Lived: Something or someone that lives for a very long time.
  • Maiden Aunt: An unmarried, elderly, female relative.
  • Methuselah Syndrome: Fantasy characters who live a long time despite medical science not having progressed.
  • Miniature Senior Citizens: Short seniors.
  • Nazi Grandpa: An elderly man who used to be a Nazi.
  • Needlework Is for Old People: Knitting, sewing, spinning, embroidery, etc. is associated with seniors.
  • Never Mess with Granny: An old woman is good at fighting.
  • Nostalgia Filter: Somebody believes that things from their childhood are better than similar things from now.
  • The Old Convict: An elderly character in prison.
  • Old People Are Nonsexual: The belief that elders don't participate in sexual acts, and are gross or funny if they do.
  • Older and Wiser: A character comes back in a sequel, having grown old and gotten wiser and they help the younger characters.
  • Older Hero vs. Younger Villain: The hero is old or borderline old, while the villain is relatively young.
  • Older Is Better: Older things are always superior to newer things.
  • Old Master: An old person who is very good at action-y things, most often due to decades of experience.
  • Older Sidekick: A sidekick older than the main character.
  • Old Soldier: An elderly veteran.
  • Old Retainer: A servant, usually elderly, who's very loyal to the person or people they work for and have worked for for a long time.
  • Old Superhero: An old (usually retired) superhero.
  • Old Windbag: An old character who goes on an on about themselves, which annoys other characters.
  • Older Than They Look: Somebody looks younger than they really are. This can include Really 700 Years Old or Time Abyss.
  • The Patriarch: A tough old man who sees himself as an authority in his family.
  • Perilous Old Fool: Subtrope of Old Soldier. An elderly soldier who thinks they can be active but they're way too weak.
  • Racist Grandma: A racist old woman who's put in as a joke.
  • Rapid Aging: Growing old way faster than normal.
  • Rambling Old Man Monologue: An old person who likes to tell long, rambling speeches.
  • Really 700 Years Old: S/he looks like a teenager or even a child, but is actually a Time Abyss.
  • Reluctant Retiree: An elderly character who is perfectly capable but gets forced to retire against their will anyway.
  • Retired Badass: An old person who used to be an action type.
  • The Scrooge: An old, greedy, rich character.
  • Scatterbrained Senior: An absent-minded senior.
  • Scratchy-Voiced Senior: An old person who talks as though they have a neverending sore throat.
  • Screw Politeness, I'm a Senior!: A rude elder who thinks they are allowed to be rude because they're old.
  • Senior Creep: A scary old person.
  • Senior Sleep Cycle: An elderly Sleepyhead.
  • Shell-Shocked Veteran: An old man with PTSD or something similar.
  • Silver Fox: An old person is presented as sexy.
  • Stern Nun: A stern, usually elderly, nun.
  • Stronger with Age: Somebody gets stronger as they get older.
  • Technologically Blind Elders: An old person who has no idea how to use a piece of technology.
  • Vague Age: A character's age is left ambiguous.
  • The Vietnam Vet: When the Shell-Shocked Veteran is from the Vietnam war.
  • Wasteland Elder: An elderly leader (usually male) of a small, rundown community.
  • When Elders Attack: An elder attacks someone or something with an object.
  • When I Was Your Age...: An elderly character complains that something was a different way from now when they were the age of the younger characters, and they preferred it that way.
  • While You Were in Diapers: An older character boasts that they did something cool while a younger character was too young to do anything that cool.
  • White-Dwarf Starlet: A female ex-celebrity senior who thinks she's still famous.
  • Who Wants to Live Forever?: Immortality is a bad thing.
  • Wise Old Folk Façade
  • Wise Old Turtle: Turtles depicted as very old and very long-lived.
  • Wizard Classic: A stereotypical wizard, often visibly old.
  • World of No Grandparents: None of the characters have grandparents.
