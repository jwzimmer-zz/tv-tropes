Terrorism Tropes

Let's hope he gets it right this time, 'kay?
Advertisement:

Terrorism is defined by The Other Wiki as "the use of violence, or threatened use of violence, in order to achieve a political, religious, or ideological aim". Some things associated with terrorism include murders, bombings, shootings, hostage-taking, and other threats. There are numerous real-world terrorist groups that make an appearance in fiction and countless fictional ones. They are usually portrayed as villainous.

While terrorism has appeared in fiction for years, the 9/11 attacks made terrorists, specifically the Islamic Fundamentalist type, as the main villains in Hollywood movies, replacing the Dirty Communists of the Cold War.

Compare Rebel Tropes, The Only Righteous Index of Fanatics!, and Organized Crime Tropes for another kind of terrorists.

Advertisement:

Tropes:

Useful Notes:
  • The War on Terror
  • African Terrorists: Ruthless militants from a country in Sub-Saharan Africa.
  • Apocalypse Cult: A religious cult which attempts to help jumpstart doomsday through terror attacks on the public.
  • Assassination Attempt: Terrorists will often try to kill an important figure whom they believe to be an enemy to their cause.
  • Bomb-Throwing Anarchists: Militant anarchists who want to overthrow all forms of centralized government and state authority.
  • Church Militant: Religious extremists who wish to enforce their super-strict theocratic laws upon society.
  • Demolitions Expert: Someone who is skilled at producing improvised explosive devices (IEDs).
  • Dirty Bomb: An IED that is designed to scatter radioactive materials around the blast radius.
  • Dirty Communists: Militant socialists who want to overthrow all forms of capitalism and private businesses.
  • Eco-Terrorist: Terrorists whose motivation is to punish people they blame for destroying the natural environment.
      Advertisement:
    • Animal Wrongs Group: Eco-terrorists who are specifically targeting people for causing harm to non-human animals.
  • External Combustion: AKA car bombing, a classic terrorist tactic in which a motor vehicle loaded with explosives is detonated to kill people or destroy a targeted location.
  • Fantastic Terrorists: Extremist militants whose political ideology can only exist or make any sense when they live in some sort of sci-fi/fantasy world.
  • Far East Asian Terrorists: Ruthless militants from a country in East (or Southeast) Asia.
  • Hostage Video: Terrorists record a video featuring someone they've kidnapped, threatening to kill them while making demands for political goals or ransom money; or they just simply murder the hostage execution-style to instill fear in their enemies.
  • Irish Explosives Expert: A (Northern) Irish terrorist who learned how to make and use bombs during The Troubles.
  • The Klan: The Ku Klux Klan, an infamous white-supremacist terrorist organization which originated from the United States.
  • Mad Bomber: A terrorist who plants a lot of explosives and blows up anything they can.
  • Middle Eastern Terrorists: Ruthless militants from a country in North Africa or West Asia.
  • Mistaken for Terrorist: An innocent person is maliciously accused of or misidentified as being some sort of dangerous extremist (or at least a sympathizer).
  • Nebulous Evil Organisation: A terrorist organization that remains mysterious and anonymous to the public.
  • Post-9/11 Terrorism Movie: Thriller films about America's War on Terror.
  • Private Military Contractors: While true mercenaries are motivated primarily by money rather than ideology, a private army of armed volunteers can be recruited by the villains to help them carry out acts of terrorism.
  • Renegade Splinter Faction: A terrorist group which split off from another older organization that wasn't radical enough for them.
  • The Revolution Will Not Be Civilized: Rebels and revolutionaries engage in a reign of terror against anyone who is (perceived to be) a counter-revolutionary or loyalist of the old regime.
  • Right-Wing Militia Fanatic: A far-right-wing militant, usually some sort of homegrown extremist from the United States, who is a member of a private paramilitary group.
  • South Asian Terrorists: Ruthless militants from a country in the Indian subcontinent.
  • Spree Killer: A terrorist may decide to commit a homicidal rampage at a crowded (public) place, trying to massacre as many people as possible with whatever weapons they have (blades, firearms, explosives, vehicles, etc).
  • Suicide Attack: Some terrorists are so devoted to their cause that they'll go far enough to get both themselves and others killed, usually through the use of explosives.
  • Terrorists Without a Cause: Criminals who commit violent acts which may resemble terrorism, or may have (nominally) ideological motives, but their end goals are mostly apolitical.
  • Those Wacky Nazis: (Neo-)Nazis and other militant fascists who wish to impose "Aryan" racial supremacy in their countries.
  • Well-Intentioned Extremist: A terrorist who commits their crimes out of a genuine (if horribly misguided) belief they are fighting for something good.
    • The Extremist Was Right: Not only were the terrorist's beliefs proven correct, but their methods may be justified too.
    • Not-So-Well-Intentioned Extremist: The terrorist claims that they have good intentions, but it's rather obvious that they're only committing their crimes for more self-serving motives.
  • Western Terrorists: Ruthless militants from a country in the Americas, Europe, or Oceania.
  • Your Terrorists Are Our Freedom Fighters: One person's evil extremist may be another's heroic rebel.
