Tiny Tropes

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Create New

"It's not the size, mate. It's how you use it."
— Nigel Powers referring to his spy-car* probably, Austin Powers in Goldmember
Advertisement:

Tropes about things that are small. Not incidentally small, like being young. These tropes require something to be physically small.

Compare Rule of Cute, Youngsters.

Contrast Bigger Is Better.

Tropes:

  • A-Cup Angst —a woman's breasts are small (or at least she thinks they are) and she feels negatively about it.
  • Adorable Evil Minions —the villain's minions are small and cute.
  • Animals Not to Scale — animals which are strangely big or small for their species.
  • Big Guy, Little Guy — two men who walk around together, one tall and one short.
  • Cartoonish Companions — the main characters have a pal who's short and a lot more cartoony-looking than them.
  • Cute Little Fangs — a character has cute, often small, fangs.
  • Dinky Drivers — very tiny people try to drive a regular-sized car.
  • "Fantastic Voyage" Plot — characters become tiny enough to go inside someone's body... then, they do.
    • Advertisement:
  • Fun Size — smaller = cuter.
  • Huge Guy, Tiny Girl — a tall man and a short woman who are lovers or best friends.
  • Huge Rider, Tiny Mount — a large character rides a small animal.
  • Incredible Shrinking Man — someone shrinks.
  • Killer Rabbit — a small, cute person or animal that is dangerous.
  • Knee-High Perspective — the work takes place from the perspective of someone who comes up to the average person's knees/ankles/waist.
  • Large Runt — a character who's small for their species but actually quite big.
  • Lilliputians — tiny humanoids (the tallest a Lilliputian can be is about knee-high).
  • Lilliputian Warriors — a species of tiny, aggressive humanoids.
  • Little People — a race of humanoids who are short but not quite Lilliputians.
  • Mascot Mook — a (usually small, but not always) Mook becomes popular among fans.
    • Advertisement:
  • Miniature Effects — film effects created with miniature props.
  • Miniature Senior Citizens — someone who's elderly and short.
  • Mini-Mecha — the tiniest mecha for a normal sized pilot.
  • Mobile-Suit Human — same for a tiny pilot
  • Model Planning — planning something to do with a place and using models of that place to demonstrate.
  • Mouse World — a story where the protagonists are very small.
  • Nanomachines — little tiny machines.
  • One-Paragraph Chapter — a chapter that is very short and/or has only one paragraph.
  • Only a Model — a model of something larger.
  • Petite Pride — the opposite of A-Cup Angst; a woman who is proud of having small breasts.
  • Pintsized Powerhouse — someone is short but very strong.
  • Scale Model Destruction — a 3D model gets destroyed.
  • Scavenged Punk (often but not always a Tiny Trope)— a culture gets/makes its technology from scavenged stuff.
  • Shameful Shrinking — someone literally shrinks when feeling guilty/ashamed/embarrassed.
  • Shoulder-Sized Dragon — a little wee dragon.
  • Shrink Ray — a gun that can shrink things.
  • Shrunken Head — a human head that's been shrunk.
  • Sizeshifter — someone can change their size.
  • Sleep-Mode Size — a character looks much smaller when they're asleep.
  • Small Girl, Big Gun — a little girl carrying a really large gun.
  • Synchronized Swarming — groups of fish/insects/etc can form shapes with one another.
  • Teeny Weenie — a small penis.
  • This Is What the Building Will Look Like — a character designs a building and makes a model of it but we never get to see the building until the very end and sometimes, not even then.
  • Tiny Guy, Huge Girl — a woman is much taller than a man she's dating.
  • Tiny-Headed Behemoth — a huge creature with a tiny head.
  • Tiny Schoolboy — a short teenage boy.
  • Token Mini-Moe — a character much smaller and/or younger than the main cast who's meant to be cute.
