Gender and Sexuality Tropes

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Create New

All about gender and sexual identity. For romance, see Love Tropes. For eroticism and sexual behavior, see Sex Tropes. For tropes that bend gender lines, see Gender-Blending Tropes.

Advertisement:
  • Adaptational Sexuality: An adaptation changes a character's sexual orientation.
  • Affirmative Action Girl: A female character is added to a team for the sake of diversity.
  • All Abusers Are Male: A work always depicts men as being guilty of domestic abuse.
  • Armoured Closet Gay: A man who is vocally homophobic turns out to be a closeted gay man.
  • Asexuality: A person does not feel any sexual attraction whatsoever.
  • Beer Goggles: Being drunk causes a person to see someone as more attractive than they actually are and/or flirt with them.
  • Best Woman: A bride and groom have friends of the opposite sex fill the role of best man and maid of honor.
  • Big Beautiful Man: A heavyset man who is considered attractive.
  • Big Beautiful Woman: A heavyset woman who is considered attractive.
  • "Billy Elliot" Plot: A boy's femininity causes a conflict.
    • Advertisement:
  • Bizarre Alien Reproduction: Aliens reproduce differently from Earth organisms.
  • Bizarre Alien Sexes: Aliens have different genders or more genders than humans do.
  • Body Sushi: Sushi being eaten while it's on a naked woman lying down.
  • Boyfriend Bluff: A man pretends to be a girl's boyfriend to protect her from predators.
  • The Bro Code: A set of unspoken rules between guys.
  • Bros Before Hoes: Putting your friend before your love-interest.
  • Bury Your Gays: Gay people get killed.
  • But Not Too Bi: Someone is bisexual but seems to favor a particular sex.
  • But Not Too Gay: Gay couples aren't seen doing romantic or sexual things as much as straight ones.
  • Butch Lesbian: A lesbian who looks and acts stereotypically tough and masculine.
  • Camp Gay: A gay man acts stereotypically feminine and artsy.
  • Cannot Talk to Women: A guy who has trouble talking to girls.
    • Advertisement:
  • The Casanova: A man who strings along many love interests, but is not in a serious relationship with any of them.
  • Cast Full of Pretty Boys: A cast full of pretty/handsome young men.
  • Category Traitor (The Gender Traitor variant)
  • The Chick: A character in a team with traits considered feminine.
  • Chickification: An action-oriented female becomes more girly and helpless.
  • Chromosome Casting: The characters are either all male or all female.
  • Coming-Out Story: A gay character comes out of the closet and reveals their sexuality to their friends, family, and associates.
  • Coming Straight Story: A straight character has to reveal to gay friends and people who assumed them to be gay that they are not gay.
  • Common Hollywood Sex Traits: Sex works in an unrealistic way in movies.
  • Crippling Castration: Mutilating or destroying a person's genitals.
  • Crotch-Grab Sex Check: Grabbing a person's crotch to find out their sex.
  • Cure Your Gays: People attempt to "cure" homosexuality under the belief that having a sexual orientation other than straight is an illness.
  • Date My Avatar: A person creates a fake version of themselves for the sake of dating or misleading people online.
  • Discount Lesbians: Nonhuman characters used to represent a homosexual relationship.
  • Dismissed Gender
  • Does Not Like Men: A woman doesn't like men.
  • Double Standard: Abuse, Female on Male: Female abusers and male victims aren't treated in the same level of seriousness as male abusers and female victims.
  • Dude Looks Like a Lady: A feminine-looking man is mistaken for a woman.
  • Effeminate Misogynistic Guy: A misogynistic man who ironically acts very feminine.
  • Entitled to Have You: Someone believes that they are more deserving of the person they're attracted to than the one the person truly loves.
  • Ephebophile: A person who is sexually interested in underage teenagers.
  • Eternal Sexual Freedom: Characters do something sexual that was not allowed in the time period they're set in, and it's not mentioned not to be allowed.
  • Even the Girls Want Her: A woman is so beautiful that even other women desire her.
  • Even the Guys Want Him: A man is so attractive that even other men desire him.
  • Everyone Is Bi: Gender does not factor into the characters' formation of relationships.
  • Everyone Is Gay: When everyone in a Fanfic is gay, even the originally straight characters.
  • Everybody Is Single: A majority of the cast is not married or in a relationship.
  • Everybody Wants the Hermaphrodite: An intersex character has the most admirers.
  • Eunuchs Are Evil: People who have had their sex organs removed are depicted as villains.
  • Experimented in College: Someone who currently identifies as straight pursued same-sex relationships when they were in school.
  • Extra Y, Extra Violent: A Y chromosome is supposedly linked to aggression, so an extra one leads to extra aggression (naturally, women are exempt).
  • Extreme Omnisexual: A character will have sex with anyone and anything, no limits.
  • Fag Hag: A woman who hangs around with gay men.
  • Fairy Sexy: Attractive fairies.
  • Fake Period Excuse: Someone fakes being on her period to get out of a situation surreptitiously.
  • Faux Yay: An extended ruse where characters pretend to be gay.
  • Faux Action Girl: A supposedly a female badass, but it's only an Informed Attribute.
  • Favors for the Sexy: A beautiful character (usually female) compels those around them to pamper them or give them things.
  • Female Gaze: Scenes where the camera focuses on the male anatomy to entice female viewers.
  • Fembot: A female-looking robot.
  • Femininity Failure: A Tomboy tries to be feminine but can't pull it off.
  • Feminist Fantasy: A story which is about a woman, or women in general, who are major characters making things happen of their own agency and volition and saving the day under their own power (likely literal "superpower").
  • Females Are More Innocent: Females are portrayed as being more innocent than males.
  • Final Girl: A female character who survives the story while everyone else dies.
  • Flawless Token: The female or gay character can do no wrong.
  • Foe Romance Subtext: A pair of rivals/enemies are intentionally given romantic subtext in their interactions.
  • Forced Out of the Closet: A closeted gay person is outed against their will.
  • Game-Favored Gender: In a game where you can pick your character's gender, one gender is considered more useful than the other.
  • Gay Best Friend: A character has a friend whose only notable quality is that they are gay.
  • Gay Option: A video game makes it possible for same-sex relations to occur.
  • Gay Panic: A character's real or perceived homosexuality is toned down to appease Moral Guardians.
  • Gayborhood: A neighborhood inhabited by gay people.
  • Gayngster: A gay gangster.
  • Gender-Equal Ensemble: The cast has an equal number of male characters and female characters.
  • Gender Equals Breed: When two animals have children, the male offspring will resemble their father and the female offspring will resemble their mother.
  • Gender Incompetence: One gender is viewed as more competent than the other.
  • Gender-Inverted Trope: Trope specific to one gender is used with the other in mind.
  • Gender Is No Object: Many jobs in fiction are equal-opportunity, even if they weren't in real life.
  • Gender Rarity Value: A person who is valuable because they are the only surviving member of their gender.
  • Gender-Restricted Ability: Only men (or only women) can use the Applied Phlebotinum or a particularly special technique.
  • Gender-Restricted Gear: Certain items can only be used/equipped by characters of a specific gender.
  • Gender Reveal: A character's true gender is revealed.
  • Gender Scoff: Expressing frustration in dealing with the opposite sex.
  • Gendered Insult: An insult having to do with a person's gender.
  • Gendercide: An entire gender is exterminated.
  • Get Back in the Closet: When a work jumps up the rough framework for what's "inappropriate" because it contains gay content.
  • Girls Are Really Scared of Horror Movies: Females are stereotyped as being unable to watch horror films without being scared out of their minds.
  • Girls Have Cooties: Boys refuse to hang around girls on account of them possessing a "virus".
  • Girls Love Chocolate: All women love chocolate, especially if they're a Girly Girl.
  • Girls vs. Boys Plot: A stock plot, where boys and girls fight in a battle of the genders.
  • Girliness Upgrade: A tomboy becomes more feminine as time goes on.
  • Girl Powered: A MacGuffin that only works for girls.
  • Guys Are Slobs: Men are stereotyped as being messy and unhygienic.
  • Harmless Lady Disguise: A man disguises himself as a lady to avoid suspicion.
  • Has Two Mommies: Someone has same-sex parents.
  • Hates Wearing Dresses: A woman doesn't like wearing dresses.
  • Have I Mentioned I Am Gay?: A work repeatedly says a character is gay, but we never see them engage in homosexual activities.
  • Have I Mentioned I Am Heterosexual Today?: A character who displays non-conforming or homoerotic behavior feels compelled to remind everyone that they're straight.
  • He-Man Woman Hater: A man who is an over-the-top misogynist.
  • Hereditary Homosexuality: Homo- or bisexuality runs in families.
  • Heteronormative Crusader: A homophobic activist who fights against what they view as sexual deviancy.
  • Hide Your Lesbians: Homosexual relationships/people hidden under a veil of subtext.
  • High-Heel–Face Turn: The sole female villain ends up reforming.
  • Ho Yay: Two characters of the same gender interact in ways that fans can interpret as indicating that the characters are a couple.
  • Homosexual Reproduction: Two people of the same gender manage to produce a child.
  • Homosocial Heterosexuality: Women are passive things for men to fight, barter, or haggle over.
  • If It's You, It's Okay: A character falls for someone of a gender they're not normally attracted to.
  • I Have Boobs, You Must Obey!: A woman tries to use her breasts to persuade men.
  • I Have Brothers: A female character who learned male oriented skills from her brothers.
  • I'm a Man; I Can't Help It: Men have limited self-control when it comes to sexual arousal.
  • Improbably Female Cast: For some reason, there are next to no men in this setting. Women do everything, plain and simple.
  • Incest Subtext: Related characters have interactions that make them look like a couple.
  • Incompatible Orientation: Someone is attracted to a person who doesn't swing the same way they do.
  • The Lad-ette: A girl with stereotypical boorish masculine traits.
  • Lady Land: A female dominated society where men are either subservient or absent.
  • Lady Looks Like a Dude: A masculine-looking woman is mistaken for a man.
  • A Lady on Each Arm: A man is shown with each arm around a woman.
  • Last Het Romance: A character realizes that they're gay after a failed heterosexual relationship.
  • Late Coming Out: A character comes out as queer later in life.
  • Leg Cling: A Stock Pose in which a powerful man stands in a dominant fashion while a woman (usually under-dressed) clings to his leg in submissive, helpless fashion.
  • Lesbian Jock: A lesbian who plays sports.
  • Lesbian Vampire: A female vampire who preys on young women.
  • Less Embarrassing Term: A character finds the word for something embarrassing, so they insist on calling it something else.
  • LGBT Fanbase: A work of fiction that has gay fans.
  • Lipstick Lesbian: A lesbian who acts feminine.
  • Madonna–Whore Complex: Women are either respectable and prude virgins or disgusting whores! There is no such thing as a woman who is in-between!
  • Magical Queer: A (usually camp) gay man who is Closer to Earth.
  • Male Band, Female Singer: A band with one female member who's the singer.
  • Male Gaze: Scenes where the camera is focused on a woman's breasts and/or butt to entice male viewers.
  • Male-to-Female Universal Adaptor: Aliens with human genitals.
  • A Man Is Always Eager: Men always want sex.
  • Mars-and-Venus Gender Contrast: Women are fundamental very different from men, almost to the point of being a whole other species.
  • Masculine–Feminine Gay Couple: A gay couple where one acts masculine and the other acts feminine.
  • Masculine Girl, Feminine Boy: A pairing consisting of a girl who acts masculine and a boy who acts feminine.
  • Masculine Lines, Feminine Curves: Curves are visibly softer and more delicate than lines, which makes them perfect for drawing women while straight lines are perfect for drawing men.
  • Matriarchy: A state or society where women are in positions of power and men have little or no role in its affairs.
  • Men Act, Women Are: Male characters get everything done, or do the hard work. The female characters are simply there to look good or to invoke audience sympathy.
  • Men Are Better Than Women: Men are more all-around capable, reliable, or flat out superior, than women.
  • Men Are Generic, Women Are Special: A male character is considered "normal" by default. A female character, by virtue of simply being female, is treated as special under the circumstances or is characterized with stereotypically womanly traits.
  • Men Are Strong, Women Are Pretty: The primary virtue of a man is to be strong, capable, tough and daring. The primary virtue for a woman is to look pretty.
  • Men Are the Expendable Gender: Male death and suffering is regarded as less important than female death and suffering.
  • Men Are Tough: The man is portrayed as a tough being.
  • Men Are Uncultured: Men love sports and hate opera, classical music, and visual arts.
  • Men Get Old, Women Get Replaced: After the characters have aged up, the male characters seem to be still around and active in the story, while women have moved on, settled down, or have simply died/disappeared.
  • Menstrual Menace: Weirdness ensues as a girl gets her period.
  • Mentor in Queerness: A, usually older, out character who asks as support for a newly-out or questioning character.
  • Mistaken for Gay: Someone is incorrectly assumed to be gay.
  • Mistaken for Misogynist: A man accidentally says or does something that could be interpreted as sexist.
  • Monogender Monsters: A male (or female) hero will fight monsters of the same gender.
  • More Deadly Than the Male: The female is more dangerous than the male.
  • Most Fanfic Writers Are Girls: If a work brings up fanfiction, it'll most likely fall into the romance genre, and the author(s) will be a girl.
  • Most Gamers Are Male: Men are the usual demographic when one thinks of "gamers", owing partly to their competitive nature, physical demands, and violent and/or sexual content.
  • Most Writers Are Male: Most published writers are male, so male protagonists, all-male teams, and misconceptions regarding women are more prevalent.
  • Mother Nature, Father Science: Nature is caring and feminine, Science is industrious and masculine.
  • Motherhood Is Superior: Women are better parents than men.
  • Moustache de Plume: A pen name used to conceal gender.
  • My Girl Is Not a Slut: Sexually promiscuous women are seen as dirty, but sexually promiscuous men are seen as cool.
  • Nature Adores a Virgin: Virgins are highly desirable.
  • Never a Self-Made Woman: Female characters in positions of power got there through their connections to more important men.
  • Nipple and Dimed: Female nipples get censored.
  • No Biological Sex: A being that does not have a gender of any sort.
  • No Bisexuals: There are no bisexuals, only straight people and gay/lesbian people.
  • No Heterosexual Sex Allowed: To solve an overpopulation problem, a government forbids heterosexual sex.
  • No Periods, Period: A female character is incapable of going through her menstrual cycle.
  • No Woman's Land: The world outside of the safe place the woman lives is deeply misogynistic and dangerous.
  • Non-Heteronormative Society: A culture or society with decidedly non-Western views of gender and sexuality.
  • Non-Human Non-Binary: A non-binary character is also a non-human character.
  • Not That There's Anything Wrong with That: A backpedaling move to avoid being viewed as homophobic.
  • Old Friend, New Gender: An old friend that you haven't seen in a while has had a sex change.
  • One-Gender Race: A race consisting of only one gender.
  • One-Gender School: A sex-segregated educational institute.
  • The One Guy: The one male member of a group otherwise composed of females.
  • Only Has Same-Sex Admirers: A character attracts people of the same sex but never anyone of the opposite sex.
  • Outfit-Rip Sex Check: A character is made to have their sex revealed by having their clothing ripped off.
  • Out of the Closet, Into the Fire: After a character comes out they are quickly killed, harmed, or cosmically punished.
  • Pale Females, Dark Males: Female non-human characters have lighter-colored bodies than male ones.
  • Paralyzing Fear of Sexuality: A character desires romance and/or sex, but is too afraid of sexuality.
  • Pass Fail: When trying to live life as another demographic proves unsuccessful.
  • Pink Girl, Blue Boy: Males are associated with the color blue, while females are associated with pink.
  • Platonic Boy/Girl Heroes: A duo consisting of a young boy and girl who are required to work together.
  • Pragmatic Pansexuality: Often going against a character's sexuality to sleep with others in an effort to achieve their goals.
  • Preserve Your Gays: A gay character is almost killed, but survives in the end.
  • Pride Parade: An annual parade (and subsequent festival) celebrating gay life and culture.
  • Promiscuity After Rape: A character who became sexually promiscuous as the result of being sexually abused in the past.
  • Property of Love: When being in love makes you essentially your lover's property.
  • Psycho Lesbian: A mentally unhinged lesbian, mostly motivated by unrequited love for a straight female character.
  • Purely Aesthetic Gender: When a video game gives the option to play as a female character, the only significant difference from playing as a male character is the player character's physical appearance.
  • Queer Character, Queer Actor: A gay character is played by a gay actor.
  • Queer People Are Funny: Homosexuality is funny.
  • Questionable Consent: It's unclear whether or not someone gave consent to have sex with another.
  • Quickly-Demoted Woman: Getting our strong, successful woman out of the way so we can make way for the male lead by making them trade roles.
  • Raging Stiffie: A man gets a large, obvious erection.
  • Rainbow Lens: A character has a queerness-unrelated trait that can be interpreted as a metaphor for queerness.
  • Raised as the Opposite Gender: A character is raised as if they were the opposite gender.
  • Raised by Dudes: A girl grew up surrounded by masculine role models (father, uncles, older brothers, etc) and becomes like them for lack of a feminine role model.
  • Rape and Switch: The idea that getting raped can change a person's sexual orientation.
  • A Real Man Is a Killer: A man discovers that he must kill something in order to prove his worth.
  • Real Men Eat Meat: Men who don't eat meat are looked down upon.
  • Real Men Hate Affection: Men who don't like hugs or other forms of affection.
  • Real Men Hate Sugar: It is considered manly to abstain from sweets.
  • Real Women Don't Wear Dresses: Traditionally feminine women are depicted as weak, worthless, and incompetent.
  • Real Women Have Curves: Relatable female characters have Weight Woe.
  • Secondary Sexual Characteristics: Elements of a creature's physiology that result from it being male or female.
  • Sensual Slavs: Slavic woman using her sex appeal to seduce, manipulate, distract or beguile a non-Slavic character.
  • Settled for Gay: A woman settles for marrying a gay man.
  • Sexbot: A robot built for the purpose of humans having sex with it.
  • Sexist Used Car Salesman: A man sells a used car to a woman they think doesn't know anything about cars.
  • Sexy Scandinavian: A hot, exoticized Scandinavian.
  • Sexy Man, Instant Harem: A man who is The Casanova will often attract women.
  • Showing Up Chauvinists: a woman surprises others by performing well in a traditionally male-dominated field.
  • Single-Target Sexuality: Someone is only attracted to a specific person.
  • Sissy Villain: A villain who acts effeminate.
  • Situational Sexuality: Someone normally heterosexual will become homosexual if there aren't any members of the opposite gender around to placate their lust.
  • Sliding Scale of Gender Inequality: A scale that examines how the relationships between men and women are portrayed in fiction.
  • Slut-Shaming: Attacking a person for being promiscuous.
  • The Smurfette Principle: The one female member of a group otherwise consisting of males.
  • Some of My Best Friends Are X: Claiming to have friends from a certain group, and then make offensive comments about their group when they're not around.
  • Sour Prudes: Someone is prudish and looks down on people who like sex.
  • Speculative Fiction LGBT: LGBT people feature a lot in sci-fi and fantasy.
  • Starring Smurfette: The main character is a different gender from the rest of the cast.
  • Stay in the Kitchen: When a character or the narrative insists that women should be barred from action and stay on the sidelines.
  • Stereotype Reaction Gag: Someone is offended by a stereotypical assumption, only to then immediately demonstrate it.
  • Stop Being Stereotypical: Wishing that other members of your group would stop acting like walking clichés.
  • Sweeps Week Lesbian Kiss: Promoting a same-sex kiss as a Ratings Stunt, regardless of context.
  • Sympathetic Adulterer: Someone cheats on their spouse and is treated as if being unfaithful was the right thing to do.
  • Tampon Run: A man has to buy tampons for a menstruating woman.
  • Territorial Smurfette: The sole female character will get antagonistic toward any new female who tries to join the group.
  • Tertiary Sexual Characteristics: Females have eyelashes, lipstick and/or bows to distinguish them from males.
  • There Are No Girls on the Internet: The idea that all women on the Internet are actually men pretending to be women.
  • Threesome Subtext: Subtext about the possibility of a shared romantic and/or sexual relationship between three characters.
  • Token Lesbian: The only member of a group who is a lesbian.
  • Tomboyness Upgrade: A Girly Girl becomes more tomboyish as time goes on.
  • Totally 18: Pointing out or claiming that someone is 18 years old and not a minor.
  • Transparent Closet: Someone denies being gay, but does a very poor job of hiding their sexuality.
  • ¡Three Amigos!: Two boys and a girl are friends.
  • Two Girls to a Team: A team otherwise consisting of males has two female members.
  • Ugly Slavic Women: Eastern European women are hideous and masculine-looking.
  • Unexpected Virgin: Someone who's revealed as a virgin and it comes as a surprise.
  • The Unfair Sex: Female characters get away with shenanigans that male characters could not or wouldn't even dare to try.
  • Urban Legend Love Life: A character is rumored to have had numerous romantic partners.
  • Used to Be a Tomboy: A Girly Girl who used to be a tomboy in the past.
  • Vanity Is Feminine: Femininity means being shallow, envious, and obsessed with beauty.
  • Virgin-Shaming: Attacking a person just because they never had sexual intercourse.
  • Wanted a Gender-Conforming Child: Parents take issue with their son or daughter not conforming to traditional gender stereotypes.
  • Wanted a Son Instead: A parent is upset that they have a daughter instead of a son.
  • Wet Blanket Wife: The wife who nags her husband to not endanger himself too much and to think about his family.
  • Where Everybody Knows Your Flame: A gay bar/club will always be very gay.
  • "Which Restroom" Dilemma: Someone has trouble deciding which restroom they should use.
  • White Male Lead: In Western Media, more often than not the protagonist is a White guy.
  • Womanliness as Pathos: Women inherently cause drama or intense feelings.
  • Women Are Delicate: The belief that women by nature are very vulnerable.
  • Women Are Wiser: Women are depicted as more intelligent and rational than men.
  • Women's Mysteries: How to handle menstruation, and maybe also how to do magic.
  • Wondrous Ladies Room: In fiction, the ladies' room takes on a kind of mystique.
  • Wrong Genetic Sex: A character's genetic sex doesn't match their actual gender.
  • Xenafication: A girly female is made more action-oriented and capable of defending herself.
  • You Go, Girl!: A girl proves that she's as good as the boys in athletics.
  • Your Normal Is Our Taboo: What's regarded as normal in one society is verboten in another and vice versa.
