TIME — All-Time 100 Albums

A list released in 2006 by Time featuring the 100 greatest and most influential music albums since 1954. See also TIME — All-Time 100 Movies.

  • In the Wee Small Hours (1955) by Frank Sinatra.
  • Songs for Swingin' Lovers! (1956) by Frank Sinatra.
  • Here's Little Richard (1957) by Little Richard.
  • Kind of Blue (1959) by Miles Davis.
  • King Of The Delta Blues Singers (1961, though the songs were recorded in the 1930s) by Robert Johnson.
    • Advertisement:
  • Modern Sounds in Country and Western Music (1962) by Ray Charles.
  • Live at the Apollo (1963) by James Brown.
  • A Love Supreme (1964) by John Coltrane.
  • Highway 61 Revisited (1965) by Bob Dylan.
  • Otis Blue (1965) by Otis Redding.
  • Rubber Soul (1965) by The Beatles.
  • Pet Sounds (1966) by The Beach Boys.
  • Blonde on Blonde (1966) by Bob Dylan.
  • Revolver (1966) by The Beatles.
  • The Velvet Underground & Nico (1967) by the Velvet Underground and Nico.
  • I Never Loved A Man The Way I Loved You (1967) by Aretha Franklin.
  • Are You Experienced (1967) by The Jimi Hendrix Experience.
  • Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967) by The Beatles.
  • Lady Soul (1968) by Aretha Franklin.
  • At Folsom Prison (1968) by Johnny Cash.
  • The White Album (1968) by The Beatles.
  • Astral Weeks (1968) by Van Morrison.
  • Stand! (1969) by Sly and the Family Stone.
    • Advertisement:
  • The Band (1969) by The Band.
  • Abbey Road (1969) by The Beatles.
  • Bridge Over Troubled Water (1970) by Simon & Garfunkel.
  • Moondance (1970) by Van Morrison.
  • Bitches Brew (1970) by Miles Davis.
  • Paranoid (1970) by Black Sabbath.
  • After the Gold Rush (1970) by Neil Young
  • John Lennon/Plastic Ono Band (1970) by John Lennon and the Plastic Ono Band.
  • Tapestry (1971) by Carole King.
  • Sticky Fingers (1971) by The Rolling Stones.
  • What's Going On (1971) by Marvin Gaye.
  • Blue (1971) by Joni Mitchell.
  • Who's Next (1971) by The Who.
  • Coat of Many Colors (1971) by Dolly Parton.
  • Led Zeppelin IV (1971) by Led Zeppelin.
  • Hunky Dory (1971) by David Bowie.
  • Exile on Main St. (1972) by The Rolling Stones.
  • The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars (1972) by David Bowie.
  • The Harder They Come (1972) (a soundtrack album full of various reggae artists)
    • Advertisement:
  • Talking Book (1972) by Stevie Wonder.
  • Call Me (1973) by Al Green.
  • Goodbye Yellow Brick Road (1973) by Elton John.
  • Red Headed Stranger (1975) by Willie Nelson.
  • Born to Run (1975) by Bruce Springsteen.
  • Horses (1975) by Patti Smith.
  • Ramones (1976) by The Ramones.
  • Songs in the Key of Life (1976) by Stevie Wonder.
  • Hotel California (1976) by The Eagles.
  • Rumours (1977) by Fleetwood Mac.
  • Never Mind the Bollocks, Here's the Sex Pistols (1977) by Sex Pistols.
  • One Nation Under a Groove (1978) by Funkadelic.
  • London Calling (1979) by The Clash.
  • Back in Black (1980) by AC/DC.
  • The Great Twenty Eight (1982, a compilation of 1950s, 1960s and 1970s singles) by Chuck Berry.
  • Thriller (1982) by Michael Jackson.
  • Legend (1984, compilation album) by Bob Marley.
  • Purple Rain (1984) by Prince.
  • Stop Making Sense (1984) by Talking Heads.
  • Master of Puppets (1986) by Metallica.
  • Raising Hell (1986) by Run–D.M.C..
  • Graceland (1986) by Paul Simon.
  • The Joshua Tree (1987) by U2.
  • Sign '☮' the Times (1987) by Prince.
  • Paid in Full (1987) by Eric B. & Rakim.
  • Document (1987) by R.E.M..
  • It Takes a Nation of Millions to Hold Us Back (1988) by Public Enemy.
  • Straight Outta Compton (1988) by N.W.A..
  • Like a Prayer (1989) by Madonna.
  • The Stone Roses (1989) by The Stone Roses.
  • Paul's Boutique (1989) by The Beastie Boys.
  • Out of Time (1991) by R.E.M..
  • Star Time (1991, compilation record) by James Brown.
  • Ropin The Wind (1991) by Garth Brooks.
  • The Low End Theory (1991) by A Tribe Called Quest.
  • Nevermind (1991) by Nirvana.
  • Back To Mono (1991,compilation record) by Phil Spector.
  • Achtung Baby (1991) by U2.
  • Slanted And Enchanted (1992) by Pavement.
  • The Chronic (1992) by Dr. Dre.
  • Live Through This (1994) by Hole.
  • Ready to Die (1994) by The Notorious B.I.G..
  • My Life (1994) by Mary J. Blige.
  • (What's the Story) Morning Glory? (1995) by Oasis.
  • Endtroducing..... (1996) by DJ Shadow.
  • OK Computer (1997) by Radiohead.
  • Time Out of Mind (1997) by Bob Dylan.
  • Car Wheels On A Gravel Road (1998) by Lucinda Williams.
  • Sunrise (1999, compilation album) by Elvis Presley.
  • The Marshall Mathers LP (2000) by Eminem.
  • Kid A (2000) by Radiohead.
  • Stories from the City, Stories from the Sea (2000) by PJ Harvey.
  • Stankonia (2000) by OutKast.
  • The Anthology (1947–1972) (2001, compilation album) by Muddy Waters.
  • Elvis Thirty Number One Hits (2002, compilation album) by Elvis Presley
  • Portrait Of A Legend (2003, compilation album) by Sam Cooke.
  • The College Dropout (2004) by Kanye West.
  • Turn Back the Years: The Essential Hank Williams Collection (2005, compilation album) by Hank Williams.
