Danny Peary Cult Movies List

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

A Cult Classic on its own.
Advertisement:

Danny Peary (born 1949) is a renowned American film critic, who published three influential books about "Cult Movies" in the early 1980s. Each book provides analytical essays about the plot and themes of all the films listed. Peary's books did a lot to attract attention to cult movies in the mainstream media and are often used as a reference guide. He established the definition of the phenomenon as a "movie that polarized audiences on its release and is only popular with a small number of enthusiasts, who spread information about it by word-of-mouth." Some of them are Vindicated by History or Vindicated by Cable. Many of the films he elevated to cult movie status are now considered Cult Classics. His list is very versatile and spans from the silent era to the midnight movie classics of the 1970s and early 1980s.

Advertisement:

Sadly, Peary hasn't released any newer editions since, bringing the total amount of movies covered in his series to a mere 200. He explained that to him the disappearance of the midnight movie phenomenon in the late 1980s was the main reason. The AV Club had tried to continue the search for new cult movies with Scott Tobias' column The New Cult Canon which ran from 2008 to 2013.

"Danny Peary's Trope Movies List":

  • Animated Films: Only one animated film made it to the list, Fantasia.
  • Author Appeal: What makes these books so enduring is Peary's personal touch. He manages to appeal both to people already familiar with the film and those who didn't. He sheds light on interesting analysis and details and often includes personal experiences. He admits he personally loves Land Of The Pharaohs and Rebel Without a Cause, because he saw them during his youth. Other films get less favorable reviews, but he tries to be fair and try to understand what made them so appealing to a cult crowd.
    • Advertisement:
  • The Caper: The Killing is a good example.
  • Christmas Special: The Christmas classics It's a Wonderful Life and Miracle on 34th Street are in the books.
  • Comedy: Peary thought of people who enjoy a good comedy, by adding pictures like Bedtime For Bonzo, Duck Soup, Sons of the Desert, Monsieur Verdoux, The Producers, Wheres Poppa, It's a Gift, La Cage Aux Folles, It's a Mad, Mad, Mad, Mad World, Monty Python and the Holy Grail, Some Like It Hot, The Little Shop of Horrors, The Nutty Professor, Petulia, Bedazzled (1967), Sullivan's Travels, To Be or Not to Be, Annie Hall, The Gods Must Be Crazy and Dr. Strangelove.
  • Erotic Film: It may surprise some people that he did include some erotic films like Emmanuelle and Behind The Green Door in the first volume and the surreal Café Flesh in the second.
  • Exploitation Film: He included The Texas Chainsaw Massacre and Pink Flamingos.
  • Fantasy: Fantasy movies like The Wizard of Oz, Beauty and the Beast, Jason and the Argonauts, Willy Wonka & the Chocolate Factory, The Thief of Bagdad (1940),... are included.
  • Le Film Artistique: Andy Warhol's Bad, Werner Herzog's Aguirre, the Wrath of God, Stanley Kubrick's 2001: A Space Odyssey, Samuel Fuller's Shock Corridor, Ingmar Bergman's The Seventh Seal,... are among the arthouse films Peary discussed.
  • Film Noir: Included are: Gun Crazy, Laura, Kiss Me Deadly, Out of the Past, The Big Heat, Nightmare Alley, Taxi Driver, In a Lonely Place, Touch of Evil, The Long Goodbye ...
  • Freaky Is Cool: Certain cult pictures that owe their infamy due to their strangeness have been included: Beyond the Valley of the Dolls, El Topo, Freaks, Eraserhead, 2001: A Space Odyssey, Harold and Maude, Faster, Pussycat! Kill! Kill!, Phantom of the Paradise, The Rocky Horror Picture Show, Zardoz, Café Flesh, Glen or Glenda, Plan 9 from Outer Space,...
  • Genre Roulette: Peary went through great lengths trying to add many movies from different genres.
  • Horror: Night of the Living Dead (1968), The Black Cat, Freaks, The Texas Chainsaw Massacre, The Brood, Halloween (1978), Black Sunday, House of Wax (1953), King Kong (1933), Gojira, Peeping Tom, An American Werewolf in London, Targets, Witchfinder General, Basket Case, Blood Feast, Bride of Frankenstein, Night of the Demon, The Wicker Man (1973), The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari, Nosferatu, Martin, The Thing from Another World,...
  • Martial Arts Movie: One example made it to the list: Enter the Dragon.
  • The Musical: Included are Singin' in the Rain, 42nd Street, Top Hat, The Red Shoes (1948) and New York, New York.
  • Nostalgia Filter: Peary also included "pictures for which we have nostalgic feelings because they had great impact on us when we were kids".
  • Road Trip Plot: Badlands, Easy Rider, Faster, Pussycat! Kill! Kill!, Pretty Poison, Mad Max,... are listed.
  • Rock & Roll: Rock movies like The Girl Can't Help It, A Hard Day's Night and The Man Who Sold The World, The Harder They Come, Performance, Rock 'n' Roll High School and Quadrophenia are in the lists.
  • Science Fiction: Peary included both cheesy science fiction as well as dystopian movies: Forbidden Planet, 2001: A Space Odyssey, Plan 9 from Outer Space, Invasion of the Body Snatchers (1956), Altered States, Barbarella, A Boy and His Dog, A Clockwork Orange, Dark Star, Blade Runner, Seconds, The Terminator,...
  • Silent Movie: Pandora's Box, Napoléon and The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari are three examples.
  • Slasher Film: The Texas Chainsaw Massacre and Halloween (1978) are included in the first volume.
  • Something Completely Different: Some movies are added because they were complete break in style for the director, like Fantasia and Monsieur Verdoux.
  • Spaghetti Western: One example made the list: Once Upon a Time in the West.
  • Stoner Flick: Up in Smoke is included. And the unintentionally hilarious anti-marihuana propaganda movie Reefer Madness.
  • Thriller: The volumes include both psychological, erotic, crime and spy thrillers: Vertigo, Marnie, Psycho, The Parallax View, Blue Velvet, Taxi Driver, Vanishing Point, Body Heat, On Her Majesty's Secret Service, The Night of the Hunter,...
  • Unintentional Period Piece: The first volume was written in 1981, when VHS wasn't yet that common and many midnight movies still had to see a video release. In a large city you could probably find a movie theater play some of these cult classics, but not in suburbs or rural areas. As a result many titles were sometimes very obscure but intriguing for the casual movie fan.
  • The Western: Included are The Searchers, Rio Bravo, The Wild Bunch, Johnny Guitar, Once Upon a Time in the West, The Shooting, Ride the High Country, My Darling Clementine and Man Of The West.
  • What Could Have Been: In the first volume of Cult Movies Peary made a list of movies he considered for future volumes. Some of those did indeed turn in Cult Movies 2 and Cult Movies 3: Others never did: The 5000 Fingers of Dr. T, Satyricon, Fritz the Cat, Head, Return Of The Secaucus Seven, Richard Pryor Live In Concert, Sextette, Jackson County Jail, Les Lèvres Rouges, Can Hieronymus Merkin Ever Forget Mercy Humppe And Find True Happiness?, ... to name a few.
