Dye Hard

Dennis Rodman: 14th in most NBA titles, 22nd in rebounds, but first in hair colors.
Advertisement:

When a media personality has dyed hair so often, or for so long, it becomes an integral part of their whole characterization, to the point that it can be jarring to see them or their actor with their natural color.

Do not confuse with Dyeing for Your Art, which is when other physical characteristics are deliberately altered to fit the demands of the producers. Though it could overlap as some actors may prefer the new hair color such as Ashley Tisdale and Emma Stone who have long stayed with the color that they were required to change to. Nor does it have anything to do with the film series Die Hard.

In anime, this crosses over into You Gotta Have Blue Hair territory.

Real Life Examples

    open/close all folders 

    Actors, Actresses and Comedians 
  • Reese Witherspoon is known for playing blonde characters, but she's actually a brunette.
  • Billie Piper naturally brunette but is better known as a blonde.
  • Jean Harlow dyed her hair platinum blonde, though she denied it, and was in fact naturally very fair — just not as fair as she appeared onscreen. Eventually, her hair actually had to be cropped short due to peroxide damage, meaning in some films she's actually wearing a wig. Some stories say that bleached her pubic hair as well to maintain the illusion, but this is likely false.
  • Marilyn Monroe, who was naturally a brunette.
  • Lucille Ball was born with brown hair but most of her appearances have her as a redhead. This was lampshaded in one episode of I Love Lucy when Ricky cut her allowance and she couldn't afford both snacks and her trip to the beauty shop, complaining "I can either be a thin redhead or a fat brunette." Conversely, Vivian Vance ("Ethel") was a natural redhead.
    • Further, in one of her earliest appearances on film (with The Three Stooges) she was a blonde!
  • Olivia Wilde is a natural blonde, but typically has her hair dyed brown.
  • Gillian Anderson, although a natural blonde, became famous as a redhead and the image has stuck.
  • Vic Reeves of the Reeves and Mortimer comedy duo. Original color: Blond. Once had to stop dyeing his hair to convince people that his new son was his own.
  • Winona Ryder is naturally blonde (the colour in Edward Scissorhands is her natural one) but dyed her hair black/dark brown prior to her feature film debut in Lucas and is what she is known for.
  • Molly Ringwald, a famous redhead, dyed it that way after an agent advised her it was a good color to get her attention in the film industry. It worked.
  • Helen Slater, normally dark-haired, dyed her hair for the film Supergirl and hasn't looked back since.
  • Uma Thurman is actually a brunette, but has her hair bleached in most of her roles.
  • Dita Von Teese dyes her hair black, and she does it herself.
  • Similarly, redhead Cassandra Peterson dyes her hair black for her far more famous persona Elvira, Mistress of the Dark, when she doesn't use a wig instead.
  • James Marsters, who supposedly had issues after he stopped playing Spike and went back to his natural brown hair.
  • Mad Men's Christina Hendricks is a natural blonde but has been dyeing her hair red since she was 10. Apparently it's because she was a massive fan of Anne of Green Gables. After Mad Men wrapped she dyed her hair a light shade of blonde as part of an advertising campaign for a hair colouring brand; it was rather jarring (and it wasn't long before she was back to red).
  • Greg Ayres, a popular anime voice actor, has a habit of constantly dyeing his hair, to the point where there was a joke about it in the first (and only) American Anime Awards ceremony and con employees have had a betting pool about what color his hair would be on at least one occasion. He himself joked about it on the Chrono Crusade DVD Commentary: "Look, I finally have the same hair color as the character I'm playing! I knew if I kept my hair purple long enough, it would happen."
  • Benedict Cumberbatch generally has dark brown hair, or sometimes blonde hair, but he's really ginger.
  • Lucy Lawless is a natural blond, but everyone thinks of her as the brown-haired Xena. Additionally, during the character's first appearances on Hercules: The Legendary Journeys, her hair was dyed black.
    • She did play the blond #3 on Battlestar Galactica.
  • Speaking of Xena, Renee O'Connor, most well known for playing her partner Gabrielle, is also a blond, and dyed her hair red for the show.
  • Meryl Streep is a natural (light) brunette, but outside of certain film roles she has had blonde hair for over thirty years.
  • Angelina Jolie's hair is naturally a dark blonde. She occasionally reverts to it (Girl, Interrupted, Gone in Sixty Seconds, Life Or Something Like It) but jet black is the most common (Salt even has her going from blonde to black). She's apparently been doing it since the age of five.
  • Katie McGrath of Merlin, practically the poster girl for Eerie Pale-Skinned Brunette is naturally dark blonde.
  • Cate Blanchett has forgotten what her actual hair color is; she's usually seen with blonde, strawberry blonde or red hair.
  • Lauren Bacall was a natural brunette who dyed her hair red as a career move.
  • While Jennifer Aniston is recognized for her honey blonde hair, she actually has "naturally wavy, frizzy Greek hair." Look no further than this picture◊ of Aniston with Chaz Bono in their senior year of high school. She kept her natural dark brown hair earlier in her career prior to Friends, but since the show she's gone through most of the shades of blonde.
  • Lisa Kudrow is also a natural brunette, but is known as a blonde.
  • Nicole Kidman, another natural redhead, often dyes her hair for movie roles, though fans generally agree that she's much more beautiful with red hair.
  • Sarah Michelle Gellar, best known for playing the blonde Buffy the Vampire Slayer, actually has brown hair and frequently played brunette characters during her early career. She also started off Buffy with slightly darker hair, and got progressively blonder with the seasons.
  • Leighton Meester of Gossip Girl is a real-life blonde but dyes her hair darker- nearly all the characters she's ever played are brunettes.
  • Kari Byron of MythBusters. Especially in earlier seasons, the dark brown (black?) can be seen through the red streaks.Later seasons have her dyeing her hair auburn with a strong reddish tone.
  • Amy Adams is actually blonde but famously dyes her hair red (as seen in Enchanted, The Fighter, The Muppets and so on).
  • Emma Stone is known for her red hair, but her natural color is actually blonde (not the bleached blond she has in The Amazing Spider-Man, though). Judd Apatow actually required her to dye it red for Superbad making this a rare combination with Dyeing for Your Art.
  • Blonde Emily Osment recently dyed her hair black for a short while, while brunette Demi Lovato dyed her hair red.
  • Ashley Tisdale had blonde hair as Sharpay Evans in the High School Musical series. With her first long layoff from the films in 2009, she's gone back to her natural dark auburn hair color. As of 2011 she's blond again. She dyed it blonde for The Suite Life of Zack and Cody.
  • Another Disney example: Bella Thorne is actually a natural blonde (see here, for example).
  • Hilary Duff, a natural brunette, is known to dye her hair blonde, namely in Lizzie McGuire. Since it's a dirty blond, she only had to have dyed lighter when she did Casper Meets Wendy, a couple years before Lizzie Mc Guire.
  • Margarita Carmen Cansino went from brunette to redhead when her studio basically Anglicized her into being Rita Hayworth. Oddly enough it was treated as being a big deal when soon-to-be-ex-husband Orson Welles made her dye her hair blonde for The Lady from Shanghai, so maybe everyone had forgotten she wasn't a natural redhead either.
  • Brittany Murphy was a natural brunette; however, she got most of her more famous roles after she dyed her hair blonde. (Clueless and Girl, Interrupted notwithstanding.)
  • Lindsay Lohan is a natural redhead, though she has dyed her hair numerous times, from brown to jet black, light blonde, platinum blonde, and finally back to red again. There was a humorous article titled "The Lindsay Lohan Hair Terror Alert Scale" that associated how much of a chance she would behave by what shade her hair is, similar to the United States Homeland Security's Terror Alert Scale.
  • Christine McIntyre was a natural brunette before she started working with The Three Stooges, when she dyed her hair blonde.
  • Science presenter on The BBC, Dr Alice Roberts used to be notorious for dyeing her hair every possible shade of red or orange, but it seems that with her shows now involving a lot of filming in remote locations, it became too much of a hassle and she doesn't bother anymore.
  • Franka Potente (The Bourne Identity, Run Lola Run) is known for dyeing her hair often.
  • Pauley Perrette of NCIS fame constantly dyes her blond hair black to achieve her character Abby's trademark goth look. Years of hair dye eventually had an adverse effect on her health, though, she eventually developed an allergic reaction to the chemicals in hair dye and her face swelled up considerably.
  • Lily Loveless is a natural brunette, but was bleached blonde for Skins and has barely gone back since.
  • Rose McGowan is naturally brunette but has alternated between that, blonde and redhead. She went through all three colours during her time on Charmed. The show hand waved the colour change saying a potion blew up on her and turned her hair that way.
  • Sofia Vergara is a natural blonde but went dark at the requests of her casting directors - feeling it gave her a better Spicy Latina image.
  • Tommy Wiseau of The Room fame has incredibly, unnaturally dark black hair that is remarked upon by Greg Sestero in The Disaster Artist to look as if he's dyed it with Sharpies. Sestero also discovered that his bathroom is covered in black stains, heavily implies Wiseau dyes the whole lot over and over instead of just the roots, and finds old photos of him with chestnut brown hair. Wiseau seems to think it makes him look vampiric.
  • Actress Trestine Harper is a natural-born blonde, but had to dye her hair brown for her role as Farrah on My Family and Me because the main character's best friend is supposed to be a brunette. You can still see her naturally blonde locks in her brown hair in her earlier appearances on the show.
  • The black-haired Sergio Fantoni plays the very blonde Captain von Hecht in Hornets' Nest. His hair in the film is a very unnatural-looking shade of nearly white bleach-blonde.
  • Alyson Hannigan's natural hair colour is a brownish auburn but she is often seen with red hair or else dark brown. She was required to dye it red for Buffy the Vampire Slayer as she had the same colour as Sarah Michelle Gellar. She ended up keeping it for most of her career.
  • Eva Green is actually a natural blonde but has favoured her hair black since she was fourteen.
  • Kristen Stewart was born strawberry blonde but is best known with dark brown hair.
  • Noel Fielding actually has brown hair (as seen in the first series of The Mighty Boosh and other early works), but he's known much better for having black hair, which is part of his image as a Perky Goth.
  • Brigitte Bardot is known for being a blonde, but is a natural brunette.
  • Sophie Turner is naturally a baby blonde, but she dyed her hair red for her role on Game of Thrones and has kept that hair colour in all of her other roles, including famous redhead Jean Grey in X-Men: Apocalypse. In 2018 she reverted to her natural blonde, coinciding with Game of Thrones finishing.
  • Keira Knightley is naturally a chestnut brown but has been better known for either blonde or dark brown hair.
  • Deborah Ann Woll is naturally a strawberry blonde. However, she began to dye it red during high school to, as she put it, no longer disappear into the background as she felt she did with her natural hair color. She retained this red hair color up through True Blood, where she played Jessica Hamby. She reverted back to her natural color prior to being cast as Karen Page in Daredevil (2015).
  • Vincent D'Onofrio shaves his head whenever he's playing Wilson Fisk in Daredevil (2015).
  • Jenna Coleman is naturally brown-haired, but in an interview with her Doctor Who co-star Matt Smith she confessed that she often dyes her hair a different shade of brown. In 2018, she revealed on Instagram that she had bleached or dyed her hair blonde for her role in the miniseries The Cry, the first time she has drastically changed her hair colour for a TV or film role.
  • Deborah Kerr was a natural redhead, just much darker. An aunt of hers persuaded her to lighten it in order to make herself more noticeable. It worked!
  • Comedian Herman José is a natural brunet, but from late 2002 to early 2010, he bleached his hair to dirty blond.

  • Ani Difranco. She has dyed her hair A LOT of colors during her lifetime. Green, purple, blonde, blue, black etc....

  • Dua Lipa, who is a natural brunette, has since dyed her hair to a two-toned blonde-brown mix.

    • Kelly Kelly, though she went with her natural brunette hair for a while after her retirement before returning to blonde.
    • Dolph Ziggler began his WWE career as Spirit Squad member Nicky (he was a brunette then). Since then, he bleached his hair blond (except for a few months in 2011 when he was a brunette). He's since stopped bleaching it, with the tips of his hair being blond and the rest brunette. As of 2017 he let his natural hair grow out.
    • Dana Brooke's platinum blond hair is obviously not natural.
    • Ric Flair is known for his bleach blond hair. He is (or was) a natural brunette. The same can be said for his daughter, Charlotte and son David.
    • Sting, a natural brunette, dyed his hair blond from 1985-1995. He went back to his natural hair color in 1996 and has kept it ever since.
    • Kaitlyn (naturally black and she stopped dying her hair after her retirement). She switched to a darker brown shortly before she left WWE.
    • Velvet Sky and Angelina Love are actually natural blondes but both bleached their hair, Angelina to platinum blonde and Velvet to a two-toned style when they formed The Beautiful People.
    • Ashley Massaro (naturally brunette)
    • Carmella (naturally brunette)

    • Hulk Hogan (who is a natural blond, but started bleaching it as he got older) He was asked to dye his hair red during his first run in WWF (since Hogan is an Irish surname), but declined because he was already balding at the time. He actually did end up frying off most of his remaining hair during a dye job gone wrong (Hogan's ex-wife Linda used to dye his hair, after their split, he tried to do it himself and messed up) and now wears extensions in public to maintain his trademark look.
  • Natural blondes:

    • Becky Lynch is a natural dark blond, but dyes her hair various shades of red.
    • Natalya Neidhart briefly dyed her hair red in 2008 because she felt there were too many blondes on the roster and wanted to stand out.
    • Nikki Bella, a natural dark blond, dyed her hair brunette during her early career in order to look more like her sister, Brie. She later went to medium brown with blond highlights.
    • Sasha Banks (naturally dark blond but has dyed her hair various shades of red, purple and pink).
  • The Undertaker is a natural redhead (or at least it was before it went gray), but dyes his hair black most of the time.
  • Alicia Fox is of course naturally brunette but has cycled through many different variations of blonde, brown and red in her time.
  • Zack Ryder is red haired but bleaches most of it, making the rest appear light brown.
  • Eve Torres is a really dark brown naturally (the colour she had when she won the Diva Search) but has since been recognised for dark blonde and light brown hair.
  • Mickie James is a natural brunette, but dyed her hair either dark blond or light brown during her time in WWE. She went back to her natural hair color in 2010 and has kept it ever since.
  • Melina would dye her hair all sorts of weird colors during her early career (most notably brown with blond highlights or burgundy) before going with her natural black hair after her Heel–Face Turn. She has dyed her hair blond as of 2016.
  • Randy Savage was naturally dark blond. His hair darkened to light brown when he got older and he started dying in black in the 1990s.
  • Maria Kanellis is a natural brunette, but is better known as a redhead. She was also blond for a brief period after her debut.
  • Lita is also a natural brunette, but is known for various shades of red.
  • Aliyah has alternated between varying shades of brown and even went black briefly before she debuted on TV. She's naturally a dark brown. In 2017 she added some blonde.
  • Eric Bischoff had been dying his hair black for many years because his hair turned gray at a young age. He reverted to his natural color in 1999, re-dyed it in 2002 at Vince McMahon's request, then went back to gray and stayed that way.

    Other 
  • This is Older Than Print (and probably much older): Abd-ar-Rahman III, the first Caliph of Córdoba (reigned from 929 to 961) had blue eyes and blond hair and beard, which he dyed black "to look more Arab" (his mother had been a Frankish slave and his paternal family had Germanic and Iberian blood as well).

Fictional Examples

    open/close all folders 

    Anime & Manga 
  • Max from Beyblade dyes his hair to attract girls, however we're never told what color it naturally is. Fan-speculation is that he lightens his natural blond hair, due to him having abnormally dark blond hair in one of the seasons.
  • Misa Amane from Death Note. The Dumb Blonde Evil Diva is shown to be a brunette and probably wears contacts as well.
  • Durarara!! :
    • Shizuo dyes his hair from black to blond to differentiate himself from his brother.
    • Kida's hair is dyed a light brown.
  • Haikyuu!!:
    • Kenma has his naturally black hair dyed blond, though his roots apparently intentionally are left un-dyed.
    • Keishin has his hair dyed blond however his sideburns are black.
  • Kaoru from I Can't Understand What My Husband Is Saying has purple hair, but started dying it blonde after getting married to Hajime. This is briefly explained in the manga: she once flipped her hair over her face to imitate Sadako, and Hajime was surprisingly overcome with moe, to Kaoru's distress. So she dyed her hair just because Hajime's "Sadako moe" thing freaked her out so bad.
  • The protagonist of Inhabitants of the Threshold has his hair dyed green.
  • Fujiko Mine from Lupin III regularly goes through various shades of blonde, brunette, and red hair, with Lupin III: The Italian Adventure cutting the knot and giving her a blonde-to-brunette-to-red blend effect.◊
    • Lupin III: The Italian Adventure has Rebecca Rossellini, who dyes her hair with a blonde-to-green blend effect but, as seen in a flashback, is a natural brunette.
  • Setsuna from Mahou Sensei Negima! is albino but dyes her hair black and wears black contacts.
  • Neon Genesis Evangelion: Ritsuko Akagi dyed her brown hair blonde, presumably to avoid resembling her mother.
  • Tomoko from No Matter How I Look at It, It's You Guys' Fault I'm Not Popular! is surprised to find her middle school friend has become fashionable since she began high school. Yuu dyed her hair a light brown, almost blonde, color.
  • Kirino from Oreimo's iconic orange hair is dyed. Her natural hair color is a dark brown.
  • Eijiro Kirishima of My Hero Academia dyes his hair bold red. His natural hair color is black.

    Comic Books 
  • Both Black Canary are naturally black haired. The second originally wore a blonde wig to hide her hair but eventually grew it out and has been dyeing it for years. At the start of the New 52 it had been retconned into being naturally blonde, but that was subsequently retconned as it was later shown that she bleached her hair blonde.
  • The Pre-Crisis Jason Todd is was a natural redhead who dyes it black, Post-Crisis writers couldn't seem to make up their minds about whether he was a natural brunette or redhead. The New 52 made it naturally black.
  • In the original Jem cartoon You Gotta Have Blue Hair was in effect, despite the fact being glam rockers gave everyone a good excuse to dye their hair green or purple. The comics makes it clear the characters dye their hair:
    • Stormer and Pizzazz appear to have naturally black hair but dye them blue and green respectively.
    • Clash apparently dyed her hair recently. It's unknown if only the blue part is dyed or if she isn't a natural redhead either.
    • Blaze's hair is mostly red with some blonde in it.
  • Ramona Flowers from Scott Pilgrim frequently changes her haircut and color. The film just has her color change three times.
  • Peter Parker's clone, Ben Reilly from Spider-Man dyes his hair blond to differentiate himself from Parker.
  • Wonder Woman: The Amazon Oenone originally bleached her hair orange, but in later appearances while wearing a head covering that covers most of her hair the fringes are her natural black.
  • X-Men:
    • Emma Frost dyes her (naturally brown) hair blonde.
    • Psylocke was originally blonde. She dyes her hair purple.

    Fan Works 
  • Father Brainstorm of Calvin and Hobbes: The Series dyes his hair green.
  • Danny Phantom: The idea that either Sam Manson or one of her parents dye their hair is popular Fanon since it handily would explain how a blond and a redhead have a child with raven hair without non-standard parentage for Sam. Based on canon evidence, namely how much Sam resembles her grandmother as a teen, one of her parents is most likely though it doesn't entirely rule out Sam dying her hair (if she does dye her hair, blond is the more popular choice for her natural coloring given how much it clashes with her personality).
  • In Friendship Is Magic, Rainbow Dash's natural hair color is blue, but she dyes it in a rainbow style to look cool.
  • Formerly Known as Harry Potter?: It's mentioned that Lily begin dyeing her naturally black hair red around age four. It's unclear if she still dyes it or whether it's magically turned red, though the later is more likely.
  • In Dragon Ball Z Abridged, Android 17 is offhandedly mentioned to dye his hair. In this continuity he's naturally a blond, making him a Half Identical Twin with his sister.

    Film — Live Action 
  • Coraline: Coraline herself. It's blue for the duration of the film, but an old family photograph reveals that it is naturally chestnut-brown (similar to her brown hair in the books).
  • Clementine in Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind. Her hair is orange, red, blue, green, and other colours over the course of the film.
  • In Adam Sandler's Mr. Deeds, Babe Bennett, upon her Heel Realization and Anguished Declaration of Love, declares she's dyed her hair for so long she has no idea what her natural hair color is anymore.
  • Gary in The World's End is in his forties but still keeps dying his blond hair black. At the very end of the movie he stops though.

    Literature 
  • Mrs. Levy in A Confederacy of Dunces has dyed her hair platinum-blonde for so long that even she forgot she was a natural brunette. (Mr. Levy didn't, though, oddly.)
  • A novelization written by Takeshi Shudō called Pocket Monsters: The Animation states that Misty's sisters wear wigs. While this could explain their different hair colors, like most in the book it has no canon evidence in the Pokémon anime itself.
  • Commander Christine Vale in the Star Trek: A Time to... and Star Trek: Titan novels has dyed her hair multiple colours. There is no indication in the books as to what her original hair colour was.
  • Myri Antilles, spy and Wraith agent from Star Wars Legends, takes this to a typically Corellian extreme. In her introductory scenes in the Legacy of the Force series, she casually mentions that she's dyed her hair seven times in the past week. Not because she's spying on anyone (although she's no stranger to that), just on a whim. In Mercy Kill, she jokes that "nature hasn't touched my hair in years".

    Live-Action TV 
  • Dottie Underwood of Agent Carter is blonde in the first season, but has changed her hair brown by the second. Hilariously enough her actress Bridget Regan is a natural redhead.
  • Mrs Slocombe of Are You Being Served? has a different, unnatural hair colour every episode. It can be strange to see Mollie Sugden in interviews with her normal brown (or grey) hair.
  • Felicity Smoak of Arrow has admitted her distinctive blonde is dyed several times, and is seen with dark hair in a flashback to her college years.
  • Charmed:
    • Paige (played by Rose McGowan mentioned above). She was black-haired in season 4, redhead in season 5 note though this was an accident - a potion blew up on her and turned it red, strawberry blonde in season 6 and brunette for the last two seasons.
    • Phoebe additionally changed her hair colour a lot on the show. She starts off with light brown hair (though when herself as a child makes an appearance she has dark hair so presumably this is dyed too), dyes her hair blonde in season 3 and actually cycles through six different hair colours in the fourth season. She sticks with a dark brown colour for the rest of the series and alludes to dyeing her hair regularly in a season 7 episode.
    • Prue had dyed her hair blonde in a season 2 episode showing a Bad Future.
  • Claire Underwood in House of Cards (US) actually is a brunette, but dyed it blonde some time prior to the start of the show. She reverts to her natural color for a couple episodes in Season 3 when she's reconciling with Frank after a falling out in Chapter 32.
  • Lucy from I Love Lucy is explicitly stated to have dyed her hair red.
  • A scene from Married... with Children where Peggy and Kelly are bonding. Peggy tells the story of buying Kelly her first bottle of hair dye at the age of five. They're laughing nostalgically, when Kelly asks Peg her natural hair colour. "I don't know," Peg says. "What's yours?" And Kelly replies "I don't know."
  • Scrubs:
    • The Janitor's girlfriend Lady is brunette but claims she is a natural redhead.
    • Elliott also dyes her naturally dark blonde hair to a bleached blonde in season 3 and keeps it that way for the remainder of the series.
  • In the first series in Star Trek, James T. Kirk generally had brown hair, but in the later films, it turned black. In the Kelvin Timeline films, Kirk is blonde for the first two, but had visible dark roots, and it started turning darker in Beyond. Chris Pine is a natural brunette, as is William Shatner, though Shatner did wear a toupee in his later film appearances.
  • Claudia Donovan in Warehouse 13 has a Skunk Stripe which is a different colour every episode.

    Roleplay 
  • Zia from Dawn of a New Age: Oldport Blues dyes her hair purple, and has done so for so long that no-one besides her childhood friends remember what her natural hair colour is.

    Toys 
  • Barbie and her sisters all have naturally blonde hair, but her (currently) third youngest sister, Skipper, has had her hair dyed black with purple highlights for several years now.

    Video Games 
  • Miki Hoshii from The Idolmaster games is depicted as a blond in around 90% of the official art and a majority of the fan-arts, but she is naturally brown-haired. It is a great plot point in her alternate route in the first game, but no mentions of this is made in the second game.
  • Life Is Strange:
    • Chloe's hair is dyed bright blue. Old photographs show it's naturally the same shade of blonde as her mother Joyce's. In the Alternate Universe of Episode 4, she never dyed it, but dialogue and her screen name imply she wants to.
    • Alyssa has her hair dyed purple. Her darker roots imply she also bleached it beforehand.
  • Persona:
    • Kanji◊ from Persona 4 is near indistinguishable without his bleached blond hair. His natural color is black.
    • Ryuji from Persona 5 dyes his hair blonde, and is a natural black as well. Supplementary material also states that Futaba dyed her hair orange.

    Visual Novels 
  • Youhei from CLANNAD has black hair but is bleached blond for most of the series. He doesn't stand out amongst the purple and green haired characters, so most fans don't even know this until they see him as an adult.
  • Misha from Katawa Shoujo dyes her hair pink. She's naturally a brunette.
  • In Zero Time Dilemma, it's revealed Phi dyes her hair white because she's ashamed of her natural hair color, red. It turns out to be a pivotal point in determining her true identity.
  • Phoenix Wright: Ace Attorney – Dual Destinies: Florent L'Belle has a different hair color every time he appears. It's to hide his "ugly" white hair, and ends up being a key plot point against him.

    Webcomics 
  • Shay from Between the Lines bleaches her hair. As an adult her hair is its natural black with pink bangs.
  • Susan in El Goonish Shive dyes her blonde hair dark blue* represented by black with blue highlights in color strips to distance herself from her father's mistress. Later, her hair turns that color permanently due to her awakening at least partially because it has become part of her identity.
  • Charlie and Tom from Khaos Komix are a blonde and raven respectively but dyed their hair green and red/orange after being the victims of a transphobic attack in high school. When Charlie first sees the color Tom picks she's dismayed because they will clash, but Tom insists they'll be like Tic-Tacs.
  • It's used somewhat as a trope in Lookism where the students of the Beauty department of the High School are allowed to do this. The only male dyed his purple with blue tips, a woman one see has yellow with red tips.
  • Slightly Damned: Angels have naturally white hair but have laws in place that make dye their hair the same color as their element (red for fire, blue for water, yellow for earth and green for wind), the way it's applied is also different depending on gender, they all have bangs in front of their ears that are dyed while the rest is left it's natural white if male, if female most of her hair is dyed while the bangs are left white.note  According to Word of God non-binary angels have their hair dyed completely with no white showing.
  • Izzy's hair in Ennui GO! is a blonde, but she dyes it pink. The only times her natural hair color is seen are during flashbacks to her childhood and the arc where she was trapped on a desereted island. It acts as a visual indicator to tell her apart from her identical twin sister Adelie (who leaves it blonde) and her clone Kirsty (who just dyes the tips).

    Web Original 
  • It's never stated in Barbie: Life in the Dreamhouse that Skipper dyes her hair, however it's implied due to her being the token raven of her family. In the toys, Skipper was a blonde until she was given a redesign in the 2010s.
  • A flashback in the final episode of Neko Sugar Girls reveals that Raku's natural hair color is blue instead of green. That is assuming the blue wasn't dyed as well.

    Western Animation 
  • Francine Smith from American Dad! is a natural brunette. note (Or not — She's been shown as a child with blonde hair, so episodes have contradicted each other. But then, real children have their hair color change as they grow up.)
  • Harley Quinn once said she wasn't a natural blonde in a Batman: The Animated Series episode, however it could have been a joke. DC always writes her as naturally blonde.
  • Nina Harper in Braceface is portrayed with long red hair, but she's not actually a natural redhead. In fact, according to some flashbacks to when she and her ex-BFF, Sharon Spitz (the show's titular "Braceface"), were little kids, Nina's natural hair color is actually brown.
  • Robbie from Gravity Falls naturally has medium brown hair but dyed it black in his teens.
  • Almost everyone in Jem naturally has hair colors like blue and pink however multiple colors at once seems to be impossible. Pictures show Danse with less colors than she has currently.
  • Ginger in The Legend of Korra is the only character to have dyed hair—specifically, it's dyed a bright red.
  • The Loud House: Possibly in the case of Lincoln Loud's Goth sister, Lucy, who has black hair while their parents and the rest of their sisters have either blonde like their mom or brown hair like their dad (Lincoln has white hair, which he apparently inherited from his maternal grandfather). It's never explained how Lucy has black hair (much like how it's never truly explained why Lincoln has white hair), but assuming that it's her natural hair color, she may have inherited it from her great-grandmother, Harriet (who, as shown in pictures, is the spitting image of Lucy).
  • In Moral Orel, it's revealed that Miss Sculptham and Nurse Bendy dyed their hair the other's natural color, blonde and black respectively, although the two characters are not connected. The former because she was obsessed with a rapist who disliked blondes. The latter was probably because revealing that she had black hair makes her look like her son Joe.
  • In My Family and Me Tegan Westbrook's hair is dyed hot pink, but she won't reveal her natural hair color to anyone.
  • The "Ponyville Confidential" episode of My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic reveals that the Mayor of Ponyville dyes her hair grey. It's actually a vivid pink. Confirming this is a flashback in Season 7 that shows pink is her natural colour.
  • In The Simpsons Marge claims in one episode her hair isn't really blue. Though this is contradicted by several childhood flashbacks that show her with blue hair. Presumably she dyes it to cover up her greys — which Homer confirms in an episode where he gets in trouble for gossiping about their personal lives.
  • Star Wars Rebels: Sabine Wren dyes her hair various colours like blue, purple and turquoise, with the colours changing every season. Seeing her with her natural dark brown hair in "The Antilles Extraction" is quite jarring.
  • Total Drama has a few:
    • As revealed in "Total Drama Drama Island", Lindsay is not a natural blonde. Judging from her eyebrows, we can assume her natural hair color is either black or dark brown.
    • Geoff's song "Her Real Name isn't Blainely" implies that Blainely/Mildred is also not a natural blonde by mentioning she has black hairs on her toes.
    • Word of God confirmed for several character such as Gwen (black hair with teal highlights), Zoey (red hair) and Sierra (purple hair) to have dyed hair. The real hair colour of the first two is stated to be brown, but in the case of Sierra, it's not mentioned. Considering her mother has black hair, it's likely for her to have as well.
  • In the first season of Xiaolin Showdown, Kimiko often dyes her hair in a rainbow of colours whenever the monks go in their mission and changes back to her black hair when they get back to the temple. She stops dyeing her hair post season 1, but she continues to wear a different outfit and change her hairstyle in every episode.
