We Interrupt This Index

  • Create New

Not now! I'm busy explaining that these are tropes about how someone or something ends up getting interr...

"Oh, sorry, carry on."

INTERRUPTED!

  • Almost Kiss: Two characters are interrupted right when they're about to kiss.
  • Astonishingly Appropriate Interruption: Interrupted words still manage to form a coherent sentence.
    • Advertisement:
  • Battle-Interrupting Shout: Somehow, someone manages to shout loudly enough over the noise of battle to interrupt it.
  • Big Damn Heroes: A heroic rescue interrupts the villain.
  • Big-Lipped Alligator Moment: a tense scene can be interrupted by a short, random scene
  • Big "SHUT UP!": Someone interrupts by shouting "SHUT UP!" or some other variant.
  • A Birthday, Not a Break: Someone's birthday celebration is interrupted by an adventure or a catastrophe.
  • Catchphrase Interruptus: Irritated by the catchphrase, someone interrupts it.
  • Caught with Your Pants Down: Masturbation is interrupted by another character's entrance.
  • Chandler's Law: Author's trick of invoking *Click* Hello to interrupt a boring part of the story.
  • *Click* Hello: Someone interrupts the onscreen action with a Dramatic Gun Cock.
  • Coitus Uninterruptus A couple is in the midst of the act, but another person interrupts. You'd expect the couple to stop having sex, but they don't.
    • Advertisement:
  • Collapsed Mid-Speech: A public speaker is interrupted by their own health crisis, which may or may not be fatal.
  • Combo Breaker: A well-timed move or dedicated game mechanic that lets you interrupt an opponent's attack string in a fighting game.
  • Conjunction Interruption: Someone is interrupted right as they are saying a conjunction.
  • Conveniently Interrupted Document: A Fictional Document which gets interrupted (either via damage or by the character reading it stopping partway through it) in a way which is convenient for the plot.
  • Curse Cut Short: A profane word is interrupted.
  • Deadly Deferred Conversation: A temporary interruption in a conversation is rendered permanent by the death of an involved conversant.
  • Disconnected by Death: A phone call is interrupted by the death of one of the conversants.
    • Advertisement:
  • Dynamic Entry: Someone's arrival is fast, surprising and violent.
  • Exposition Already Covered: A character is about to explain something but another character beats them to it, much to their frustration.
  • Intermission: A deliberate interruption to allow the audience time to resolve their Potty Emergency.
  • Impeded Messenger: An interrupted messenger can't deliver a plot-important message on time.
  • Inconvenient Summons: A summoned character is teleported onto the scene, along with the paraphernalia of whatever activity was interrupted by the summons.
  • Interrupted Bath: A character's bathing ritual is interrupted.
  • Interrupted by the End: The characters are about to do something, but are interrupted by the end of the episode, often in the form of a "The End" card.
  • Interrupted Cooldown Hug: A peaceful resolution is interrupted by some Trigger Happy Leeroy Jenkins.
  • Interrupted Declaration of Love: Someone is interrupted while trying to confess their feelings.
  • Interrupted Intimacy: A couple is about to have sex, or are in the midst of the act, but another person interrupts somehow. They have to stop to deal with the interruption.
  • Interrupted Suicide: Someone interrupts someone else's suicide.
  • Interrupting Meme: An interruption turns into a meme.
  • I Was Having Such a Nice Dream: Someone is not happy that their dream was interrupted.
  • Killed Mid-Sentence: Someone is interrupted by their own death.
  • Kinda Busy Here: A call on a cell phone interrupts an action scene.
  • Lag Cancel: when the player can interrupt a video game character's action animation by doing something else.
  • Letting the Air Out of the Band: The music is interrupted and slowly winds down to a halt.
  • Mamet Speak: A kind of dialogue where characters speak in quick succession, frequently cutting each other off, finishing each other's sentences, and repeating themselves whilst the other speaks.
  • Mid-Battle Tea Break: Characters take a break in the middle of a fight. Doing something silly in midst of the break is optional.
  • Moment Killer: Something happens to break up a romantic moment.
  • Musicalis Interruptus: Someone interrupts a musical number.
  • Not Now, Kiddo: A child is interrupted by the adults before he can say much of anything.
  • Parents Walk In at the Worst Time: Parents interrupt their sons or daughters, catching them in a suggestive scene that might or might not be what it looks like.
  • Paused Interrupt: An unintentional beat that occurs between someone's line of dialogue being cut off mid-sentence and the interruption.
  • Percussive Prevention: Someone thwarts a Leeroy Jenkins with an interrupting knockout.
  • Pre-Violence Laughter: Someone invokes Mood Whiplash by interrupting the laughter they initiated with a sudden act of violence.
  • Primal Scene: Parents having sex are interrupted by their kids.
  • Pulled from Your Day Off: A character's vacation is interrupted by their work calling them to cancel and come back to deal with an urgent situation.
  • Rapid-Fire Interrupting: Someone is repeatedly interrupted while trying to get a word in edgewise.
  • Record Needle Scratch: An interruption or Shocking Swerve is punctuated by the sound of a record needle scratch.
  • "Shut Up!" Gunshot: Someone interrupts a yelling mob by firing a bullet into the air.
  • Shut Up, Hannibal!: Hero interrupts the villain by attacking them during their monologue.
  • Shut Up, Kirk!: Same as Shut Up, Hannibal!, but it's the villain interrupting the hero.
  • "Shut Up" Kiss: Someone interrupts someone else with a kiss.
  • Sneeze Interruption: A plot device or gag about someone being interrupted by a sneeze.
  • Sorry to Interrupt: Someone opens a door, interrupts something unexpected, and does an about face.
  • Speak Now or Forever Hold Your Peace: Now is your last chance to interrupt the wedding with an objection.
  • Speech-Bubbles Interruption: Visually representing an interruption by having the interrupted speech bubble partially obscured.
  • Taking the Fight Outside: A fight, or the buildup to it, is interrupted by a request to settle the score outside.
  • Talk to the Fist: Someone's speech is interrupted by a fist to their mouth.
  • Thwarted Coup de Grâce: Someone stops the villain from making the killing blow.
  • Third Party Stops Attack: A third party physically interrupts the attacker before the attack can land on the victim.
  • A Truce While We Gawk: Something so astonishing happens, it interrupts a fight as the fighters turn to gawk at it.
  • *Twang* Hello: An arrow, throwing knife, or any other throwing implement thrown or shot from offscreen interrupts the onscreen action.
  • Walk-In Chime-In: A person enters a conversation he hasn't overheard with a completely appropriate response to what was just said.
  • Wedding Smashers: Someone or a group interrupts a wedding concession/ceremony.
  • We Interrupt This Program: A TV show is interrupted to deliver a (usually) shocking announcement.
  • You Just Ruined the Shot: Someone mistakes a performance for an actual emergency and interrupts the actors.
