Pregnancy Tropes

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

"Pregnancy is a kind of miracle. Especially so in that it proves that a man and woman can conspire to force God to create a new soul."
— Robert Anton Wilson

How is babby formed? Read these pages and find out!

Comes after Sex Tropes if you're not lucky, and is followed by Birth Tropes if you are. Unsurprisingly, unless there's a Mr. Seahorse involved, these tropes are Always Female.

Tropes:

  • All Lesbians Want Kids: A lesbian couple want kids, and for some reason, they only want biological kids.
    • Advertisement:
  • Babies Make Everything Better: If a couple have a problem, having babies will resolve it.
  • Babies Ever After: The work ends with the main couple being pregnant or giving birth.
  • Baby Be Mine: A woman goes to extreme lengths to have a child.
  • Baby Factory: Women are used to make babies for profit.
  • The Baby Trap: Somebody tries to deliberately get pregnant without her partner's permission so that they (according to her at least) will have to stay together.
  • Breeding Cult: A Cult that focuses on having many children, either to breed a "master race," or to breed a Messianic Archetype.
  • Breeding Slave: A character or group of characters that are used for breeding against their will.
  • But I Can't Be Pregnant!: Someone's pregnant but they thought she or her partner was infertile or they were a different species or she was too old to have kids or otherwise it seems impossible for her to be pregnant.
    • Advertisement:
  • But We Used a Condom: Two people have sex and they use a condom but one of them gets pregnant and/or one or both of them get an STD.
  • Child by Rape: Somebody was conceived by rape.
  • Chocolate Baby: When a mother and father of the same race/ethnicity, or the same species, are gleefully awaiting the birth of their child, only for said child to be born racially mixed or with some attributes that could not have been biologically passed on from the father.
  • Chosen Conception Partner: Someone decides they want to have kids with someone else in particular, despite not being attracted to/in a relationship with them.
  • Conceive and Kill: A man and a woman have sex because she wants a baby, then when she's pregnant, she kills him.
  • Convenient Miscarriage: A woman has a miscarriage because having a baby would shake up the work too much or it's convenient for her because she doesn't want kids.
    • Advertisement:
  • Death by Childbirth: A woman dies in childbirth.
  • Deliver Us from Evil: A female villain accidentally gets pregnant.
  • Express Delivery: The opposite of the Longest Pregnancy Ever: a woman is pregnant for an unrealistically short time.
  • Extra Parent Conception: More than two people are involved in the conception of a baby.
  • Fake Pregnancy: A woman lies and pretends she's pregnant.
    • Pillow Pregnancy: A woman stuffs a pillow up her shirt so that people will think she's pregnant.
  • Fantasy Contraception: Made-up birth control.
  • Fetus Terrible: An evil fetus.
  • Forced Miscarriage: A pregnant person is forced to miscarry.
  • Give the Baby a Father: A woman is pregnant and a man who's not the father proposes to her to protect her honor in a society that frowns on sex/conception outside of marriage.
  • Good Girls Avoid Abortion: Virtuous women don't want to get an abortion, even if they don't want kids.
  • Hand on Womb: A woman rubs her belly and it serves as the way the audience finds out she's pregnant.
  • Heir Club for Men: The king wants specifically a son to be his heir. Sometimes, a daughter will do, but he'd prefer a son.
  • Hide Your Pregnancy: A Trivia page: when the actress is pregnant but the character isn't, measures are taken so her baby bump is not visible.
  • Homosexual Reproduction: Somebody is somehow conceived from two people of the same gender.
  • Honorable Marriage Proposal: A woman getting pregnant (for real or mistakenly) results in the father offering to marry her.
  • I Want Grandkids: One or two people want their adult daughter to get pregnant so they will be grandparents.
  • Imperiled in Pregnancy: A pregnant woman in danger.
  • Knitting Pregnancy Announcement: Somebody finds out a woman is pregnant because she's knitting baby clothes.
  • Lamaze Class: A sitcom character who's pregnant enters a class that teaches her exercise to do with dealing with labor pain.
  • Law of Inverse Fertility: If someone doesn't want to become pregnant, she likely will, but if she does, she likely won't.
  • Longest Pregnancy Ever: A pregnancy that lasts a ridiculously long time.
  • Magical Abortion: A fetus is terminated through magic.
  • Making Room for Baby: Moving to a bigger place to make space for a new baby.
  • Mama's Baby, Papa's Maybe: A man doesn't know that he's the dad of his partner's baby.
  • Mandatory Motherhood: A character doesn't want kids but society expects them to have them or face negative consequences.
  • Maternity Crisis: A pregnant character goes into labor at the worst possible time.
  • Medical Rape and Impregnate: A woman is forcibly impregnated artificially.
  • Miss Conception: Someone doesn't know where babies come from.
  • A Mistake Is Born: A couple have a biological child by accident and see the actual child as a "mistake".
  • Mistaken for Pregnant: Someone mistakenly believes a woman to be pregnant.
  • Mr. Seahorse (an exception to Always Female): A man gets pregnant.
  • Morning Sickness: Pregnant women throw up in the morning.
  • My Secret Pregnancy: A woman is pregnant and tries to keep her pregnancy a secret.
  • Mystical Pregnancy: Someone is conceived via magic, or something magical happens to a fetus.
  • New Child Left Behind: The woman hears that she is pregnant after the father had to go away.
  • No Periods, Period: No women are seen or mentioned to menstruate.
  • No Pregger Sex: A straight couple avoid having sex while the woman is pregnant.
  • Not Wanting Kids Is Weird: Someone states that not wanting to start a family is both unusual and negative.
  • Obvious Pregnancy: Someone has a baby bump right at the beginning of her pregnancy.
  • One Drink Will Kill the Baby: Pregnant ladies refuse to drink alcohol out of fear that the fetus will get sick/die.
  • One Night Stand Pregnancy: A short fling that leads to a pregnancy.
  • Panicky Expectant Father: When a woman is in labor, the dad freaks out.
  • Pregnancy Makes You Crazy: Pregnancy hormones make you act weirdly.
  • Pregnancy Scare: A woman worries that she might be pregnant but actually is not.
  • Pregnancy Test Plot: A character takes a pregnancy test.
  • Pregnant Badass: Someone temporarily becomes an Action Girl while heavily pregnant.
  • Pregnant Hostage: A pregnant woman is taken hostage.
  • Pregnant Reptile: A non-mammal bears live young.
  • Premature Birth Drama: A baby is born long before the due date and, as a result, has to be hospitalized since he or she can't survive otherwise.
  • Prenatal Possessions: An unborn baby somehow has material objects in the womb or egg with it.
  • Prom Baby: Going into labor at prom.
  • Shotgun Wedding: Someone forces another person to marry somebody else, usually, though not always, because the bride is pregnant.
  • Someone to Remember Him By: Someone finds out she's pregnant when the father (who's her lover) has recently died.
  • Stigmatic Pregnancy Euphemism: People don't want other people to find out that someone is pregnant because she's not married, so they make excuses.
  • Surprise Litter of Puppies: Someone's pet has had babies/gotten another animal of the same species pregnant, and they didn't know the female was pregnant.
  • Surprise Pregnancy: A woman doesn't realize she's pregnant until the baby is actually born.
  • Teen Pregnancy: A teenage girl gets pregnant.
  • Tragic Stillbirth: Stillbirth Played for Drama.
  • Traumatic C-Section: A woman's fetus is violently removed from the uterus.
  • Uterine Replicator: The baby is grown inside a machine instead of a woman.
  • Wacky Cravings: A pregnant woman wants all sorts of strange food and drink.
  • Wedding-Enhanced Fertility: A couple conceives very soon before or after their wedding.
  • What If the Baby Is Like Me?: A pregnant character and/or her partner worries that certain unpleasant or inconvenient genetic traits will be passed on to the baby.
  • Who's Your Daddy?: A woman is pregnant and doesn't know who the father is.
    • Law of Inverse Paternity: If the audience wants someone to be the father of a baby or fetus, he's likely not, and vice versa.
  • Womb Horror: Pregnancy or its related imagery, played for horror.
  • Written-In Infirmity: The actor is injured or sick, the writers have to compensate. This is Trivia and doesn't necessarily involve pregnancy.
  • You're Drinking Breast Milk: Someone accidentally drinks breast milk instead of regular milk.
  • Your Tomcat Is Pregnant: An animal is mistaken for the opposite sex (the most common being an animal that is thought to be male but then gets pregnant.)
