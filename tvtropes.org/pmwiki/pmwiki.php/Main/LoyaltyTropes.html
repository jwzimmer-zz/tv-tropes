Loyalty Tropes

Tropes about being loyal to someone or something and the reasons behind said loyalty.

Compare A Trusting Index. Contrast Betrayal Tropes.

Tropes:

Categories:
  • Face–Monster Turn
  • Ambiguous Allegiance: It isn't clear where a person's loyalties lie.
    • Advertisement:
  • Anti-Mutiny
  • Because You Were Nice to Me: Someone shows loyalty towards a person in gratitude of showing them kindness.
  • Black Shirt: A member of the team who secretly wishes for the enemy to win so they can side with them.
  • Blind Obedience
  • Blood Brothers
  • Blood Oath
  • Captain Patriotic: A superhero who serves the ideals of their nation.
  • The Caretaker
  • The Cavalry
  • The Champion
  • Cloudcuckoolander's Minder: An associate of a dumb or eccentric person whose purpose is to prevent their stupid or loony friend's antics from causing too much trouble.
  • Conflicting Loyalty
  • Declaration of Protection
  • Desperately Needs Orders: Someone who is so dependent on following the lead of others that they struggle to act on their own initiative.
  • The Dutiful Son
  • Failure Knight
  • Family of Choice
  • Financial Test of Friendship
  • Fire-Forged Friends: Allies who are brought together by difficult times.
    • Advertisement:
  • Happiness in Mind Control
  • Happiness in Slavery: A person who is content with being a slave.
  • Heroic Vow: The hero makes a promise that they intend to keep at all costs.
  • Heterosexual Life-Partners: Two friends of the same gender who are so close that it would be understandable to mistake them for lovers.
  • Honor Before Reason: Doing the honorable thing even when it wouldn't be the sensible thing to do.
  • I Am Spartacus: When the villain comes looking for the hero, everyone else claims to be the hero to protect their savior.
  • I Control My Minions Through... Loyalty variant
  • I Gave My Word: Honoring a promise you made.
  • I Owe You My Life
  • In Its Hour of Need
  • Instant Allegiance Artifact
  • The Jeeves
  • Just Following Orders: The subordinates try to excuse that they don't deserve punishment on the grounds that their actions were instructed and it was their job to do as they were told.
    • Advertisement:
  • Just Giving Orders: The one in charge tries to shift the blame for their actions onto the subordinates who followed their orders.
  • Lawful Evil
  • Lawful Good
  • Lawful Neutral
  • Lawful Stupid
  • Legacy of Service (in the case of servants)
  • Let the Bully Win: Staying on your superior's good side by letting them win whenever the two of you are in a competition.
  • Loyal Animal Companion
  • Loyal to the Position
  • Loyalty Mission
  • Morality Chain
  • Morality Chain Beyond the Grave
  • My Country, Right or Wrong
  • My Master, Right or Wrong
  • Nested Ownership: Servants have their own servants.
  • The Oath-Breaker
  • Ordered to Die: A person is instructed to kill themselves.
  • Overzealous Underling
  • Patriotic Fervor
  • Poisonous Friend
  • Professional Butt-Kisser
  • Psycho Supporter
  • Recruited from the Gutter
  • Releasing from the Promise
  • The Reliable One
  • The Renfield: A vampire's loyal human servant.
  • Sarcastic Devotee
  • Screw the Money, I Have Rules!: A character shows their integrity by refusing to accept a bribe.
  • Seduction-Proof Marriage: A married person refuses to cheat on their spouse no matter how much they may be tempted to do so.
  • Sergeant Rock
  • Servile Snarker: A servant who isn't above making remarks at their boss's expense.
  • Sour Supporter: An ally who helps out in spite of making no effort to hide their contempt for the people they're assisting.
  • Species Loyalty
  • Subordinate Excuse
  • Supporting the Monster Loved One
  • Sycophantic Servant
  • Thicker Than Water
  • Treachery Is a Special Kind of Evil
  • True Companions
  • Undying Loyalty
  • Villainous Friendship: Villains who are the best of friends.
  • Vow of Celibacy
  • Yes-Man: A subordinate who agrees with everything their boss says.
