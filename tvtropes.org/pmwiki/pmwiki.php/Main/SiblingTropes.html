Sibling Tropes

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Create New

These two are a model of sisterly love.
Advertisement:

About people who share parents. Many Sibling Tropes are also foils. Not to be confused with Sister Tropes.

Tropes:

Subcategory:
  • Twin Tropes
  • Aloof Big Brother: An older brother who is very impressive and treats his siblings coldly.
  • Annoying Younger Sibling: A younger sibling who's seen as annoying to their older one.
  • The Baby of the Bunch: The youngest and most childlike of The Team and is treated as such.
  • Big Brother Attraction: A character (usually female) is intensely attracted to their older sibling (usually their brother).
  • Big Brother Bully: A big brother/sister who is a Jerkass to their younger sibling.
  • Big Brother Instinct: A big brother/sister who is protective of their younger sibling.
  • Big Brother Mentor: A big brother/sister who mentors their younger sibling.
  • Big Brother Worship: A younger sibling who really, really admires their older brother or sister.
    • Advertisement:
  • Big Little Brother: A younger sibling who is taller than their older sibling.
  • Black Sheep: A character who doesn't fit in with their family.
  • Blood Brothers: Two people who have made a pact with each other.
  • A Boy, a Girl, and a Baby Family: A family where the children consist of one son, one daughter, and a baby of either gender.
  • Brother–Sister Incest: Romantic or sexual attraction between siblings
  • Brother–Sister Team: Siblings who work together.
  • Cain and Abel: Sibling rivalry turns into bitter hatred with murderous intent.
  • Cain and Abel and Seth: Two well known siblings have a third sibling no one really pays much thought to.
  • Childish Older Sibling: A character acts far less mature than their younger sibling.
  • Cool Big Sis: An older female is seen as cool to a younger person.
  • Dark Horse Sibling: A sibling that everyone thought was a loser ends up being the most successful.
    • Advertisement:
  • Disappointing Older Sibling: An older sibling who lets down his younger siblings.
  • Don't Split Us Up: Siblings are threatened with being separated from each other by divorce or foster care.
  • The Dutiful Son: The child who dedicates themselves to taking care of their family.
  • Family Relationship Switcheroo: A lie about a family relationship is revealed.
  • Father, I Want to Marry My Brother: A child expresses an innocent desire to marry a family member.
  • Flirty Stepsiblings: Teenage stepsiblings tend to have romantic subtext with each other.
  • Foolish Sibling, Responsible Sibling: A duo of siblings respectively consisting of a doofus and a mature person.
  • "Getting My Own Room" Plot: A child who has always shared a room with siblings finally gets their own room.
  • The Glorious War of Sisterly Rivalry: Two sisters, a smart one and a pretty one, often quarrel with each other.
  • Half-Sibling Angst: A character being a half-sibling to their siblings causes them grief.
  • The Hecate Sisters: A trio of female archetypes: an alluring young maiden, a matronly mother, and a wise crone.
  • Hypocritical Heartwarming: A Big Brother Bully gets enraged if someone else wrongs their sibling.
  • I Have Brothers: A female character who learned male oriented skills from her brothers.
  • Infant Sibling Jealousy: A character gets annoyed that their cute baby brother/sister is getting all their parents' attention.
  • Jacob and Esau: Each child favors one parent or vice versa.
  • Knight Templar Big Brother: A sibling who would protect/avenge his or her fellow siblings by any means necessary.
  • Like Brother and Sister: A close but platonic relationship between an unrelated male and female that is similar to that of siblings.
  • Little Big Brother: An older sibling who is shorter than their younger sibling.
  • Little Sister Heroine: A character's little sister serves as a Love Interest option.
  • Long-Lost Relative: A character's relative returns after having vanished a long time ago.
  • Massive Numbered Siblings: A character has five or more siblings.
  • Middle Child Syndrome: The middle child in a family is The Unfavorite.
  • Monster Brother, Cutie Sister: A couple of siblings where the older brother is menacing and mean, and the younger sister is cute and nice.
  • My Sibling Will Live Through Me: A character impersonates their deceased sibling.
  • My Sister Is Off-Limits!: A brother who forbids anyone from dating his sister.
  • Not Blood Siblings: An incestuous relationship is justified by the couple not being related by blood (e.g. being stepsiblings or adopted siblings).
  • Oblivious Younger Sibling: A younger sibling of a character who is "blind" to their actions.
  • Onee-sama: A classy highly respected older sister.
  • Only Child Syndrome: A work of fiction where none of the characters have any siblings.
  • Outnumbered Sibling: A character is the only son or daughter in a family with three or more children.
  • Parental Favoritism: A child is given preference over their siblings.
  • Posthumous Sibling: When a character is born after their older sibling has already died.
  • Practically Different Generations: Siblings with a huge age gap.
  • Princeling Rivalry: A younger sibling will plot to overthrow, kill, or banish their older sibling in order to seize power for themselves.
  • Promotion to Parent: The oldest child takes it upon themselves to parent their younger siblings.
  • Relative Error: Siblings are mistaken for lovers by someone who doesn't know them.
  • Settle for Sibling: A character marries the sibling of their unrequited Love Interest.
  • Shared Family Quirks: Two otherwise different siblings share the same quirk.
  • Sibling Murder: A sibling kills their sibling.
  • Sibling Rivalry: Siblings who don't get along well.
  • Siblings in Crime: Siblings who commit crime together.
  • Siblings Wanted: A child asks their parents for a sibling.
  • Sibling Switch Squick: Characters who are relatives in a story are made lovers in an adaptation.
  • Sibling Team: Siblings who work together side by side.
  • Sibling Triangle: Two siblings fall in love with the same person.
  • Sibling Yin-Yang: Siblings who have opposite traits to each other.
  • Siblings Share the Throne: Siblings rule a kingdom together with equal authority.
  • Spartan Sibling: A character who has absolutely no regard for their younger sibling's well being.
  • Stronger Sibling: A strong villainous character has an even stronger sibling.
  • Successful Sibling Syndrome: Someone lives in the shadow of their more successful sibling.
  • This Is My Side: Two siblings who are mad at each other draw a line to divide each other's side of the room and their belongings.
  • Two Siblings In One: A character absorbs the soul or life energy of their deceased sibling.
  • The Unfavorite: A character whose family prefers their sibling over them.
  • Unrelated Brothers: Unrelated professional wrestlers who claim to be relatives.
  • White Sheep: A non-evil member of an evil family.
  • You Never Did That for Me: A child complains to their parents that they are treating a sibling more favorably.
  • Youngest Child Wins: The youngest sibling has the best luck in life.
