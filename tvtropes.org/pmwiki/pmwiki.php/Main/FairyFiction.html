Fairy Fiction

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Main
  • Laconic
  • Create New

This index is for works that focus on fairies, pixies, and sprites. A work just having a fairy does not qualify. The fairy must be one of the main characters, the main threat, or otherwise central to the plot. Such works will invariably include one or more Fairy Tropes. Works that feature the Land of Faerie as a significant part of the setting also qualify.
Advertisement:

Also see the related indexes Dragon Works, Genie Works, Mermaid Media, and Unicorn Works. Not to be confused with Fairy Tale, which, despite the name, does not always feature fairies.

Works:

    open/close all folders 

    Anime and Manga 
  • Cinderella Monogatari: Paulette, the Fairy Godmother, subtly watches the eponymous character and tries to influence events to fix Cinderella's life without her noticing.
  • A Little Snow Fairy Sugar
  • Pokémon: As in the games, there are both Fairy types and Trainers who focus on them.

    Comic Books 
  • The Dresden Files: Faeries are a major political force.

    Films — Animation 
  • Barbie Fairytopia
    • Barbie Fairytopia: Mermaidia
    • Barbie Fairytopia: Magic of the Rainbow
    • Barbie: Mariposa: The eponymous Mariposa is a butterfly fairy.
    • Barbie: Mariposa and the Fairy Princess
  • Barbie: A Fashion Fairytale
    • Barbie: A Fairy Secret
  • Cinderella: A Fairy Godmother is one of the major characters.
    • Cinderella II: Dreams Come True: A Direct-to-Video sequel to the above, the Fairy Godmother returns as an important character.
    • Cinderella III: A Twist in Time: The Fairy Godmother has her magic wand stolen by Lady Tremaine.
  • Disney Fairies:
    • Tinker Bell (2008)
    • Tinker Bell and the Lost Treasure (2009)
    • Tinker Bell and the Great Fairy Rescue (2010)
    • Pixie Hollow Games (2011)
    • Tinker Bell and the Secret of the Wings (2012)
    • Tinker Bell and the Pirate Fairy (2014)
    • Tinker Bell and the Legend of the NeverBeast (2015)
  • Faeries (1999)
  • FernGully: The Last Rainforest: Crysta, one of the two main characters, and many of her family and friends are fairies.
    • FernGully 2: The Magical Rescue: A Direct-to-Video sequel to the above, Crysta and the other fairies return as major characters.
  • Peter Pan: One of the main characters is a female fairy named Tinker Bell. Also, the eponymous protagonist can fly in part due to pixie dust.
    • Return To Never Land: A Direct-to-Video sequel to the above, the fairy Tinker Bell returns as a major character.
  • Rise of the Guardians: As in the book it's based on, one of the main characters is the Tooth Fairy.
  • Sleeping Beauty: The main villain (Maleficent) and three of the primary characters (Flora, Fauna, and Merryweather) are fairies.
  • Strange Magic: The heroine is a Fairy Princess who resides in an entire kingdom of fairies.
Advertisement:

    Films — Live-Action 
  • Cinderella (1947): A Fairy Godmother is an important character.
  • Cinderella (2015): As in the original animated film above, Cinderella has a Fairy Godmother.
  • Maleficent: A live-action Perspective Flip of the above-mentioned Sleeping Beauty, Maleficent and the three good fairies return as important characters.
  • Pan's Labyrinth: A dark, modern-day fairy tale with fairies as major players.

    Franchises 
  • Cinderella: A Fairy Godmother is one of the major characters across the franchise.
  • Disney Fairies
  • The Dresden Files: Faeries are a major political force.
  • Pokémon: Fairy is both an Egg Group and one of the eighteen elemental types. There are also a few Fairy-type specialists.

    Literature 
  • 13 Treasures: Main character Tanya can see and hear fairies and other magical creatures.
  • Artemis Fowl: A Teen Genius Magnificent Bastard criminal mastermind vs. fairies with laser guns.
  • Bones of Faerie
  • Cinderella: The Fairy Godmother is a main character.
  • A Court of Thorns and Roses: Young Adult fantasy romance series about a girl who falls in love with a fae and becomes caught up in a war with a Sorcerous Overlord.
  • Crescent City: The main character is half-fae.
  • The Dresden Files: Faeries are a major political force, and more than one plot of the novels is due to the actions of faeries, when it's not the vampires or human mages.
    • Summer Knight: The fourth book in The Dresden Files series, Summer Knight heavily features and focuses on fae.
    • Proven Guilty: The eighth book, it is also fae-dominant.
    • Cold Days: The fourteenth book, there's almost more fae characters in the story than humans.
  • The Faerie Path
  • Faerie Wars
  • Faeries of Dreamdark
  • Flower Fairies
  • The Guardians of Childhood: One of the main characters is the Tooth Fairy.
  • The Gypsies in the Wood: A novella in the Diogenes Club series by Kim Newman, The Fair Folk are the main antagonists.
    • Angel Down, Sussex: Another story in the Diogenes Club series, Alien Fair Folk are featured prominently.
  • How to Ditch Your Fairy
  • I Was a Teenaged Fairy
  • The Iron King: The main character's best friend and late father are among The Fair Folk.
  • Lords and Ladies: The fourteenth Discworld novel, this story revolves around The Fair Folk invading the kingdom of Lancre when the barrier between their worlds wears thin.
    • The Shepherd's Crown: The Fair Folk from Lords and Ladies return.
  • Modern Faerie Tales
    • The Darkest Part of the Forest: A standalone story set in the Modern Faerie Tales universe.
    • The Folk of the Air: A YA trilogy set in the Modern Faerie Tales universe, a fairy kills the two main characters' parents.
  • Land of Oz: Oz is referred to as a fairyland, and is ruled by the fairy Princess Ozma, and long ago was enchanted by the fairy Queen Lurline. Fairies in the series are human-sized and wingless, but very beautiful and possess magical abilities.
  • October Daye: Main character Toby Daye is a changeling, daughter of a faerie creature of the Summerlands and a human.
  • Peter Pan: One of the major characters is a fairy named Tinker Bell.
  • The Spiderwick Chronicles: Three kids stumble upon a Great Big Book of Everything containing knowledge about the hidden world of faeries and have to keep it out of the hands of the Big Bad.
  • Under the Pendulum Sun: During the Victorian era, Catherine Helstone travels into the heart of the newly discovered lands of The Fair Folk in search of her brother Laon, a missionary who disappeared while trying to convert the fae to Christianity.
  • Violet Wings: Main character Zaria and the main villain are fairies.

    Live-Action TV 
  • Fate The Winx Saga: A Darker and Edgier Live-Action Adaptation of the below-mentioned Winx Club.

    Tabletop Games 
  • Changeling: The Lost: RPG where crazy abuse-survivor fairies go crazier.
  • The Dresden Files: Faeries are a major political force.
  • The Small Folk: The titular Small Folk are modern-day descendants of The Fair Folk.
Advertisement:

    Theatre 
  • Cinderella (Rodgers and Hammerstein): The Fairy Godmother from the original tale returns as a major character.
  • A Midsummer Night's Dream: A comedy by William Shakespeare about a Love Dodecahedron that gets out of control thanks to the meddling of fairies with a Love Potion.

    Video Games 
  • Atelier Lina The Alchemist Of Strahl: Lina being Mis-blamed by fairies for burning their home / tree, and having to help them, is the basics of the plot of the game.
  • Chantelise: Chante, one of the two main characters, is cursed by a witch and turned into a fairy.
  • Dragalia Lost: Notte is the Fairy Companion to the main cast who provides items every day and provides moral support for raid bosses.
  • Eternal Senia: "Little Fairy," a Fairy Companion, is a major character.
    • Eternal Senia: Hydrangea After The Rain: "Little Fairy" from the above returns as an important character. There are also tiny fairies and roughly human-sized fairy queens.
  • Faery: Legends of Avalon
  • Fairy Bloom
    • Fairy Bloom Freesia
  • Fairy Fencer F
  • The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time: Navi, a literal Fairy Companion, is gifted to Link by the Great Deku Tree (the guardian of the forest).
  • Recettear: An Item Shop's Tale: Tear, one of the major characters, is a fairy.
  • ZanZarah: The Hidden Portal: The game revolves around catching, collecting, and training Fairy Companions.

    Webcomics 
  • Dan and Mab's Furry Adventures: Fae are one of the major races, with exceptional shapeshifting powers, strong magic, a bizarre moral code, and an even more bizarre realm.
  • Fairy Dust

    Websites 
  • Neopets: The faeries are pretty central to the game.

    Western Animation 
  • Butterbean's Cafe: The five main characters and main villain are fairies.
  • The Fairly OddParents: A 10-year-old has fairy godparents that grant his crazy wishes. Many of the other major or supporting characters are also fairies.
  • Winx Club: The show centers on the titular Winx Club, a group of fairy warriors attending the Alfea College for Fairies.
    • Pop Pixie: A Spin-Off of the above, pixies are the main characters.
    • World of Winx: Another short-lived Spin-Off of Winx Club.
