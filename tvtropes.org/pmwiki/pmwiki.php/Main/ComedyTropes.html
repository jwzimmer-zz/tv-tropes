    Subcategories and Trope Indexes 
Main topics
  • Comedy: The whole genre itself.
  • Funny Moments: Humorous moments in various works of media.
  • Played for Laughs: A trope is used for humor instead of being treated seriously.
  • Rule of Funny: Impossible things that are tolerated because they are funny.

Sub-genres/types of comedy

  • Armed Farces: Military humor and war comedy.
  • Black Comedy: Humor derived from taboo or offensive subjects.
  • Bromantic Comedy: Story about male friendships that uses Romantic Comedy tropes.
  • Comedy Series: Comedy on television.
    • Sitcom: Situational comedy, driven by scripted plot and characters.
    • Sketch Comedy: A comedy show that consists of multiple unrelated skits put together in one episode.
  • Cringe Comedy: Humor derived from embarrassment and humiliation.
  • Dramedy: The hybrid genre of comedy and drama.
  • Fantastic Comedy: Comedic takes on fantasy and science fiction.
  • Horror Comedy: The hybrid genre of comedy and horror.
  • Romantic Comedy: The hybrid genre of comedy and romance.
  • Satire/Parody/Pastiche: Defining the similarities and differences between the three.
    • Parody: Comedy that makes fun of another work of media.
    • Satire: Comedy that makes fun of contemporary culture, politics, or society.
  • Rant Comedy: Humorously trash-talking about something that really annoys you.
  • Rapid-Fire Comedy
  • Recorded and Stand-Up Comedy: The art of comedians sharing jokes for a live audience.
  • Screwball Comedy
  • Self-Referential Humor
  • Slapstick: Humor derived from people getting physically injured.
  • Surreal Humor: Comedy about absurd and bizarre things that make little sense.
  • Vulgar Humor: Jokes about gross things, like bodily functions and sexuality.
    • Toilet Humour: Jokes about the excretion of waste from the body.

Other trope categories

  • Acceptable Targets: People and subjects that are common targets for jokes and mockery.
  • Accidental Index: Things that don't happen the way you intended can be funny.
  • Bathos
  • Chewing the Scenery: Exaggerated, over-the-top behavior can often be funny.
  • Comedic Relief Characters: Stock Characters who are inherently funny.
  • Dude, Not Funny!: If you cross the line too far with humor, expect some people to be offended instead of amused.
  • Innuendo Tropes: Jokes alluding to sex or other risqué behavior.
  • Memetic Mutation: Humorous cultural references and other jokes that become popular enough to be widely shared and spread, especially over the Internet.
  • Mistaken for Index: When people make incorrect guesses or wrong assumptions about someone or something else, it can lead to a very humorously awkward situation.
  • Narm: Something that tries (and fails) to take itself seriously turns into unintended comedy instead.
  • Nothing Is Funnier
  • Oh Great, a Snark Index: Jokes derived from sarcasm and sardonicism.
  • Parody Tropes: Tropes about anything that can be parodied.
  • Room Shuffle
  • Sitcom Tropes: Tropes commonly found in sitcoms, obviously enough.
  • Stock Jokes: Common gags you've seen many times before.
    • Stock Parodies: Common spoofs of specific works.
      • Stock Parody Jokes: Specific gags that are often used when spoofing something.
  • Stupidity Tropes: People making fools of themselves are usually funny.
  • Subverted Innocence: A joke about something that's often thought of as being harmless and inoffensive, turning out to be anything but.
  • This Index Is a Joke
