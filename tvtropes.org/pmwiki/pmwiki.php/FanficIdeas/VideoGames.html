While Heather became a Raposa, Mike still retained his humanity. So after Wilfre is defeated, the Hero and the Raposa help Heather and Mike return to their world without destroying theirs, making the Bittersweet Ending a little happier.

Dept. Heaven

  • Riviera: The Promised Land
    • A retelling of the game, albeit with Canon Ein's personality undergoing Adaptational Nice Guy treatment (thus allowing Ein to do the same kind actions from the game) without affecting the ending, allowing Ein to use the wish-granting potion on Rose. While Ein treats all the girls in his party kindly (only being Innocently Insensitive at worst) and all the girls get a chance in the spotlight, Ein and Rose's relationship gets the most focus when they're reunited, with flashbacks showing how they first became partners. Rose's ending gets some foreshadowing when the reader hears some of her private thoughts about how she falls in love with her master, but keeps them to herself because she's a cat, causing her to wish to subconsciously wish to become a cute girl so she can be alongside him. Once said wish is granted, she can finally work to win Ein's heart, eventually succeeding. Ein and Rose also stay in Riviera and eventually get married, but not without writing down their adventures together in their history book.

EXA_PICO

  • Ciel nosurge and Ar nosurge: Ode to an Unborn Star
    • In an idea inspired by My Next Life as a Villainess: All Routes Lead to Doom!, at the beginning of Ciel Nosurge while Ion is leaving for the trials, Zillillium Rimonite trips and hits her head - and memories of her past life come flooding in. She was an ordinary Earth-born girl whose favorite game series happened to be Surge Concerto - and she's doomed to die a villain?! With these new memories in place, Zill decides to fight against fate and do better than her character in the game did, including trying to stop Revelt's plans and helping Ion return to her home world. The thing is, Zill's past incarnation happened to have a huge crush on Ion, and it's reemerged quite strongly. As Zill tries to fix the world, she and Ion might actually be falling for each other...

Horizon Zero Dawn

  • An AU where a discovered infant Aloy is revered as a gift from All-Mother by the Nora like Teersa believed instead of a "motherless" outcast.
  • An AU where Aloy (banished beyond the Sacred Land at birth for being "motherless" instead of just being Outcast), happens upon Sun-King Avad's diplomatic party being attacked by the Eclipse. She saves them and is mortally wounded in the skirmish. Avad and Erend take her back to the capital with them where they nurse her back to health. They offer her a home at the palace for her heroic deed and Ersa offers her a spot in the royal vanguard where she rises through the ranks after foiling Dervahl's terrorist attempts. Aloy/Avad Ship Fic.
  • Instead of being found in the mountain by the Nora, an infant Aloy is found and raised as Shadow Carja.
  • A young Aloy happens upon a strange sight: a humanoid machine that can speak like a human. It has no real memory of its origins and it slowly learns about how humans act by observing Aloy, who inturn sees it as her only friend. As the years go by, the machine becomes more and more intelligent and starts developing real emotions beyond curiosity. Eventually it learns that it was an ordinary subroutine AI (kind of like a sufficiently advanced Siri or Alexa) that eventually gained self awareness over the years and built a body by hacking into an old, offline Cauldron, having come to the conclusion that it could accomlish more from the perspective of a free-roaming entity (i.e. like a human). The downloading process was wracked with errors and the copied consciousness was full of corrupt data, hence why it had amnesia by the time it wandered into Nora territory.
    • It can be named GALATEA, or "GAL" for short (since the other half of its name was lost in the download), named after the artificial woman of Classical Mythology.
    • It could also be CYAN (from The Frozen Wilds DLC) who copied her consciousness into a robot while HEPHAESTUS had her enslaved to make machines as a last ditch effort to escape him.
  • Aloy takes Sun-King Avad up on his offer and becomes his queen, creating all sorts of political intrigue not just for the Carja, but for the Nora and the other tribes.
  • Instead of being a bully to Aloy in their youth, Bast secretly becomes her friend and eventual secret-lovers.
  • Instead of finding her corpse at the end of the game, Aloy instead finds a still alive Elisabet Sobek in an experiment cryo-chamber, Elisabeth having trekked there after her Heroic Sacrifice. Elisabet is portrayed as a Jerk with a Heart of Gold (think Sylens, but with a conscience based around empathy instead of pragmatism) who is trying to process everything that has happened (the fact that she has a daughter, that GAIA might be dead, how Project: Zero Dawn was all-but destroyed by the whims of the man she grew to hate and now humanity has regressed to a feudal-tribal era, etc) by taking the Straw Vulcan route and trying to deconstruct everything into statistics. While she finds the superstitious people disheartening and a little annoying, she grows fond of Aloy for using her brain and starts to teach her more and more about robotics and other things and soon they develop a close friendship, if not a shadow of a mother-daughter relationship.

inFAMOUS

  • Cole survives the RFI. When the DUP is formed, Cole becomes its #1 target and is retroactively blamed for Empire City's destruction in a government campaign to absolve themselves of blame, Cole evading them for years as he, Zeke and the Anti-D.U.P. Movement find and protect conduits before Augustine can get her hands on them. One mission has Cole stop a cargo van carrying Fetch, Eugene and Henry, Henry runs off due to his criminal history and has his run-in with Delsin, activating his powers. Cole takes Delsin under his wing to help him learn how to control his powers (even letting him copy his electricity powers) and helps him find Augustine in a mission to save the Akomish, Cole and Zeke seeing an opportunity to destroy Curden Cay with his help.
  • Cole activates the RFI and the split-second he activates it, he manifests Kessler's time-travel power and goes back to before the Ray Sphere explosion gave him his powers.
  • An AU where the RFI killed the Beast but all of the other conduits survived. The DUP was formed not as an anti-conduit military force led by Augustine, but as a Heroes "R" Us government organization led by Lucy Kuo who are charged with acclimating society to conduit's presence and to help them. When Henry evades the DUP (not for being a conduit, but for using his powers for illegal activities like robbery and murder), they discover Delsin and offer to train him in using his powers.
    • Instead of being used as Augustine's DUP Police State, Seattle is being used as the Headquarters of Kuo's DUP. With the government's budget, all buildings used were legally purchased, all surveillance used is non-sinister in nature, apartment buildings are desegregated and being used by both regular humans and conduits and lots of pro-conduit activism (and a lot of Muggle Power anti-activism) is being ran there. It's basically like a college town, but for the DUP, trainees and new conduits instead of universities and college students.
    • Delsin acts as the Class Clown among the DUP's conduits. He tags Seattle buildings with anti-establishment graffiti (sort of a cross between Good!Delsin's harmless art and Evil!Delsin's Anti-DUP art, with plenty of them mocking Kuo), plays pranks on the DUP's personnel, encourages his fellow students to act out a little, but he has a big heart none-the-less. Seeing a lot of potential in Delsin's unique abilities and natural affinity in using them, she calls Cole to help him. He is also issued a pair of gloves so that he doesn't start copying everyone's powers left and right, having been talked into wearing them as a show of trust.
    • Fetch and her brother ran away because their parents are a part of a Muggle Power group and the DUP offer her a place in their ranks when she wanted to atone for accidentally killing her brother on a drug-fueled haze. She now acts as a field agent for the DUP, part-time artist (with permission from the DUP and the city of Seattle to use her powers for street-art) and an anti-drug activist.
    • Eugene Sim's mother was a politician who voted against many of the more humane pro-conduit laws and regulations that Lucy Kuo had bankrolled because of a Fantastic Racism against them. When he manifested powers, she disowned him. Having nowhere else to go, Eugene allowed the DUP to take him into their program so that they could help him control his powers and give him shelter. With his training under the DUP (and near limitless budget for his gaming addiction), he became one of the DUP's biggest trump cards.
    • Cole acts as a semi-retired agent of the DUP who Lucy will occasionally call in when there is a problem that requires his experience and immense threshold of power. He has long-since buried the hatchet for Lucy's Face–Heel Turn with the Beast and various DUP employees and attendees claim that they are banging in secret.
    • Zeke is now a high-ranking member of the DUP and will often act as the "folksy, southern wisdom" voice when Lucy's bureaucratic mind needs a new angle. He occasionally helps teach new conduits how to control their powers, is like A Father to His Men and is a public-speaker on behalf of the DUP to help with human-conduit relations, often sent to help win over any Muggle Power humans still capable of seeing reason.
    • Nix never forgave Lucy for siding with the Beast and with Bertrand gone, she had retreated deep into the Bayou where Lucy, Cole and Zeke barely ever see or hear from her. While having never agreed to it, Cole had all the paperwork filled out to make her an honorary member of the DUP anyway considering their history together. When Delsin has trouble figuring out his new smoke-powers and none of his fire-power teachers are helping, Cole decides to take Delsin to Nix's abode, convinced that their complimentary abilities and shared rebellious spirits will provide good results. She begrudgingly takes the offer as a favor to Cole "for old time's sake" and her methods of teaching Delsin involve setting fire to a chunk of the swamp and making him smother the flames with his smoke, throwing him off of the boat into gator-infested waters and just straight up beating him up. It works, of course.
    • Brooke Augustine is a powerful member of the DUP who agreed to join after her powers first manifested when the Beast destroyed her city. She is later revealed to be the secret-leader in a splinter-group of the DUP who sees the DUP's agenda of co-habitation to be a pipe-dream and plans on overthrowing Kuo and turning the DUP into its canon-counterpart. She finds various prototype Power Transfer Devices cooked up by the First Sons (Lucy having squashed any offers to use it on their human agents due to what happened to Vermaak 88) and uses it to imprint her powers onto the human members of her splinter-cell.
    • Celia Penderghast was captured by Augustine and then brought into the DUP's orphanage program where they helped her control her powers. When she came of age, she joined the DUP as a member. She is secretly a member of Augustine's splinter-group and her second-in-command.
    • Sasha was found by the DUP and detained. With all of her fighting spirit gone after years of therapy enforced onto her by the DUP, she is now just a quiet detainee living out her life with nowhere to go. She is offered a position onto the splinter-cell by Augustine - who recognizes her immense power since she was exposed to a ray sphere and has had her powers for far longer - but she declines. When Augustine tries to enact her mass insurrection, she uses her tar-powers for the first time in years to give Kuo and the rest of her DUP a leg-up by thinning the splinter-group's numbers.
    • Lucy Kuo keeps the various files that the First Sons kept on potential historical instances of activated conduits and Bloody Mary's name is on that list as a Mythology Gag.
    • A Stan Lee cameo is made when an In-Universe counterpart works as a comic book author hired by the DUP to help drip-feed people to the idea of conduits via pop culture.
    • The crater that was once Empire City is currently being used as America's largest national park and is being used as a monument to those who had lost their lives in the Beast's rampage.
    • Kuo explains to Delsin that while the RFI killed the Beast and nullified the Ray Field Radiation killing everyone, the radiation caused an originally harmless pathogen to mutate into an unstoppable and invisible pandemic. While regular humans only display cold-like symptoms before their immune systems flush the virus out, it activates the conduit gene in anyone who has it, explaining why conduits are activating left and right without a Ray Sphere.
  • When Cole, Nix, Kuo and the Freedom Fighters storm Fort St. Phillipe at night instead of the day, they accidentally release Bloody Mary in the ensuing battle. Now they have the Militia, Bertrand, the Beast and a vampire queen with a vendetta against mankind to deal with.

Kingdom Hearts

  • What if Riku didn't accept the Darkness in the first game and lose his keyblade to Sora?
  • Destiny Island isn't just an insignificant little world. It is a tropical world that was one of the first in a new campaign for Radiant Garden to colonize other worlds with the intent of furthering their research. The Radiant Garden colonizers brought with them advanced technology and magic that the natives of this world could use and became members of this budding new trans-worldly Empire.
    • Kairi and her family were colonizers that moved to the Islands, Kairi becoming friends with Sora and Riku this way.
    • Instead of Terra-Xehanort and his cronies beginning their Heartless experiments in Hollow Bastion, they did so in their scientific facilities in one of the islands, experimenting on some of the inhabitants. It was all a part of an attempt to reveal the keyhole hidden there and uncover its secrets.
  • With all of the chaos the Heartless has been causing, Kingdom hearts itself takes on physical form to take care of the problem itself... and he looks like Walt Disney.
  • Sora and the gang find Maleficent trying to steal a Magic Mirror that can invert anything from deep within Diney Castle's deepest locked vault, planning on using it to gain an edge. What follows is the mirror accidentally gets shattered and it inverts the light and darkness within Sora and Maleficent. What follows is Sora being trapped in his Anti-Form and going on a rampage, while Maleficent becomes the light-fairy Beneficent.
  • Master Xehanort intends on turning Maleficent into one of his Thirteen Seekers and attempts to take her body. It goes horribly wrong, the resulting amalgamation becoming an embodiment of hatred and greed, a heartless that intends to devour the universe. It becomes so big of a threat, the Seven Lights and the Thirteen Seekers decide to put their war on hold to stop it together.
  • Maleficent sees the raw power and bottomless darkness that is Sephiroth and tries recruiting him.
  • There is a group of Keyblade wielders who have managed to master the Darkness, removing the corrupting influence and turning it into The Sacred Darkness, allowing it to truly coexist with the Light. These benevolent Darkness-using Keyblade wielders all had their own journey to go through before meeting Sora and his friends, becoming allies with them as they show them how to rid themselves of the corrupting influence.
    • While they intend to fight alongside the Warriors of Light against the Heartless and the damage Xehanort had caused, their primary goal is to induct one of them into their Order. While they are considering Riku and Terra to join them - both of them having a deep enough understanding of the Darkness to have been immunized from its corruption - they add Sora to that list when he takes on his Anti-Form when they are all cornered.
    • Some of these Darkness-using Keyblade wielders each have unique personality that allowed them to form friendships with a different Warrior of Light. Some of these include:
      • A shy girl, who is nevertheless very polite and well-spoken, getting along along with Kairi.
      • A Tsundere who speaks abrasively, but still cares for her friends. Becomes Vitriolic Best Buds with Lea.
      • A girl with a Sugar-and-Ice Personality, who is also a skilled strategist, becoming a friend to Sora, Donald, and Goofy.
      • A Yamato Nadeshiko, who becomes good friends with Kairi, Naminé, and Xion.
      • A Team Mom, often paired with the Team Dad, forming a kinship with Terra and Aqua.
      • A Genki Girl who is also good at understanding people who also gets along with Kairi.
      • A Bruiser with a Soft Center
      • A Gentle Giant paired with the Token Mini-Moe.
      • A hotheaded young man with a competitive streak, forming a friendly rivalry with Sora.
  • The friendlier Heartless, such as the White Truffles, Blue Rhapsody, and the Flan Seven, have a reason for their lack of hostility: they were originally children with very little darkness in their hearts, and their innocence allowed them to retain their kindness and love of games. These Heartless would eventually become allies to the heroes.
  • When Destiny Islands is being invaded by the Heartless, Kari is the one who receives the keyblade and Sora's heart is taken.

  • One day, a Mass Super-Empowering Event suddenly happened and everyone in Arcadia Bay suddenly manifests superpowers. The US Government has the entire town quarantined, afraid of what might happen if it goes public.
  • A series of Lime drabbles where Max is a naturist and an exhibitionist, preferring to strip when she is alone (or when she thinks she's alone) and feeling more comfortable in her own skin when she has nothing on. Chloe finds out about this and encourages her to do it more, eventually stripping for her as a model in her photographs. Eventually they find their favorite spot to hang out is at the beach, attendance there slowing down after summer and leaving them to their own devices and making it their own unofficial nudist beach.
  • Chloe and Rachel run away together on their way to LA. Along the way, they end up at a nudist colony where they find Max, her parents having found a job there at some point after they moved to Seattle and becoming permanent residents.
    • Max works at the nudist colony on the weekends.
  • Instead of Max having time powers, Max discovers that Chloe is a werewolf, having been turned by her girlfriend Rachel and they were originally going to runoff together to form a pack before Rachel disappeared. Then it is revealed that Jefferson was a Serial Killer, but changed his M.O. from drugging and kidnapping "innocent" girls to capturing and killing werwolves after discovering that they were real like the Serial Killer from Nightbreed.
  • Chloe finds Max at Blackwell after years without contact, only to find that Max is more introverted and has panic attacks when touched, clear signs that something awful happened to her friend. She finds out later that Max was ganged up and raped by a group of jocks at her old school which has left her with permanent depression and suicidal thoughts. Having realized that her own problems pale in comparison to her best friend's, Chloe decides to help bring her friend out of her shell, and maybe fall in love in the process. (Max/Chloe)
  • After sacrificing Chloe to save Arcadia Bay, Max is left in a depressed state. After she prevents Kate's suicide, Kate sees that her savior is going through something too and wants to help. After she is discharged from the hospital, she hangs with Max and they wind up developing something together. (Max/Kate)
  • A Lemon drabbles series where Max is working on a portrait series for her Photograph portfolio featuring nudes and people mid-strip. Despite Max being a mousey and shy person, she certainly isn't unattractive and she makes people feel safe. When she asks them to strip, things get hot and bothered. Such models she "works" with include:
    • Chloe

Live A Live

  • Oersted has a female Childhood Friend in the form of an Original Character. She admired Oersted's courage and kindness growing up and secretly fell in love in love with him, which Oersted is unaware of. She becomes a female Magic Knight as she joins Oersted in becoming a knight, albeit not without struggling at first and receiving some of Oersted's help. While she is saddened to see Oersted loves Alicia, she is willing to accept if it means Oersted can be happy. When Oersted is framed as the Demon King, she is among those who still believes in her friend and tries to help clear Oersted's name, only to be killed, which is a big step towards Oersted's Sanity Slippage and Start of Darkness. It's later revealed that she became a Messiahic Archetype who also gained the power to place incarnations of herself throughout time and space to help the heroes of different periods stop Odio's incarnations, while secretly guiding them in their battle against the Demon King, all in order to save Oersted from his dark path. She is the one who comforts Oersted in their final moments together before they pass on peacefully, with Oersted finally realizing her love for him and reciprocating.

Nippon Ichi

    • What if other characters throughout the franchise had undergone a Personality Swap just like Flonne and Etna did in the Makai Senki Disgaea? Some examples could include Laharl & Flonne, Laharl & Sicily, Etna & Sicily, Adell & Rozalin, Taro & Hanako, Mao & Almaz, Almaz & Sapphire, Mao & Raspberyl, etc.
    • What if Sicily became a Fallen Angel just like Flonne?

    • What if both Almaz and Sapphire were turned into demons?
  • The Guided Fate Paradox
    • A Continuation of Renya and Liliel's life on Earth following the ending of the game, with Liliel learning to live life as a human.
  • The Witch and the Hundred Knight
    • A retelling of the first game, with some minor changes (Metallia's more villainous qualities are toned down enough to make her still a Jerkass Pre-Character Development, but not enough to cross the Moral Event Horizon for some fans; The Hundred Knight slowly gaining sapience and eventually the ability to talk; Belda the Scum Witch doesn't receive any sympathetic treatment from the narrativenote Her backstory remains the same, but it won't elicit any sympathy at all. If anything, both her backstory and her behavior towards other people will just call into attention just how horrible and unlikable she is. or from the characters, truly becoming a Hate Sink while also being the one to remain dead in Sabrina's place; Visco is shown fighting and winning some battles, especially when she awakens her hidden powers as a witch, and more of her backstory will be shown in flashbacks. Visco will also receive a loyal Childhood Friend in the form of an Original Character). The retelling uses First-Person Perspective and switches viewpoints between Metallia, Visco, the Hundred Knight, and the OC.
    • The sequel will also have a retelling with some minor changes (Amalie will become more capable and intelligent, and receive Character Development to make her a more relatable character. She also becomes a witch while retaining her personality due to artificially being made one, finally allowing her to protect her little sister more efficiently; The Hundred Knight, just like in the previous game, gains sapience and the ability to talk; Amalie and Milm's backstory will also be shown in some flashbacks; Amalie will also have an Original Character as a Childhood Friend who tries to help her and her sister in their journey) The story, just like its predecessor, is told in First-Person Perspective, this time from the point-of-views of Amalie, the Hundred Knight, Milm, Chelka, and the OC.
  • Soul Nomad & the World Eaters
    • Taking place sometime after the Normal Ending, Revya and their companions end up in the Demon Path timeline, where Revya must face their greatest enemy... a version of themselves as the Devourlord.
  • The Liar Princess and the Blind Prince
    • Sometime after the events of the game, something has transformed the Prince into a wolf and his memories have been rewritten to make him think he was always a wolf, while everyone in his kingdom is made to forget his existence. With the Witch unable to undo this transformation, she instructs the wolves to find the cause of the transformation somewhere in another forest far away their location. But as they travel, the Prince starts to have second thoughts about becoming human again, finding that he enjoys his life as a wolf far more than as a human and wanting to stay by the Wolf's side instead. Can the Wolf convince the Prince to regain his humanity before he fully embraces his transformation, or will he convince her to accept the new circumstances so they can finally be together, especially when he heard what his parents did to him as a human?

    • A retelling of the game, with the Masked Man's identity and origins explained.

Shin Megami Tensei

  • Shin Megami Tensei IV: Apocalypse
    • Asahi becomes Dagda's Godslayer instead of Nanashi, albeit still having her crush on Nanashi and feeling unworthy of him now that she is undead. When Nanashi sacrifices himself in her place, Asahi has to make the choice between siding with Dagda or her friends.
      • The Bonds route has Asahi choose not to go through with Dagda's plan, but she still convinces him to stop by reminding him of his own bond with his daughter, averting the "replace Dagda" method. Nanashi gets revived as a result.
      • The Massacre route has Asahi side with Dagda, turning against her friends in the process. She revives Nanashi as her goddess, while having Flynn and Isabeau revived as her Co-Godslayers.
  • Persona:
    • A Role Swap Plot between the protagonists and their Implied Love Interests (For example, P3 Protagonist and Aigis, Yu Narukami and Marie, Ren Amamiya and "Kasumi" (actually Sumire) Yoshizawa) while still retaining their personalities.
    • What if Naoto was a transgender girl?
    • What if Marie and Nanako awakened their Personas and became members of the Investigation Team while still retaining their roles in canon? Marie also gets to interact with Nanako Dojima and develop a soft spot for her. Nanako in turn ships Marie with her Big Bro and sees her as Cool Big Sis. Their relationship causes Marie to become just as protective of Nanako as Yu is.
    • What if Sumire Yoshizawa joined the Phantom Thieves on their vacation in Persona 5 Scramble: The Phantom Strikers?
    • What if Persona Users ended up switching bodies, in a manner similar to Silver Chariot Requiem's power (The bodies keep their voices, but the Persona User keeps their Personas since they're a manifestation of their mind).

Silent Hill

  • The Order succeeded in birthing their god. What follows is every human being on Earth becomes trapped in the Fog World/Other World, everyone hunted and psychologically tormented by monsters and other supernatural horrors that lurk in the darkness.

Sly Cooper

  • Sly, Bentley, and Murray enter an Alternate Universe where people they know have swapped roles. There they meet the Fox Gang, consisting of Carmelita Fox, Penelope Mouse, and Jing King, who are the honorable and kind thieves of this universe. The Sly Cooper of that universe is an Interpol Agent hunting down Carmelita, Alternate Bentley is a backstabber who broke Penelope's heart, while the Murray of that universe takes the role of Muggshot, who becomes an ally of the Fox Gang. Neyla serves as the Arch-Enemy to Fox that Clockwerk is to Sly.
  • The Fiendish Five kill Sly's family at a much earlier point in time than in canon. Because of this, Sly never learned of his thieving heritage or who was responsible for leaving him an orphan. In adulthood, Sly, Bentley and Murray become police officers and eventually become agents for Interpol. They help Carmelita stage a raid on Sir Raleigh's operation in the Isle of Wrath and they discover pages of the Thievius Raccoonus in his most secure vault. They interrogate Raleigh at Interpol HQ about their importance and it inspires Sly to investigate further and begins planning a campaign with Bentley and Murray to find and arrest the rest of the Fiendish Five to discover who he really is.
    • While Sly is a field agent, Bentley works as their omnidisciplinary desk-jockey, using his intellect to analize evidence, draw up plans for Interpol raids and other purposes. Murray is usually the driver, but he has more experience as a field operating police officer.
    • While Sly has been in Interpol for far less time than her, the success rate that comes with his natural talent lands him as Carmelita's partner. They have a Straight Man and Wise Guy dynamic going on, Carmelita being the serious By-the-Book Cop and Sly being the Cowboy Cop willing to bend the rules.
    • Clockwerk kept the Cooper Cane as a souvenir along with the rest of the book.
    • When Sly discovers who his father truly was, he finds out that he had an old gang member that was in prison and decides to pay him a visit. While McSweeny is shocked that his friend's son and his godson is a cop and is grieved that he wasn't there for the orphaned raccoon, he helps fill in a few blanks about his lineage and reveals to him that the Cooper Cane - which is now in storage as evidence after Interpol's attempt to capture Clockwerk - is the key to the Cooper fortune on Kaine Island.
    • Bentley interned under the Contessa during college in learning criminal psychology. After the Cooper Gang become official Interpol agents, they bring criminals to the Contessa's asylum, only for Bentley to notice various peculiar things - the sudden spike in the prison's budget with only vague statements on who has been making the "donations", implications of abuse from the staff to the inmates, a trend of lack of stolen property from the criminals being found using her methods - and he finds out that she has been using her hypnosis techniques for unethical experimentation and theft of their stolen goods.
  • Sly is stranded in Ancient Egypt at the end of Sly Cooper: Thieves in Time. He is injured, dehydrated and without help and loses consciousness. When he wakes up, he is in a dungeon, under arrest for the crimes of his ancestor Slytunkhamen I due to his likeness. Just as he is about to be executed for stealing from the pharaoh, he is rescued. By who? By a young Clockwerk! He is non-robotic, young, full of hope and is revealed to be Slytunkhamen's equivalent to Bentley and Dr. M, having staged the breakout when he heard his lifelong friend was going to be executed. While Sly is suspicious of his hated enemy, Clockwerk is proven to be an upstanding fellow, even going through with the breakout because he didn't want an innocent raccoon to be executed for crimes he did not commit.
    • It is revealed that Clockwerk began his lifelong vendetta against the Cooper Clan when he found out that Slytunkhamen had left his gang behind, having found a new partner that Sly recognizes will be Slytunkhamen II's mother. Angry, Clockwerk finds the remains of Le Paradox's ship and, with his ahead-of-his-time genius, reverse engineers it and becomes the mechanical monstrosity that lived on.

Sonic the Hedgehog

  • Maria Robotnik is brought Back from the Dead as a hedgehog, befriending Sonic and his friends, reuniting with Shadow the Hedgehog, and becoming part of the core cast.
  • After having worked for years at G.U.N., Shadows finally works up the nerve and decides to use his new government resources to find Maria's body to pay his last respects. He discovers that she didn't die that day 50 years ago and goes on a wild goose chase and discovers the truth: she survived the raid on the ARK, was brought down to Earth where she lived the rest of her life as a normal human citizen. She found love, married, had kids and grandkids and no lives in a nursing home, old and senile but having lived a rich, full life.
  • Cream the Rabbit bakes cookies for everyone, including Doctor Eggman and Mephiles.

Spider-Man (PS4)

  • The Avengers return from their mission abroad, only to hear that not only is the entire city suffering from a horrifying disease, the city is overrun with criminals and super-criminals and all of it is being blamed on Spider-Man. The plot can go one of two ways:
    • The Avengers believe Norman Osbourne without question and issue a manhunt for Spider-Man. By the time the truth comes out and Spider-Man's name is cleared, he gives a cathartic "The Reason You Suck" Speech for believing him despite the fact that Spider-Man has more than earned their trust over the years.
    • Norman acts like he owns the Avengers out of the narcissistic idea that being rich gives him the right to tell them what to do and they all immediately suspect that Spider-Man is being scapegoated for the very thing he is trying to stop.
  • Spider-Man finds Octavius right as he breaks the Devil's Breath vial and gets a face full of it. While Spider-Man's superhuman physiology makes him immune to its detrimental effects, the brief moment it spends in his body mutating the concoction. Instead of slowly killing everyone, it instead imprints his DNA onto half of the city, giving all of them Spider-Man's powers.
    • Instead of Miles getting his powers from Norman's bootleg mutant spider, he gets it from this. He shows it to Peter before this new "Spider Plague" becomes public and he agrees to teach him how to use his powers.
    • When MJ gets it, she realizes why Peter is always so jumpy and easily distracted when she develops a Spider-Sense, the buzzing in her head constantly going off due to the micro-aggressions and everyday hazards living in New York presents.
    • J. Jonah Jameson contracts it, but his mood picks up immensely when the first "symptoms" are that his ailing health (due to his age) suddenly gets better, loosing his arthritis and develops energy he hasn't had in years. During one of his podcasts, his body starts sticking to everything in his office and it sounds like he gets covered in everything on his desk and his chair before he falls to the floor.
  • What if Black Cat actually did have a son?
  • Instead of pretending to think that Peter was giving Spider-Man his tech, Otto makes it clear that he knows that Peter is Spider-Man from the get-go.
  • What if Otto adopted the newly orphaned Martin Li?
  • After Martin Li gets his powers and is left an orphan, he slowly works to hone his new abilities. While the idea of revenge against Norman Osbourne floats through his mind, he winds up accidentally saving someone from a mugger while he is practicing using his powers. From then on, he becomes one of New York's superpowered vigilantes Mr. Negative, using his powers to fight crime and even is on a first name basis with the Avengers.
    • Instead of corrupting others by exacerbating their negative qualities, Martin learns to gain power by taking that negativity and channeling it into raw psionic energy. Simply put, he sucks out the criminal's inner-darkness and spits it back at them as firepower. The more enslaved by their sins his opponents, the stronger he is. When he is at work, Martin uses it to slowly siphon that negativity from the homeless at F.E.A.S.T., alleviating the troubled minds of those in his care. Those who don't know him as Mr. Negative describe him as having an "aura of positivity," their anxiety and despair replaced by calm and hope.
    • While Mr. Negative can use his own anger and hatred to fuel his powers, it can put him into an Unstoppable Rage which can prove disastrous, hence why he would rather not use it. Whenever he feels like he loses control, he uses Taoist prayers his father taught him to calm his troubled mind.
    • When Martin spends enough time around Peter when he and May begin working with him, he slowly pieces it together that Peter is Spider-Man and ends up becoming his mentor.
    • J. Jonah Jameson thinks of Martin as a "true hero" for his contributions at F.E.A.S.T. while Mr. Negative is just another "costumed freak" who causes more problems than they solve.
  • Dr. Octavius decides that spreading Devil's Breath isn't worth his revenge and decides to end his Evil Plan before it begins.

Spyro the Dragon

  • A drabbles series where Hunter and Bianca pursue a relationship after the Sorceress is finally vanquished for good in Spyro: Year of the Dragon.
    • One chapter involves bringing her to Avalar to meet Elora and the Professor, only for the both of them to embarrass him like family would.
  • After the Sorceress is vanquished and all of the eggs hatch by the end of Spyro: Year of the Dragon, the Dragons come to a consensus and decide to open relations up with the various worlds that now make up the Forgotten Realms.

Stella Glow

  • A retelling of the game with an amnesiac Original Character joining the 9th Regiment and growing close to Alto and the Witches, as well as Marie. That character also ends up saving Queen Anastasia from dying, though she's still taken out of the action for a long period of time. While Eve still has to stay on the Moon to prevent Cartesia from dropping it onto Earth, the OC can help the Tuning Knights and Marie find an alternative and free Eve from her duties without sacrificing the world.
  • What if Marie had been adopted by Queen Anastasia as a sister somehow?
