Fan Fiction / All Star WarZone

Go To

Edit Locked

  • Create New

All-Star War Zone is a DeviantArt project by user Ultimate Saiyan Eddy. It is a collaborative project where several users wrote (and still write) hypothetical fighting game movesets for countless fictional (and non-fictional) characters.

All of the profiles can be found on Eddy's DeviantArt page here.

These movesets provide examples of:

  • Adaptational Badass: Characters who weren't fighters in their home series, such as Meat Boy and Both Pinocchios are now more than capable of dishing out beatings.
  • Breaking the Fourth Wall: A few examples.
      Advertisement:
    • As expected, Deadpool uses this to his advantage, hitting the opponent with his own health bar and Super Meter much like in Marvel vs. Capcom.
    • SCP-3999 completely destroys the 4th wall in his Ultimate Move, where the description is suddenly interrupted by him as he starts talking in first person and addressing the players themselves.
  • Cherry Tapping: Some of the Ultimate Moves fall into this category.
    • Kelly Kelly's Stinkface. Imagine being a godly entity with cosmic powers, a legendary warrior of untold might or a supernatural powerhouse with decades of martial arts training, only to be defeated by a hot blonde bombshell twerking and rubbing her ass against your face.
    • The French Knights absolutely humiliate their opponent in their Ultimate Move, taunting them non-stop only to run and barricade themselves inside their castle. As the foe tries to enter, they drop buckets of flop on top of them and start flinging all sorts of objects at the foe, from waste to live chickens and badgers, finishing them off by hurling a cow at the enemy.
      • Advertisement:
    • Aki Shiina, being from a romantic comedy anime, has a moveset full of cherry-tapping moves. Whoever is facing Aki might have a risk of getting defeated in the following ways: Getting smothered between Ayaka's Gag Boobs, K.Oed by taking a selfie with Nana and Aki, being defeated by a small kiss from Mea or getting assaulted and plastered with kisses by a very drunk Ayaka. The worst one by far is his Ultimate Move, where the girls drag Aki and his foe and force them to play the King's Game. The foe is them subjected to a lap pillow from Ayaka (who accidentaly drops her heavy breasts on the foe's head), a smothering hug from her as well (with Aki also receiving one) and a kiss from Yuzu (who, being a tsundere, punches the opponent in the face), and for the final humiliation the girls then force the enemy to wear a cute maid outfit and start taking cute pictures of them, all while Aki hides himself, terrified of the fate he left for his poor opponent...
    • Advertisement:
  • Cuteness Overload: Kirari Moroboshi has this as a unique mechanic. Her moves consist of big hugs, cute poses, squeezing her opponent's cheeks and other cute gestures. Every time the opponent is hit by one of these moves, a "Happy Meter" fills up, and if Kirari manages to fill it completely the enemy completely drops their guard due to her contagious cuteness, temporarily losing the ability to block.
  • Final Boss: Galaxy Monitor, a fusion between Galactus and The Anti-Monitor, who is also the Big Bad of All-Star War Zone.
  • Homing Lasers: Darkseid makes an appearance and brings his Omega Beams with him, which can change their trajectory mid-flight to track his opponent.
  • Limit Break: Ultimate Moves, which are much more powerful than a regular Super Move.
  • Loads and Loads of Characters: As of the time of this writing, there are 5789 profiles.
  • Pet the Dog: Big Bird manages to fit this trope in a fighting game. At the end of his Ultimate Move, instead of finishing the opponent off in any way like most characters do, Big Bird just takes them out for some ice cream and the two walk happily towards the sun.
    • Mutsuki Ichimonji also uses kindness to defeat her foes. Her Ultimate Move has her become her opponent's private tutor, giving them 4-hour classes every day to help them improve their grades, with a montage of scenes showing the foe getting bored out of their mind and constantly getting scolded by Mutsuki. At the end of the movie, however, after suffering through all the boring lectures, the enemy manages to ace Mutsuki's test. How does she finish the attack? By pulling the opponent close and giving them a lovely hug! And judging by the small heart that pops up once she embraces them, the foe seems to enjoy it and forfeits the fight as Mutsuki hugs them even closer and winks at the camera.
  • There Is No Kill Like Overkill: Plenty of examples.
    • Doomguy's Ultimate Move, the BFG 9000. One-shot is enough to completely obliterate the opponent and impress the Doomslayer himself.
    • Thanos hurls an entire MOON at his enemy and then proceeds to finger-snap them out of existence in his Ultimate Move, Wrath of Infinity.
    • While Galactus is also present in the roster, his daughter Galacta actually overshadows him when it comes to overkill. In her Ultimate Move titled Cosmic Feed, Galacta fires an energy blast at her foe and then increases her size to become as big as her father. She then fires a barrage of energy beams at the opponent that start converting their body into energy. The foe barely has time to process what happens before they're eaten by Galacta and erased from existence.
    • Misogi Kumagawa summons a vortex of darkness that swallows not only the opponent, but the entire world, and replaces everything with pure darkness.
  • This Is Your Premise on Drugs: Considering that the likes of Thomas the Tank Engine and a Wii Remote both have sets, it's an absolute given.
  • Tsundere: More than one example, but Osana Najimi practically weaponizes this trope. Some of her attacks include force-feeding a bento to her opponent and angrily slapping them for disliking it, bashfully calling them an idiot to stun them, and her Ultimate Move has her insult the enemy to hurt their feelings, only for Osana to regret her actions and pull her foe into a Big Damn Kiss that leaves them shocked...right before Osana becomes embarrassed about it and knocks the daylights out of them with a slap to the face.
  • Ultimate Showdown of Ultimate Destiny
  • World of Ham: The result of there being so many hammy characters.
  • Would Hurt a Child: Many combatants such as Riley Anderson and Billy and Mandy are children, but they still beat up and get beaten up just like the rest of the roster.
  • Wrestler in All of Us: A majority of the WWE roster is featured in this game, from legends such as Triple H and the Undertaker to newer additions such as The Fiend and Aleister Black.
    • Outside of WWE, Kenny Omega also makes an appearance, being able to summon the Bullet Club to aid him in the fight.
    • Rosemary from Impact Wrestling, bringing Abyss and Crazzy Steve alongside her, is also playable.
    • Many wrestlers from videogames are also included such as Zangief, Mike Haggar and Quetzacoatl.
  • Yandere: Gasai Yuno, probably the most infamous example of this trope, is part of the roster.
    • Yandere-Chan from Yandere Simulator is also playable and incorporates aspects of her own game such as knives, poison and her Yandere Vision into her moveset. Her Ultimate Move even has her kidnap the opponent and trap them in her basement, which is something you can do to other people in her home game.
    • Probably the one that incorporates this trope more than any other is Sayuri Miyasu, whose moveset has her basically become a yandere for whoever she's facing. From kind and romantic acts like distracting them with her smile or burying their face between her breasts in a hug to violent actions like stabbing her foe with scissors and making them eat a poisoned candy. Her Ultimate Move also involves her drugging the enemy and trapping them inside her basement, although unlike Yandere-Chan who very clearly stabs her victim to death, whatever Sayuri does to her opponent once she kidnaps them is never shown, making this a very disturbing way to finish a fight.
