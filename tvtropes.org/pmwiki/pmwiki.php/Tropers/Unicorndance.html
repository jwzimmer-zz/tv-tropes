Hi, my username is Unicorndance, and I am a troper.

     Tropes that apply to me 
  • Abandoned Catchphrase: I used to have these weird things I'd say all the time as a kid. Not anymore, thank goodness.
  • Animal Lover: With a few exceptions, such as mosquitoes, I love animals!
  • Asexuality: I'm asexual and probably aromantic.
  • Berserk Button:
    • When people are... I can't even bring myself to say/type the word, but it rhymes with "spastic" and means "talking in opposites", it makes me livid.
    • I also get pretty steamed about being patronised/not taken seriously.
    • Memes, especially the word 'yeet' and that stupid ugly ree-ing frog. Similarly, when people use names (such as Karen and Becky) as insults.
    • Bigotry.
  • Book Worm: I like to read.
  • Censorship by Spelling: I have a habit of spelling out words I don't like.
  • Constantly Curious: Technically not since "constantly" means "literally with no end", but I am very inquisitive.
  • Cope by Pretending: Downplayed. As a little girl, I used to deal with stories I hated by "rewriting" them. Thank goodness I don't do that anymore.
  • Cordon Bleugh Chef: Not nowadays, but when I was seven, I tried to invent a snack made from grapes and rosemary, and made my dad a marshmallow sandwich for Father's Day.
  • Does Not Like Spam: I hate porridge and coffee.
  • Early Personality Signs: I love animals and one of my first words was "cat".
  • Favourite Trope: Some tropes I like include:
    • Christmas Episode: What can I say? I love Christmas!
    • Cute Kitten: I'm a huge animal lover, so that's a given.
    • Dark Is Not Evil: I just find it interesting.
    • Friend to All Children: It's just cute to see!
    • Happy Rain: I actually don't find rain all that bad, so I like seeing it portrayed positively.
    • Magic A Is Magic A: I find magic more compelling when I know how it works.
    • Out-of-Context Eavesdropping, when done well. I know it's been done, but it's still funny.
    • Undying Loyalty, Platonic Life-Partners, and Heterosexual Life-Partners: it's just cute to see friendships like that.
  • Friend to All Living Things: There are exceptions, but animals tend to like me as much as I like them. Downplayed though as birds still fly away from me, etc.
  • Genki Girl: I'm optimistic and probably the most active in my household.
  • Gosh Dang It to Heck!: I am very creative with exclamations.
  • Hair of Gold, Heart of Gold: I'm blonde and I'm usually very polite and try to be very morally good.
  • Kind Hearted Cat Lover: I love cats and I'm quite polite.
  • Ms. Imagination: I have an extremely active imagination.
  • Nature Lover: While I don't like the reasons why Nature Is Not Nice, I do have an appreciation for the natural world and am an environmentalist.
  • Nerd: I am very passionate about my interests.
  • Nice Girl: I try to be as nice as possible.
  • Old Shame: I write stories about a girl named Monica Ryder, but when I first started writing them at age fourteen, her friends were blank slates and I stole other people's characters. I cringe whenever I think of the early Monica Ryder stories.
  • Pet-Peeve Trope: Some tropes I just don't like...
    • Catch Your Death Of Cold: It appals me that some people still think this is true.
    • Even Nerds Have Standards, because what's wrong with being a nerd?
    • Fainting: Too scary.
    • Grey Rain of Depression: Too much of a cliche.
    • Humans Are Bastards and especially Humans Are the Real Monsters: Such a cliche, yet at the same time it makes me feel too sad to go all heckler on it.
    • Out-of-Character Moment, unless it's sufficiently explained in-universe.
    • Potty Failure: Too gross.
    • Science Is Bad: Being a science fan, it's a given that this doesn't sit well with me.
    • Sick Episode: I don't mind plots where all the sick characters get better and/or the sick characters aren't important, but otherwise, it's too sad and can be a bit boring.
    • Sleep Deprivation: Makes me feel sorry for the character as I was only sleep-deprived once in my whole life but do not want to relive it.
    • Snarking Thanks: Any form of "being ironic" (I can't even bring myself to say/write/type the S-word because I hate it so much) makes me livid, but when people are... that... about thanking someone especially, it flips my lid.
    • Any Stereotype.
    • Symbolism— Downplayed. It's not that big of a pet peeve, but I find it kind of creepy. After all, what if I don't pick up on it? Especially if it's in a kids' book.
    • Your Tomcat Is Pregnant— Overused and a bit contrived.
  • Proud to Be a Geek: I'm one of those proud geeks.
  • Sweet Tooth: I like sugar a lot.
  • Why Did It Have to Be Snakes?: While I have no crippling phobias, I'm a little creeped out by any kind of medical horror, the idea of sleep deprivation, and people behaving uncharacteristically.
  • Would Not Tell A Lie: I'm a very honest person.
