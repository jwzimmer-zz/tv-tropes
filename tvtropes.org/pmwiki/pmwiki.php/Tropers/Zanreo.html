Hey! I'm a 26 year old girl from Norway who's been a troper since 2010, but only started becoming active on the forums again since August 2017 note I've also had some activity during 2011-2012 and the very occasional post between then and 2017. Currently I'm mostly known as a Forum Games regular, mainly various avatar games and Hunger Games Simulation, and I regularly drop by Yack Fest too.

One of my biggest hobbies are video games, and I especially love Nintendo and rhythm games - rhythm games are a really underrated genre! I'm also known for loving bad translations and So Bad, It's Good works, and also like various strange works, weird memes, cosplaying, cooking (especially desserts and other snacks) and drawing.

Some of the fictional characters who best describe me are Chiaki Nanami, Osaka and Midori Asakusa.

Advertisement:

I also made the webcomic known as Consolers, a comic about game history, news and fun facts, presented through personified versions of game companies. While it's been on a hiatus since mid-2018, I hope to get back to it some day... though I also have other webcomic ideas...

(last updated: 29th August 2020)

     Tropes that apply to me 
  • 100% Completion: I'm a bit of a completionist and love going for all the trophies/achievements (even if just in-game achievement systems rather than account-based ones), 100% save files and similar. Usually what makes me try out extra challenges outside of the "regular" difficulty - generally I won't go for a super hard challenge unless I get something out of it (that "something" typically being a trophy). In fact, "play through game with an extra challenge" type trophies like speedruns, no damage runs and "play the entire game again on extra hard mode" tend to be some of my least favorite trophies/achievements, along with heavy grinds.
  • Accidental Misnaming: It's not "Zanero", it's Zanreo. Somehow, people keep writing my name as Zanero...
  • Adorkable, at least I like to think so
  • Asexual: And aromantic.
  • Asperger's Syndrome: Diagnosed as a kid.
  • Berserk Button: Well, not exactly "berserk" as I don't tend to go into a flying rage, but these are things that annoy me:
    • Cringe culture. I really do not care for cringe compilation videos, "bad art" blogs and similar types of mocking random people (often kids) on the internet for doing basically nothing wrong except daring to post something deemed "embarrassing" and "cringy". Just let people have their fun! Honestly, the word "cringe" in itself has almost been ruined for me at this point.
    • On a somewhat overlapping note, extreme Fan Haters - see "Least Favorite Tropes" section for more details
    • "Autism/autistic" used as an insult. As an autistic person, I really hate seeing these words used like this
    • Sexism, racism, homophobia, transphobia, ableism and other kinds of bigotry, pretty much an obvious one but listing it anyway
    • Pokémon "genwunners" - people who claim the 1st gen are the only "real" Pokémon. (In fact, 1st gen is overall one of my LEAST favorites design-wise.) If someone prefers 1st gen, that's cool, but if they start claiming anything after the first 151 "doesn't count" or "doesn't even look like Pokémon"...
    • When people look down at and make a big deal out of the very existence of optional easy modes/"help" modes, or just the very idea of it hypothetically being added to certain games. I pretty much never use these "help modes" myself in most games because I think it makes it too easy and I want the challenge even I have to try again and again, but I'm all for people playing on the difficulties they like and am sick of how part of the gaming community look down on and seem to hate "easy modes" like this, even just the existence of optional ones (emphasis on optional - no one forces you to use them and a lot of the time they're disabled by default). Why does it matter how others choose to play and enjoy the game on their own playthough, and is it really that important that everyone who beat the game must go through the very same difficult challenges you suffered through? Isn't just enjoying the game enough? To paraphrase Jim Sterling: "Nobody cares that a literal baby can beat the game just as well as you"
    • Fandom elitists/gatekeepers, claiming you aren't a Real Fan™ if you don't know everything, don't have certain opinions about things or in some cases even if you haven't been there from the start. Just... shouldn't someone liking the thing be enough?
    • Claiming internet friends aren't and can't be "real" friends, or that they can't be as important as offline, local ones. Plus, as someone who grew up with barely any friends at school or nearby for years, but found people to talk to on the internet, sometimes it can just be hard to find "irl" friends you get along with and share common interests with.
    • The phrase "git gud", even used jokingly, tends to get on my nerves a bit. There are less obnoxious versions to say "just keep practising and you'll get better".
    • To a lesser extent than most other stuff on here, but certain misconceptions about Nintendo (like "Nintendo doesn't allow any "mature" games on their systems") that still seems to be around somehow tend to annoy me.
  • Big Eater: Downplayed, but I love food, and can eat a lot of it at times. I also love trying out new kinds of food!
  • Boyish Short Hair: I like keeping my hair short, it looks good on me and is more convenient
  • Buffy Speak: I tend to speak like this a bit, using "thing" and "stuff" a lot.
  • Chaotic Good: According to several tests, and it seems to fit me pretty well - I try to be a good person, but am not always too concerned about the rules. (And sometimes, "following the rules" and "doing the right thing" aren't the same...) Though I guess I might really be somewhere in the middle of Chaotic Good and Neutral Good?
  • Cloudcuckoolander: I can be pretty weird and often have my head in the clouds.
    • Cuckoo Snarker: Though I do deliver some "sick burns" and snarky quips from time to time, often about the game industry.
  • Color Motif: Purple and blue, my favorite colors
  • Cosplay Otaku Girl: Cosplay is one of my big hobbies, some of my costumes include Ken Izumi, Aoba Suzukaze, CATS and Nintendo.
  • The Ditherer: Yep... I often have a hard time making decisions, and I've been most of the types listed at some point.
  • Exhausted Eye Bags: From many late nights staying up playing games or browsing the internet.
  • Fan of the Past: In a way: game history is one of my big passions, and I love reading up on game history trivia. Especially in relation to game companies rather than just the games themselves. (Though how big of a fan I am of the actual times rather than just finding the trivia amusing can vary)
    • I'm also a shameless fan of various old memes (sometimes preferring to call them "classic memes"), even long after the rest of the internet has moved on. In fact, it would be easier and quicker to list the memes I used to like and HAVE gotten tired of...
  • Gamer Chick: I play a lot of video games, in particular I'm a big Nintendo fangirl and love rhythm games, though I will play a lot of different games, preferring more stylized and cartoony games to more "realistic", dark and gritty ones.
  • Hates Wearing Dresses: Well, I'm fine with them if it's part of a cosplay, but outside of that you won't see me in a skirt or dress (including special/fancy occasions, where I'll wear a suit instead), they just aren't my style.
  • Iconic Item: Lasers. Also banhammers when I was still a mod at a forum.
  • Intentional Engrish for Funny: Along with quoting and referencing bad translations, I sometimes phrase things with incorrect grammar in more casual speech for a fun and silly effect.
  • Kuudere: More in real life than the internet, but for some reason can I seem kinda shy and "cold" around strangers sometimes? However my friends know this is not the case at all.
  • Master of None: Probably as a result of having lots of hobbies and not enough time to put effort into just one at a time (along with general laziness and procrastination). While I'm decent at drawing, making cosplays, making games and animations I wouldn't really call myself great at any of them.
  • Meganekko: My glasses are an important part of my look, and I think they look pretty good on me.
  • Ms. Imagination: I tend to spend a lot of time daydreaming and in my own world
  • Nice Hat: I often wear a Waluigi cap.
  • Never Gets Fat: I don't seem to gain much weight not matter what I eat or how little I exercise
  • Not a Morning Person: I generally hate getting up early. Though on days where this doesn't matter, my sleeping schedule is all over the place.
  • No Pronunciation Guide: ZAN-ree-oh? Zan-RE-oh? Sanrio? I don't really mind either way.
  • Older Than They Look: Due to me being short and somewhat young-looking, I'm sometimes assumed to be younger than I actually am.
  • Opaque Lenses: I usually draw myself with these. Unfortunately averted in real life.
  • Promoted Fangirl: After only two years of joining a Nintendo forum I hang out on a lot, they decided to make me a moderator. (Sadly, the servers broke years ago and the forum and site never got back after that :c)
  • Proud to Be a Geek: Despite... less pleasant parts of geek and gamer culture, I'm still very much this.
  • Pungeon Master: I wouldn't say "master", but I love puns and will usually take the opportunity to make one
  • Ridiculous Procrastinator: I have way too many unfinished projects, a giant game backlog and often delay things to the last minute.
    • Attention Deficit Creator Disorder: And combined with this, it's a wonder I get anything done...
  • Save Scumming: I fully admit to doing this at times when possible, typically to beat That One Level or easier get an annoying trophy.
  • Schedule Slip: Can be pretty bad at this regarding my webcomic... even if I don't actually have a set "schedule" anymore
  • Significant Anagram: My real name is an anagram of "Laser Linked", appropiate considering my fondness for lasers.
  • Silly Rabbit, Cynicism Is for Losers!: Too much cynicism tends to just be exhausting, and I at least try my best to stay optimistic and hopeful despite everything. I also can't get into the "keep low expectations and you won't be disappointed" attitude - I usually have high hopes, but if they don't get fulfilled I rarely get really disappointed, my reaction is more likely to be "oh well, that's too bad:
  • The Slacker: Yep.
  • Sweet Tooth: I love candy, desserts and all kinds of sweet snacks!
  • The Teetotaler: I don't drink despite being of legal age to do so, mainly because I hate the taste of alcohol.
  • The Quiet One: I usually don't talk a lot and will especially be quiet around people I don't know
  • Viewer Gender Confusion: Several people online have assumed I'm a guy (especially in more male-dominated forums), possibly because of my ambiguous-sounding nickname.
  • You Gotta Have Purple Hair: Been dying my hair purple for a while now. Might try out other colors too...

     Least favorite tropes 
  • Accidental Pervert: Often just comes across as cheap excuse to have characters creeped on/groped/ect.
  • Black Comedy Rape: Rape jokes as a whole are very much Dude, Not Funny! for me.
  • Comedic Lolicon
  • Continuing is Painful
  • Darkness-Induced Audience Apathy: If something is just too dark and hopeless, I'm likely to lose interest.
  • Easy-Mode Mockery: mainly the "humiliating" type that outright insults the player for it (Silliness Switch-type stuff is... better but as someone who loves Silliness Switch it would be better as an actual option for anyone who wants it rather than just part of easy mode). Just let people play the difficulty they want without mocking them for it! As for the kind that locks you out of content like the final levels/chapters, that's not great either (though certain extra content, extra hard Bonus Bosses ect. being exclusive to harder difficulties is fine)
  • Escort Mission: While the concept itself is fine, it's rarely done well and I can't remember ever playing one of these I actually liked.
  • Fan Hater/Hate Dumb: A lot of the time, hatedoms are even more annoying than so-called "bad fandoms". Especially when their main complaints are based around about how "cringy" and annoying the fandom is - as long as they're not hurting anyone, just let them have fun! While some fandoms do have their less pleasant parts (and I'm talking more about "attack and harass people in the name of fandom" rather than "make "~*cRiNgY*~" fanworks oh noooo" because honestly I don't really care about the latter) those are just Vocal Minorities and I try my very best to not let that affect my opinion on the work itself.
  • The Glasses Gotta Go: As a proud glasses-wearer who think people often look good wearing glasses, the implication that glasses are inherently "ugly" annoys me.
  • Gratuitous Rape
  • Gross-Up Close-Up
  • New Media Are Evil
  • Not Wanting Kids Is Weird
  • Permanently Missable Content: The bane of every completionist, and a reason I often look up if there are any missables (mainly collectible/achievement-related ones) before starting a game.
  • Point-and-Laugh Show, most of the time
  • Romanticized Abuse
  • Romantic Plot Tumor
  • Certain audio-based kinds of Sensory Abuse, as someone sensitive to certain types of sounds it can get really grating and unpleasant. Notably, this includes what's known as "ear rape"note a term I'm not really a fan of - it's just annoying (which I guess is part of the point, but...)
  • Silly Rabbit, Idealism Is for Kids!
  • "Stop Having Fun" Guys
  • Straw Feminist: And really, most strawman characters in general but this one tends to be especially annoying and often seems to completely misunderstand what feminism is actually about
  • True Art Is Angsty
  • Unnecessary Makeover in many cases
