WELCOME, ladies, gentlemen, and tropers, to Darth Wiki's Favorite Game Show...

WARP... THAT... AESOP!!!!!

[Catchy Theme Song plays]

Hello! Hello! How are you all doing today? How are the kids?

All right, you all know how to play the game! Contestants will take a piece of media... and, through a number of deliberate misunderstandings, a few Alternate Character Interpretations, taking some things out of context, assuming that everything presented was meant to be taken as instructional and serious, and reading in more than a little Fanon, make it seem like the creators were attempting to deliver a terrible, terrible message to the kids at home... no matter how utterly off-base it is!

Advertisement:

But, before we begin, let's just go over the three rules of the game, shall we? Rule #1! Make sure to read the above paragraph before adding a example. Rule #2! This is not Headscratchers with a sarcastic tone, and if you think it is, Get Out!. And, last but not least, rule #3! This page is Just For Fun, serious attempts at finding a Hard Truth Aesop, and Real Life examples will be purged. Oh, and remember, folks, Whining costs twenty million points, so there's that.

...and whatever you do, don't change the channel to Sugar Wiki for Sweeten That Aesop or the Neutral Zone for Boil That Aesop. And don't mix this show up with Things We Have Learnt from Media — that's just facts, this show is about 'truths' and principles. Change to our sister channel, the TV Tropes Forum for That's an awful Hindsight shoehorn, where you can connect all sorts of coincidences and see how Life Imitates Art!

Advertisement:

Ok, now that we've got all of that out of the way, first to 10,000 points gets an extra lifenote to replace the one you ruined, a copy of our Home Game, and Rice-A-Roni, the San Francisco treat!

Round One! Ready.... GO!

  • Chick-fil-A: Eating fried chicken means you hate the gays.
    • Chick-fil-A Cows: Meat is murder, but only if its beef. Screw the chickensnote NOT LITERALLY! Dear god!.
      • Cows are super-racist against chickens and are plotting for genocide.
  • McDonald's: Your restaurant can make any kind of food regardless of your customer base as long as you give it a cute prefix; McDLT, McRib, McPizza, McNoodles, McFlurry, McLobster, McCurry Pan, McNürnburger, you get the Mcpicture.
  • Messin' with Sasquatch:
    • If you buy Jack Link's Beef Jerky, you're Too Dumb to Live.
    • Nature is so boring people will mock a killer ape-monster like it was a nerd in gym class.
  • M&M's: Rule 34 really can apply to anything.
  • Never Say No to Panda:
    • Pandas are abusive assholes.
    • Capitalist Egypt is one big Protection Racket and they will cut a bitch if you don't buy their products.
  • Pepsiman:
    • Getting seriously maimed is funny if the person getting hurt is Serkis Folk. Check your privilege, meat-spacers!
    • If your Super Hero is so lame that he gets himself seriously maimed every time he gets to the scene of the crime, make him a delivery boy and call him a Good Samaritan.
    • Being out of Pepsi is serious business.
  • Progressive Insurance:
    • Sequin socks are a desired Christmas gift.
    • Muy Thai is a completely different skill set from Karate.
    • Sprinkles are for winners.
    • If you think a spokesperson is annoying, just wait until you meet their family.
  • The Real Cost: Don’t smoke or you’ll be left in a freakish labyrinth to die!
  • Trix Rabbit: Kids Are Cruel and prejudiced against rabbits.

    Art 
In-General
  • True Art Is Ancient.
  • True Art Is Angsty.
  • True Art Is Boring.
  • True Art Is Incomprehensible.
  • The only thing that determines artistic genius is if enough people say it is. Actual adherence to the fundamentals of art has nothing to do with it.
  • Once enough people with degrees say a work is good, or that a work is bad, no one is allowed to disagree with that sentiment, ever.
  • All artistic movements were given pejorative names by people who irrationally hate them, yet fail to explain themselves, or actually say that the movement sucks. Never mind that exactly the same criticisms were made at the critics when they were younger.
  • Public nudity is only fine if it's a mockup of a person. Sometimes.
  • Self-Portraits: Narcissism is integral to being a content-creator of any kind.
  • Still lifes: You and your life is defined by your possessions.

Movements

  • Academic Art: People in the future will hate you for no reason, despite the quality of your work.
  • Cubism: Screw you, anatomy!
  • Dadaism: Anything you pick up from the dump can be art if you sign your name on it. Screw you, intellectual property laws!
  • Expressionism:
    • Screw you, coherency!
    • Abstract expressionism: Let's all make art a child could do, and say it’s great.
  • Fauvism: Screw you, color theory!
  • History Painting: You’re being patronized by nobles and the Church, so make nothing but propaganda.
  • Impressionism: Screw you, proper technique!
  • Minimalism: True Art Is Incomprehensible and should exist in a vacuum. Relatability via representationalism is for uneducated pions.
  • Modern Art: Screw it, anything goes. Even if it has no redeeming qualities whatsoever.
  • Naive Art: Amateurs sure are stupid, huh? And even though we used to be no better, let's mock them by making bad art on purpose!
    • Kitsch: Wait, the amateurs are actually good? Let's just say they suck!
  • Neoclassicism: Only Ancient Grome is allowed.
  • Pop Art: Being sarcastic about commercialism is brilliant and totally isn't hypocritical and doesn't get old after five minutes.
  • Post-Impressionism: Impressionism wasn’t that great, but we’re gonna continue everything silly about it.
  • Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood: Paint the same woman over and over, just in different costumes.
  • Primitivism: Non-whites sure are stupid, huh?
  • Surrealism: Screw you, making sense!
    • If you’re not a communist fellow traveler, then you’re not a true surrealist even if you paint exactly like us.note This is a reference to Dalí being ejected from the original group over politics.

Art

  • Civic Virtue Triumphant over Unrighteousness:
    • Metaphor? What's that?
    • Americans are all prudes who are intimidated by immodesty.
  • The Mona Lisa: National icons can only be forged from attempted theft.
  • The Rapture of Psyche: Being abducted by guy who's infatuated with you is awesome!

Artists

  • Banksy: Vandalism is legal if it makes you famous.
  • Jackson Pollock: You can literally throw anything on a canvas and become a national and artistic icon in the process.
  • Takashi Murakami: When sensory deprivation is that artistic status quo, the best way to get noticed is inflicting sensory overload.
  • William-Adolphe Bouguereau: Modern Art sucks.

    Asian Animation 
  • Motu Patlu: Eating a ton of snack food will always solve all your problems.
  • Noonbory and the Super 7:
    • Weaponize your allergies.
    • If someone wants to join your clique, deny them every chance you get, because they're not worthy. Don't even help them to be more like you and your friends, because it takes a long time.
    • "Wangury and the Windbike": Assault via dragging someone around by their tongue is a justifiable retribution for stealing a bike.
  • Pleasant Goat and Big Big Wolf: Trying to feed your family is evil. Not only is it evil, it will also result in you failing and being injured.

    Meta 
  • Warp That Aesop: It's okay to distort the truth and promote bad morality if it entertains you.
    • Complaining about aesops you don't like is okay if you cover it under a thin coat of snark.
    • Everything you watch, everything you read and every game you play is teaching you a horrible lesson.
    • The My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic subpage: The people who write that show are all naive Glurge Addicts who clearly have no clue how Real Life works.
  • Darth Wiki: Ruining your eyesight and being snarky go hand in hand.
  • Dethroning Moment of Suck: One bad episode is all it takes to permanently damage an entire series for a single person.
    • Lacking a sense of humour is a valid reason for hating something that wasn't made just for you and you alone.
    • It is a-okay to drop a show for having politics you don't agree with!
    • The Pokémon (Anime) subpage: It's okay to have unrealistically high expectations for characters that are around 7-10 years old.
    • The Western Animation subpage: Cartoons are very Serious Business. Enough to write academic essays on why they're terrible.
      • Most Tropers Are Young Nerds... very easily offended young nerds eager to go off at anything that rubs them the wrong way.
      • The Robot Chicken subpage: If you don't like Black Humour, you should watch shows filled with it anyway just to complain about it on the Internet.
      • The My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic subpage: Having hate boners of fictional characters are completely healthy.
  • Nightmare Fuel: Don't worry, it's more than perfectly okay to read an entire page of things guaranteed to give you nightmares at night, hence the page title!
    • Children can't handle anything remotely scary in movies or shows made for them. Most are too scary for adults too.
    • Tropers are gigantic pansies who are afraid of their own shadows.
    • Via the power of mental gymnastics, even innocent children's media can be high-grade horror!
  • No Real Life Examples, Please!: You aren't allowed to say that Adolf Hitler, Josef Stalin, or Pol Pot were evil.
    • You can never have nice pages on The Internet because people get so butt-hurt over things they disagree with.
  • Poison Oak Epileptic Trees: Tropers are all sickos.
  • Sugar Wiki: All good things have to have girly color schemes.
    • If you aren't an edge-lord on Darth Wiki, you're apparently Barbie and friends.
  • Sweeten That Aesop: People try to fish for a family friendly aesop, even when there isn't one.
    • People want their media to be safe and wholesome and will delude themselves into thinking that it is no matter what.
  • Troper Tales: Complaining about your life on the internet will solve all your problems.
    • Bragging about being a badass makes you awesome!
  • Trope Pantheons: Anime, video game and cartoon characters are all really Odd Job Gods who demand your sacrifices.
    • Want to circumvent the rules on Tv Tropes? Just make a massive group-written crossover Fan Fiction.
  • That Troper: You never make mistakes or do anything wrong on TV Tropes, it's always some other evil troper doing it on purpose.
  • Welcome to Heaven: People's idea of what Heaven is like is having their every hedonistic desire met with no effort.
  • Welcome to Hell: People want to be tortured for all of eternity so badly that they'll freely give their torturers ideas how to do it.
  • YMMV Only: Do not put non In-Universe examples on the main pages!... unless for some reason we let examples on these, in that case proceed to moan about shows you don't like to your heart's content.
  • Ask The Tropers: There's nothing wrong with snitching on your fellow tropers for usually minor infractions or pretending you're one of the moderators.
  • Zero-Context Example: Anyone who posts an example without explaining how it fits the trope doesn't know how the character or work they're talking about fits, even if the trope name is so self-explanatory that elaboration would be redundant.
