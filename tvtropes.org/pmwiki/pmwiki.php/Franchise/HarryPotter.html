Advertisement:

Harry Potter began as a series of seven fantasy novels by J. K. Rowling and later expanded into a multimedia franchise. Following the release of Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them, the franchise as a whole has been rebranded as Wizarding World (or J.K. Rowling's Wizarding World). We have separate pages for its book, film, and video game incarnations; this one is a disambiguation page to help you locate the more specific one you were looking for.

You can vote for your favorite installment here and you can vent all complaints here.

Advertisement:

Literature

  • Harry Potter, the 1997-2007 book series consisting of 7 novels:
    • Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (1997)
    • Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (1998)
    • Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (1999)
    • Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (2000)
    • Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (2003)
    • Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (2005)
    • Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows (2007)
  • Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (2001)
  • Quidditch Through the Ages (2001)
  • The Tales of Beedle the Bard (2008)
  • Harry Potter prequel (2008): An untitled short story written for charity.
  • Pottermore Presents, a line of eBooks which is a combination of existing Pottermore material and new information.
    • Hogwarts: An Incomplete and Unreliable Guide (2016)
    • Short Stories from Hogwarts of Heroism, Hardship and Dangerous Hobbies (2016)
    • Short Stories from Hogwarts of Power, Politics and Pesky Poltergeists (2016)

Advertisement:

Film

  • Harry Potter, the 2001-2011 series of film adaptations based on the books:
    • Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (2001)
    • Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (2002)
    • Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (2004)
    • Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire (2005)
    • Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix (2007)
    • Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince (2009)
    • Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows, Parts 1 and 2 (2010, 2011)
  • Fantastic Beasts, a canonical film series based on the adventures of magizoologist Newt Scamander several decades before the birth of Harry:
    • Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (2016)
    • Fantastic Beasts: The Crimes of Grindelwald (2018)

Theatre

  • Harry Potter and the Cursed Child (2016), an eighth Harry-focused installment in the form of a theater play, which debuted at West End.

Rides

  • The Wizarding World of Harry Potter areas at the Universal Studios parks, which in particular include the rides:
    • Harry Potter and the Forbidden Journey
    • Harry Potter and the Escape from Gringotts
    • Hagrid's Magical Creatures Motorbike Adventure

Tabletop Games

  • Harry Potter Trading Card Game, a Collectible Card Game.
  • Harry Potter: Hogwarts Battle, a cooperative Deckbuilding Game.
  • Harry Potter Miniatures Adventure Game, a 35mm miniature strategy game

Video Games

  • Harry Potter, a series of video games from Electronic Arts, most straight adaptions of the films, although including some original titles.
  • LEGO Harry Potter, a duology of LEGO Adaptation Games.
  • Book of Spells (2012) and Book of Potions (2013), Augmented Reality games that use the PlayStation 3's Wonderbook peripheral.
  • In 2017, Warner Bros. created a subsidiary of its interactive entertainment division called Portkey Games solely dedicated to developing games set in the Wizarding World. These games are considered non-canon. Portkey Games's projects include:
    • Harry Potter: Hogwarts Mystery (2018), a mobile Role-Playing Game set in 1984note Charlie, born in December 1972, is sorted in 1984 along with Tonks. Both appear at the Player's sorting. Also, Bill Weasley, born in December 1970, mentions in the 1984 - 1985 school year that he wants to be a Prefect next year, meaning he's a fourth year student. -1991.
    • Harry Potter: Wizards Unite (2019), an augmented reality game for mobile devices.
    • Harry Potter: Puzzles & Spells (2020), a Match-Three Game.
    • Hogwarts Legacy (2021), the subsidiary's first feature length project. It is an open-world Action RPG set in the late 19th century.

Web

  • Pottermore, an Expanded Universe website which is part online manual, part web game.
