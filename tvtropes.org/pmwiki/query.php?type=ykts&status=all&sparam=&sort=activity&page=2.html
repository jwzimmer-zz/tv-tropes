A rhyming history of weaponry Western Animation

I remember once seeing this animated short on afternoon TV when I was at primary school, which would put it in the late 70s (1981 at the latest, but I am certain it was earlier than that). I'm pretty sure the cartoon itself dates from the late 60s/early 70s, as it carries a really strong anti-war message.

The cartoon was a brief animated history of weaponry: starting with two cavemen fighting with rocks and ends (after an atomic bomb goes off) with two cavemen fighting with rocks.

The accompanying narration was done in the form of rhyming doggerel. The only lines I can remember with any clarity went something like:

The rifle marched through hundreds of years of warring and battling
Till someone invented a gun called a Gatling.

The art style (as I remember) was the sort of thing I'd expect from DePatie–Freleng (the makers of Pink Panther and The Blue Racer), but I can't see anything obvious on their filmography.

Please tell this not something I imagined.
