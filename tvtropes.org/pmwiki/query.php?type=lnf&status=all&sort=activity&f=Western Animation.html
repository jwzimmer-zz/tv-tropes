Hard Work Insurance Western Animation

A whole plot trope.

A character, typically a child, desires an object, usually a toy, but lacks the funds to purchase it themselves. Rather than beg off a parent, the character attempts to earn the necessary funds, either through a series of small paying jobs or one more complicated task that pays the needed amount. Unfortunately Fate conspires to deny the character their prize by making the normally easy chore more arduous and costly, thereby depleting the character's funds even more than they started, or causing the object to be removed either due to a time limit or being purchased by a third party. But, surprise surprise, another party, usually the character's parents, having observed the effort their progeny has put into their goal, has intervened to acquire the object for them, gifting it as a reward and preventing that hardwork from being for naught.

Two examples would be "Roo Bear The Babysitter" from Adventures of the Little Koala and "Whip And Top" from "The Adventures of Will Quack Quack."
