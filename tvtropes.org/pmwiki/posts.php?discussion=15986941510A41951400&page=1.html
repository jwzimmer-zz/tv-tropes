Is Jerkass Facade worth restarting?

Go To

  • Trope Talk
4tell0life4 Relationship Status: Giving love a bad name
Aug 29th 2020 at 3:10:40 AM

Inspired by a talk in another thread.

The trope called "Jerkass Facade" was cut and apparently assimilated to Hidden Heart of Gold. But someone pointed out that it brings problems of its own, namely that the "facade" may be done for reasons other than hiding a heart of gold.

I'm thinking of:

  • Sour Outside, Sad Inside: someone puts a jerk front to hide their insecurities, sorrow and misery.
  • Never Be Hurt Again: someone puts a jerk front (among other means) to protect themselves from being wronged again.
  • Cruel to Be Kind: someone puts a jerk front because it'll somehow help someone else. Same as Tough Love, Stern Teacher and possibly Drill Sergeant Nasty.
  • Zero-Approval Gambit: Someone puts a jerk (or even evil) front so other people would either break their ties with him or even antagonize him as part of his plan.

At worst, JF could be a disambig or an index. At best, it could be a supertrope page.

Thoughts?

Bringing back the trope sounds a bit dubious to me.

My suggestion would be that if there really are some super-trope examples that genuinely don't have an existing trope, you don't bring back Jerkass Façade. All you'll be doing is undoing the previous clean-up decisions and restarting all the misuse and potholing that was going on before.

You'd be creating something from scratch in the TLP and with, I would hope, an entirely different name. In other words, it would be something that's genuinely new and genuinely focussed on the examples that are slipping through the cracks, and not just 'Jerkass Facade restarted'.

I'd suggest collecting all the tropes first as if you're going for a disambig page. It'll be the sorting of those that will help identify whether any examples exist that genuinely don't fit in any existing trope and what the pattern of those left-over examples may take. But you need to know what the existing tropes are first, so it's best to begin there.

Do you have any suggestions for more subtropes, Wyldchyld? Or some examples that aren't categorized in any of the subtropes?

Recognising trope relationships is not one of my strong points, so I don't know if the following could be classified as 'sub-tropes' (especially Beneath the Mask, which seems more like a super-trope), but here are some tropes I think could have a Jerkass Façade filter applied to them.

  • Beneath the Mask: someone puts on a front to fit in with society and hide a less socially acceptable reality.
  • Conflicting Loyalty: The character behaves like a jerk to everyone to hide how much his clashing loyalties are hurting him.
  • Enemy Within: Terrified of what they could do to others should the evil within take over, they act like a jerk to drive people away for their own safety.
  • Inferiority Superiority Complex: A character projects arrogance and ego to hide how lacking in self-belief and self-worth they really are.
  • On the Rebound: A character rubs their new relationship in everyone's faces (especially their ex) to hide how devastated the break-up left them.
  • Stepford Snarker: A person who hides their true self through sarcasm so rude or cutting that they're regarded as a jerk.
  • Undercover When Alone: When the character is putting on the facade for the audience.
