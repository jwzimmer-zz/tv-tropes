Why do I have to be the bad guy? Live Action TV

Basically, Alice has power over Bob and Charlie, and Bob has power over Charlie. But, since she doesn't want Charlie to hate her, she secretely orders Bob to give Charlie orders, while she pretends to be on Charlie's side. This, of course, puts Bob in a periculous position, since he now has to pretend to rebel against Alice, with all the punishments that ensues.

The most classic example is - Alice and Bob are Charlie's parents. Alice doesn't want Charlie to have candy, so she secretly orders Bob, the Henpecked Husband, to forbid Charlie from having candy. Thus, Bob is the "bad guy" and Alice pretends to be the Reasonable Authority Figure.

Misunderstanding Evidence Debuked Live Action TV

Alice thinks X about Bob. Throughout the episode, she sees a bunch of stuff to comfirm her hypothesis.

In the end, she (or someone she talked to) confronts Bob, and Bob explains what every piece of evidence really meant.

The example I'm thinking of, from The Nanny, Fran assumes Niles is a serial killer because he's been shopping for knives, calling the morgue and wearing sneakers. In the end, Niles reveals he's written a play - the sneakers were so he could walk on stage, the knives were props and the morgue was research. Often, the explanations are increasingly ridiculous as a form of Lampshade Hanging.
