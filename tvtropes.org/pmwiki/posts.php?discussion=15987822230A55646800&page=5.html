The Monkey King's Misfits: Chapter 7

The story tells of how after the demon-concealer had been hit with lightning and dropped almost dead in the ground, his three demons went and looked at his body.

The white tiger prodded his prone body awkwardly. "Summoner? Is...Is that it?" He muttered in the language of the Kingdom of the Rising Sun.

"Wow, what a sucker." Insulted the little devil after seeing his master's near death. "Shucks, he's got an awfully deep voice for someone with no balls. And here I actually joined this guy." It then began to leave the scene, waving a hand over its shoulder and insulting a goodbye.

As for the metal monster, he could only clang its tongs morosely. His voice rumbled. "No more building walls..."

The fat monk, somewhat tired after healing his sworn brother, had seen the scene that was happening nearby. He cursed: "You evil beast! The ancients said: 'One day as master, one lifetime as father.' No matter what happens, you should have at least a shred of regret towards him."

At the same time, the crow was angry at the uncivilized and destructive behavior of the attacking demons. She took the knife dropped by the demon hit with her crossbow, who was still wincing in pain, and then shot the remaining demon fighting around, hitting an eye again, and making the demon drop down in pain. The short monk then punched it until it died and the Daoist beheaded the stabber demon.

It was now then when they noticed the demon-concealer, and gritting their teeth at the sight of him, they covered his body excluding his nostrils in a layer of water, and the tall monk healed him until he could open his eyes. The white tiger came to notice too.

"Ah, Summoner — you're still alive?" He said. "...If you would know, it seems like the invasion was successfully halted while you were out. No need to exert yourself."

"Eat me," spat the demon-summoner. The fat monk, ignoring his words, healed him again.

The crow asked the demon-summoner if he was all right and offerred him a hand, while saying:

"I suppose it is true what they say in my village:

A feather can support you in flight

Or be an implement to write,

These attackers let themselves be mere feathers to allow a malevolent will to fly. Claws tearing at the hide of civilization. It is not understandable to me."

She looked around warily to be sure there are no more demons lurking about, and listened for any sounds of battle she couldn't see. While looking around, she walked over to the garden and tried to pick more plantain. Unexpectedly, the plantain turned out to be a demon, who then showed a human form and attacked with a steel-like hard fan.

"You ungrateful wench." She said. "You already did enough by picking some of my hair to heal your monk, and now you are going to pick more for a stupid idiot who almost killed himself? Know your place, crow spirit, eat this old lady's fan!"

We will say no more of the crow, the demon-concealer and their company as we go and check on the eldery monk and the White Bull Spirit.

The eldery monk looked at the oxen skeleton which used to be the burly man fighting alongside him the whole time. He was horrified. The ground around them had all split open, and it made the White Bull Spirit fall down and the eldery monk almost slip, and alerted the Earth and City Gods, who appeared and knelt down in a solid part of the land and begged the fallen ox skeleton to have mercy on them.

We will say no more of the eldery monk, the White Bull Spirit and the two local gods as we go and check on the travellers on the streets.
