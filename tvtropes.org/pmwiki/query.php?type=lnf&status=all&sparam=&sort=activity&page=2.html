Connected by a shared event

A major, singular Event that happened in the backstory, often years ago, that was experienced by multiple characters in the current plot. This leads to present-day friendships or grudges when the characters realize they both experienced this event, or recognizes someone else because they are Famed in Story or The Dreaded based on their prominent role in this Event. Or it could be two Action Survivors who just happened to meet at that time.

The shared event often involves great suffering or death, like a particularly deadly battle in wartime, mass execution, forced marches, etc. Even for people who didn't participate in it, pretty much everyone has heard about it.

When referencing this past event, expect stock quotes like "I remember that day", or "I was there", or "I remember you. You're the one who...".

The closest thing I could find is Mass Super-Empowering Event where many of the characters are connected by having gained superpowers from this shared Event, but I'm talking about cases where no superpowers are involved.

I don't think it's Noodle Incident or Cryptic Background Reference because usually such an Event gets heavily explained because it had such a lasting impact on so many lives.

Character is confronted with something so out of the ordinary that their priorities flip completely.

Do We Have This One If not, does it even qualify as a trope?

A character comes to see something so far out of their everyday experience that their lives have to be completely rethought. Examples:

In One Hundred Years of Solitude, there's a character "Remedíos the beauty", who has to be kept away from the public view, because any man that sees her beauty becomes obsessed with her, leading to several deaths.

There's a Czech short story. A foreigner comes to work on a lumbermill, but the other workers are suspicious of him and try to get him drunk. He takes revenge by getting them all to visit his house, where he is greeted by his wife, who, as a tender greeting, washes his feet (Jesus style). This comes across to the workers as impossible in their own lives, with marriages full of resentment. They beat up the foreigner for exposing the truth to them and get him to leave, but they cannot forget.

The Total Perspective Vortex (from The Restaurant at the End of the Universe). Seeing the entire universe and yourself in relation to it is certain to annihilate your brain.

The whole point of the Hero's Journey is the hidden world serving as this with regards to the known world, with the hero emerging into their "normal life" with a massive shift in priorities or perspective.

A couple tropes come close: Awful Truth, but that is purely negative and usually personal. These Are Things Man Was Not Meant to Know, but that is always destructive and typically coupled with Take Our Word for It. Opinion-Changing Dream is a subtrope as long as the opinion change comes from merely an unexpected combination of what you aready know (and not psychic means).

Steady Mood Deterioration

Watching a character slowly get upset over the course of a scene/event/etc, often changing subtly, with the implication being that the character is struggling to keep it together and isn't trying to be affected by everything.

I'm inspired by an episode of Electronic Game Information where Robby invites his friends Ben and Cricket on. Instead of being supportive, the two are dismissive, distracting, and stubborn about pushing their own ideas over Robby's attempt to explain the lore of his game (with Ben outright encouraging him to restart it from scratch). During this whole ordeal, Robby is blatantly struggling to keep his cool, and we see his mood progressively worsen as the episode goes on, with him being close to tears at the end of it. (It's all scripted, of course!)
