Ask The Tropers

Go To

Have a question about how the TVTropes wiki works? No one knows this community better than the people in it, so ask away! Ask the Tropers is the page you come to when you have a question burning in your brain and the support pages didn't help. It's not for everything, though. For a list of all the resources for your questions, click here.

Ask the Tropers is for:
  • • General questions about the wiki, how it works, and how to do things.
  • • Reports of problems with wiki articles, or requests for help with wiki articles.
  • • Reports of misbehavior or abuse by other tropers.

Ask the Tropers is not for:
  • • Help identifying a trope. See TropeFinder.
  • • Help identifying a work. See YouKnowThatShow.
  • • Asking if a trope example is valid. See the Trope Talk forum.
  • • Proposing new tropes. See TropeLaunchPad.
  • • Making bug reports. See QueryBugs.
  • • Asking for new wiki features. See QueryWishlist.
  • • Chatting with other tropers. See our forums.
  • • Reporting problems with advertisements. See this forum topic.
  • • Reporting issues on the forums. Send a Holler instead.

Is this an acceptable example of a Memetic Mutation (Eekum Bokum - hololive/Inugami Korone)? Web Original

So in the YMMV/Hololive page, there has been numerous attempts by different tropers to add Eekum Bokum, a meme associated with hololive vtuber Inugami Korone playing Banjo Kazooie, under the Memetic Mutation entry. For example, this is the most recent write-up:

  • Eekum Bokum Explanation Korone's playthrough of Banjo-Kazooie was her first exposure to the game as well as many of the voice lines the characters make; she found the noise for collecting a Mumbo Token particularly entertaining and would call them "Eekum Bokums" as well as imitating the sound for them. A Youtuber then made two animations using Korone's noises as audio, which then became formats of their own for many different fan edits, having either Korone or the Mumbo token saying different things or having conversations.

With somebody else mentioning this as a reason for justifying it as a Memetic Mutation entry:

"There are hundreds of edits of this animation on Youtube made by different people to the point that just searching "eekum bokum" there will flood the search page with them."

Now, under the ruleset we have in the source code of YMMV/Hololive (our way of handling the big misuse issue that has been plaguing Memetic Mutation since forever), it technically isn't a suitable entry. But as we have had similar dispute in the past, such as https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/posts.php?discussion=15914174190A77533500 , where a mod defined Memetic Mutation as follows:

"The original intent of Memetic Mutation is a meme... that mutated. That means it became something other than what it was originally based on. All fandoms develop memes, but this is TV Tropes, not TV Memes or TV Inside Jokes. By the original definition of Memetic Mutation, we wouldn't document in-jokes and fandom memes in Skyrim any more than we would some obscure MLP fanfic, only those memes that escaped and entered popular culture."

What if we also take this definition into account on whether an entry is acceptable or not? Would that change anything? I would like you guys' opinion on whether this Eekum Bokum meme would fall under said definition, thanks.
