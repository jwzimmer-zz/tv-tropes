The pages Trope Kilowicked and Trope Overdosed are meant to be a challenge: there are so many tropes in them that there are countless pages that link to them (at least 1000 and up to 1500 for the first one, 1500 or more for the other). However, there is a caveat to it: a work that comprises multiple works can be added if the unique wicks of the work itself and the sub works are enough. There are many problems with this.

  • First, what is, exactly, a work comprised of multiple works? For example, in "The Lord of the Rings" (both book and film) it may make sense to take the 3 entries in a single group, as it is not a standard film with 2 sequels but a single huge story released in 3 parts. Most of the examples, however, do not have anything special to them: they are common series, franchises, works and their adaptations, bands and members with solo careers, etc. Even a pair of myth pages, grouping their adaptations!
  • Second, to prevent abuse, the idea is to limit this to "unique wicks". Many related works are likely to repeat tropes (every Terminator film is likely to have A.I. Is a Crapshoot, many Batman works are likely to have a Jack Bauer Interrogation Technique, many X-Men works feature Fantastic Racism, etc), and so they should count as 1. Problem is, that's incredibly complex to count, both for adding and for checking. Most of the time things are simply based on the added numbers seen in each "Related".
  • Add to that that the hidden text invites to organize things by franchise bullets (franchise, subgroup, work), and we get nonsense like "Alien->The first film", instead of simply listing the first film of "Alien". In the end, it looks like a glorified repeat of the Franchise Index.

In the end, all this subverts the purpose of those pages: we are not really listing the work pages with the most links, but basically all works with other related works.

So I propose a change: each page listed on those pages should be kilowicked or overdosed on its own right, and go to the category it deserves according to the number seen in "related". No exception for series or franchise pages: judge them on their own specific number as well. And simplify the groupings: if "The First Avenger" and "Civil War" are both kilowicked (they are) group them under a "Captain America" bullet, but that's it.

To prove my point, let's do a check to Trope Kilowicked, a page that is supposed to list pages with 1000 wicks or more. We have 556 (unless I made a mistake when counting). The ones that do not meet those numbers, and are listed just because of this loophole, are the following.

Phediuk
Sep 29th 2020 at 12:58:14 PM

I agree with Piterpicher. Only a tiny minority of works have even reached Kilowicked status as-is, and there are already several grouped rankings for people who want the threshold to be even higher. Like him, I view these pages in a similar fashion Wikipedia's "Good" articles, in that they're an indication of which works have received considerable attention from the community; the most-wicked works are generally also the most-developed work pages.

Separating franchises strictly into individual pages would really kneecap a lot of them; the Super Sentai franchise, for instance, is Saturated, consistently racking up hundreds of wicks every single season, without any individual season being Kilowicked. Nonetheless, the franchise considered as a whole is extremely well-developed and highly-wicked. This information would be lost if franchises were not grouped together.

My point is that some works are better considered as series rather than as individual pages. Grouping franchises keeps diffuse series with many medium-wicked pages on the list while also retaining individual highly-wicked pages. It's the most inclusive option, but still a high enough threshold that only a minuscule portion of all works on the site meet it. Again, I see these pages as first and foremost a way to see which works have reached a "developed" state of troping, and often, that development spans multiple works within a franchise. Both paths of development are valid and should, I believe, be recognized.

I'd be just fine creating a separate page listing works on a strictly-individual basis, but implementing that at the expense of the current page would be a real loss IMO. What we've got here is a really useful tool and I believe we should keep it.
