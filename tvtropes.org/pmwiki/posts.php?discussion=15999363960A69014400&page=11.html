Anyway, this discussion has spurred my desire to work on one of my new LGBT trope ideas so we can cover more ground and maybe get into fewer stupid discussions like this. The two ideas I've pitched before that I wanna work on:

  • Being LGBT used to emphasize a character's status as a social outcast of some sort, whether it's a loner or a Manic Pixie Dream Girl or a Cloudcuckoolander. Not sure exactly how to narrow this down, though. Mean Girls is one example I have in mind (a gay character and a character Mistaken for Gay, who is Ambiguously Gay in the musical adaptation, make up the "outcast" group).
  • Plots where a character gets pressured by other LGBT characters, usually their partner, to come out, usually to avoid having a Secret Relationship. Examples in The Prom, Out, and a musical called In Transit that doesn't seem to have a page yet.

The second is the one I could work on sooner - it's definitely a trope, and quite a controversial one in the community. The first one is something I can't quite place, but it's the closest I can find to another distinct bisexual trope.

It's one of these things that I know exists in some way but can't narrow down to a specific pattern. It's hard to explain.
