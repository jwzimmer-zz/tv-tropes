  • Chainsaw Good: Crazy Carl busts out a chainsaw to help protect Tom from Robotnik. Wade has to move it aside because Carl is standing uncomfortably close to him with it running.

  • Chekhov's Skill:
    • Sonic finds that under the right emotional stress he can generate extreme amounts of electrical power, he blows his cover with a massive EMP that alerts the military. He further catches on in a highway chase against Robotnik's drones. He masters the spinball power against Robotnik in the climax, ricocheting hundreds of times off his vehicle before shattering it completely.
    • Subverted with Maddie; she is a veterinarian and when Sonic is injured Tom expects her to figure out his biology to help him. She points out it doesn't work that way, considering she has been trained to handle regular animals and he is an anthropomorphic alien, but she uses traditional human first aid to get Sonic back on his feet.

  • Chewing the Scenery: Robotnik does this in just the first few minutes of his introduction, interrupting one of the generals with a loud, demanding voice. He doesn't slow down from there.

  • Clear My Name: Robotnik frames Tom for the EMP blackout after the latter and Sonic escape, resulting in him being branded as a terrorist.

  • Companion Cube: In the first stinger, Robotnik, stranded on the Mushroom Planet, has made himself a new "Agent Stone" to keep him company/boss around... by taking a literal stone, carving it into the likeness of the real one, and throwing it around to go on "Rock-connaissance".

  • Costume Evolution:
    • The film provides an interesting example with Sonic's shoes: when he's forced to flee his homeworld as a child, he wears a pair of brown boots; later replaced with a series of beat-up running shoes as a teenager hiding out in Green Hills, finally acquiring his friction-proof red running shoes before the finale of the film.
    • Robotnik first appears wearing an all-black ensemble before switching his wardrobe in the film's climax to a red flight suit with a pair of goggles. When he ends up stranded on the mushroom planet, his flight suit becomes tattered, and his mustache becomes wild and frizzy. He also completely shaves his hair with a sharp piece of his destroyed hoverjet, establishing his Bald of Evil.

  • Crazy-Prepared:
    • When Tom's letter from the SFPD arrives, Maddie actually got two celebration cakes ready in case he was accepted or rejected by the letter.
    • Robotnik sends a large, six-wheeled tank drone after Tom's truck when he manages to locate it. Sonic accidentally takes it out with a spin dash, so Robotnik causes a second, car-sized drone to deploy from the wreckage. Sonic disables that one using its own EMP discs, and from that emerges a unicycle drone. When that's taken out, its eye opens up and deploys a tiny helicopter drone with a cutting laser. When Sonic dismantles the thing, its camera is revealed to be a Sticky Bomb that takes them some effort to remove. They manage, but Sonic fails to get clear in time and is knocked out by the blast.

My standards are (probably not an exhaustive list):

  • does it describe their character from a general standpoint? (e.g. personality, archetype, background)
  • does it describe their character development?
  • is it something repeatedly associated with this character?
  • is it an appearance or ability trope that does not apply to every member of a group to which they belong?
    • (this one can be tricky. if all vampires in a setting are said to have red eyes, but only one vampire character ever shows up, I'd be fine putting Red Eyes, Take Warning in their character section. on the other hand if half the cast are vampires, I would not put it in each individual vampire character's section)
