Lightysnake
Aug 31st 2020 at 6:43:44 AM

Here's my proposal:

  • The character is working towards some sort of goal, often towards liberation/salvation, at least outwardly. For whom can depend on context, hence why Griffith could make it with all his messianic trappings as he presents as a messiah while being self-inerested, in comparison to the main villain of Drifters who is a genuine messiah to the downtrodden monster races while seeking the elimination of humanity.
  • The character has, or is able to garner, a large following as a result of thee goals/their charisma
  • The means must be extreme or evil.

There's nothing that should prevent an overlap between an antichrist and a dark messiah, but they're not the same thing as the anti-christ is a specific role, meant to serve as an evil counterpart to a directly messianic figure or to be a figure bent on achieving the world's damnation.

Religious trappings can enhance it, of course. "Figure who gives themselves the appearance of a savior but is actually evil or working to dark/well extremist intention goals" doesn't seem too hard.
