Cleo's Ship; Nighttime

(It's very late in the evening. Cleo is in the captain's quarter's of her boat, lying in her bed. Her sister and Melody have long since been asleep, but Cleo remains awake. She has been tossing and turning all throughout the night, thinking about this whole situation and now also her concerns about Melody's anomalous abilities as a Psydon. Feeling that she needs some way to relax, she reaches under her bed and pulls out her diary.)

(Cleo has kept a diary since she was around Melody's age, and it goes as far back as the first time that Cleo and Ira first met. After reading over the entries she wrote about that for a bit, Cleo closes her eyes and sighs as she reminiscences on these happier times when she and Ira were friends and she wasn't trying to stop him from using the Legendary Egg.)

Zzzzz...

Years Ago

(A young Cleo is sitting behind her house in Scale Cove, crying distraught tears. Her crying gets the attention of a young Ira as he passes by the house. Wondering what could be wrong on a day like this, he leans against the fence.) Hey, what's wrong? (He asks Cleo.)

(Cleo is briefly startled upon hearing someone speak to her, and her slender eyes get as big as saucers when she sees that the person who walked up was none other than the chief's son.) Wha-...you're...I... (She stammers, trying to stop crying.) Oh, how embarrassing...I don't want you to see me like this!

Sorry, I just heard you crying and was wondering what was wrong. (Ira says, shrugging.)

(Cleo starts to stand up, but pauses.) Well...uh...wait, why is this any of your business?

(Ira shrugs again.) My mom and pop always tell me to lend a claw if someone looks like they need it. I though might have wanted help, so I came over.

I dunno what you could even do about what's going on. (Cleo says, rubbing her eyes.) My parents just told me that they are going to go to a council meeting tonight.

(Ira looks confused.) So...they're going to a council meeting? That's i-...OH...oh, it's the one over in Rialon my mom and pop are going to, too, isn't it?

(Cleo nods.) Tonight's my sister's graduation from Psydon training and our parents are going to miss almost all of it! (She says.) They're always going to meetings, and it makes my sister sad, and when she's sad, I get sad...

(Ira frowns.) Oh...that really stinks. I'm sorry.

(Cleo nods.) Yeah...I mean, again, I dunno what you could do about that. (She says.)

(Ira thinks for a moment before coming up with an idea.) Maybe I could pull some strings and convince your parents to go to your sister's graduation after all?

(Cleo looks shocked.) How could you even do that?

I'll just tell my dad what's going on and maybe he'll tell your parents that they don't have to go. Then they can go to your sister's thing. (Ira says.)

(Cleo isn't sure what to say.) I mean...um...you really don't HAVE to do that...

It's at least worth a shot, isn't it? (Ira says, turning to go.) I'll tell you if it worked!

(He pauses as he starts to leave.) Hey, what's your name by the way?

Cleo. (Cleo says.) It's...um...it's an honor to meet the chief's son.

(Ira waves good-bye as he heads back in the direction of the chief's mansion. Cleo finds herself feeling both giddy about getting to meet the chief's son in person and a bit uneasy about what said chief's son plans to do for her and Jasmine at the same time...)

Around two days' sailing later...

It's mid-afternoon as the Zenpance finally draws near its destination.

The mood surrounding Abasta Island couldn't be more different from Klaer. It's a calm, cool day, with minimal clouds and no wind to speak of, leaving the island clearly visible the moment it passes over the horizon. In contrast to Klaer's harsh appearance, Abasta has a sleepy, pleasant look, with small villages dotted across a landscape of gently rolling green hills. There is, however, one slightly imposing feature. Positioned near the exact centre of the island is a vast cathedral, its gleaming spires towering so far over the rest of the landscape that even from this distance, it completely dominates the skyline. On the beach the Zenpance is approaching is a small, rustic, but clearly manned harbour, with a few small fishing boats docked beside the stone pier.
