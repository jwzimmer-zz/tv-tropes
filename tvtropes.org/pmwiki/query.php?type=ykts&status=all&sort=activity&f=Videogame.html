I strongly believe this game was downloaded and not a browser game; it was in many of the units of the computer lab in my former British school (in the name is the word British, but it's not in Britain). I take this to be about 7 to 9 years ago.

I remember at least two stages of the game, but I never got that far into them. The first stage starts outside a cottage in broad daylight, which we have to click on to enter. Going upstairs leads to an obstacle which can be passed by picking an apple back at the outside area of the cottage(because my memory's kinda fuzzy, it could've been a different object, like a stick). I don't recall what happens after that. But going to the right when you enter the house leads you to the kitchen, where the only interactive (maybe) was a bubbling cauldron over a fire. But it's actually a trick! Clicking on it will show the wicked witch's unnerving face cackling at you before sending you back outside. The most unnerving feature of her face are her animated eyes, although still in the 2D kid-fantasy style, they were rather spooky.

—

The second stage I remember instead displayed a map with all the locations you can go to, some of them being a marsh, a castle, maybe a house among a couple other places. The key to get into the castle has something to do with the marsh, but I don't recall exactly if that's where the key is retrieved or where we have to feed the key to someone. It's probably the latter; and feeding the key to whatever was at the marsh before unlocking the castle, once you go there and it reveals the need of the key, the witch appears again as a sign of the player's failure. Even if I can't play it anymore I'd like to know how it ends lol.

Okay, I've only seen this game once at a video arcade, and I can't find out anymore about it.

During the intro animation, bounty hunter says something along the lines of "Nice planet! Think I'll save it, for a price!" He's wearing full-body power armor in the intro but the game is first-person so you can't see yourself while playing.

Only other thing I remember about it is there's flying saucers at the top of the screen that will try to abduct human civilians on the ground with tractor beams. I think the screen warns "MUTATED LIFEFORM" when this happens.

No, it's not "Area 51," the tone is a little more cartoonish and the characters are all animated, no live-action screen captures. This felt like a post-"House of the Dead" style game, either from the late '90s or early 2000s.

3D Computer game teaching human anatomy Videogame

I wrote the following description in the comments of another query of mine years ago. I did it because both this game and the other game I asked about were part of a CD collection I used to have in the mid-to-late 2000's. I see now that it was a dumb idea to post a query in the comments, so I'm reposting it here. I added a little more info I remember after the second line.

It is a 3D first-person game meant to teach kids about the human body. You're in some sort of laboratory, with multiple buildings connected by bridges, and each building contains info on each system of the body.
I think the buildings for each body system were arranged in a circle around a central building, but I can't remember what was in the central building. Each system building had a bridge connecting to the central building and to the adjacent buildings.

I also remember that there were some 3D simulations within the game. There's one where you take the first-person perspective of a blood cell travelling through the circulatory system. You could see other blood cells around you, going in the same direction at various speeds. I can't remember if you could really do anything, though.

2D Bringing Back Martian Life Game Videogame

It was a 2 dimensional kinda-Platformer. Player was an astronaut sent to explore some caverns on Mars which could maybe have life. Plants were found — lots of types, introduced one or two at a time. Player picks up a seed by stepping on a plant — can shoot/launch the seed at a Fertile Spot to grow that plant. Lots of seeds have effects.. One of the earliest introduced plants had water-seeds. Water seeds would explode in a little burst of water on impact if they didn't hit a Fertile Spot. Plants, especially when first introduced, would often be Dead, and require the player to shoot them with a water seed in order to bring them back to life. Fertile Spots also could be particular conditions — I think Normal, Acidic, Basic? Plants would only be able to grow in one of those three. Certain seeds could be used to change one type of soil to another. As the player went on and explored more of the caverns, there were more and more puzzles to be solved with using specific seeds on specific targets in order to progress. The player also discovered more and more about the planet — I think there was something about an intelligent race on Mars which went extinct and the player discovered that/what happened to them from some building they left in the caverns?
